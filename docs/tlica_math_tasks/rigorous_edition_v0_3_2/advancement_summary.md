## Part B. v0.3 corrections applied to the rigorous edition

The v0.3 revision applies the following categories of correction, each addressing one or more findings in the external verification report.

The verification-pathway typing in §0.14 is repaired. Tools are now typed as relations on the enlarged content domain $\mathsf{C}^m_t = \mathsf{Int} \cup \mathsf{A}^m_t$, with pathways permitted to begin at an intrinsic terminus and end at an asymptotic-content head. This closes the typing defect by which the first transition of any pathway was ill-typed in v0.2.

A new section §1.7.3 in `01_objects.md` introduces the content-to-history projection $\pi^m_t : \mathsf{A}^m_t \to \mathsf{H}^m_t$ by $\pi^m_t(x) = [(t, x)]$. The convention $\rho_{m,t}(x) := \rho_{m,t}(\pi^m_t(x))$ for $x \in \mathsf{A}^m_t$ closes the typing gap between $\rho$'s definition on $V^m_t$ and its use in profile constructions on $\mathsf{A}^m_t$. The projection is applied throughout the dependent files: §3.6 of the coordinates file, §4.1 through §4.5 of the profiles file, and §6.2 through §6.5 of the independence file.

Two new axioms are introduced in §0.14 to close the φ-tier defect. The φ-admissibility axiom, $\epsilon_{m,t}(x) + n_{m,t}(x) \cdot \delta \le 1$ for every $x \in \mathrm{Verif}(m, t)$, restores the bound stated in line 181 of the core architecture but omitted in v0.2. The Tier-3 strictness axiom, $n_{m,t}(x) \cdot \delta + \epsilon_{m,t}(x) > \delta$ for every $x \in \mathrm{Verif}(m, t) \cap \mathsf{A}^m_t$, is the cogito-uniqueness postulate at the φ level: no Tier-3 content attains the cogito's structural saturation. Proposition 3.3.7 in §3.3 is reproven on the basis of both axioms, yielding a genuine theorem rather than the defective v0.2 proof.

The profile definition in §4.1 is split into three layers to restore core faithfulness. The base identity-correlation profile $P^\rho_{m,t} = \{(x, \rho_{m,t}(\pi^m_t(x))) : x \in \mathsf{A}^m_t\}$ matches the core's primary structural object exactly (core line 219). The mode-vector profile $\vec P^\rho_{m,t}$ matches the core's "full vector profile, retaining mode-specific structure" (core line 223). The full-coordinate profile $P^{\rho\phi\kappa}_{m,t}$ retains the v0.2 four-tuple over the common domain $\mathrm{Dom}(m, t)$ but is now explicitly labeled as a derived object rather than as the architecture's primary structural object. This corrects the silent strengthening flagged by the verifier.

The lived-I definition in §4.2 is split into two layers. The support-lived-I network $\mathfrak{L}^m_t = \{x \in \mathsf{A}^m_t : \rho_{m,t}(\pi^m_t(x)) > 0\}$ matches the core (line 232). The thresholded lived-I network $\mathfrak{L}^{m,\theta}_t$ at level $\theta_{m,t} = \Theta^\rho_m(\cdot)$ is retained as a derived or application-level refinement. The threshold open function is renamed from $B^k_m$, which collided with the imprinting open function, to $\Theta^\rho_m$ per verifier §8.7.

The shell partition in §4.3 is corrected so that all seven shells together cover $V^m_t$ exhaustively; the v0.2 version omitted the interval $(0, r_5)$. The strict-not-I region is now defined separately as $\mathsf{N}^0_t = \{x \in V^m_t \setminus \{\hat\iota_m\} : \rho_{m,t}(x) = 0\}$, a subset of $\mathsf{Sh}_6$ recognized as a limit notion per the core's "approaches zero" language (line 232).

The update-rule range commentary in §5.2.2 is corrected. Range preservation under $\Pi_{[0,1]}$ holds unconditionally for $\alpha, \beta \in [0, 1]$, including when $\alpha + \beta > 1$. The condition $\alpha + \beta \le 1$ is reclassified as a convexity or interpretability condition rather than a range condition.

Theorem 6.5.1 is restated as the nonexistence of three specific typed functions $F_\rho$, $F_\phi$, $F_\kappa$ across admissible model instances, rather than as a single-model statement. A new Remark 6.5.2 clarifies that the universal non-reducibility is compatible with model-local statistical or functional dependencies, which are part of dynamic entanglement.

Several minor presentational corrections are applied. The reference to "Lemma 2.3" in Proposition 1.1's proof is corrected to "Lemma 2.4.2". The convergence-related "for some $k^*$" phrasing in Proposition 2.5.1 is rewritten as "for every $k$". The "infimum $\inf_x$ approaches 1" slip in §2.5.2 is corrected to "supremum $\sup_x$ approaches 1". Proposition 2.8.1's proviso is clarified to a subnetwork-embedding statement. The local-finiteness / finite-reach decomposition in §2.2 is explicitly factorized so that local finiteness is not claimed to entail finite max-flow, and background max-flow theory is cited (Aharoni, Berger, Georgakopoulos, Perlstein, and Sprüssel; Lochbihler).

## Part E. Recommended next moves after v0.3

The verification report's recommendations remain the natural next steps.

The verifier identifies Lean as the natural first pass for ordered-field arithmetic, summable weighted aggregation, uniform convergence by the M-test, the persistence-quotient directed system, and the finite witness models of Chapter 6. Isabelle/HOL is identified as the natural framework for the infinite max-flow background, given Lochbihler's existing formalization of the countable max-flow/min-cut theorem. Rocq is identified as workable for the finite witness models and inductive pathway systems but less attractive for the infinite-network material absent a pre-existing graph-theory library commitment. The recommended priority order is to Lean-encode Lemma 2.4.2, Proposition 2.5.1, Proposition 5.7.2, Proposition 1.2, and Theorem 6.5.1, in that order, and to cite Lochbihler's Isabelle formalization for the countable max-flow background rather than re-proving it from scratch.

The verifier identifies ten substantive open-function commitments implicit in the application papers that could be lifted to the foundation. The strongest near-term candidates are the live-option construction operator from the agency and free-will papers, the Mode-B reflective availability predicate, the operator-bundle selection schema, the effort or resistance cost profile, the ownership-integration predicate, the attribution profile and empirical interpretation profile, the temporal integration-cost or bandwidth-scaling function from temporal phenomenology, the profile-shape comparison metric from affect and agency, the Π-position or Π-preservation ranking from affect and religiously-charged experience, and the toolset-capacity and verification-toolkit development functions. The verifier recommends lifting only the functions needed to close profile comparison, access dynamics, and agency-facing live-option construction in the next revision, rather than attempting all ten at once.

The verifier identifies the profile-comparison metric (`oq:profile-comparison`, Gap 12) as the highest-leverage next target. It directly addresses one of the two genuinely open foundation gaps and would be reused across the affect, agency, and temporal-phenomenology applications. The natural form is either a restriction-to-common-subdomain construction with pointwise distance in a chosen norm, a Wasserstein-style transport distance on the distribution of triples, or a topological invariant of the lived-I network. The latter two are conceptually heavier but more robust against domain mismatch.

A constructive theory of the equivalence relation $\sim^m_H$ remains genuinely open and conceptually substantive. The current apparatus uses $\sim^m_H$ axiomatically. A constructive criterion, perhaps based on continuity of substrate state and continuity of source attribution, would advance the formal apparatus considerably and is recommended as a medium-priority target.

## Part F. v0.3.1 micro-corrections from the second-round verification

The second-round engagement-forcing verification, conducted with a protocol requiring verbatim quotation of specified file content before any closure judgment, returned eleven of thirteen tasks as verified, one as verified with corrections, and one closure judgment as defective. The two corrections required, both surgical and applied in v0.3.1, are as follows.

The first correction addresses an arithmetic typo in Part A of this summary. The corrected gap-count summary sentence at the end of Part A previously read "seven fully resolved by commitment or proof" while parenthetically listing only five gaps (1, 2, 4, 6, 10). Five plus three (clean-up) plus one (tentative) plus one (signature-only) plus two (open) sums to twelve; seven plus the same residuals would not. The word "seven" was therefore a typo for "five", introduced while correcting the corresponding v0.2 arithmetic error. The sentence has been rewritten to read "five fully resolved by commitment or proof" in v0.3.1.

The second correction addresses a domain-typing error in Theorem 6.5.1. The functions $F_\rho$ and $F_\kappa$ were declared in v0.3 with first input domain $(1 - \delta - \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0})$, intended to capture admissible φ-values. The notation is inadequate in a non-Archimedean field because the admissible φ-values produced by the witnessing scenarios of Chapter 6 include values such as $1 - 2\delta - e$ for real $e$, which are not of the form $1 - \delta - r$ for any real $r \ge 0$ since $\delta + e$ involves the non-real infinitesimal $\delta$. In v0.3.1 the admissible φ-domain is defined as the $\mathbb{K}$-valued half-open interval $\mathsf{D}_\phi := \{\alpha \in \mathbb{K} : 0 \le \alpha < 1 - \delta\}$, and the function declarations are updated to use $\mathsf{D}_\phi$. A short notational prelude before Theorem 6.5.1 records the definition and explains why it strictly contains the previous interval-style notation. The proof of the theorem is unchanged.

These two corrections complete the verification cycle that began with the first-round v0.2 verification report and continued through the v0.3 revision and the second-round engagement-forcing verification. The next-iteration recommendations of Part E remain in force, with the additional note that the v0.3.1 state is now ready for proof-assistant encoding without further hand-level repair.

## Part G. Lean 4 verification milestone (v0.3.2)

The v0.3.1 state, with both the hand-level rigorous edition and the second-round engagement-forcing verification report having concluded the editorial cycle, was identified as the natural starting point for proof-assistant encoding. Lean 4 with mathlib4 was selected as the encoding framework on the verifier's recommendation, which cited the priority targets' character — ordered-field algebra with named axioms, finite-countermodel constructions, and weighted-sum convergence — as the natural fit for Lean's tactic and typeclass infrastructure.

Five priority theorems were identified for first-pass encoding. The first is Lemma 2.4.2 (the strict ρ-bound `C / (1 + C) < 1` for non-negative real `C`), a two-line warm-up. The second is Proposition 3.3.7 (the cogito as the unique supremum of φ), a single-step linarith reduction after unfolding the φ-admissibility and Tier-3 strictness axioms. The third is Proposition 2.5.1 (the scalar mode aggregation: summability, non-negative lower bound, ≤ 1 upper bound, and strict-off-cogito bound). The fourth is Proposition 5.7.2 (uniform convergence of the mode aggregation by the Weierstrass M-test). The fifth is Theorem 6.5.1 (formal non-reducibility of the three coordinates), in the v0.3.1 reformulation with the admissible φ-domain $\mathsf{D}_\phi$.

The encoding work was conducted in two compilation rounds with Claude Code as the proof-assistant operator. The first round closed nine iteration cycles, all of which were either mathlib API drift between drafting and the v4.11.0 environment (eight of nine) or typeclass-inference issues under relaxed auto-implicit (one of nine). No category-3 tactic divergence and no category-4 substantive logical issues were encountered, indicating that the proofs as drafted were mathematically correct and that only setup adjustments were needed. Four of the five theorems compiled green at the end of round one; the fifth was deliberately left as a statement-only skeleton with a `True`-typed placeholder body, awaiting completion. The second round closed one additional iteration cycle (an unused-hypothesis lint correction on the new Dynamics body) and added the Weierstrass M-test proof body via the `tendstoUniformly_tsum` lemma in `Mathlib.Analysis.NormedSpace.FunctionSeries`. The final build is green at mathlib v4.11.0 with no warnings, on 1295 modules.

All five priority theorems now type-check with their substantive conclusions intact. The statements were not modified during the encoding; only proofs, imports, and a small number of type ascriptions and `noncomputable` annotations were adjusted. The v0.3.1 $\mathsf{D}_\phi$ repair was explicitly exercised in the encoding: the proof of `no_F_phi` (a constituent of Theorem 6.5.1) uses a coordinate-triple witness with φ-value $1 - 2\delta - 1/2$, which lies in $\mathsf{D}_\phi$ but is not in the v0.3 notation $(1 - \delta - \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0})$ because $\delta + 1/2$ is not a real number in a non-Archimedean field. Without the v0.3.1 repair, the witness would have been ill-typed and Theorem 6.5.1 would have been provable only in a narrower form. The proof assistant confirms this dependency.

The Lean project is packaged as `tlica-lean-round2.zip`. Mappings from each Lean declaration to its location in the v0.3.1 rigorous edition are documented in the project's `MAPPING.md`. The mathlib version is pinned at v4.11.0 in the lakefile; future maintenance can either keep this pin and bump Lean and mathlib together periodically, or move to mathlib master and bump the toolchain pin in lockstep. The former is more reproducible; the latter follows the latest API.

The Lean verification establishes that the priority slate of the rigorous edition is mechanically correct at the level of formal type-checking. It does not, by itself, establish faithfulness to the substantive doctrine of the core architecture — that work was completed in the v0.3 / v0.3.1 cycle through the engagement-forcing external verification protocol. The combination of the two is the appropriate two-track validation for the project: external substantive verification on the rigorous edition's faithfulness to the core architecture, plus machine verification of the proofs themselves. The v0.3.2 milestone closes the proof-assistant encoding phase for the priority slate and frees the next phase of work to address the genuinely open foundation questions, of which `oq:profile-comparison` (Gap 12) and `oq:phenomenology-status-axioms` (Gap 11) remain the highest-leverage targets.
