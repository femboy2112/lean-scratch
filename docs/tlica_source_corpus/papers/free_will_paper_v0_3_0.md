# 1. Abstract

The classical free-will debate proceeds as if "free will" named a single contested property that agents either possess or lack. Compatibilist, libertarian, hard-incompatibilist, illusionist, phenomenological, and wisdom-tradition voices each advance powerful arguments, none compels the others, and the debate has shown the shape of an unresolvable trichotomy because the question they share bundles structurally distinct issues — live-option construction, evaluative engagement, sourcehood, ownership-integration, reflective availability, condition-failure modes, and metaphysical residue — into one binary verdict. This paper does not argue for or against any of the standard positions. Drawing on the Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture (Foundation v5.3.3), it replaces the single contested property with a structural definition of free choice through six interdependent conditions: contrastive live-option construction by the verification-toolkit, evaluative engagement, operator-bundle selection, non-preemptive substrate coupling, non-confabulatory Mode B reflective availability, and ownership-integration. The conditions yield a three-tier configuration of free selection, free choice proper, and wholehearted free choice, and the definition is implementation-neutral on substrate-metaphysics within a lawful-frame qualifier specifying minimal structural regularity for the architecture's coordinates and persistence relations to be definable.

Manipulation supplies the paradigm case. An agent may act wholeheartedly from an option-space another agent has shaped, with ownership intact while sourcehood is compromised, exposing dissociations the standard categories cannot track. The architecture therefore separates ownership, source-status, responsibility, and accountability as distinct structural relations rather than collapsing them into a single verdict. The paper engages eight alternative frameworks — Frankfurt-class hierarchical identification, reasons-responsiveness compatibilism, attributionism, hard incompatibilism, libertarianism, illusionism, predictive coding and active inference, and the phenomenology / existentialism / wisdom-tradition family — under a translation-not-refutation discipline that identifies overlaps, refused reductions, and discriminating commitments without claiming any framework is wrong. It catalogues principled metaphysical residue (ultimate sourcehood, primal not-I dependence, lawful-frame grounding, normative sufficiency) and theological resonance under a strict translation-not-derivation discipline.

The architecture is compatibilist-coherent without being a compatibilist proof, sourcehood-sensitive without satisfying ultimate-sourcehood demands, and open to no-agent translation without erasing the indexed modeling I. It does not solve the metaphysical problem of free will, prove or refute any standard position, or license theological identification. It transforms the problem-space: from undifferentiated debate over a single contested property to a structured field of analyzable distinctions. The companion paper *Agency Architecture* develops the structural taxonomy of agency variation, attribution apparatus, empirical interface, and formal predictions that this paper's six-condition definition anchors.

**Keywords:** free will; free choice; manipulation; sourcehood; ownership; compatibilism; hard incompatibilism; Frankfurt-class hierarchical identification; reasons-responsiveness; phenomenology; Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture

# 2. Introduction: from the free-will question to a structural architecture


## 2.1 The problem with "the problem of free will"

Consider an agent who chooses wholeheartedly from an option-space that another agent has quietly shaped. The action feels owned. It expresses real priorities. It integrates seamlessly into the agent's lived sense of self. It meets every internal condition the agent could check from the inside: live alternatives were considered, reasons were weighed, the chosen option resonated with the agent's values, the act issued from those values rather than from intrusive impulses. By any first-person measure available to the agent in the moment, the choice was free. Yet the option-space itself was source-compromised: the alternatives among which the agent deliberated had been engineered by another agent's interests, and the agent did not know this.

A theory that asks only "Did the agent act freely?" or "Was the choice theirs?" flattens this case. The available answers — *yes*, *no*, *yes but constrained* — are too coarse to capture what is structurally true: ownership is intact while sourcehood is compromised; the agent's reflective endorsement of the action is real while the upstream shaping of the option-space remains hidden; responsibility may be partial along several dimensions; accountability for repair may still obtain even where original-act responsibility is reduced. Each available framework can capture some of these features at the cost of losing others. Compatibilist endorsement-only accounts capture the genuine ownership but risk over-attributing responsibility. Hard-incompatibilist ultimate-sourcehood accounts capture the source-compromise but risk erasing the real ownership that remains. Illusionist or report-fallibility accounts can capture the limits of introspective access but typically do not distinguish source-compromised-but-owned action from confabulated or alienated action. Phenomenological and wisdom-tradition accounts can illuminate the lived experience but do not yet supply apparatus for tracking the dissociations themselves.

The difficulty is not that any of these accounts is wrong. The difficulty is that the question they share — *was this an exercise of free will?* — bundles too many structurally distinct issues into a single verdict. Live-option construction, evaluative engagement, operator-bundle selection, substrate coupling, reflective availability, ownership-integration, source-status, developmental-window status, cross-order coordination, cascade effects, empirical-marker interpretation, responsibility-attribution, accountability for repair, and phenomenological integrity are not one property. They are a structured family of dissociable relations. The free-will literature recurrently forces them through a single contested label, and the resulting debate has the shape of an unresolvable trichotomy: powerful arguments on each side, none compelling the others, all turning on intuitions about a single property whose internal structure remains unanalyzed.

This paper proposes a different move. Instead of arguing for or against any of the standard positions, it replaces the single contested property with a structural architecture. Ownership, source-status, responsibility, accountability, Mode B reflective availability, condition-failure modes, order-engagement profiles, and cascade-attribution become dissociable relations that can be tracked separately and recombined without zero-sum collapse. The classical debate is not refuted or absorbed; its components are decomposed into structurally specifiable distinctions, each available for separate philosophical, empirical, and practical engagement. The architecture's promise is not that "free will is real" or "free will is illusory" but that the question becomes structurally analyzable once its components are given independent analytical apparatus.

As an orientation-level illustration rather than a completed attribution analysis, the manipulation case can be parsed using the architecture's apparatus as follows: ownership-integration is intact (the action expressed the agent's evaluative profile); sourcehood is compromised (the option-space was upstream-shaped); ownership-recognition by the agent is unimpaired (no alienation report); Mode B reflective availability is intact for the chosen action but not for the source-shaping pathway; responsibility-attribution for the act in question is qualified by the source-compromise rather than settled by ownership alone, with the full attribution profile determining whether and how original-act responsibility is reduced; accountability for explanation, mitigation, or future repair may obtain despite the source-compromise. None of these claims requires the manipulation case to be either a genuine exercise of free will or a non-exercise of agency. The case is what it structurally is — a particular configuration of profile-values along several dissociable axes — and the architecture's job is to make that configuration analytically tractable. The same disposition extends across all the configurations the literature has called "free," "unfree," "compelled," "manipulated," "alienated," "automatic," "chosen," and "wholehearted." Each is a distinct structural configuration; none is a single binary verdict.


## 2.2 The architectural wager: agency as inside-perspective structure

The architecture's positive stance is that agency is best understood as an inside-perspective structural configuration anchored in the indexed modeling I. The primary object is not behavior viewed from third-person observation, not neural readiness signals, not folk responsibility judgments, and not a metaphysical power added to the substrate. It is the I-relative agency profile: the configuration of live-option construction, evaluative engagement, operator-bundle selection, substrate coupling, Mode B reflective availability, ownership-integration, and identity-correlation that obtains at any moment across the agent's developed orders of willing. This profile operates under what the paper calls the lawful-frame qualifier — the minimal structural regularity required for the architecture's coordinates and persistence relations to be definable — but the architecture takes no further position on whether the substrate is deterministic, stochastic, or otherwise. Implementation candidates from neuroscience and cognitive science remain open; they are not reductions of the architecture's structural object.

The inside-perspective commitment is load-bearing. The architecture is perspectivally realist about other agents and can analyze collective and institutional structures through role-indexed profiles and relational cascades, without positing an unindexed group mind. It refuses to collapse the first-person structural reality of agency into third-person control-system variables, neural correlates, or social-attribution practices, while remaining open to mapping these as implementation candidates or empirical markers of architecturally specified objects. The architecture's task is to describe agency as it is structurally available from the inside, with apparatus precise enough to engage external paradigms without being reduced by them.

This stance is not new to this paper. The Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture, established in Foundation v5.3.3, introduces the inside-perspective discipline at the foundation level: the indexed modeling I as primitive anchor; identity-correlation $\rho$ as the relation by which contents are integrated with the I; the verification-toolkit as the developed capacity that constructs, evaluates, and inhabits available contents; the foundation coordinates $\kappa$ (contact), $\phi$ (source-pathway / truth-verification access), $\rho$ (identity-correlation), and $\Pi$ (preservation-ranking), together with the phenomenal-availability status predicate $A_{m,t}(x)$; the prerogatives $PtCns$ (consistency) and $PCE$ (continued existence); and the three modes of I-development (A, B, C). Two prior application papers — *Temporal Phenomenology* (Application 1) and *Differentiated Affect* (Application 2) — have already extended that foundation to specific phenomenal domains, showing how the inside-perspective architecture can support structural accounts of temporal experience and love-anchored affective configurations without requiring new foundation-level primitives.

The present paper is the third application. It applies the same foundation to agency, and like its predecessors it adds no new foundation-level primitives. What it does add at the application level are four section-level structural objects that perform separate analytical work — the attribution profile, the empirical interpretation profile, the prediction profile, and the framework translation profile — each tailored to one of the agency domain's distinctive analytical tasks. These objects are introduced where they are needed (Sections 9 through 12) and are not interchangeable; the paper's analytical leverage depends on keeping them distinct.


## 2.3 Why free choice is the canonical case

Free choice proper serves as the paper's definitional anchor. This is a methodological choice, not a metaphysical privilege. Free choice is the configuration in which the architecture's structural commitments are most fully exercised and therefore most visible: all six interdependent conditions of free choice hold simultaneously — contrastive live-option construction by the verification-toolkit, evaluative engagement, operator-bundle selection, non-preemptive substrate coupling, non-confabulatory Mode B reflective availability, and ownership-integration — across one or more orders of willing. When all six conditions are active, the structural roles that distinguish free choice from compelled, manipulated, automatic, or thinner agency-configurations are maximally legible. From that fully-exercised case, every other configuration in the agency taxonomy can be derived by structural variation: by relaxing a condition, narrowing the order-engagement, compromising the source-pathway, restricting Mode B availability, weakening ownership-integration, or introducing cascade dependencies.

The choice of free choice as anchor is therefore architectural rather than evaluative. The paper does not claim that free choice is morally superior to thinner agency forms, that wholehearted free choice is always preferable to free selection, or that agency events failing to satisfy all six conditions are defective. The three-tier configuration the paper develops — *free selection*, *free choice proper*, and *wholehearted free choice* — names three genuine configurations that exercise different subsets and depths of the same six conditions. Free selection is a thin but real exercise of agency; wholehearted free choice is a deeply integrated exercise; free choice proper sits between them as the configuration in which all six conditions are present without further integration-depth requirements. None of these tiers is ranked above the others as a normative ideal. The architecture's job is to make their structural distinctness specifiable.

This canonical-case methodology mirrors the pattern established in *Differentiated Affect*, where love at constitutive-extension topology served as the canonical case from which the broader affect taxonomy was derived. The choice of love there was likewise architectural: love made the foundation's coordinates maximally legible for the affect domain. Free choice plays the same role here for agency. The paper begins not because every agency event is a free choice, but because free choice is the configuration in which the architecture's six conditions, three-order generalization (foundational orders 1, 2, and 3, plus constructed orders 4 and above through toolkit-development and mastery), and structural-variation methodology can be introduced without ambiguity. The rest of the agency landscape — including agency-pathologies, manipulation, addiction, akrasia, alienation, automatic action, habituated routine, skilled flow, group action, and institutional roles — is then derived by controlled structural variation rather than by independent definition or by symptom-cataloguing.


## 2.4 Methodology and discipline

The paper follows a single methodological template: definition-anchored taxonomic construction by structural variation. Free choice proper supplies the base definition. Every other agency configuration is derived by modifying one or more of the six conditions, the order-engagement profile, the source-status overlay, the developmental-window status, or the cascade structure. This approach guarantees that the taxonomy remains internally consistent with the foundation and that variations remain structurally specifiable rather than gestural. It also enforces a discipline: variation-families are introduced only where the architecture's parameters genuinely diverge, not wherever folk-psychological vocabulary suggests a new category.

Five further disciplinary commitments govern the entire paper:

**Inside-perspective architecture.** Agency is analyzed from the first-person structural configuration of the modeling I. The architecture is perspectivally realist about other agents and can analyze role-indexed profiles, relational cascades, and institutional structures, but it refuses group-mind reification. Multi-agent and group cases are decomposed into indexed agent-profile sets plus relational structure, not aggregated into a single unindexed perspective.

**Implementation-neutrality within lawful-frame qualifier.** The paper takes no position on whether the substrate is deterministic, stochastic, or indeterministic. It requires only enough structural regularity for the architecture's coordinates, profiles, persistence relations, and substrate-mediation pathways to be definable. Specific implementation routes — predictive coding, executive control, accumulator models, neural network dynamics, embodied or extended-cognition implementations — remain open as candidates. None is treated as a reduction of the architecture's structural object; the architecture's commitments do not depend on any particular implementation succeeding.

**Distinct section-level structural objects.** The paper introduces four application-level structural objects — the attribution profile $AP_{m,t;a}$ (Section 9), the empirical interpretation profile $EIP_p$ (Section 10), the prediction profile $PP_i$ (Section 11), and the framework translation profile $FTP_f$ (Section 5). Each performs a different analytical task: attribution, paradigm-mapping, prediction-promotion, and framework-translation respectively. The paper's analytical leverage depends on keeping these objects distinct rather than collapsing them into a single unified profile-schema. The architecture's unity is at the level of the foundation's I-relative agency field; the analytical specificity is at the level of these four section-level objects.

**No new foundation-level primitives.** The paper introduces no new commitments at the foundation level. Every architectural commitment traces back to Foundation v5.3.3's coordinates, the indexed modeling I, $PtCns$ and $PCE$, Modes A/B/C, the verification-toolkit, identity-correlation $\rho$, and the three-order pathway typology. The paper's contribution is application-level: it shows that the foundation's apparatus, applied to the agency domain through the four section-level structural objects, yields a structurally precise account of free choice, agency-variation, responsibility-attribution, empirical interpretation, prediction, and framework translation.

**Boundary discipline.** The paper does not claim to solve the metaphysical problem of free will, prove compatibilism, refute hard incompatibilism, deliver moral or legal verdicts, reduce agency to neural implementation, claim that the architecture is a synthesis of existing frameworks, or license clinical or therapeutic application beyond research predictions. It supplies structural inputs to responsibility-attribution, empirical interpretation, and philosophical translation, not normative-sufficiency closure or metaphysical completeness. Section 6 catalogues principled limits the architecture acknowledges as outside its scope rather than deferring as eventually-solvable. this paper's §7 (or [Agency §13]) returns to the transformation framing without claiming metaphysical closure.

These commitments are stated here so that subsequent sections can operate within them without repeated rehearsal.

# 3. The structural definition of free choice

The agency paper takes free choice as its methodological anchor. The choice is not arbitrary: among agency phenomena, free choice is the configuration in which the architecture's structural commitments are most fully exercised, the philosophical literature most pointedly engaged, and the empirical pressure most concrete. By beginning with a structural definition of free choice and constructing the rest of the agency taxonomy as variation on that definition, the paper achieves the same disciplinary anchor that the affect paper achieved through love at constitutive-extension topology.

The definition has three architectural commitments that the rest of the section develops. First, free choice is *tiered* — not all configurations of choice are alike, and the architecture distinguishes free selection (thin), free choice proper (substantive), and wholehearted free choice (deep) as structurally distinct configurations along an integration gradient. Second, free choice proper has *six conditions*, each load-bearing and each architecturally specifiable. Third, the conditions apply across *three orders of willing* — focus, thought, and substrate action — with abstract structure invariant across orders and implementation semantics order-translated. The section establishes these commitments in turn, then addresses what fails when conditions fail, what the conjunction is sufficient for, and how free choice is exercised across temporal patterns.

## 3.1 The three-tier configuration

The phrase "free choice" does not name a single configuration. The choice between strawberry and chocolate ice cream, the choice to leave a job, the choice to vote a particular way, the spontaneous left-versus-right button press in a Libet experiment, and the long-deliberated decision to pursue a vocation are not architecturally identical. They differ in the depth of the I's engagement with the option-space, the breadth of evaluative work performed, and the degree to which the selected option integrates with the maximally-self-defined I. The architecture distinguishes three tiers along this integration gradient.

**Free selection** is the thinnest tier. The verification-toolkit constructs an option-space, the operator-selection bundle produces a selection, the substrate implements it, Mode B reflective access remains available, and the action is owned. But evaluative engagement may be implicit, minimal, or absent. The Libet-paradigm subject pressing left or right "spontaneously," the diner choosing one of two equally-attractive desserts, the routine skilled execution where evaluation is minimal — these are free selections. They are not unfree, but they are not deliberated. (Skilled action specifically can appear at either tier: routine skilled execution may be thin free selection when evaluation is minimal; *skilled free action* — a configuration discussed in Section 3.8 — becomes free choice proper when implicit evaluative engagement and ownership-integration are active across the coordinated orders.)

**Free choice proper** is the substantive middle tier. All six conditions of the definition (presented below) are met. The toolkit constructs a contrastive live-option space; PtCns, PCE-over-projection, $\Pi$-weighting, or frame-relevance evaluation engages with the options; the operator-selection bundle produces the selection; substrate enables the selection without preemption, bypass, or capture; Mode B reflective access is available without confabulation; and the selected option is integrated as the I's action. This is the configuration the philosophical free-will literature most often has in mind when it speaks of free choice — deliberated, evaluated, owned.

**Wholehearted free choice** is the deepest tier. All six conditions hold, *and* the selected option is in deep $\Pi$-alignment with the I's preservation-priorities — the choice is not merely owned but expressive of the I's most centrally-held commitments. The Frankfurt-style wholehearted agent, the Aristotelian agent acting from cultivated character, the contemplative practitioner whose action expresses sustained training — these are wholehearted free choices. The integration runs deeper than ownership; the action belongs to the I's structural center, not just to its action-history.

The three tiers are *not* a hierarchy of value. Free selection is not lesser free choice; it is *thin* free choice, appropriate to its content domain. The architecture refuses the philosophical move of treating only wholehearted free choice as "really" free, and the contrary move of treating all genuine choice as basically the same. Different content domains, different agents, different occasions exercise different tiers. The expert knife-skill chef performs continuous third-order free selections without engaging second-order deliberation; the lifelong-decision interlocutor engages all three tiers across orders; the impulsive action that nonetheless meets the conditions is free selection, not unfree action. The tiers structure the analysis; they do not rank the phenomena.

The empirical philosophical literature on free will often implicitly assumes one tier and generalizes its conclusions. The Libet paradigm tests free selection (subjects instructed to act spontaneously, with no second-order deliberation invited) and is sometimes taken to refute free choice generally; this is a tier-conflation. Wholehearted free choice in Frankfurt's sense is sometimes treated as the only genuine case; this is also a tier-conflation. The architecture's three-tier framing provides discriminating leverage on the experimental literature and on the philosophical disputes alike.

## 3.2 The six conditions of free choice proper

Free choice proper is the configuration in which all six conditions hold:

**Condition 1: Contrastive live-option space.** The verification-toolkit has constructed at least two structurally distinguishable live courses for the I, including withholding, refusal, delay, or non-action where those are genuinely representable, inhabitable, and implementable. "Live" means the I can construct the course as a represented future *at sufficient resolution to be engageable*, evaluate it, connect it to action-implementation, and inhabit it as a possible course for selection.

*Live courses can be fuzzy.* The toolkit need not have pre-constructed every detail of execution. A course is live when its general structure is constructible, its rough trajectory is representable, and the I has reasonable confidence — or trained expectation supplied by mastery and toolkit-richness — that details will elaborate dynamically as execution proceeds. Most actual will-acts proceed on partial construction with elaboration during execution: the cook who plans "pasta tonight" without scripting every motion; the negotiator who plans "press for the higher offer" without prepared responses to every counter-move; the writer who plans "an essay on X" without constructing every paragraph in advance. This dynamic refinement during execution is structurally normal — it is what mastery's construction-compression (Section 5.7) and the trying-under-incomplete-guarantee structure (Section 6.3) presuppose.

*The trained-expectation discipline.* Trained expectation cannot be mere hope that refinement will occur during execution. It must be grounded in prior successful refinement in the same or analogous domain, trained competence, environmental regularity that supports refinement, scaffold availability that the agent can rely on, or domain-specific mastery — *not in wishful representation alone*. A fuzzy course backed by these grounding conditions is live; a fuzzy course backed only by wishful expectation that "I'll figure it out" without any of these grounds is imagined rather than live. The discipline distinguishes liveness from wishful representation through architectural conditions on the trained expectation itself: *what makes the expectation trained is the architectural fact of prior refinement-capacity in the domain, not the I's confidence that refinement will succeed.* A course the toolkit cannot represent at all, even at the general-skeleton level, is not in the option-space; a course the toolkit can represent at the skeleton level with grounded operating expectation of dynamic refinement is live.

Hobson's-choice cases pass condition 1 only when refusal or withholding is itself live — which it often is, but not always.

**Condition 2: Evaluative engagement.** PtCns (over frame-coherence), PCE-over-projection (over preservation-implications), $\Pi$-weighting (over preservation-priority), or frame-relevance evaluation engages with the option-space. Without evaluative engagement, the I's relation to the option-space lacks normative structure — the I selects but does not evaluate, which is free selection, not free choice proper. *Evaluative engagement, like option-space construction (Condition 1), can be fuzzy or implicit under trained expectation: in mastered domains, the engagement may be pre-compiled and operate as compressed evaluation rather than explicit deliberation, while remaining genuinely engaged for free choice proper (Section 5.7's six-condition mastery table). The fuzzy-construction discipline propagates here: implicit evaluation under trained expectation is engagement; absence of any evaluative apparatus operating on the option-space is not.*

**Condition 3: Operator-bundle selection.** The selection emerges from the operator-selection bundle: the application-level coordinated activity comprising PtCns, PCE-over-projection, $\Pi$-ranking, focus, substrate-readiness, and Modes A/B/C where relevant. The bundle is the application-level construct; Mode A/B/C *shape, constrain, or express* selection but are not treated as a foundation-level action-selection algorithm. The architecture is implementation-neutral on what triggers selection within the bundle in any given encounter — consistent with foundation v5.3.3's refusal to predict per-encounter mode firing (Foundation File 2, Section 4).

**Condition 4: Substrate coupling without preemption, bypass, or capture.** The substrate-host supplies readiness, affordance, motor capacity, affective pressure, and implementation conditions, but does not (i) *preempt* — produce action before operator-bundle selection occurs; (ii) *bypass* — produce action through substrate pathway without entering live-option selection; or (iii) *capture* — exert pull so dominant that alternatives are not live from inside. The three failure modes are structurally distinct: preemption is reflex; bypass is some forms of compulsion; capture is addiction-pattern dominance.

**Condition 5: Non-confabulatory Mode B reflective availability.** The choice-process can be taken as object by Mode B under intact conditions without requiring confabulation. Reflective availability is *not* exercised reflection (the agent need not actually reflect at the moment of choice) and *not* later narrative report (which can confabulate). It is the structural property that the choice-process is reflectively-accessible if access is sought under intact conditions. This is the condition the Wegner experiments engineer the failure of: under specific paradigms, Mode B narrative access can be confabulated rather than tracking the actual selection process.

**Condition 6: Ownership-integration.** The selected option is integrated into the lived-I profile as the I's action — PtCns-endorsed — rather than as alien, merely undergone, externally inserted, or dissociatively produced. In identity-significant choices, ownership-integration may propagate into the maximally-self-defined I; in ordinary free choices (turning left, choosing tea, answering a routine question), integration into the lived-I profile suffices and need not be constitutive of the maximally-self-defined I. Ownership-integration is what alien-hand syndrome lacks (action occurs without integration), what dissociative action lacks (action emerges from a profile-region not integrated as "mine"), and what manipulation cases reveal as separable from internal-condition satisfaction (an agent may own an action while the option-space was source-compromised externally).

The six conditions are not a checklist of independent items. They are interdependent specifications of a single configuration. Condition 3's bundle-selection presupposes condition 1's option-space and engages condition 2's evaluation. Condition 4's substrate-coupling enables condition 3's selection. Condition 5's reflective availability operates over the choice-process condition 3 produces. Condition 6's ownership-integration depends on conditions 1–5 collectively yielding a selection the I can integrate. Failure at any condition is a structurally specific failure that propagates through the rest. The variation taxonomy in Section 8 walks through these failure-propagations explicitly.

## 3.3 The choice-process stack diagram

The conditions structure a choice-process stack that the rest of the paper develops. The stack is order-stratified — toolkit construction operates in parallel across first-, second-, and third-order option-spaces — but its overall topology is unified:

```
                    [substrate readiness]
                            ↓
           verification-toolkit constructs option-space
                  (across all three orders)
                            ↓
            evaluative engagement
   (PtCns / PCE-over-projection / Π-weighting / frame-relevance)
                            ↓
           operator-selection bundle produces selection
                  (at one or more orders)
                            ↓
                   substrate implementation
                            ↓
        Mode B reflective access (object-domain by order)
                            ↓
                ownership-integration
                            ↓
       post-choice integration into lived-I profile
```

The stack runs each time a free choice occurs, from substrate-readiness through post-choice integration. Each stage corresponds to one or more of the six conditions: stage 2 is condition 1 (live-option construction); stage 3 is condition 2 (evaluative engagement); stage 4 is condition 3 (operator-bundle selection); stage 5 is condition 4 (substrate implementation without preemption); stage 6 is condition 5 (Mode B reflective availability); stage 7 is condition 6 (ownership-integration). The diagram is not a strict temporal sequence — stages 5 and 6 may overlap; deliberation may iterate across stages 3 and 4 — but it captures the structural ordering of dependencies.

The architecture's claim about the stack is that all stages operate in any free choice configuration, even when the conditions are met implicitly or rapidly. The free selection of left-versus-right in a Libet paradigm runs the full stack with thin engagement at stage 3 (no substantive evaluation), rapid execution at stages 4 through 7, and high reliability of ownership-integration. The wholehearted free choice runs the full stack with rich engagement at every stage. Variations and pathologies are *failures or modifications at specific stages*, not failures to engage the stack.

## 3.4 The three orders of willing — bridge introduction

The foundation gives three orders of pathway / imprinting (Foundation File 3, Section 8): first-order (salience capture), second-order (cognitive-substrate mediation producing thoughts), third-order (somatic-then-cognitive mediation producing emotions and physical sensations). These describe how content reaches and imprints the I — the *input* direction.

The agency paper develops *order-indexed complementary willing-operations*: the same three-order typology structures how the I directs operations *outward*, producing three orders of willing. The three orders introduced here are the *foundational orders* — they correspond directly to the foundation's three substrate-mediation pathways and are universally available across embodied agents. Section 5.7 generalizes the typology to N-order will, with constructed orders 4 and above (asking, directing, team-coordination, institutional engagement) emerging through mastery as application-level extensions that use lower orders as their implementation medium. The three foundational orders introduced in this subsection are load-bearing throughout the paper; the constructed-order extension is structurally specifiable but is developed in Section 5.7 to keep the bridge introduction focused on the foundation-supported orders.

| Order | Willing-operation domain |
|---|---|
| First-order willing-operation | Focus-level direction by the I over available salience / attention targets within substrate-bound focus capacity, anchored in the cogito's field but not an operation of the bare cogito |
| Second-order willing-operation | Directed thought / deliberation / mental operations |
| Third-order willing-operation | Direct motor action and mediated embodied regulation; autonomic processes only when the toolkit can represent, modulate, or scaffold them (breath, posture, training, biofeedback, somatic practice) |

The architecture commits to *order-indexed complementarity*, not strict mirror symmetry. Input can occur without willing — osmotic imprinting, trauma-cue activation, salience capture, and substrate activation can happen without operator-bundle selection. Willing is operator-bundle-mediated by definition: there is no willing without operator-level selection at some order.

This does not yield a clean activity-passivity asymmetry between input and output. Sufficiently rich verification-toolkits construct as live options not only direct outcome-pursuit but also *configurations of future conditions* and *opportune timing of willing-acts*. The agent uses present willing-acts to arrange future osmotic-imprinting pathways (habit architecture, environmental design, social-context arrangement, information-diet curation, pre-commitment devices), or times willing-acts to leverage available substrate and environmental dynamics. Each willing-act remains active and operator-bundle-mediated; what is willed expands beyond direct effort to include arrangements of future passive processes and gradient-leveraged trajectories.

The architecture thereby distinguishes three *strategic profiles* of operator-bundle-mediated willing — not three separate willing-mechanisms but profiles on a toolkit-richness continuum, all free choice configurations meeting the six conditions:

- **Direct willing** — operator-bundle engagement aimed at a direct outcome, sustained against substrate-pull and competing dynamics. The agent fires engines toward the goal continuously; effort tracks the magnitude of operator-substrate tension.
- **Configurational willing** — operator-bundle engagement aimed at *arrangements of future conditions* that subsequently move osmotically toward goals. The agent uses present effort to redirect future osmotic-imprinting pathways: habit architecture, environmental design, social-context arrangement, pre-commitment devices, information-diet curation. The *free choice* is the present operator-bundle-mediated act of arranging the configuration; the subsequent osmotic imprinting is downstream causal structure, not the willing-act itself. Effort is distributed over time; future passive processes do work the agent would otherwise have to do directly.
- **Slingshot willing** — operator-bundle engagement *timed to leverage available gradients*. The agent reads the configuration of substrate and environmental dynamics through opportune-timing / gradient-reading, then times small precise willing-acts to use them as motive force. Less effort per unit goal-progress; the energy comes from existing dynamics rather than from sustained operator-effort.

A novice's toolkit constructs as live primarily direct-pursuit options. As the toolkit develops, configurational and slingshot options become live. Several apparently distinct cases turn out to be mixed or temporally extended profiles of these three strategic forms rather than additional mechanisms: *receptive willing* (low direct intervention with high gradient-reading) is usually configurational or slingshot willing with minimized direct component; *distributed willing* (across extended timescales and social/institutional scaffolds) is configurational willing distributed temporally; *counter-cyclical willing* (deliberately willing against current gradients to reshape future toolkit or substrate conditions) is direct willing exercised under unfavorable gradient conditions, often configurationally aimed at future capacity. These cases do not require new strategies; they are mixed or temporally extended profiles of the same three forms.

The wisdom traditions track motifs that can be structurally translated into the architecture's direct/configurational/slingshot distinction — Aristotelian *phronesis* (practical wisdom in knowing when to act, what gradients are available, when to wait), Greek *kairos* (opportune-moment timing as distinct from clock-time), Daoist *wu-wei* (often mistranslated "non-action," really effortless action through gradient-alignment), Hebrew *et* (appropriate season for an action), modern self-regulation theory (willpower as depletable, conserved through pre-commitment and stimulus control). The architecture offers a structural translation of one shared pattern these traditions track in their own idioms; it does not claim to reduce those traditions to this taxonomy. Full development of willing-strategies, including effort-economy comparisons and the developmental trajectory from direct-effort agency to configurational and slingshot agency, occupies Section 5 (live-option construction) and Section 6 (will, effort, trying).

The architecture treats willing as a function or operation, not a substance or faculty. The phrasing *first/second/third-order willing-operation* emphasizes the activity (the I willing at level X) rather than positing distinct entities (a "first-order will," a "second-order will"). The discipline matters because the philosophical literature has a recurring tendency to reify will as a faculty the agent has; the architecture's three-orders framing must not encourage that error. Where this paper uses noun-phrasing ("the I's willing at first order") for ease of exposition, the underlying claim is always about an operation the I performs, not an entity it possesses.

The full development of the three orders — with the agency derivation schema, the foundation-signature requirements, the order-specific Mode B object-domains, the cross-order integration analysis — occupies Section 4 and threads through Sections 5–11. This subsection introduces the framing and establishes the discipline; the bridge does its substantive work in Section 4.

## 3.5 The order × condition matrix

Because the six conditions of free choice apply at each order of willing, the architecture acquires a 3 × 6 order × condition matrix. The conditions have *structurally identical abstract form* across orders; their *implementation semantics* are order-translated:

| Condition | First-order (focus) | Second-order (thought) | Third-order (action) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Contrastive live-option space | toolkit constructs ≥2 live focus-targets | toolkit constructs ≥2 live thought-paths | toolkit constructs ≥2 live action-patterns |
| 2. Evaluative engagement | salience / focus-worth evaluable | reasons, frame-coherence, $\Pi$ engage thought-paths | action consequences evaluable |
| 3. Operator-bundle selection | bundle selects focus-target; focus shifts/sustains | bundle selects thought-path; mental operation carried out | bundle selects action-pattern; substrate executes or scaffolds |
| 4. Substrate coupling without preemption | substrate enables focus-direction without preempting it | substrate enables deliberation without preempting it | substrate enables action-execution without bypass or capture |
| 5. Mode B reflective availability | Mode B can take I-as-currently-focusing as object | Mode B can take I-as-currently-thinking as object | Mode B can take I-as-currently-acting-through-substrate as object |
| 6. Ownership-integration | I owns the focus-direction | owns the thought | owns the action |

The matrix is not 18 different things. The abstract structure is identical across cells; only the implementation domain differs. "Implementable" means *focus shifts/sustains* at first-order, *mental operation carried out* at second-order, *substrate executes or scaffolds action* at third-order. "Live" means *attention can move there* at first-order, *thought can be pursued* at second-order, *action can be inhabited* at third-order. These are order-translated specifications of the same abstract conditions.

The *content* of the option-space differs radically across agents and contexts. The expert violinist and the non-violinist face structurally identical free-choice conditions when deciding to perform a passage, but radically different actual option-spaces — the violinist's third-order toolkit constructs as live a vast space of action-patterns the non-violinist's toolkit cannot construct at all. Both face free choice; what differs is *what they can choose among*. The chess grandmaster's second-order toolkit constructs as live a space of thought-paths the novice's toolkit cannot construct. The contemplative practitioner's first-order toolkit constructs as live a space of focus-targets and focus-stabilities the untrained practitioner's toolkit cannot construct. The architecture's specific account of how genuine constraint and genuine freedom coexist is this: *toolkit-bounded option-spaces differ across agents and contexts; the structural conditions of free choice over those option-spaces are the same.*

The full 18-cell matrix and its applications across pathology, development, and recovery are developed in Section 4. This subsection establishes the matrix as an application-level structural lemma, not a foundation primitive — the foundation supplies the three-order pathway typology, and the agency paper applies that typology to the output direction with order-translated implementation semantics for each free-choice condition.

## 3.6 Necessity of each condition

Each of the six conditions is necessary in the sense that its failure produces a configuration that is *not* free choice proper. The variation taxonomy in Section 8 develops these failures as full configurations; this subsection states the necessity briefly for each condition.

**Condition 1 fails (toolkit constructs no contrastive option-space).** The result is *forced choice* (Hobson's-style: only one option live, including refusal not live), or *constrained choice* (option-space narrower than circumstances should permit). Without contrastive plurality, there is no choice — only acceptance, acquiescence, or substrate-output. The I can act, but the act is not a *choice*.

**Condition 2 fails (no evaluative engagement).** The result is *free selection* (option-space exists, selection occurs, but no evaluation engages the options). The architecture treats this as a real configuration — the spontaneous button press, the impulsive flavor choice, the skilled passage execution — but distinguishes it from free choice proper. Free selection is not unfree; it is thin-free.

**Condition 3 fails (selection does not emerge from operator-bundle).** Two structurally distinct sub-failures: *substrate preemption / bypass* — selection produced by substrate alone before or without operator-bundle engagement (reflex, some compulsions); *evaluative-bundle dissociation* — evaluation produces one outcome and selection produces another at the same level, the structural mechanism of *akrasia*. Akrasia is a same-order condition-3 failure: the bundle selects against its own evaluation.

**Condition 4 fails (substrate preemption, bypass, or capture).** The three failure modes are architecturally distinct. *Preemption*: substrate produces action before operator-bundle selection — reflex, startle, autonomic response. *Bypass*: action occurs through substrate pathway without entering live-option selection — some compulsions, automatism. *Capture*: substrate pull dominates such that alternatives are not live from inside — addiction, severe drives. The variation analysis distinguishes these in Section 8.

**Condition 5 fails (Mode B reflective access confabulated).** The result is *apparent choice* — Mode B narrative access supplies a story about the choice-process, but the story does not track the actual selection. This is the configuration the Wegner paradigms engineer experimentally. Apparent choice is not free choice proper; the agent's reflective access is confabulated, and the conditions of free choice are met only superficially.

**Condition 6 fails (ownership-integration absent or compromised).** Two sub-failures: *alienated action* — action occurs but the agent cannot integrate it as the I's own (alien-hand syndrome, dissociative action, possession reports, action attributed elsewhere); *manipulation* — the agent integrates the action as their own, but the option-space was source-compromised externally. The first is internal ownership failure; the second is internal ownership with external source-compromise. Both diminish responsibility-attribution but along different axes.

Each failure mode has its own structural signature, its own phenomenology, and its own clinical or philosophical relevance. The variation taxonomy in Section 8 develops them in full; this subsection establishes that each condition is doing distinct architectural work.

## 3.7 Sufficiency of the conjunction

When all six conditions hold, what would still be missing? The free-will literature presses three challenges. The architecture's response to each clarifies what the conjunction is sufficient *for*.

**Challenge 1: substrate-indeterminism.** Libertarian theorists insist that genuine free choice requires the substrate not to be deterministic — that the choice must be metaphysically open, not just structurally specifiable. The architecture rejects this requirement. Free choice is a structural feature of the operator-bundle / toolkit / PtCns / ownership-integration level; it does not require substrate-indeterminism. Whatever substrate-metaphysics turns out to be — Bohmian deterministic, Everettian deterministic at the multiverse level, Copenhagen indeterministic, objective-collapse stochastic — the architecture's claim is invariant: substrate stochasticity, even if present, is *not* the architectural seat of freedom. Free choice requires live-option construction, evaluative engagement, operator-bundle selection, non-preemptive substrate coupling, non-confabulatory Mode B availability, and ownership-integration. Randomness at the substrate level supplies none of these by itself.

This is not implementation-neutrality as evasion. The architecture has a positive philosophical position: standard empirically-equivalent interpretations of quantum mechanics (Bohmian, Everettian, Copenhagen) cannot be discriminated under any feasible measurement, and Bell's theorem rules out only *local* hidden variables. Even if an objective-collapse theory like GRW were eventually confirmed and substrate-stochasticity established as physical fact, the architecture's account of free choice would not be strengthened — because the architecture locates freedom at a different level entirely. The libertarian must defend not just "the substrate may be indeterministic" but "freedom is *grounded in* substrate indeterminism." The architecture denies the second claim regardless of how the first resolves.

**Challenge 2: ultimate origination.** Some theorists insist that genuine free choice requires the agent to be the *ultimate originator* of the choice — that nothing prior to the agent caused the choice. The architecture rejects strong ultimate origination (which would require agent self-causation, an incoherent demand). It accepts a structural version: when the operator-bundle produces a selection from PtCns-engaged operations on toolset-output and the action is integrated as the I's own, the action *originates from the I* in the only sense in which origination can coherently be required. The toolset, the bundle, the substrate, the prior history all contribute to the configuration; the I is not the unmoved mover. But the I is the configuration in which these contributions are coordinated into a selection that integrates as the I's action. Strong ultimate origination would deny the I authorship because of substrate; the architecture grants the I authorship *through* the structural integration that condition 6 requires.

**Challenge 3: could-have-done-otherwise.** The libertarian sense of "could have done otherwise" requires that, holding all prior conditions fixed, the agent might have selected differently — substrate, history, evaluation all the same, but the selection different. This is a metaphysical demand the architecture does not meet. The architecture meets a structural version instead: when conditions 1–6 hold, the option-space contained options not selected, the operator-bundle was not pre-empted by substrate, and the selection was integrated as the I's own. Other options were live; they were within the I's choice-space; the I could have selected them in the structural sense that they were toolkit-constructed live options the bundle could have selected. The architecture does not pronounce on whether they would have been selected under counterfactual rerun-the-tape conditions, because that question turns on substrate-metaphysics the architecture refuses to ground freedom in.

The architecture's *structural could-have-done-otherwise* is what inside-perspective freedom amounts to. The libertarian's metaphysical version is empirically and conceptually beyond reach — and, the architecture argues, beyond what is needed for genuine freedom. When the toolkit constructs alternatives as live, the bundle engages them, the substrate enables operator-level selection, Mode B can reflectively access the choice, and the I integrates the action as its own — the I is free in every structurally specifiable sense. The metaphysical residue is not what freedom is; it is what some philosophical traditions have demanded freedom *include*, on grounds the architecture finds inadequate.

The conjunction is sufficient for inside-perspective free choice. It is not sufficient for libertarian metaphysical free will, by design. The architecture is not a libertarian theory; it is also not a compatibilist theory, in the sense that it does not reconcile freedom with determinism by relaxing what freedom means. Free choice as the architecture defines it is a real structural configuration — distinguishable from compulsion, akrasia, manipulation, and confabulation — that does not require deterministic substrate, indeterministic substrate, or any specific substrate-metaphysics to exist *among substrate-metaphysics that supply lawful structural frame*. The architecture is neutral across deterministic substrates (Bohmian, Everettian at the multiverse level), substrates with embedded stochasticity (Copenhagen, GRW-style objective collapse, classical statistical-mechanical chance), and mixed-structure substrates with lawful frame and parameter-level chance. It is *not* neutral across the limit-case of intrinsically structurally-indeterminate substrate, because the architecture's coordinates ($\kappa$, $\phi$, $\rho$, $\Pi$), the phenomenal-availability predicate $A_{m,t}(x)$, persistence-across-time, identity-correlation profiles, and substrate-mediation pathways all presuppose lawful structural frame — the operations the architecture specifies, including the consistency of the I across network updates, would themselves be undefined without it. Substrate-stochasticity at the parameter level is fully compatible with the architecture; structural indeterminacy at the frame level would prevent the architecture from being defined at all. This is a strictly weaker commitment than substrate-determinism: lawful frame admits stochastic dynamics, parameter-level chance, and indeterministic measurement-outcomes, all of which the architecture handles without modification.

*Lawful structural frame*, in this context, means sufficient structural regularity for the architecture's objects to be defined: contents must be individuable enough for $\kappa$, $\phi$, $\rho$, and $\Pi$ to apply, and for $A_{m,t}(x)$ to assign phenomenal-availability status; profile-updates must be persistent enough for identity-correlation structure to be tracked across time; substrate-mediation pathways must be stable enough for focus, action, and embodied implementation to occur. Stochasticity, parameter-level chance, and indeterministic measurement outcomes are all compatible with such a frame. What is excluded is not randomness but frame-level structural indeterminacy so radical that no determinate architecture for free choice exists for it to occur within. The lawful-structural-frame qualifier is therefore an *architecture-applicability condition*, not a seventh condition of free choice — it is upstream of all six conditions in the same way that *there must be an I and a substrate* is upstream of any architectural claim. Adding it to the six-condition definition would be a category error; specifying it explicitly here protects the implementation-neutrality claim from overshooting into an undefined limit-case.

The architecture's relation to compatibilism, libertarianism, and hard incompatibilism is developed in detail in Section 5; the present commitment is that the six conditions, jointly, specify what inside-perspective free choice *is* over any substrate supplying lawful structural frame.

## 3.8 Onset and exercise of free choice

Free choice can be exercised in different temporal patterns, each compatible with the six conditions. The architecture distinguishes five exercise patterns; the variation taxonomy in Section 8 develops their pathological extensions, but each healthy form is structurally sound.

**Spontaneous free choice.** Conditions met without explicit deliberation. The toolkit constructs the option-space rapidly; evaluative engagement is implicit (PtCns operates without explicit reflection); the bundle selects; substrate implements; Mode B remains available throughout but is not exercised; the action integrates as the I's own. The skilled chef chopping vegetables, the experienced driver navigating familiar streets, the conversationalist responding to a friend's question — these are spontaneous free choices when conditions 1–6 hold without explicit deliberation.

**Deliberative free choice.** Conditions met with explicit deliberation at stage 3 of the choice-process stack. Mode B reflectively engages the option-space; the agent thinks through the alternatives; PtCns and PCE-over-projection are explicitly exercised; the selection emerges from this reflective engagement. The career decision, the political vote, the philosophical question worked through — these are deliberative free choices.

**Skilled free action.** Conditions met across orders coordinated, with second-order Mode B object-domain available but not exercised at the moment of action. The expert performance, the flow-state engagement, the practiced execution — these involve the toolkit-output running through the bundle without explicit second-order deliberation, while ownership-integration remains intact and Mode B reflective access can be invoked if needed (the expert can pause and reflect on what they were doing). Skilled free action is not unfree; it is free choice exercised through developed cross-order coordination.

**Veto / withholding.** Conditions met for selection of a live withholding-pathway. The Libet-paradigm subject who holds back the imminent button press, the agent who refuses to act, the contemplative who returns to the breath rather than following the thought — these exercise free choice at the omissive form. Withholding is not absence of choice; it is selection of a non-action option that the toolkit constructs as live. Section 8 develops omissive agency in detail.

**Post-hoc recognition.** Conditions met at the time of action, with full $\phi$-construction (source-pathway access) emerging only later, possibly through reflection, conversation, or therapy. The agent acts on conditions 1–6, with Mode B reflective access available, but without articulating why at the moment of action. Later — perhaps significantly later — the I constructs $\phi$-access to the action's sources. The architecture treats post-hoc recognition as compatible with free choice at the time of action: condition 5 requires reflective availability, not exercised reflection or articulable reasons. Many genuine free choices are recognized only retrospectively as such.

These five patterns do not exhaust the temporal forms of free choice, but they cover the central cases. The architecture's claim is that free choice is the configuration specified by the six conditions across one or more orders of willing — the temporal pattern in which the configuration is exercised is variable.

# 4. Manipulation, ownership, and sourcehood

The six-condition definition of §3 is necessary but not sufficient for the philosophical work the architecture undertakes. The classical free-will literature recurrently presses cases in which an agent appears to satisfy the internal conditions of free choice — live options were considered, reasons were weighed, the chosen option resonated with the agent's values, ownership of the action was intact — yet something is structurally wrong. Manipulation cases are the canonical example. An agent acts wholeheartedly from an option-space another agent has shaped; ownership is intact while sourcehood is compromised. A theory that treats free will as a single property delivers either *yes the agent acted freely* (missing the source-compromise) or *no the agent did not act freely* (missing the genuine ownership). The architecture refuses both verdicts in favor of structural decomposition: ownership, source-status, responsibility, and accountability are dissociable relations, each separately analyzable.

This section develops the dissociation. Section 4.1 introduces the triadic apparatus that the companion *Agency Architecture* paper develops in full (its §9). Section 4.2 makes the ownership / sourcehood separation explicit: ownership is not source-cleanliness by default; manipulation is not ownership-failure by default. Section 4.3 develops the manipulator-and-manipulated-agent attribution structure: responsibility is not a conserved quantity transferred between agents. Section 4.4 states the architecture's compatibilist-coherent stance: the structural inputs to responsibility-attribution can be specified without substrate-indeterminism, but the architecture is not a compatibilist proof and remains open to hard-incompatibilist residue at the foundation-level.

## 4.1 The triadic apparatus: ownership, responsibility, accountability

The architecture distinguishes three structural relations that the philosophical literature has often run together. *Ownership* is the structural integration of an action with the agent's lived-I profile and (for identity-significant cases) the maximally-self-defined I. It is what Condition 6 of free choice (§3.2) requires. *Responsibility* is the structural attribution of source for the decision-event, action, omission, or cascade. It depends on the source-status of the option-space, the developmental-window status of the agent, the live mitigation or repair options at the time of the action, and the cascade-foreseeability profile. *Accountability* is the structural addressability of the agent for explanation, demand, mitigation, repair, or response — it can obtain even where original-act responsibility is reduced (repair-accountability for downstream effects), and it can fail to obtain even where ownership and responsibility are intact (developmentally-shaped or context-shaped unaddressability).

The companion *Agency Architecture* paper develops this triadic apparatus in full, including the ten-component attribution profile $AP_{m,t;a}$ that organizes attribution into a structural diagnostic [Agency §9]. For the philosophical work of this paper, the key claim is that *responsibility is not a conserved quantity*: attributing responsibility to one agent does not subtract responsibility from another. Manipulation cases can leave both the manipulator and the manipulated agent with their own profile-indexed attributions without zero-sum trade-off (developed in §4.3 below).

## 4.2 Ownership without clean sourcehood; source-compromise without ownership-failure

This is one of the section's hard-discriminator subsections. Section 8.15 established that *manipulation is not ownership-failure by default* — the agent may own the decision (Condition 6 intact) while sourcehood is externally compromised. Section 9 develops the attribution-implications of the dissociation.

| Configuration | Ownership | Sourcehood | Responsibility implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Source-clean owned action** | High | Clean | Strong default attribution; agent is the appropriate target of explanation, repair, and (in normative mappings) praise / blame |
| **Source-compromised but owned action** | High | Compromised | Responsibility remains possible but must be qualified by source-status; the agent is responsibility-relevant for the act they own but not for the source-shaping that produced their option-space |
| **Source-poisoned wholeheartedness** | High | Poisoned (deliberate input-shaping) | Strong ownership; reduced responsibility for option-space construction; manipulator-attribution becomes load-bearing (per §4.3) |
| **Alienated but source-clean act** | Low (Condition 6 compromised) | Clean or neutral | Weak ownership; responsibility may fail at the moment of action; may relocate to prior dissociation-formation or current repair |
| **Manipulated non-owned action** | Low | Compromised | Attribution shifts substantially toward manipulator; residual agent-attribution is typically repair / accountability attribution rather than direct responsibility for the non-owned act, where mitigation / repair options were live and developmentally available |
| **Coerced prudent compliance** | Often owned (the survival-rationality is the agent's own selection) | Coerced (option-space contracted by coercer) | Responsibility for prudential selection differs from responsibility for the coerced option-space; coercer attribution-relevant for option-space contraction |
| **Owned action with osmotic source-history** | High | Long-shaped, often pre-agentially | Strong ownership; responsibility qualified by developmental-window overlay (per [Agency §9.6]) and by whether mature-period mitigation / re-evaluation options are or were live |

The architectural commitment, repeated for emphasis:

> *Ownership and source-status are dissociable. The agent can own from inside while sourcehood is compromised from outside; conversely, the agent can fail to own a substrate-clean act due to dissociation-failure of Condition 6. The dissociation is what makes manipulation philosophically and ethically difficult, and the architecture's apparatus is engineered to handle the dissociation without collapsing either direction.*

The discipline against two errors. *First*, the architecture does not say manipulation eliminates responsibility — the manipulated agent who owns the decision retains some responsibility-relevance, particularly for cascade-management, post-hoc verification, and repair where these were developmentally available. *Second*, the architecture does not say manipulation transfers all responsibility to the manipulator — the manipulator is attribution-relevant for their constructed-order act of source-compromising, but the manipulated agent's ownership of the act is not erased by that attribution. Both attributions exist; both are profile-indexed; neither subtracts from the other in zero-sum fashion.


## 4.3 Manipulator-responsibility and manipulated-agent-responsibility

This subsection handles the inter-I attribution problem the manipulation literature finds most difficult. Section 9 takes a sharp stance against both naïve absolution of the manipulated agent and naïve transfer-of-all-responsibility to the manipulator.

The structural positions:

| Position | Structural role |
|---|---|
| **Manipulator** | Constructed-order agent whose bundle-mediated act aims to shape another agent's option-space, evaluation, source-access, or ownership-integration; the manipulator's responsibility derives from their bundle-mediated constructed-order act with the manipulated agent's compromised condition as the manipulator's outcome (per 5.6 Stage 2 cascade) |
| **Manipulated agent** | Agent whose selection may remain owned (Condition 6 intact) while sourcehood is externally compromised; the agent's responsibility derives from the act they own, qualified by source-compromise, with reduced responsibility for the option-space construction the manipulator shaped |
| **Manipulated outcome** | The compromised condition in the manipulated agent is the manipulator's structural outcome — the manipulator's cascade-effect through Stage 2 to Stage 3 closure |
| **Attribution result** | Asymmetric, shared, reduced, or relocated responsibility; *not* automatic transfer; *not* zero-sum |

The non-negotiable rule:

> *Manipulation increases the need for attribution analysis; it does not automatically remove the manipulated agent's responsibility or transfer all responsibility to the manipulator. Both attributions can be real where each agent's attribution-profile supports them. They are profile-indexed rather than zero-sum: manipulator-attribution does not by itself erase manipulated-agent attribution, and manipulated-agent ownership does not erase manipulator-attribution.*

The asymmetric attribution is what the architecture supplies:

- *Manipulator responsibility* is concentrated, deliberate, and constructed-order. The manipulator's bundle-mediated act produced the source-compromise; the manipulator owns the constructed-order act of source-compromising; cascade-attribution via Stage 2 makes the manipulator structurally responsible for the manipulated agent's compromised condition.
- *Manipulated-agent responsibility* is qualified, partial, and configuration-specific. The manipulated agent owns the act they performed within the compromised option-space; their decision-responsibility is real for the within-compromised-space selection; their option-space-construction responsibility is reduced because the option-space was source-shaped by the manipulator.

What the architecture rules out:

- *Naïve absolution.* "The manipulated agent isn't responsible at all because they were manipulated." This collapses ownership into source-status and erases the agent's own selection-event within the compromised space.
- *Naïve transfer.* "All responsibility transfers to the manipulator." This treats responsibility as a fixed quantity that moves between agents rather than as profile-indexed structural attribution that can apply to multiple agents simultaneously.
- *Naïve preservation.* "The manipulated agent is fully responsible because they made the choice." This ignores the source-compromise and treats option-space-construction as agent-internal when it was externally shaped.

*Acute vs distributed manipulation.* The attribution-profile differs structurally between acute and distributed manipulation cases. Acute manipulation — a one-time deceptive act, a specific gaslighting episode, a deliberate input-shaping for a particular decision — typically produces concentrated manipulator-attribution (one identifiable constructed-order agent, clear bundle-mediated act, traceable Stage 2 output) and qualified manipulated-agent attribution (the agent's selection within the acutely-compromised space remains owned but with reduced source-responsibility for the option-space). Distributed manipulation — long-osmotic propaganda, comprehensive ideological scaffolding, multi-actor cultural source-shaping — typically produces distributed manipulator-attribution (multiple constructed-order agents, sustained bundle-mediated propagation, indirect Stage 2 outputs through institutional and cultural channels) and developmentally-conditioned manipulated-agent attribution (the agent's selection within the long-shaped space is owned but with attribution-strength conditioned on developmental-window per 9.6, particularly for scaffold-captured cells). The architecture treats acute and distributed manipulation as the same structural family with different temporal and role-distribution profiles; the attribution-implications differ accordingly.

The architecture's position is that all three errors come from treating responsibility as a single scalar that distributes across agents in conservation. Profile-indexed attribution allows multiple agents to be substantially responsibility-relevant for a single act where their respective profiles support that result, with each agent's responsibility computed from their own attribution-profile rather than subtracted from someone else's.


## 4.4 The architecture's compatibilist-coherent stance

Section 9 must locate the architecture on the conceptual map without overcommitting on the determinism / responsibility debate. The stance:

> *The architecture is compatibilist-coherent because responsibility-attribution is computed inside the agency-configuration: live-option construction (C1), evaluative engagement (C2), operator-bundle selection (C3), non-preemptive substrate coupling (C4), Mode B availability (C5), ownership-integration (C6), source-status, developmental-window, cascade-foreseeability, and live mitigation / repair options. The architecture's only substrate requirement is the lawful-frame (3.7); it does not require substrate-indeterminism, and it does not reduce responsibility to post-hoc authorship-feeling.*

The architecture's commitments:

- *Implementation-neutral.* The attribution apparatus operates on the inside-perspective configuration regardless of whether the substrate is deterministic or indeterministic. The lawful-frame is the only substrate requirement, and the lawful-frame is compatible with both possibilities.
- *Compatibilist-coherent.* The structural inputs supplied by the six conditions, the source-compromise continuum, the developmental-window overlay, and the cascade apparatus supply the kind of architecture-level resources a compatibilist can use for responsibility-attribution without libertarian metaphysics. Whether those inputs are *normatively sufficient* for responsibility-assertion depends on the reader's responsibility theory; the architecture's contribution is structural inputs, not normative sufficiency.
- *Not a compatibilist proof.* Section 9 does not argue that compatibilism is correct. It locates the architecture's commitments on the conceptual map: the architecture supplies what compatibilist responsibility-attribution requires, while not foreclosing positions that demand additional metaphysical commitments the architecture does not make.
- *Honest about hard incompatibilism.* Hard incompatibilists who demand ultimate origination — sourcehood that traces the agent's act to an uncaused causation by the agent themselves — will not be satisfied by the architecture. The architecture does not claim to meet that demand. Its compatibilist-coherence is structural; its silence on ultimate origination is deliberate.

What Section 9 rules out:

- The claim that the architecture proves compatibilism (it does not; it locates commitments).
- The claim that determinism is true or false (the architecture is implementation-neutral).
- The claim that responsibility depends on metaphysical indeterminacy (it does not; the architecture's responsibility-apparatus operates on inside-perspective structure regardless of substrate).
- The claim that the architecture is identical to standard compatibilism (some standard compatibilist views collapse responsibility into reasons-responsiveness, hierarchical identification, or counterfactual capacity; the architecture's structural-perspectival attribution is distinct from each).

The position is sober: the architecture supplies structural inputs to responsibility-attribution; whether and how those inputs underwrite responsibility for any particular reader depends on the reader's normative framework. The architecture is compatibilist-coherent and implementation-neutral; it is not a metaphysical theory of free will and does not pretend to be one.

# 5. Translations: positioning the architecture against alternative frameworks


## 5.1 Translation discipline: compatibility is not equivalence

Translation is not reduction. The fact that the architecture can translate into a framework's vocabulary does not mean the framework already contains the architecture. The fact that a framework can be translated through the architecture's apparatus does not mean the architecture replaces the framework. The central comparative question is not "which framework is closest?" but "which structural distinction does each framework preserve, flatten, or mislocate?"

Four methodological commitments govern the section:

*Translation is not reduction.* The architecture and a framework may share vocabulary while making different structural commitments. Frankfurt's "wholeheartedness" and the architecture's "ownership-integration" overlap but are not identical: ownership-integration is one of six conditions plus overlay-status, while wholeheartedness is treated by Frankfurt as a key signature of the agent's relation to motivation.

*Comparison is with representative forms.* No single paragraph exhausts every variant of compatibilism, libertarianism, predictive coding, phenomenology, or wisdom-tradition no-self theory. The section engages canonical or representative versions, with the architecture's engagement remaining fair to each framework's own structure.

*Local agreement, global divergence is a real configuration.* The architecture may agree with compatibilists that responsibility need not require indeterminism, while refusing to become standard compatibilism. The architecture may agree with hard incompatibilists that manipulation and ultimate-source worries are serious, while refusing the conclusion that responsibility is impossible. Local agreements are not concessions; global divergences are not refutations.

*Section 11 predictions matter as discriminators.* The translation work is structurally serious only if it specifies what discriminates the architecture from each framework. A translation that produces no discriminator — empirical, structural, or conceptual — is not yet doing Section 5's comparative work. Section 11 predictions are the preferred empirical discriminators, but some framework comparisons may also retain conceptual residue that Section 6 must develop.

To organize the framework comparisons consistently, Section 5 uses a *Framework Translation Profile* schema $FTP_f$ for each framework $f$:

$$FTP_f = \langle O_f, T_f, K_f, R_f, D_f, P_f, L_f \rangle$$

where:

| Component | What it specifies |
|---|---|
| $O_f$ — Framework object | What the framework primarily explains: responsibility, authorship, freedom, control, autonomy, lived agency, no-self, or other |
| $T_f$ — Translation route | How the architecture can translate the framework's central insight |
| $K_f$ — Kept / overlapping commitments | What the architecture accepts or can reconstruct from the framework's commitments |
| $R_f$ — Reduction refused | What the architecture refuses to collapse into that framework |
| $D_f$ — Discriminator | Which architectural distinction matters most against this framework |

| $L_f$ — Limits / open residue | What remains unresolved and is flagged in the open-problems catalogue (this paper §6 for foundation-level residue / [Agency §12] for agency-domain problems) |

The compact formulation: *Translation = overlap + refusal + discriminator + open residue.*

The $FTP_f$ profile is parallel to Section 9's $AP_{m,t;a}$ (attribution), Section 10's $EIP_p$ (paradigm-interpretation), and Section 11's $PP_i$ (prediction-promotion). Each schema does different work at a different section: $AP$ computes attribution, $EIP$ identifies what an empirical paradigm measures, $PP$ promotes operationalizations into discriminating predictions, $FTP$ positions the architecture against alternative frameworks. They share architectural lineage but are not interchangeable.

## 5.4 Hierarchical identification and Frankfurt-class views

Frankfurt-class hierarchical identification frameworks treat agency, freedom, or moral responsibility as constituted by reflective endorsement of one's first-order desires by higher-order desires, with wholeheartedness (the absence of reflective conflict) as a signature of integrated agency.

Architectural derivation: Frankfurt-class views correctly identify that endorsement and reflective integration matter for agency. The architecture incorporates this through Condition 6 ownership-integration and through Mode B reflective access. But the architecture refuses to treat hierarchical endorsement as a *responsibility solvent*. The wholehearted propaganda-shaped agent, the wholeheartedly-addicted agent in scaffold-capture (per [Agency §9.6]), the wholeheartedly-coerced agent acting under manipulation continuum pressure (per 5.9, 9.12) — all show that wholeheartedness can be present without sourcehood being clean.

The architecture's hard discriminator: *Wholeheartedness is an integration depth, not an all-purpose responsibility solvent.* The §4.2 commitment that manipulation is not ownership-failure by default, combined with the source-status overlay's structural role in attribution, gives the architecture an apparatus Frankfurt-class views typically lack. Source-sensitive descendants of Frankfurt's view (those that build sourcehood-conditions into hierarchical analysis) reduce the discriminator-distance, and the architecture's comparison with such descendants engages them on their own terrain rather than dismissing the entire hierarchical-identification family.

The flagship discriminating prediction is P5: ownership-reports and source-access reports should dissociate under at least some manipulation configurations. If hierarchical endorsement and wholeheartedness were sufficient for responsibility-attribution, this dissociation would have no independent architectural role; P5 pressures endorsement-only hierarchical views because it makes source-status empirically and structurally visible even where ownership or identification remains high.


## 5.5 Reasons-responsiveness compatibilism

Reasons-responsiveness compatibilism (Fischer-Ravizza-class and broader variants) treats agency or moral responsibility as constituted by an agent's mechanism being appropriately responsive to reasons across nearby counterfactual configurations — receptive to reasons, suitably reactive to them, and operating from an own mechanism.

| $FTP_{R-R}$ component | Specification |
|---|---|
| $O_f$ Framework object | Mechanism-responsiveness to reasons across nearby counterfactual configurations |
| $T_f$ Translation route | Reasons-responsiveness maps onto Condition 2 (evaluative engagement), $\Pi$-weighted evaluation, Mode B access to reasons (7.5), and contrastive live-option construction (Condition 1) |
| $K_f$ Kept / overlapping commitments | Sensitivity to reasons across configurations is architecturally significant; the responsiveness-ownership combination tracks something the architecture also tracks (Condition 6 with C2 engagement) |
| $R_f$ Reduction refused | Reasons-responsiveness alone does not capture: ownership / sourcehood dissociation; Condition 4 substrate-coupling failure; Mode B object-domain asymmetry; developmental-window overlay; cascade / profile-formation responsibility |
| $D_f$ Discriminator | Multi-axis agency-profile vs single-axis (reasons-responsiveness) framework; the architecture treats reasons-responsiveness as one profile-axis among several |

Architectural derivation: reasons-responsiveness compatibilism correctly identifies that an agent's responsiveness to reasons (receptive sensitivity, appropriate reactivity) is structurally important. The architecture incorporates this through Condition 2 (evaluative engagement), $\Pi$-weighted evaluation, Mode B access to reasons. But the architecture refuses to treat reasons-responsiveness as the whole agency-profile.

Several Section 9 / Section 11 commitments outrun reasons-responsiveness:

*Ownership / sourcehood dissociation (P5).* An agent may respond appropriately to reasons inside a source-compromised scaffold without the agent's responsibility for the act being equivalent to a source-clean reasons-responsive agent's. Reasons-responsiveness alone cannot mark the difference.

*Condition 4 substrate-coupling failure.* An agent may recognize the relevant reasons under Mode B reflective access and yet fail implementation under substrate-coupling compromise (compulsion, ADHD-like configuration per 8.9). P12 specifies that this configuration should be empirically recognizable: Mode B identification without reliable implementation repair, not motivational deficit alone.

*Mode B object-domain asymmetry (P7).* An agent's reflective access may be reasons-responsive in one architectural domain (e.g., the action) while being limited in another (e.g., the source-pathway). Reasons-responsiveness as a single capacity cannot capture this asymmetry.

*Developmental-window overlay.* A reasons-responsive agent in a scaffold-captured developmental-window cell may be subject to different attribution-profile than a reasons-responsive agent without that scaffold-history, even with identical reasons-responsiveness measurements.

*Cascade and profile-formation (P18).* Responsibility may concern downstream profile-formation rather than only momentary reasons-responsiveness at $t_{select}$.

The architecture's hard discriminator: *Reasons-responsiveness is one axis inside the attribution and agency profile; it is not the whole profile.* Source-sensitive variants of reasons-responsiveness compatibilism (those that build sourcehood and historical conditions into the framework) reduce the discriminator-distance, but the multi-axis structure of the architecture's profile-objects ($AP_{m,t;a}$ ten components, $EIP_p$ eight components, $PP_i$ seven components) is structurally richer than the reasons-responsiveness mechanism alone. This comparison targets reasons-responsiveness where reasons-responsiveness is the dominant explanatory axis; historical and source-sensitive versions reduce the distance correspondingly.

Pure attributionism (Watson-class quality-of-will accounts; Scanlon-class moral-attributability accounts) treats moral responsibility primarily through the structure of agent's relation to value, with reactive attitudes (Strawson-class) and social practices of blame, demand, and answerability as constitutive elements.

| $K_f$ Kept / overlapping commitments | Attribution-practices track real social-structural facts; reactive attitudes track real interpersonal-architectural relations; accountability is genuinely distinct from ownership and from responsibility |

Architectural derivation: attributionist views correctly identify that moral responsibility is not reducible to control-freedom in some narrow sense — the structure of agent's relation to value, the structure of interpersonal demand, and the practices of blame and answerability all matter. The architecture incorporates these insights through Section 9's accountability-as-addressability framing and through the triadic dissociability that explicitly allows attribution-practice to differ from ownership-status while remaining grounded in agency-configuration.

But the architecture refuses to let attribution become merely a *social practice detached from agency-configuration*. Attribution-practices may reveal what communities track, but they do not constitute the agency-configuration itself. The architecture's structural commitments — ownership / sourcehood dissociation, source-status overlay, developmental-window, profile-indexed attribution — are candidate inputs that downstream attribution-practices may track, ignore, distort, or contest. Where practices fail to track those inputs, the architecture treats the mismatch as an empirical-and-normative question about the relation between structural attribution-inputs and communal attribution-practice, not as evidence that the structural inputs are absent.

The Section 11 P17 patch is critical here. P17 was originally formulated as predicting "responsibility judgments" — but the v0.1.10.1 patches revised this to predict *role-indexed attribution-input structure* rather than folk-psychological judgments. The architectural prediction is that role-indexed structural variables (authority, information-access, discretion, expertise, mitigation-power, repair-option availability, cascade-foreseeability) provide the architecture's attribution-input profile better than unindexed group-membership. Experimental-philosophy paradigms varying role-profiles are admissible *only as indirect probes of whether human assessors track those structural inputs*; folk judgments themselves are not the architectural object.

The architecture's hard discriminator: *Attribution practices may reveal what communities track, but they do not constitute the agency-configuration itself.* Attributionists who treat attribution as the *structure* of responsibility (rather than as a downstream practice grounded in agency-configuration) face the architectural challenge that profile-indexed structural inputs precede attribution-practices in the architecture's analysis.


## 5.7 Sourcehood, manipulation arguments, and hard incompatibilism

Hard incompatibilism (Pereboom-class, Caruso-class) argues that genuine moral responsibility requires ultimate sourcehood — being the ultimate source of one's actions in a sense that neither determinism nor adequate-event-causal libertarianism can supply. Manipulation arguments are the central rhetorical engine: if a manipulated agent intuitively bears reduced responsibility for a manipulated act, and if ordinary causal histories are relevantly similar to manipulation, then ordinary agents bear correspondingly reduced responsibility.

| $FTP_{HI}$ component | Specification |
|---|---|
| $O_f$ Framework object | Ultimate sourcehood as constitutive of full moral responsibility; manipulation arguments as the wedge against ordinary-responsibility intuitions |
| $T_f$ Translation route | Source-status overlay (5.9, 8.15, 9.5, 9.12) translates the sourcehood concern; the manipulation continuum is the architecture's structural taxonomy of source-compromise |
| $K_f$ Kept / overlapping commitments | Manipulation and sourcehood are architecturally central; the architecture has unusually fine apparatus here (source-clean, source-compromised, source-poisoned, coerced, osmotically shaped, scaffold-captured, developmental-window-qualified, manipulated-but-owned, manipulated-and-non-owned) |
| $R_f$ Reduction refused | The architecture refuses two moves: (1) manipulation automatically erases agency; (2) ownership automatically defeats manipulation worries. Manipulation is not ownership-failure by default, and ownership is not source-cleanliness by default |
| $D_f$ Discriminator | Source-status overlay as structurally separable from ownership; profile-indexed attribution rejecting responsibility-as-conserved-quantity (per §4.3) |
| $P_f$ Prediction link | P5 (ownership / sourcehood dissociation under manipulation, flagship); P6 (source-compromised recognition-timing or report-stability differences); P16 (social-influence varying by transparency / alternatives / source-access / verification-bypass) |
| $L_f$ Limits / open residue | The architecture's sourcehood apparatus does not satisfy the demand for *ultimate sourcehood* in the strongest hard-incompatibilist sense; this is a real architectural limit and is flagged in the open-problems catalogue (this paper §6 for foundation-level residue / [Agency §12] for agency-domain problems) as the metaphysical-residue open problem |

Architectural derivation: hard incompatibilism correctly identifies that sourcehood is critical for responsibility-attribution, and that manipulation cases reveal something deep about how ordinary causal histories shape agency. The architecture takes these concerns seriously — more seriously than most compatibilist responses — by building a multi-grade source-status overlay (per 5.9 manipulation continuum) and by explicitly committing (per §4.3) to profile-indexed rather than zero-sum responsibility-attribution.

The architecture's response to manipulation arguments is structurally different from most compatibilist responses. It does not say "manipulation is just like ordinary causation, so we should preserve ordinary responsibility." It says: manipulation is *one configuration* in a continuum that the architecture's apparatus structurally specifies, and the manipulated agent's attribution-profile is computed from the joint configuration of ownership-status, source-status, developmental-window, and Mode B availability — not from a single dimension. The manipulated-but-owned configuration (per §4.2) is genuinely different from the manipulated-non-owned configuration; both are different from the source-clean configuration; the manipulator's attribution-profile is independently computed from their own configurational decisions.

But the architecture explicitly does not satisfy the demand for *ultimate* sourcehood. The architecture's source-status overlay is structurally graded, not metaphysically ultimate. Sourcehood within the architecture is a function of transparency, alternative-availability, source-access, verification-availability, developmental-window, and toolkit-development — not a function of being uncaused-by-anything-prior. Hard incompatibilists who require ultimate sourcehood as a precondition for responsibility-attribution can still reject the architecture as supplying merely *partial* sourcehood-sensitivity rather than the metaphysically ultimate version they require.

The architecture's stance, stated explicitly:

> *Hard incompatibilism can still reject the architecture if it demands ultimate sourcehood beyond any structural profile available inside a lawful frame. The architecture's response is not to refute that demand, but to show that a rich sourcehood-sensitive responsibility apparatus can be constructed without it. Whether this is enough for responsibility-attribution depends on the reader's responsibility-theory.*

This is the same epistemic-humility posture the architecture takes toward compatibilism (per §4.4): the architecture's contribution is structural inputs, not normative sufficiency. The hard incompatibilist who requires ultimate sourcehood will not be satisfied by the architecture's apparatus, no matter how fine-grained. That is a real limit, and Section 6 will engage it as the metaphysical-residue open problem.

The flagship discriminating prediction is P5 — ownership / sourcehood dissociation under manipulation. The architecture predicts that manipulation paradigms can produce source-compromised-but-owned configurations detectable through ownership / source-access dissociation. If this dissociation never arose, the architecture's structural analysis of manipulation would lose empirical traction. If the dissociation does arise, hard incompatibilists must decide how much structural significance to grant source-compromised-but-owned agency inside their ultimate-sourcehood framework. The architecture treats source-clean, source-compromised-but-owned, and non-owned configurations as structurally distinct; whether those distinctions suffice for moral responsibility remains exactly the normative-metaphysical dispute Section 5 does not settle.


## 5.8 Libertarianism, alternative possibilities, and indeterminism

Libertarian free will (agent-causal and event-causal versions) holds that genuine free will requires either (a) alternative possibilities open in the metaphysical sense — the agent could have done otherwise given the same past and laws — or (b) indeterministic agent-causation that is nonetheless meaningfully agent-attributable.

| $FTP_{Lib}$ component | Specification |
|---|---|
| $O_f$ Framework object | Alternative possibilities and agent-sourcehood as constitutive of free will, requiring indeterminism at the substrate or agent-causal level |
| $T_f$ Translation route | Alternative possibilities translate to live-option construction (Condition 1); agent-sourcehood translates to operator-bundle selection-event under ownership and source-status; openness translates to non-preemptive substrate-coupling plus live-option inhabitability; self-forming action translates to profile-formative configurational decisions |
| $K_f$ Kept / overlapping commitments | Live alternatives matter; sourcehood matters; selection as a structural event matters; profile-formation through self-forming actions matters |
| $R_f$ Reduction refused | The architecture does not require substrate-indeterminism; alternative possibilities are architectural live-options, not necessarily metaphysically undetermined branches; randomness is not sourcehood; indeterminism alone does not generate agency |
| $D_f$ Discriminator | Lawful-frame as only substrate requirement; live-option construction as architectural rather than metaphysical; the architecture's agency-analysis is definable on lawful-frame stochastic, deterministic, or mixed substrates |
| $P_f$ Prediction link | P1 (thin / rich multi-marker divergence) — predictability of choices (Soon-class) does not threaten architecture's tier distinction unless content-specific decoding outpaces architectural-structure operation; P2 (prediction-lead-time varies with option-space richness); P3 (preparation signals do not uniformly map to selection); P7 (Mode B domain-specificity); P8 (flow / mastery preserve post-action Mode B) |
| $L_f$ Limits / open residue | Whether the architecture's "live options" satisfy libertarian intuitions about openness in the strongest metaphysical sense is flagged in the open-problems catalogue (this paper §6 for foundation-level residue / [Agency §12] for agency-domain problems); libertarians who require metaphysical indeterminism may treat the architecture's live-option apparatus as merely psychological |

Architectural derivation: libertarian frameworks correctly identify that alternative possibilities and sourcehood matter for agency. The architecture preserves these concerns through Condition 1 (live-option construction) and through the source-status apparatus. But the architecture refuses to make free choice depend on substrate-indeterminism.

Three commitments structure the architecture's relation to libertarianism:

*Indeterminism alone does not generate agency.* Random substrate fluctuations crossing a threshold for action do not constitute selection-events (per 8.18 reflex / non-agency boundary). Quantum or stochastic micro-events at the substrate level may be present without bearing on the operator-bundle's selection of the act. What makes a selection a *selection* is the structural configuration of operator-bundle, ownership, source-access, evaluative engagement, and substrate-coupling — not the underlying physics of the substrate.

*Randomness is not sourcehood.* Even if substrate-level indeterminism could produce agency-relevant variation, randomness does not provide the *agent's authoring* of the act — it provides only undetermined occurrence. Sourcehood in the architecture's sense is a function of source-access, source-pathway clarity, transparency, and verification — none of which require substrate-indeterminism, and none of which are supplied by it.

*A lawful structural frame is required for agency-analysis to be definable.* The architecture's profile-objects ($\kappa$, $\phi$, $\rho$, $\Pi$, $A$, $AP_{m,t;a}$, etc.) require structural stability for their definitions to apply. Stochasticity at the substrate level is compatible with structural stability — many natural systems exhibit stochastic micro-dynamics with stable macro-structure. But pure substrate-randomness without structural stability would defeat the architecture's apparatus, just as it would defeat any agency-analysis.

The architecture's hard discriminator: *Alternative possibilities are architectural live-options, not necessarily metaphysically undetermined branches.* Live-option construction (Condition 1) requires that the agent can construct paths as live and contrastive — but this is an architectural construction, not a metaphysical fact about the universe's branching structure. An agent constructs option $A$ and option $B$ as live; the operator-bundle selects between them; the selection is the agent's act. Whether the universe was metaphysically open at $t_{select}$ is a separate question the architecture does not legislate.

Some libertarians may respond that without metaphysical openness, the agent's "selection" is merely epistemic and the agent could not really have done otherwise. The architecture's response: within the architecture, "could have done otherwise" is an architectural predicate — the option-space contained live alternatives, the operator-bundle was not preempted, and the selected option was owned. Libertarian metaphysical openness remains a separate demand that the architecture does not legislate. From inside the agent's perspective, alternatives are constructed as live; from outside the architecture's apparatus, the substrate-level question of metaphysical openness is open and implementation-dependent. Libertarians who treat metaphysical openness as constitutive of free will may treat the architecture's apparatus as merely psychological; the architecture treats this as a real disagreement that lawful-frame neutrality does not pretend to resolve.


## 5.9 Illusionism, apparent will, and agency skepticism

Illusionist accounts — Wegner-class apparent mental causation and broader confabulation accounts — argue that the experience of conscious will, agency, and authorship is often systematically misleading: that what we report as our authoring of action is in many cases (or all cases, in stronger versions) confabulated narrative imposed on processes that do not actually involve a willing self. Adjacent agency-skeptical views, including Galen Strawson-class ultimate-responsibility skepticism, supply a different pressure: not primarily report-confabulation, but skepticism about whether the agent can be the kind of ultimate source required for full responsibility. Section 5.9 engages illusionism proper; Galen Strawson-class ultimate-responsibility skepticism is structurally closer to the hard-incompatibilism family engaged in 12.7, where ultimate-sourcehood demands are the central concern.

| $FTP_{Ill}$ component | Specification |
|---|---|
| $O_f$ Framework object | Confabulation; report fallibility; post-hoc authorship; apparent-will-without-actual-causation |
| $T_f$ Translation route | The post-hoc recognition / source-opacity / confabulation triad (per 7.9, 9.7, 10.7) translates illusionist concerns; some reports are genuinely confabulated; choice-blindness paradigms illustrate / support the architecture's recognition that confabulation is real |
| $K_f$ Kept / overlapping commitments | Some agency reports are post-hoc, source-opaque, or confabulated; introspective transparency is not assumed; agent reports are fallible; preparatory neural activity precedes report (per 10.4); the structural framing of Wegner's priority / consistency / exclusivity conditions tracks real configuration-of-experience-of-agency |
| $R_f$ Reduction refused | The architecture rejects the global inference: *some reports are confabulated, therefore agency as such is confabulation*. Post-hoc recognition is not confabulation by default; source-opacity is not confabulation by default; the three-way distinction is empirically separable (P4) |
| $D_f$ Discriminator | The post-hoc / source-opacity / confabulation triad as architecturally separable; the multi-marker divergence between thin-selection and wholehearted choice (P1); the timing-marker non-identity chain ($t_{prep} \not\equiv t_{select} \not\equiv t_{report}$, per 10.3) |
| $P_f$ Prediction link | P3 (preparation signals do not uniformly map to selection); P4 (post-hoc recognition / source-opacity / confabulation empirically separable); P5 (ownership / sourcehood dissociation); P6 (source-compromised recognition-timing differences) |
| $L_f$ Limits / open residue | The architecture's response to illusionism is structural rather than experimental; whether the architecture's three-way report-distinction can be cleanly operationalized in well-designed paradigms is flagged in the open-problems catalogue (this paper §6 for foundation-level residue / [Agency §12] for agency-domain problems) |

Architectural derivation: illusionism correctly identifies that some agency reports are confabulated, that introspective transparency is not assumed, and that preparatory neural activity precedes reportable awareness. The architecture incorporates these insights through Section 7.9's three-way distinction (post-hoc recognition / source-opacity / confabulation) and through Section 10.7's Wegner-paradigm reading. The architecture also accepts that paradigms like choice-blindness reveal genuine confabulation — the post-hoc justification narratives produced when participants are presented with a swapped outcome are confabulations of source / content / justification (per 10.7).

But the architecture rejects the global inference from "some reports are confabulated" to "agency as such is confabulation." This inference requires the additional premise that the only alternative to confabulation is fully transparent, real-time, accurate introspection — a premise the architecture explicitly rejects through the post-hoc recognition / source-opacity distinction.

The three-way structure:

*Post-hoc recognition* — the I correctly recognizes an owned action after $t_{select}$ has occurred. The recognition is delayed but accurate; $t_{recognize}$ tracks an actual selection-event. This is not confabulation.

*Source-opacity* — the action is owned (Condition 6 intact), but the source-pathway is conscious-fuzzy or low-$\phi$. The agent cannot reliably articulate why they selected as they did, without the act being unowned or the selection being absent. This is not confabulation.

*Confabulation* — the $\phi$-narrative supplies apparent source-access for a process that was absent, bypassed, or differently structured than the narrative reports. $t_{recognize}$ may fail to track the actual source, timing, content, or ownership-relation of $t_{select}$, even where an actual selection-event occurred (per v0.1.10.1 Patch 4). Choice-blindness confabulation is one configuration of this, where the actual prior selection occurred but the post-swap narration tracks the wrong outcome.

The architecture's hard discriminator: *Illusionism is right against naïve introspective transparency and wrong if it globalizes from report-failure to agency-failure.* P4 predicts the three configurations are empirically separable; if they collapsed into a single configuration across paradigms designed to dissociate them, the architecture's response to illusionism would lose empirical traction. The flagship discriminator (P5) further pressures illusionism: ownership-reports and source-access reports should dissociate under manipulation paradigms. Global illusionism cannot accommodate this dissociation while preserving its general claim that agency reports are confabulated; the architecture's structural distinction predicts the dissociation arises naturally from ownership / sourcehood separability.

This subsection is not a debunking of Wegner. The architecture acknowledges that Wegner's apparent-mental-causation account tracks something real about how the experience of will arises under conditions of priority, consistency, and exclusivity (per 10.7). The disagreement concerns whether *that fact about the experience of agency* generalizes to the *structure of agency itself*. The architecture says no — the experience of agency may be configured by priority / consistency / exclusivity cues in ways that sometimes produce false ownership-reports, but agency-as-structure includes ownership-status, source-status, evaluative engagement, Mode B availability, and substrate-coupling configurations that the experience-of-agency may track only partially or incorrectly.


## 5.10 Predictive coding, active inference, and executive-control models

Predictive coding and active inference frameworks (Friston-class, Clark-class) treat perception and action as hierarchical generative-model dynamics: the brain is treated as continuously predicting sensory consequences, generating actions to minimize prediction error or reach preferred states, with action understood as policy selection over possible behaviors. Executive-control models (working memory, inhibition, task-switching, sustained attention, response-inhibition apparatus) treat agency-relevant capacities through specific control-mechanisms.

Executive-control maps onto inhibition (8.5 omissive decision), working memory, task-switching, sustained implementation (Condition 4), and substrate-coupling capacities, without reducing Mode B object-domain availability to a single executive-control capacity |

Architectural derivation: predictive coding correctly identifies that action involves prediction-and-control dynamics — affordance-construction, policy selection, error-correction. Executive-control models correctly identify that specific mechanisms (working memory, inhibition, task-switching, sustained implementation) are involved in agency-relevant performance. The architecture incorporates these insights as *implementation candidates* — the architecture's projection-toolset, operator-bundle selection, and substrate-coupling apparatus may be implementation-mapped onto predictive-coding and executive-control variables, without the architecture's apparatus being *reducible* to those variables.

The architecture's refusal: agency is not third-person control dynamics alone. Several architectural commitments are not captured by predictive-coding or executive-control alone:

*The modeling I as architectural anchor.* Predictive-coding and executive-control descriptions are typically third-person — they describe the brain's dynamics from an external perspective. The architecture's primary object is the I-relative agency profile, which requires the inside-perspective anchor of the modeling I (per File 1's foundation framing). A third-person description of the same dynamics may produce useful implementation-mapping, but the inside-perspective object is structurally distinct.

*Ownership-integration as not reducible.* Condition 6 ownership-integration requires that the agent's relation to the act is configurationally integrated with their identity-correlation profile. This is not a prediction-error variable or an executive-control mechanism — it is a relation between the act's source-pathway and the I's structural identity-configuration.

*Source-status overlay as not captured.* Source-status (transparency, alternative-availability, source-access, verification-availability) is a function of the agent's relation to the option-space-construction process, not a function of generative-model variables alone. Predictive-coding can describe the dynamics of how affordances are constructed; it does not by itself decide whether those affordances are source-clean or source-compromised.

*Mode B object-domain availability as not reducible to single-capacity executive function.* P7 specifies that Mode B availability is domain-specific — agents may have reflective access to action but not source-pathway, intention but not affective-contraction, outcome but not selection-pathway. A single global executive-function capacity (working memory, attention, inhibition) cannot capture this asymmetry.

The architecture's hard discriminator: *The architecture can use predictive-control and executive-control models as implementation-level candidates, but its primary object is the I-relative agency profile, not the control system described from nowhere.* This is not a claim that predictive coding is wrong or that executive-control is wrong — both frameworks are useful at the implementation level. It is a claim that the architecture's structural-perspectival object is not reducible to control-system variables alone, and that several Section 11 predictions (P7, P8, P9, P12, P18) discriminate the architecture from frameworks that treat agency as exhausted by control-system dynamics.

The table establishes that the architecture's discriminating commitments are distributed across the prediction registry, not concentrated in any single prediction. No single prediction can decide between the architecture and any single framework; convergent evidence across multiple predictions is required. This is the same convergent-evidence discipline that Sections 9.10, 10, and 11.11 establish for any architectural commitment.

# 6. Foundation-level metaphysical residue and principled limits

The architecture has substantial structural commitments, but it does not solve every question the free-will literature raises. Some questions sit upstream of the architecture's structural vocabulary — at the metaphysical foundation level the architecture treats as background — and others fall outside the architecture's explicit scope. This section catalogues these as principled limits the architecture acknowledges as outside its scope rather than deferring as eventually-solvable. Section 6.1 sets the boundary discipline: this section catalogues residue, it does not resolve it. Section 6.2 surfaces the three foundation-level metaphysical residues (ultimate sourcehood, primal not-I dependence, lawful-frame grounding). Section 6.3 develops theological resonance under strict translation-not-derivation discipline. Section 6.4 addresses contemplative and no-agent translation limits. Section 6.5 engages source-sensitive Frankfurt-class descendants — the variants of hierarchical identification that build sourcehood-conditions into their analysis — as the cases where the architecture's discriminator-distance from compatibilist-friendly views narrows but does not close. Section 6.6 engages self-determination theory and the autonomy-psychology literature.

The companion *Agency Architecture* paper catalogues additional open problems specific to the agency-domain (formal weighting among profile axes, cascade dynamics, neural implementation of the operator-bundle, group-agency modeling without group-mind, therapeutic translation, and the broader research-program agenda) [Agency §12].


## 6.1 Boundary of Section 6: cataloguing residue, not resolving it

This section has strict boundary discipline. Six commitments govern what the section does and does not do:

*This section does not solve the open problems.* It catalogues them. Each open problem is anchored to a specific architectural commitment or gap, classified by the kind of work its resolution would require, and stated without an attempt at resolution within this section. Any proposed resolution belongs to future application papers, foundation extensions, or empirical research programs.

*This section does not present open problems as failures of the architecture.* Open problems are a structural feature of any architectural framework that takes itself seriously. The governing principle: an open problem is not a contradiction; it is a structurally visible dependency whose formal, empirical, philosophical, or translational resolution is not supplied by the present paper.

*This section does not introduce new architectural apparatus.* It does not introduce a new section-level formal profile parallel to $AP_{m,t;a}$, $EIP_p$, $PP_i$, or $FTP_f$. It does not promote new predictions; prediction-promotion belongs to Section 11. It does not assign responsibility; attribution belongs to Section 9. It does not compare frameworks; framework-translation belongs to Section 5. Where a Section 5 framework comparison surfaces an architectural gap that requires further work, that work is flagged in the open-problems catalogue (this paper §6 for foundation-level residue / [Agency §12] for agency-domain problems) as residue rather than resolved within Section 5 itself.

*This section does not claim that the architecture will eventually solve all open problems.* Some open problems are principled limits — they mark where the architecture's commitments (implementation-neutrality, lawful-frame dependence, indexed modeling I) intersect with demands that lie outside the architecture's structural scope. These principled limits should be acknowledged as such, not deferred indefinitely as if every open problem were eventually solvable within the same architecture.

*This section does not produce a single "problem of free will" subsection.* Such a subsection would collapse the architecture's structural distinctions back into the very undifferentiated object the paper has spent ten sections disaggregating. The open problems below are deliberately structured around specific architectural commitments and gaps, not around the omnibus "free will" debate.

*This section does not over-formalize the open problems beyond what the architecture already supports.* The formal-weighting and formal-mathematical residues identify where formalization is needed; they do not perform premature formalization. A weighting formula stated prematurely would be a different architectural commitment, not an open problem.

The section is organized in three layers. The first layer addresses *foundation-level metaphysical residue* — open problems that exceed the agency paper's scope and reach into the foundation's commitments about the modeling I, the not-I, the lawful frame, and substrate givenness (6.2), with controlled translation into theological vocabulary (6.3). The second layer addresses *research-program open problems* — formal weighting, accountability thresholds, source-status and Mode B operationalization, source-compromise dynamics, repair-accountability, developmental-window operationalization, neural implementation, cross-order temporal binding, group-agency modeling, therapeutic and clinical translation, cross-cultural attribution practice, contemplative translation limits, source-sensitive Frankfurt descendants, Self-determination theory engagement, and empirical-design problems not yet promoted (13.4–13.18). The third layer synthesizes a priority map for future work (13.19) and the handoff to this paper's §7 (or [Agency §13]) (13.20).


## 6.2 Foundation-level metaphysical residue: ultimate sourcehood, primal not-I, and lawful-frame dependence

The deepest open problems exceed the agency paper's scope and reach into the foundation's commitments. The architecture is structurally complete enough to define free choice, source-status, responsibility-attribution, empirical predictions, and framework translations. It is not metaphysically complete in the stronger sense of resolving ultimate sourcehood, primal dependence, or the final ground of lawful-frame structure.

*Ultimate sourcehood.* The architecture supplies *structural sourcehood* — a graded apparatus that distinguishes source-clean, source-compromised, source-poisoned, coerced, osmotically shaped, scaffold-captured, developmental-window-qualified, manipulated-but-owned, and manipulated-and-non-owned configurations. It does not supply *metaphysical ultimacy*. The hard incompatibilist's question — can any finite agent be the final source of itself? — is not an omitted variable inside $AP_{m,t;a}$. It is a demand for a different kind of explanation: not "what is the source-status of this action?" but "can any finite agent be the final metaphysical source of itself?" These are different questions, and the architecture's structural sourcehood apparatus does not answer the second. Hard incompatibilists who require ultimate sourcehood as a precondition for moral responsibility may continue to reject the architecture's apparatus as supplying merely partial sourcehood-sensitivity rather than the metaphysical ultimacy they require. The architecture's response is to construct a rich structural sourcehood apparatus without claiming to satisfy ultimate-sourcehood demands; whether the structural apparatus is *normatively sufficient* for responsibility-attribution remains exactly the dispute the architecture does not settle.

*Primal not-I dependence.* Foundation v5.3.3 establishes the indexed modeling I against what is not-I. The agency paper inherits this primal distinction — the I develops against the not-I, the I's substrate is given rather than chosen, the I's initial focus-capacities and developmental conditions arrive rather than being constructed by the I from nothing. The agency paper does not explain the final ground of this primal distinction. The not-I is given; the I's relation to it is foundational; the structural conditions under which an I can develop at all are presupposed rather than derived. This is foundation-level residue, not agency-paper residue: it would not be resolved by elaborating the agency apparatus further. It would require foundation extension.

*Lawful-frame dependence.* The architecture requires a lawful structural frame for its profile-objects ($\kappa$, $\phi$, $\rho$, $\Pi$, $A$, $AP_{m,t;a}$, etc.) to be defined (per 3.7). This is the only substrate-metaphysical commitment the architecture makes — substrate-determinism, substrate-stochasticity, and mixed substrates are all admissible if the structural frame is stable enough. But the architecture does not explain *why* there is a lawful frame, or what would obtain in its absence. The lawful-frame qualifier is a precondition of agency-analysis being definable, not a derived consequence of the architecture's other commitments. It marks a structural dependency that exceeds the agency paper's scope.

*Tautological cogito limit.* The indexed modeling I anchors the architecture; it does not generate a full metaphysics of existence. The architecture treats the I-anchor as primitive — a structural posit required for the inside-perspective profile-objects to have a referent. It does not ground the I in something prior, does not derive the I from non-I substrate dynamics, and does not legislate the relationship between the indexed I and the underlying substrate that supports it. Foundation v5.3.3 marks this as a tautological limit: the I is what is indexed, not what is explained. The agency paper inherits this limit and does not exceed it.

*Substrate givenness.* The I inherits focus-capacity, projection-toolset constraints, $\phi$-availability, affect-capacity, and operator-bundle architecture from substrate conditions it did not choose. Toolkit-development (5.4, 5.5) operates on substrate-given starting conditions; mastery (5.7) operates within substrate-given constraints. The architecture's six conditions of free choice presuppose this substrate givenness rather than explaining it. An agent is *thrown* (in the existentialist phrase) into an initial substrate configuration that shapes which willing-operations are available and which orders of will are reachable. The agency paper specifies the structure of agency under these conditions; it does not explain why these particular conditions obtain rather than others.

*Normative sufficiency.* The architecture supplies structural inputs to responsibility-attribution. It does not supply a final proof that these inputs are *normatively sufficient* for moral responsibility. The compatibilist-coherent stance (per 9.15, v0.1.8.1 Patch 6) commits the architecture to specifying inputs without claiming normative-sufficiency closure. Whether structural ownership-integration, source-cleanliness, evaluative engagement, Mode B availability, developmental-window status, and cascade-foreseeability are *enough* for moral responsibility-attribution is a normative-philosophical question that the architecture does not legislate. Hard incompatibilists may judge them insufficient; standard compatibilists may judge them superabundant; the architecture supplies the structural decomposition, not the normative verdict.

These residues also mark prior foundation "unknowns" that the foundation paper itself flags: II-formalisation (the formal treatment of other-I / inter-I structure), verification-toolset development, contact interface specification, the formal relationship between the I-anchor and substrate physics. The agency paper inherits these unknowns. They are not resolved by the present paper, and they should not be expected to be resolved by application-level work alone.

The principle: ultimate sourcehood, primal not-I dependence, lawful-frame dependence, tautological cogito limit, substrate givenness, and normative sufficiency are not omitted variables inside $AP_{m,t;a}$. They are demands for different kinds of explanation that the architecture acknowledges as structurally outside its scope.


## 6.3 Theological resonance: translation, not derivation

The architecture has theological resonance because its agency apparatus naturally raises questions about created dependence, freedom under lawfulness, sourcehood, fallenness, repair, grace-like restoration, and providence-like lawful ordering. But the architecture does not derive theology, prove theology, or require theology. The resonance is translational and exploratory, not foundational. This subsection is included because the original drafting-order plan preserved theological resonance as a scope item, because the foundation-level residue (6.2) raises questions that have been classically engaged in theological idiom across multiple traditions, and because making the resonance explicit prevents readers from drawing theological conclusions from the architecture's structure that the architecture does not license.

The discipline is strict: *the architecture permits theological translation but does not license theological identification.* "Primal not-I," "lawful frame," "sourcehood," and "grace-like repair" are not theological terms inside the architecture unless an explicitly theological extension supplies that mapping. The translations below are presented as one possible theological vocabulary among several; comparable translations could be developed in non-theistic, non-monotheistic, or naturalistic theological frameworks. The architecture is not committed to any of them.

The translation table is offered respectfully and with explicit boundary discipline. The architecture's structural apparatus is recognizable to theological readers as engaging with concerns those traditions have worked on for centuries, but the architecture does not claim to resolve those concerns or to validate any particular theological framework. Comparable translations could be developed in Buddhist, Daoist, Hindu, Jewish, Islamic, Christian, Sufi, Stoic, or contemporary post-religious frameworks, each preserving the architecture's structural commitments while engaging the framework's theological vocabulary.

These resonances do not establish a theological interpretation of the architecture. They only show that certain agency-structural problems — dependence, lawfulness, sourcehood, impairment, restoration, repentance-like reconfiguration, discipline, and providence-like order — can be translated into theological vocabulary by a future extension. The architecture itself remains formally non-theological: it neither identifies primal not-I with God nor treats repair, grace, sin, providence, or spiritual discipline as architectural primitives. Theological resonance is also not Section 5-style framework comparison (no $FTP_f$ profile is constructed for any theological tradition) and not foundation-level derivation (no theological extension is performed). The subsection records the resonance and constrains its drift; nothing more.

The architecture's stance: theological resonance is real, theological translation is possible, theological derivation is not licensed. A theological extension of the architecture would have to be separately built, with its own foundation, its own warranting commitments, and its own boundary discipline. Such an extension is outside the agency paper's scope and outside this section's scope. The present subsection records the resonance and constrains its drift.


## 6.4 Contemplative and no-agent translation limits

Section 5.11 translated wisdom-tradition no-agent themes cautiously: deconstruction of egoic authorship, receptive willing, low-appropriation action, non-attachment, decentering, and effortless action. It also preserved a strict refusal: no-agent translation cannot deny the indexed modeling I without dissolving the architecture's formal anchor.

The open problem is dialogical: would contemplative practitioners, scholars, or traditions recognize this translation as structurally faithful?

This cannot be answered from inside the architecture alone. The architecture can say: no-agent does not mean no indexed modeling I; egoic authorship can be deconstructed; receptive willing is a real agency configuration; effortless action is not necessarily non-agency; Mode B decentering can alter relation to thought, affect, and action; non-attachment can reduce compulsive identity-correlation binding. But contemplative traditions may reject the translation as too formal, too I-anchored, too cognitive, too agency-preserving, or insufficiently soteriological. That residue should be acknowledged rather than prematurely absorbed.

Future work would need: textual engagement (traditions differ; "wisdom traditions" is too broad); practitioner dialogue (the architecture cannot validate contemplative phenomenology by armchair translation alone); cross-cultural vocabulary work (terms like "self," "agent," "intention," and "ownership" may not map cleanly); practice-stage sensitivity (early mindfulness, nondual realization, ritual discipline, and ethical cultivation may involve different agency profiles); affect integration (devotion, equanimity, compassion, shame, awe, and fear may alter agency in tradition-specific ways).

The architecture preserves humility here. It can offer a structural translation; it cannot claim to exhaust the traditions. The architecture's translation of "no-agent" insights is offered as a hypothesis for dialogue, not as a settled rendering. Whether contemplative practitioners accept the translation is an empirical and dialogical question that future work must engage on the traditions' own terms.


## 6.5 Source-sensitive Frankfurt-class descendants

Section 5.4 compared the architecture against Frankfurt-class hierarchical-identification views, but it also acknowledged that source-sensitive descendants reduce the discriminator-distance. That remains a serious open problem.

The architecture's main criticism of endorsement-only hierarchical identification is clear: wholeheartedness and ownership-integration do not settle source-status. A manipulated agent may wholeheartedly endorse a choice whose source is compromised. This is why P5 — source-compromised-but-owned action — is a flagship discriminator.

But sophisticated hierarchical theories may incorporate history, manipulation, responsiveness, authenticity, or sourcehood constraints. The architecture should not treat all hierarchical identification views as endorsement-only. Future work should compare the architecture against source-sensitive hierarchical views along at least six axes:

| Axis | Comparison question |
|---|---|
| Ownership | Does the theory distinguish ownership from responsibility? |
| Source-status | Does it distinguish wholehearted but source-compromised endorsement? |
| Developmental history | Does it treat immature, trauma-shaped, or scaffold-captured formation differently? |
| Mode B | Does it require reflective access, and if so, global or domain-specific? |
| Cascade | Does it handle profile-formation and downstream self-shaping? |
| Manipulation | Does it treat manipulation as ownership failure, responsibility failure, or source-status compromise? |

The architecture likely remains distinct, but the gap is narrower than with simple hierarchical endorsement theories. This catalogue (§6) flags this as future philosophical comparison, not as already-exhausted Section 5 work.


## 6.6 Self-determination theory and autonomy psychology

Section 5 flagged self-determination theory (Ryan-Deci-class) as a candidate expansion rather than a fully engaged framework. This was the right choice. The agency paper has not yet done enough with autonomy, competence, and relatedness to claim a serious translation.

Still, the connection is promising. Self-determination theory's autonomy concept maps to live-option inhabitability, source-status, non-coerced option-space, and ownership-integration. Competence maps to toolkit capacity, order-specific option construction, and implementation reliability. Relatedness maps to connection / extension affects from the *Differentiated Affect* paper, social scaffolding, and constructed-order agency. Internalization maps to Mode C integration, profile-formation, source-history, and $\Pi$-alignment. Autonomy support maps to transparency, alternatives, source-access, and recipient-aim preservation.

But the architecture should not simply absorb self-determination theory. Several questions remain: does autonomy in the SDT sense map most closely to ownership, source-cleanliness, live-option availability, or psychological need-satisfaction? Does competence map to toolkit-development, effort-economy, implementation capacity, or perceived efficacy? Does relatedness require importing more of the *Differentiated Affect* connection-cluster? Does internalization map to Mode C integration, source-compromise, scaffolded agency, or all three depending on case? Can P14 (order-matched training) and P16 (social-influence by source-status factors) be tested using SDT-style measures without reducing the architecture to SDT?

The open problem is therefore comparative and empirical. Self-determination theory may become one of the architecture's most useful psychological interlocutors, but that engagement requires a dedicated subsection or future paper.

# 7. Conclusion: free choice as structural architecture

## 7.1 What this paper has done

This paper has not addressed "the problem of free will" as a single contested question. It has constructed free choice as an inside-perspective structural architecture and replaced the undifferentiated debate with a structured field of analyzable distinctions. The paper defined free choice proper through six interdependent conditions and three structurally distinct tiers (free selection, free choice proper, wholehearted free choice), analyzed the manipulation paradigm to display the ownership / sourcehood dissociation, translated eight alternative frameworks under a translation-not-refutation discipline, and catalogued foundation-level metaphysical residue under principled-limit discipline.

The architecture's contribution to the free-will debate is precise: it supplies structural inputs to the analysis of free choice, manipulation, and responsibility, without claiming normative-sufficiency closure or metaphysical completeness. It is *compatibilist-coherent without being a compatibilist proof*, *sourcehood-sensitive without satisfying ultimate-sourcehood demands*, *implementation-friendly without reducing free choice to control-system variables*, *phenomenologically serious without remaining descriptive phenomenology*, and *open to no-agent translation without erasing the indexed modeling I*. It does not solve the metaphysical problem of free will, prove or refute any standard position, claim empirical confirmation, or license theological identification.

## 7.2 What free choice becomes under the architecture

Under the six-condition definition, free choice is a real structural configuration — distinguishable from compulsion, akrasia, manipulation, and confabulation — that obtains over any substrate supplying lawful structural frame. It is not contingent on substrate-indeterminism, not contingent on libertarian metaphysical openness, and not contingent on theological license for ultimate-sourcehood. It is also not the same as wholeheartedness, reasons-responsiveness, hierarchical endorsement, or post-hoc authorship attribution. Each of those is a partial reading of free choice that the six-condition definition incorporates without collapsing.

The three-tier configuration recovers what the philosophical literature has tracked in different vocabularies: thin spontaneous choice (free selection), substantive deliberated choice (free choice proper), and deeply integrated choice expressing the agent's most centrally-held commitments (wholehearted free choice). The architecture refuses to rank these tiers normatively. Each is a genuine configuration; each obtains in different content-domains and developmental windows; each is real free choice, structurally specifiable.

## 7.3 What manipulation reveals

The manipulation paradigm is the architecture's hardest discriminator against ownership-only and sourcehood-only views. An agent who acts wholeheartedly from a source-compromised option-space *retains ownership* (the action expresses the agent's evaluative profile) while *sourcehood is compromised* (the option-space was upstream-shaped by another agent's interests). Frankfurt-class hierarchical identification accounts must over-attribute responsibility (wholeheartedness present); hard-incompatibilist sourcehood accounts must under-attribute ownership (sourcehood compromised). The architecture's triadic dissociation (§4.1) and profile-indexed attribution (§4.3) handle the case structurally: ownership intact, sourcehood compromised, responsibility partial along several dimensions, accountability for repair available even where original-act responsibility is reduced. This is what the classical free-will categories have been unable to track.

## 7.4 What the framework translations clarify

Section 5's eight framework translations established that the architecture is not a refutation, absorption, or synthesis of any framework. It is a structural decomposition that allows each framework to be located precisely: which structural distinction does each framework preserve, flatten, or mislocate? The result is not a scorecard of framework-defeats but an analytical map of where the architecture overlaps with each, where it refuses reduction, what discriminates it, and what residue remains for further engagement. Section 11 of the companion paper specifies the empirical predictions that discriminate the architecture from each framework where empirical work is feasible [Agency §11.13].

## 7.5 What remains residue

Section 6 catalogued the principled limits the architecture acknowledges as outside its scope. Ultimate sourcehood, primal not-I dependence, lawful-frame grounding, normative sufficiency, theological identification, and certain contemplative no-agent translations remain residue the architecture cannot resolve from inside its structural vocabulary. A reader who insists on resolution of these residues as a precondition for accepting the architecture's free-choice account may legitimately decline the architecture; the architecture operates at a level where its commitments hold under both ultimate-sourcehood-preserving and ultimate-sourcehood-denying assumptions, and similarly under multiple substrate-metaphysical, theological, and contemplative commitments.

## 7.6 Place in the larger book projection

This paper is split A of the Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture's third application paper, focused on the philosophical contribution. The companion paper *Agency Architecture: Verification-Toolkit, Variation, and Empirical Interface* develops the structural and empirical contribution: order-indexed willing, the verification-toolkit, will / trying / effort, decision-events and Mode B availability, the seventeen-family variation taxonomy, the full triadic responsibility-attribution apparatus, the empirical-interface mapping (Libet / Wegner / Soon), the eighteen formal predictions across seven families, and the open-problem catalogue specific to the agency domain. Readers wanting empirical operationalization, clinical-adjacent variation analysis, or the predictions that discriminate the architecture from each framework's empirical commitments should consult the companion paper.

The agency domain is the third application of the architecture; the first was *Temporal Phenomenology* and the second was *Differentiated Affect*. The architecture's projected book series will continue with applications to the narrative self, dreams and altered states, intersubjectivity beyond the prerogative of consistency, aesthetic experience, and the formal derivation of the structural prerogatives from the operating lived-I architecture. The free-choice definition this paper supplies will be used as the canonical agency-anchor wherever subsequent applications engage agency-relevant configurations.

## 7.7 Final synthesis

The architecture transforms specific debate questions into specific architectural questions. Where the classical free-will debate poses single undifferentiated questions, the architecture supplies decomposed analytical replacements:

| Classical debate question | Architecture's structural replacement |
|---|---|
| "Is free will real?" | Which agency configuration is present in this case? Is the configuration free choice proper, free selection, wholehearted free choice, manipulation, akrasia, compulsion, or another structural configuration? |
| "Was the action free?" | Which of the six conditions hold, thinly or fully, at which order of willing? |
| "Was the agent responsible?" | What is the joint profile of ownership, source-status, condition-profile, developmental-window, Mode B availability, and live mitigation options for the relevant attribution-object? |
| "Did manipulation erase responsibility?" | Was ownership intact? Was sourcehood compromised? Was Mode B reflectively available? Was repair live? Each is a separate question with a separate structural answer. |
| "Is free will compatible with determinism?" | The architecture is implementation-neutral within a lawful-frame qualifier; substrate-determinism, substrate-stochasticity, and lawful-frame indeterminism are all compatible with the six-condition definition; ultimate-sourcehood remains a foundation-level metaphysical residue the architecture does not address. |

This transformation does not dissolve the original questions; some of them retain force at foundation-level or normative-philosophical levels the architecture does not address. The architecture supplies analytical decomposition that lets each question be engaged separately, with appropriate apparatus, rather than rehearsed as a single contested intuition.

The paper's conclusion is therefore neither triumphalist nor skeptical. Free choice is real as an inside-perspective architectural configuration, but it is not simple. It is conditioned, ordered, developmental, source-sensitive, report-mediated, empirically indirect through the empirical-interface apparatus the companion paper develops, and philosophically residual at the foundation-level. The architecture has not solved the philosophical problem of free will. It has transformed the problem-space: from undifferentiated debate over a single contested property to a structured field of analyzable distinctions, framework-translation profiles, and visible residue.

Free choice retains its complexity, felt depth, phenomenological integrity, and practical stakes. What changes is that we now have apparatus for analyzing it. Future work — empirical (developed in the companion paper), philosophical, clinical-adjacent (also in the companion), cross-cultural, dialogical, and (where explicitly licensed) theological-translation work — will test, refine, and extend that apparatus.

> *The agency architecture's free-choice account does not end the free-will debate by declaring victory. It makes the debate structurally navigable.*

# Glossary of architectural terms


## Usage note

This glossary stabilizes the terminology used in the agency paper. It defines terms according to their role inside the Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture and the present agency application. It is not a replacement for the foundation document set, not a general philosophy dictionary, and not a source of new architectural commitments. Where a term comes from an external framework, the entry defines only the sense relevant to this paper. Foundation-level terms are summarized at the level needed for this paper's usage; for full derivation, see Foundation v5.3.3. The glossary defines already-stabilized usage; it does not create new usage.

## Status tags

Each entry carries one or more tags indicating its role in the architecture:

- **Foundation** — Term inherited from Foundation v5.3.3.
- **Agency** — Term introduced or specialized in this agency paper.
- **Attribution** — Section 9 ownership / responsibility / accountability apparatus.
- **Empirical** — Section 10 / 11 empirical-interface or prediction term.
- **Translation** — Section 5 framework-comparison or external-framework term.

## Notation and formal objects

The architecture uses the following symbols and formal objects throughout the paper:


**Accountability** — *Attribution.* Structural addressability for explanation, demand, mitigation, repair, or response. Accountability is not identical to blame, punishment, legal liability, or moral desert; nor is it identical to ownership or responsibility. It is the third leg of the triadic dissociation in Section 9 (ownership / responsibility / accountability) and depends on the full attribution profile, including ownership, source-status, developmental-window, Mode B availability, and live mitigation or repair options. See also: ownership; responsibility; repair-accountability; $AP_{m,t;a}$. (Sections 9.1, 9.4, 9.6, 9.10)

**Addiction-like capture** — *Agency.* A variation-family in which substrate coupling is captured by sustained reward-pathway activity, producing condition-failure modes typically including sourcehood compromise (substrate-driven option-shaping), evaluative narrowing, and reduced operator-bundle reach. Addiction-like capture is not the same as compulsion, habit, or akrasia; it has its own structural signature in $AP_{m,t;a}$. (Section 8.7)

**Agency** — *Agency.* The inside-perspective structural configuration by which the indexed modeling I constructs, evaluates, selects, and integrates action through order-indexed willing across foundational and constructed domains. Agency is not a single binary property, a third-person behavior, a neural readiness signal, a folk responsibility judgment, or a metaphysical power added to the substrate. The architecture treats agency as a multi-dimensional structural configuration with dissociable components. See also: agency event; agency-as-such; free choice proper. (Sections 2, 4)

**Agency event** — *Agency.* A specific instantiation of agency in time: a particular live-option construction, evaluation, selection, substrate-coupling, Mode B availability, and ownership-integration profile, indexed to moment, agent, target-domain, and order. Agency events are the basic units of structural analysis in the paper. (Section 4.3)

**Agency-as-such** — *Agency.* Agency considered abstractly as a property an agent has or lacks. The paper does not treat agency-as-such as a meaningful direct-measurement target; agency is structural configuration, and only specific architectural objects (live-option construction, evaluative engagement, substrate coupling, Mode B availability, ownership-integration, source-status, etc.) are directly probeable. (Section 10.2)

**Agency-quality** — *Agency.* The architectural assessment of how well a given agency event satisfies the six conditions of free choice across order-engagement, ownership-integration, and source-status. Agency-quality is *not* the same as effort: a high-effort agency event can be low-quality (capture under struggle), and a low-effort agency event can be high-quality (mastery in flow). See also: effort; effort-economy; flow; mastery. (Sections 6.8, 6.9)

**Akrasia** — *Agency.* A variation-family in which evaluative engagement (Condition 2) is intact but operator-bundle selection (Condition 3) does not deliver the evaluatively-favored action; the agent acts against their own evaluation. Akrasia is *not* the same as compulsion: in compulsion, evaluative engagement itself is bypassed or dominated by capture; in akrasia, evaluation occurs but is not action-determining. (Section 8.4)

**Alienation** — *Agency.* A variation-family in which ownership-integration (Condition 6) fails: the action does not register as the agent's own. Alienation is *not* the same as compulsion (which can preserve ownership-integration) or confabulation (which can preserve a false sense of ownership over manipulated action). (Section 8.11)

**Answerability** — *Attribution.* A scoping form of accountability: the structural condition in which an agent is appropriately addressed for explanation or response regarding an action or cascade. Answerability is one mode of accountability and does not exhaust it. (Section 9.6)

**Asymptotic layer** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the layer of the indexed modeling I that develops asymptotically toward the maximally-self-defined I through identity-correlation operations. The paper uses this only as background for application-level commitments; for full development, see Foundation v5.3.3.

**Attribution** — *Attribution.* The structural assessment of whether and how the dissociable relations of ownership, responsibility, and accountability hold for a given agent, moment, and action or cascade. Attribution is computed through the attribution profile $AP_{m,t;a}$; it is not a single binary verdict and is not a conserved quantity transferred between agents. See also: $AP_{m,t;a}$; profile-indexed attribution. (Sections 9.2, 9.4)

**Attribution profile $AP_{m,t;a}$** — *Attribution.* The ten-component structural object that organizes the triadic dissociability of ownership, responsibility, and accountability for a given act or cascade $a$ at moment $m$ in agent / modeling I $t$. Its ten components are $V$ variation-family, $O$ ownership-status, $S$ source-status, $C$ condition-profile, $B$ Mode B object-domain availability, $D$ developmental-window, $K$ cascade profile, $M$ mitigation / repair options, $R$ role / order profile, and $T$ temporal locus, written $AP_{m,t;a} = \langle V, O, S, C, B, D, K, M, R, T \rangle$. The profile yields a structural attribution rather than a binary free / unfree verdict. See also: profile-indexed attribution; ownership; responsibility; accountability. (Section 9.4)

**Attribution-object** — *Attribution.* A specific architectural object to which attribution applies: typically the decision-event, trying, action, outcome, cascade, or source-shaping pathway. The architecture distinguishes attribution-objects rather than treating attribution as applying to "the act" undifferentiated. See also: cascade-attribution. (Section 9.2)

**Backbone predictions** — *Empirical.* Seven of the paper's eighteen predictions identified as the paper's empirical spine: P1 (thin / rich choice multi-marker divergence), P5 (ownership / sourcehood dissociation), P8 (Mode B availability vs exercise vs report), P9 (effort / agency-quality dissociation), P10 (chosen-habit vs compulsive-habit signatures), P14 (order-matched training and intervention), and P18 (longitudinal cascade and profile-formation). The seven backbone predictions carry the principal empirical load; the other eleven predictions extend or specify them. The architecture's empirical traction depends primarily on the backbone profile holding under pressure conditions. See also: pressure condition; prediction profile. (Section 11.4)

**Cascade-attribution** — *Attribution.* The structural assessment of attribution for outcomes downstream of an action through cascade pathways (foreseeable consequences, source-shaping of others, institutional propagation, longitudinal effects). Cascade-attribution operates through cascade-foreseeability, role-indexed attribution-input, and the cascade-attribution test; it is not the same as attribution to the original action and may distribute differently across agents. (Sections 9.7, 9.10)

**Cascade-attribution test** — *Attribution.* The six-gate procedure for assessing cascade-attribution: foreseeability, controllability, role-position, mitigation-availability, source-shaping pathway, and downstream-cascade-density. Each gate generates a structural finding; the test does not deliver a single binary verdict but a profile-indexed cascade attribution. (Section 9.10)

**Cascade-foreseeability** — *Attribution.* The structural assessment of whether and how an agent could foresee the cascade-effects of an action, given the agent's profile, position, and toolkit at the moment of action. Cascade-foreseeability is one input to cascade-attribution; it is not itself an attribution verdict. (Section 9.7)

**Clinical translation** — *Open problem.* The translation of the architecture's variation-family analyses into clinical context for diagnostic, therapeutic, or rehabilitative purposes. The architecture supplies structural inputs for clinical translation but does not license clinical authority, diagnostic verdicts, or treatment recommendations; clinical translation requires independent clinical validation and is treated as a research-program problem. ([Agency §12.10])

**Coercion / coerced choice** — *Agency / Attribution.* A source-status and option-space configuration in which threat, pressure, or imposed constraint structures the live-option space. Coercion may leave ownership intact while compromising sourcehood; it therefore requires attribution-profile analysis rather than automatic exculpation or automatic responsibility. See also: source-status; manipulation; constrained choice; $AP_{m,t;a}$. (Sections 5.9, 8)

**Cogito (tautological cogito)** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the foundational self-reference operation by which the indexed modeling I is structurally available to itself. The agency paper inherits the cogito as background for the indexed modeling I; it does not redefine or develop it. See also: indexed modeling I.

**Compatibilist-coherent** — *Translation.* The architecture's stance toward compatibilism: the structural commitments of the paper are coherent with compatibilist positions and translate cleanly to many compatibilist concerns, but the paper does *not* claim to prove compatibilism. The compatibilist-coherent stance preserves hard-incompatibilist residue (ultimate sourcehood remains an open question the architecture does not answer) and does not foreclose libertarian or hard-incompatibilist positions on substrate-metaphysical grounds. (Sections 9.15, 12.3)

**Compulsion** — *Agency / Attribution.* A variation-family in which substrate-coupling is compromised by capture, bypass, or preemption, making it primarily a Condition 4 failure or thinning. Compulsion may secondarily distort evaluative engagement (Condition 2) or operator-bundle selection (Condition 3), but it is not defined by evaluative misalignment alone. *Not* the same as akrasia: akrasia is, by default, a Condition 3 evaluation / selection misalignment without Condition 4 capture. *Not* the same as habit (which may preserve ownership-integration) or addiction-like capture (which has its own profile). See also: capture; akrasia; addiction-like capture; substrate coupling. (Section 8.5)

**Confabulation** — *Agency / Empirical.* The post-hoc construction of a report about one's own action that does not accurately track the action's actual source-pathway. Confabulation is *not* the same as post-hoc recognition (which tracks the source-pathway accurately given source-opacity) or alienation (which is failure of ownership-integration itself). The post-hoc recognition / source-opacity / confabulation triad (7.9) distinguishes these structurally. (Section 7.9)

**Configurational willing** — *Agency.* A willing-strategy in which the agent configures the substrate (environment, body, toolkit) to make a desired action more accessible or a competing action less accessible, without willing the action itself directly at the moment of action. Configurational willing is one of three principal willing-strategies (alongside direct and slingshot). It is often more effort-efficient than direct willing for high-resistance actions. (Section 6.7)

**Constructed order** — *Agency.* An order of willing beyond the three foundational orders (focus, thought, substrate-mediated action), constructed through toolkit-development and mastery in target-domains such as asking, directing, team coordination, institutional engagement, and beyond. Constructed orders extend the order-indexed willing pathway by N-order generalization (5.7). They are not foundation-level primitives; they are application-level extensions of the toolkit-development pathway. See also: order-indexed willing; N-order will; toolkit-development. (Sections 5.7, 5.8)

**Contact ($\kappa$)** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The foundation coordinate by which a content makes contact with the indexed modeling I. Inherited from Foundation v5.3.3.

**Contrastive live-option space** — *Agency.* The structured space of multiple distinguishable, evaluable live options that Condition 1 of free choice requires. The contrastive nature of the option-space is essential: a single available action without contrast is not free choice, and a contrast that is merely nominal (the alternative is not actually live) does not satisfy Condition 1. See also: live option; live-option space; six conditions of free choice. (Section 3.2)

**Cross-cultural attribution practice** — *Open problem.* The cross-cultural variation in attribution practices around ownership, responsibility, and accountability, which interfaces with the architecture's attribution apparatus but is treated as a separate research-program problem. The architecture supplies structural inputs for cross-cultural attribution analysis but does not claim cross-cultural universality of any specific attribution-verdict. ([Agency §12.11])

**Decision-event** — *Agency.* The structural moment at which operator-bundle selection (Condition 3) yields a specific action-direction. The decision-event is not identical to the readiness-potential, the moment of subjective awareness, the verbal report, or the action onset; the timing-marker non-identity chain (10.6) distinguishes these. The decision-event is inferentially located rather than directly observable. (Sections 7.10, 10.6)

**Deliberation** — *Agency.* The process of constructing, evaluating, and contrasting live options before operator-bundle selection. Deliberation can be brief or extended, explicit or implicit, and is not a precondition of free choice in every case (mastery and flow can satisfy the six conditions with minimal deliberation). The deliberation / decision / trying / action / outcome separation (7.10) is preserved throughout the architecture. (Section 7.5)

**Developmental-window** — *Attribution.* The structural period during which an agent's evaluative profile, toolkit, or option-space is being formed and is therefore not yet an appropriate locus for full attribution. The developmental-window is a profile-component in $AP_{m,t;a}$ and bears on whether responsibility-attribution applies in mature, immature, pre-agential, scaffold-captured, or trauma-shaped configurations. (Section 9.4)

**Direct willing** — *Agency.* A willing-strategy in which the agent wills the target action itself, at the moment of action, through operator-bundle selection. Direct willing is one of three principal willing-strategies (alongside configurational and slingshot). It is appropriate for low-to-moderate-resistance actions and high-mastery domains; for high-resistance actions, direct willing is often less effort-efficient than configurational or slingshot strategies. (Section 6.7)

**Dissociation** — *Agency.* A variation-family in which structural coordination across the conditions of free choice is partially disrupted, often producing split or layered agency configurations. Dissociation is *not* the same as alienation (failure of ownership-integration), confabulation (false report), or compulsion (capture); it has its own structural profile and may co-occur with these. (Section 8.12)

**Effort** — *Agency.* The structural assessment of resistance encountered and managed during an agency event. Effort tracks resistance — the difficulty the action poses given current toolkit, configuration, and substrate state — *not* the quality of the agency exercise. High-effort agency events can be low-quality (capture under struggle); low-effort agency events can be high-quality (mastery in flow). Effort moralization — treating high effort as virtuous or low effort as suspect — is a recurring drift the architecture rejects. See also: agency-quality; effort-economy; resistance; flow. (Section 6.8)

**Effort-economy** — *Agency.* The comparative assessment of effort across willing-strategies (direct, configurational, slingshot) for a given action under given resistance. Effort-economy is a structural property of the strategy-action pairing, not a moral measure of the agent. (Section 6.9)

**Empirical interpretation profile $EIP_p$** — *Empirical.* The eight-component structural object that organizes the interpretation of a given empirical paradigm $p$ in terms of which architectural objects it probes. Its eight components are $T$ task-structure, $P_o$ probed object / empirical proxy, $O_d$ order-domain, $C_x$ condition-target, $B_x$ Mode B status, $R_s$ report-structure, $M_s$ marker-signal, and $Q$ interpretive caution, written $EIP_p = \langle T, P_o, O_d, C_x, B_x, R_s, M_s, Q \rangle$. The $EIP_p$ does *not* treat any paradigm as a direct measure of agency-as-such, free will, or sourcehood. See also: empirical paradigm; timing-marker non-identity. (Sections 10.2, 10.4)

**Empirical paradigm** — *Empirical.* A specific experimental or measurement paradigm used in the empirical study of agency-related phenomena (e.g., Libet-class, Wegner-class, Soon-class, Schurger-class). Empirical paradigms probe specific architectural objects through empirical proxies; they are not direct measures of agency-as-such. See also: empirical proxy; $EIP_p$. (Section 10.3)

**Evaluative engagement** — *Agency.* Condition 2 of free choice: the active evaluation of contrasted live options through the agent's evaluative apparatus (operating through $PtCns$, $PCE$-over-projection, $\Pi$-weighting, or frame-relevance, depending on configuration). Evaluative engagement can fail by absence (no evaluation occurs), capture (evaluation is dominated by drive or compulsion), or narrowing (evaluation operates over an artificially restricted space). (Section 3.2)

**First-order willing** — *Agency.* See order-indexed willing; first-order willing operates over the foundational order of focus.

**Flow** — *Agency.* A variation-family characterized by deep substrate-toolkit coupling, minimal effort, low explicit deliberation, and high agency-quality. Flow is *not* loss of agency or absence of choice; it is high-mastery configuration in which the six conditions of free choice are satisfied through mastery rather than through effortful deliberation. The flow / mastery vs capture distinction is critical: flow looks similar to automaticity-capture from the outside but has a fundamentally different structural profile (mastery preserves ownership-integration and source-status; capture does not). See also: mastery; automaticity. (Section 8.13)

**Foundation coordinates and status predicate** (Foundation v5.3.3):

- $\kappa$ — *contact* coordinate; the relation by which a content makes contact with the indexed modeling I.
- $\phi$ — *source-pathway / truth-verification access* coordinate; the relation by which the I has access to the source-status and verification-pathway of a content.
- $\rho$ — *identity-correlation* coordinate; the relation by which a content is integrated as an I-relative content (the architecture's central relation).
- $\Pi$ — *preservation-ranking* coordinate; the relation by which a content is ranked for preservation across operations (not the same as $\rho$).
- $A_{m,t}(x)$ — *phenomenal-availability status predicate*; $A_{m,t}(x)=1$ means content $x$ is phenomenally available to modeling I $m$ at time $t$, and $A_{m,t}(x)=0$ means it is not phenomenally available at that moment. $A$ is not a coordinate parallel to $\kappa$, $\phi$, $\rho$, and $\Pi$.

**Foundation prerogatives:**

- $PtCns$ — *prerogative of consistency*; the foundation-level structural commitment to internal consistency.
- $PCE$ — *prerogative of continued existence*; the foundation-level structural commitment to continued existence.

**Foundation modes** (the three modes of I-development):

- *Mode A* — pre-reflective mode of I-development.
- *Mode B* — reflective mode of I-development; supplies reflective object-access.
- *Mode C* — integrative mode of I-development.

**Foundation-level metaphysical residue** — *Open problem.* The metaphysical questions the architecture identifies as foundation-level rather than agency-application-level: ultimate sourcehood, primal not-I dependence, lawful-frame grounding, and normative sufficiency. These residues are catalogued in Section 6.2 as principled limits the architecture acknowledges as structurally outside its scope rather than deferring as eventually-solvable. See also: principled limit; ultimate sourcehood; lawful-frame grounding. (Section 6.2)

**Framework translation profile $FTP_f$** — *Translation.* The seven-component structural object that organizes the architecture's engagement with a given alternative framework $f$ (Frankfurt-class hierarchical identification, reasons-responsiveness compatibilism, attributionism, hard incompatibilism, libertarianism, illusionism, predictive coding and executive control, phenomenology / existentialism / wisdom-tradition family). Its seven components are $O_f$ framework object, $T_f$ translation route, $K_f$ kept / overlapping commitments, $R_f$ reduction refused, $D_f$ discriminator, $P_f$ prediction link, and $L_f$ limits / open residue, written $FTP_f = \langle O_f, T_f, K_f, R_f, D_f, P_f, L_f \rangle$. The $FTP_f$ does *not* claim refutation, absorption, or synthesis of the framework. See also: refused reduction; translation. (Section 5.1)

**Free choice proper** — *Agency.* The middle tier of the three-tier configuration: an agency event satisfying all six conditions (contrastive live-option space, evaluative engagement, operator-bundle selection, non-preemptive substrate coupling, non-confabulatory Mode B reflective availability, ownership-integration) at the order or orders relevant to the action. Free choice proper is the canonical case from which the agency taxonomy is derived by structural variation. It is *not* the same as wholehearted free choice (which adds deeper integration) or free selection (which is thinner). See also: free selection; wholehearted free choice; six conditions of free choice. (Section 3.2)

**Free selection** — *Agency.* The thinnest tier of free agency: the toolkit constructs an option-space, operator-bundle selection occurs, substrate coupling enables implementation, Mode B reflective availability remains intact, and the action is owned, while evaluative engagement is implicit, minimal, or absent. Free selection is thin free choice, *not* unfree action and *not* a defective form of free choice proper. The three tiers (free selection / free choice proper / wholehearted free choice) are *not* normatively ranked; they are structural configurations that occur in different domains, conditions, and developmental windows. See also: free choice proper; wholehearted free choice; evaluative engagement; six conditions of free choice. (Section 3.4)

**Fuzzy live option** — *Agency.* A live option whose contours are not sharply specified at the moment of contrast, but which is sufficiently distinguishable for evaluative engagement and operator-bundle selection. Fuzzy live options are common in practical decision-making and do not by themselves disqualify Condition 1; the contrastive live-option space requires distinguishability, not full specification. (Section 3.2)

**Habit** — *Agency.* A variation-family in which routinized substrate coupling implements an action through low-deliberation channels. Habit is *not* the same as compulsion: chosen-habitual action preserves prior ownership-integration and can be high-quality (Condition 6 satisfied through prior selection); compulsive-habitual action does not preserve ownership-integration and operates through capture. The chosen-habit / compulsive-habit distinction (P10) is one of the architecture's backbone predictions. See also: automaticity; compulsion; flow. (Section 8.6)

**Hard incompatibilism** — *Translation.* In this paper, the family of free-will views that hold no agent can be ultimately responsible for any action because no agent can be the ultimate source of their own evaluative profile. The architecture preserves hard-incompatibilist residue: ultimate sourcehood remains an open question (Section 6.2) the architecture does not answer, and a reader committed to ultimate-sourcehood demands may legitimately decline the architecture's compatibilist-coherent stance. The architecture does *not* refute hard incompatibilism; it operates at a level where the architectural commitments hold under both compatibilist and hard-incompatibilist substrate-metaphysical assumptions. (Sections 5.5, 13.2)

**Hierarchical identification** — *Translation.* The Frankfurt-class family of free-will views that ground freedom in the identification of the agent's first-order desires with second-order desires. The architecture translates the structural insight (ownership-integration involves higher-order endorsement) but does not collapse ownership-integration to second-order endorsement alone; the architecture's six-condition account is broader and more structurally specified. (Section 5.4)

**Identity-correlation ($\rho$)** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The foundation coordinate by which a content is integrated as an I-relative content. The architecture's central relation. *Not* the same as preservation-ranking ($\Pi$): identity-correlation is the integration relation; preservation-ranking is the preservation-priority relation. (Foundation v5.3.3)

**Illusionism** — *Translation.* The family of free-will views that hold the experience of free will is systematically illusory, often paired with claims about the unreliability of introspective reports and the post-hoc nature of decision-recognition. The architecture preserves the illusionist insight regarding source-opacity and confabulation risk but distinguishes confabulation from post-hoc recognition (which can accurately track source-pathway given source-opacity). The architecture does not adopt the illusionist conclusion that agency is unreal; it treats agency as structural configuration whose lived character is preserved. (Section 5.4)

**Indexed modeling I** — *Foundation.* The primitive anchor of the inside-perspective architecture: the I-as-modeling-itself, structurally available to itself through the cogito, and indexed to its own modeling-pathway. The agency paper applies the indexed modeling I to the agency domain without redefining or developing it; the I is the perspective from which agency is structurally analyzed. The architecture is perspectivally realist about other indexed modeling I's (other agents) but does *not* posit a group-mind. (Foundation v5.3.3; Section 2.2)

**Intrinsic layer** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the layer of the indexed modeling I constituted by intrinsic structural relations. The agency paper inherits the intrinsic layer as background; for full development, see Foundation v5.3.3.

**Lawful-frame grounding** — *Open problem.* The metaphysical question of why the substrate exhibits the lawful regularity the lawful-frame qualifier requires. Lawful-frame grounding is a foundation-level metaphysical residue (13.2) the architecture does not address; the architecture takes lawful-frame regularity as a precondition for its coordinates and persistence relations to be definable, and treats the question of why such regularity holds as outside its scope. See also: lawful-frame qualifier; foundation-level metaphysical residue. (Section 6.2)

**Lawful-frame qualifier** — *Foundation / Agency.* The minimum structural regularity the architecture requires of the substrate for its coordinates and persistence relations to be definable. The lawful-frame qualifier is *not* a deterministic, stochastic, or indeterministic substrate-metaphysical commitment; it specifies only that there is sufficient structural regularity for the architecture's relations to hold. The architecture is implementation-neutral within the lawful-frame qualifier. (Section 3.7)

**Libertarianism** — *Translation.* In this paper, the family of free-will views that require metaphysical openness, alternative possibilities, or agent-sourcehood stronger than the architecture's structural live-option account. The architecture translates some libertarian concerns (the architecture preserves contrastive live-option space and structural sourcehood-sensitivity) but does *not* adopt substrate-indeterminism as a condition of free choice. A reader committed to libertarian metaphysical openness as a necessary condition may legitimately decline the architecture's lawful-frame qualifier; the architecture operates at a level where its commitments hold under both libertarian and non-libertarian substrate-metaphysical assumptions. (Section 5.4)

**Libet-class paradigm** — *Empirical.* The family of empirical paradigms (originating with Libet's 1983 readiness-potential studies) measuring neural preparation signals prior to subjective awareness of decision. In the architecture, Libet-class paradigms probe specific architectural objects (preparation events, selection events, ownership-recognition events) through empirical proxies; they do *not* directly measure free will, agency-as-such, or the decision-event itself. The timing-marker non-identity chain (10.6) distinguishes the empirical signals from the architectural objects. See also: $EIP_p$; readiness potential; timing-marker non-identity. (Section 10.7)

**Live option** — *Agency.* A specific option that is genuinely available within the contrastive live-option space at the moment of choice, given the agent's current toolkit, configuration, and substrate state. A merely nominal alternative (one the agent could not actually select given current constraints) is not a live option. (Section 3.2)

**Live-option space** — *Agency.* The structured set of multiple distinguishable live options available at a moment of choice. The contrastive live-option space is what Condition 1 of free choice requires; the verification-toolkit constructs the live-option space through order-indexed live-option construction. See also: contrastive live-option space; verification-toolkit. (Sections 3.2, 5.4)

**Lived-I** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the indexed modeling I as it is lived from the inside, with its full set of identity-correlations, preservation-rankings, and phenomenal availabilities. The agency paper inherits the lived-I as the perspective from which agency is structurally analyzed.

**Manipulation** — *Agency / Attribution.* A variation-family in which an agent's option-space, evaluative pathway, or substrate state is upstream-shaped by another agent in a way that compromises sourcehood. Manipulation is *not* ownership-failure by default: a manipulated action can be ownership-integrated (the agent recognizes it as theirs and acts wholeheartedly within it) while sourcehood remains compromised. The manipulation-not-ownership-failure-by-default discipline (8.15, 9.5) preserves the ownership / sourcehood dissociation that is central to the architecture's attribution apparatus. See also: ownership; sourcehood; source-status; ownership / sourcehood dissociation (P5). (Sections 5.9, 8.15, 9.5)

**Mastery** — *Agency.* The structural condition of high toolkit-development in a target-domain, characterized by deep substrate-toolkit coupling, low effort relative to action complexity, and ability to satisfy the six conditions of free choice with minimal explicit deliberation. Mastery is *not* loss of agency or absence of choice; it is high-quality agency through developed toolkit-coupling. The flow / mastery vs capture distinction is critical: mastery preserves ownership-integration and source-status; capture does not. See also: flow; toolkit-development. (Section 5.8)

**Maximally-self-defined I** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the asymptotic limit toward which the indexed modeling I develops through identity-correlation operations. The agency paper inherits the maximally-self-defined I as the asymptotic horizon of agency-development; for full derivation, see Foundation v5.3.3.

**Mitigation** — *Attribution.* A repair-mode of accountability: structural addressability for action that reduces, prevents, or compensates the cascade-effects of an action. Mitigation may obtain even in cases where original-act responsibility is reduced or unclear; mitigation-availability is a profile-component in $AP_{m,t;a}$. See also: repair; repair-accountability. (Section 9.6)

**Mode A** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The pre-reflective mode of I-development. Inherited from Foundation v5.3.3.

**Mode B** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The reflective mode of I-development; supplies reflective object-access. *Not* the same as exercised reflection, verbal report, introspective certainty, or source-transparency. The Mode B availability vs exercise vs report distinction is critical and is preserved across Sections 7, 10, and 11. See also: Mode B reflective availability; Mode B exercise; Mode B object-domain availability. (Foundation v5.3.3; Section 7.6)

**Mode B object-domain availability** — *Agency.* The order-asymmetric availability of Mode B reflective object-access across different agency-domains. Mode B is not uniformly available across all order-engagements: an agent may have Mode B availability for the action itself while lacking it for the source-shaping pathway, or vice versa. The order-asymmetry is preserved throughout the architecture's analysis of source-compromised agency. (Section 7.6)

**Mode B reflective availability** — *Agency.* Condition 5 of free choice: the structural availability of Mode B reflective object-access for the agency-domain relevant to the action (focusing, thinking, acting, source-shaping, or ownership-integration). Mode B reflective availability must be non-confabulatory: it must accurately track the source-pathway given source-opacity, rather than constructing post-hoc reports that do not track. *Not* the same as exercised reflection, verbal report, introspective certainty, or source-transparency. Source-opacity may coexist with Mode B availability where the agent can take the relevant agency-structure as object without having full $\phi$-access to the source-pathway. See also: Mode B; Mode B exercise; post-hoc recognition; confabulation. (Sections 3.2, 7.6)

**Mode C** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The integrative mode of I-development. Inherited from Foundation v5.3.3.

**N-order will** — *Agency.* The general form of order-indexed willing extended through toolkit-development to constructed orders beyond the three foundational orders. N-order will preserves the structural form of willing across extended target-domains (asking, directing, team coordination, institutional engagement, and beyond). See also: order-indexed willing; constructed order. (Section 5.7)

**No-agent translation** — *Translation.* The family of contemplative and wisdom-tradition views that translate agency-talk into no-agent, dependent-origination, or non-self frameworks. The architecture translates no-agent insights at the experiential and structural-availability levels (the indexed modeling I can be experienced as transparent or absent under contemplative conditions) but does *not* erase the indexed modeling I as architectural primitive: the I remains the perspective from which no-agent translation itself is structurally available. The architecture is open to no-agent translation without erasing the I. (Section 5.4)

**Normative sufficiency** — *Open problem.* The metaphysical question of whether the architecture's structural attribution-apparatus is sufficient to ground normative claims (moral desert, justified blame, justified punishment, etc.). The architecture supplies structural inputs for normative attribution but does not claim normative-sufficiency closure; normative sufficiency is treated as a foundation-level metaphysical residue (13.2) requiring engagement with normative-ethical and meta-ethical apparatus the architecture does not develop. See also: foundation-level metaphysical residue. (Section 6.2)

**Not-I** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, contents that fall outside identity-correlation with the indexed modeling I. The agency paper inherits the not-I distinction; for full development, see Foundation v5.3.3.

**Open problem** — *Open problem.* A structural question the architecture identifies as relevant to the agency domain but not resolved within the present paper. Open problems are catalogued across the two companion papers as the seventeen-entry open-problem catalogue: foundation-level metaphysical residue (this paper §6.2), theological resonance (this paper §6.3), and fifteen research-program problems ([Agency §12.1–§12.12] plus this paper §6.4–§6.6). Open problems are *not* failures of the architecture; they are principled limits or research-program directions the architecture identifies and structures for future engagement. See also: principled limit; seventeen-entry open-problem catalogue. (this paper §6 / [Agency §12])
**Operator-bundle selection** — *Agency.* Condition 3 of free choice: the structural selection of an action-direction by the operator-bundle (the configuration of will-operators, evaluative-operators, and execution-operators) at the moment of action. Operator-bundle selection is *not* the same as deliberation (which precedes selection) or action onset (which follows selection); the deliberation / decision / trying / action / outcome separation (7.10) is preserved. (Sections 3.2, 7.10)

**Order-indexed willing** — *Agency.* The structural commitment that willing operates at distinct orders, beginning with three foundational orders (focus, thought, substrate-mediated action) and extending to constructed orders (asking, directing, team coordination, institutional engagement, and beyond) through toolkit-development and mastery. The order-indexed willing pathway generalizes by N-order will (5.7); the architecture treats agency configurations as order-engagement profiles, not as undifferentiated will. See also: first-order willing; second-order willing; third-order willing; N-order will; constructed order. (Sections 4.6, 5.7)

**Osmotic imprinting** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the gradual integration of contents into the indexed modeling I through repeated low-level contact rather than explicit endorsement. The agency paper inherits osmotic imprinting as a source-status modifier (osmotically-shaped) within the broader source-status spectrum. See also: source-status; output-imprinting cascade. (Sections 5.6, 5.9)

**Output-imprinting cascade** — *Agency.* The structural pathway by which an agent's actions imprint on the substrate, environment, or other agents in ways that become source-shaping inputs for subsequent agency events. The output-imprinting cascade is one of the architecture's central mechanisms for understanding longitudinal agency-formation, source-compromise dynamics, and cascade-attribution. See also: source-status; cascade-attribution. (Section 5.6)

**Ownership** — *Attribution.* The structural integration of an action, decision, or output as the agent's own under Condition 6 of free choice (ownership-integration). Ownership is *not* the same as responsibility, accountability, or sourcehood. Ownership concerns the agent's structural integration of the action with their own evaluative profile and modeling I; sourcehood concerns the source-status of the option-space, evaluative pathway, or shaping pathway from which the action emerges. The ownership / sourcehood dissociation (P5) is one of the architecture's backbone predictions: a manipulated action can be ownership-integrated while sourcehood is compromised. See also: ownership-integration; sourcehood; responsibility; accountability; manipulation; ownership / sourcehood dissociation (P5). (Section 9.4)

**Ownership / sourcehood dissociation (P5)** — *Empirical / Attribution.* Backbone prediction (P5 of the prediction profile $PP_i$) stating that under well-designed manipulation paradigms, ownership-reports and source-access reports can dissociate: an agent may own an act (Condition 6 intact) while source-status is externally compromised. This prediction discriminates the architecture from frameworks that collapse ownership and sourcehood into a single dimension. The dissociation is one of the architecture's central architectural commitments and is preserved throughout the attribution apparatus and the framework-translation engagement. See also: ownership; sourcehood; manipulation; backbone predictions; $AP_{m,t;a}$. (Sections 9.5, 11.4)

**Ownership report** — *Empirical.* The verbal or behavioral report by which an agent expresses ownership of an action. Ownership reports are empirical proxies for ownership-recognition; they are *not* identical to ownership-integration itself, and they can dissociate from it (manipulated action may produce accurate ownership reports despite source-compromise; alienated action may produce inaccurate non-ownership reports despite preserved ownership-integration). (Section 10.7)

**Ownership-integration** — *Agency.* Condition 6 of free choice: the structural integration of the action with the agent's own evaluative profile and indexed modeling I. Ownership-integration can succeed or fail independently of source-status; manipulation cases typically preserve ownership-integration while compromising sourcehood. The ownership / sourcehood dissociation runs through Sections 8 and 9 and is central to the architecture's attribution apparatus. See also: ownership; sourcehood; six conditions of free choice. (Section 3.2)

**PCE (prerogative of continued existence)** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The foundation-level structural commitment to continued existence. Inherited from Foundation v5.3.3.

**Phenomenal availability ($A_{m,t}(x)$)** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The foundation status predicate by which a content is marked as phenomenally available or unavailable to the modeling I at a time. $A_{m,t}(x)=1$ means content $x$ is present-to-the-I; $A_{m,t}(x)=0$ means it is not presently phenomenally available. $A$ is not a fourth coordinate parallel to $\kappa$, $\phi$, $\rho$, and $\Pi$. Inherited from Foundation v5.3.3.

**Phenomenology / Existentialism** — *Translation.* Families of frameworks engaged in Section 5 that analyse agency from the first-person perspective and preserve the structural reality of lived experience: phenomenology (intentionality, embodiment, temporality, first-person seriousness); existentialism (choice, self-making, anxiety, authenticity, situated existence). The architecture translates their insights into inside-perspective structural profiles, formal profile-objects, and falsifiable predictions while refusing reduction to descriptive-only phenomenology or unconstrained radical-freedom existentialism. Architectural overlap is most visible in the inside-perspective discipline, the indexed modeling I, and the developmental-window / profile-formation apparatus. See also: framework translation profile; no-agent translation; ultimate sourcehood. (Section 5)

**Post-hoc recognition** — *Agency.* The structural recognition by an agent of an action's source-pathway after the action has occurred, given source-opacity at the moment of action. Post-hoc recognition is *not* the same as confabulation (which constructs reports that do not track source-pathway) or source-transparency (which has access at the moment of action). The post-hoc recognition / source-opacity / confabulation triad (7.9) preserves these distinctions structurally. See also: confabulation; Mode B reflective availability. (Section 7.9)

**Prediction profile $PP_i$** — *Empirical.* The seven-component structural object that organizes a prediction $i$. Its seven components are $S$ structural source, $T$ target architectural object, $M$ marker set, $D$ domain, $C$ comparator, $F$ failure / pressure condition, and $E$ exclusions, written $PP_i = \langle S, T, M, D, C, F, E \rangle$. The eighteen predictions of Section 11 are organized through $PP_i$. The $PP_i$ does *not* claim empirical confirmation; predictions remain prediction-ready until tested. See also: backbone predictions; eighteen predictions; pressure condition. (Sections 11.2, 11.3)

**Predictive coding** — *Translation.* In this paper, the family of computational and neuroscientific frameworks (active inference, hierarchical predictive coding, generative modeling) that explain perception, action, and cognition through prediction-error minimization across hierarchical generative models. The architecture treats predictive coding as an open implementation candidate (substrate-level mechanism that may instantiate the architecture's coordinates and persistence relations) rather than a reduction; the architecture's commitments are implementation-neutral within the lawful-frame qualifier. (Section 5.4)

**Preservation-ranking ($\Pi$)** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The foundation coordinate by which a content is ranked for preservation across operations. *Not* the same as identity-correlation ($\rho$): preservation-ranking is the priority relation; identity-correlation is the integration relation. (Foundation v5.3.3)

**Pressure condition** — *Empirical.* The structural condition under which a prediction in the prediction profile $PP_i$ is most likely to be tested under conditions that discriminate the architecture's expected outcome from rival outcomes. Pressure conditions are not the same as ordinary experimental conditions; they specify the configurations under which the prediction has discriminator value. Pressure-condition language preserves epistemic humility: predictions remain prediction-ready, not confirmed. (Section 11.3)

**Primal not-I** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the foundational not-I from which the indexed modeling I emerges through identity-correlation operations. The agency paper does not develop the primal not-I; it appears in the agency paper only as a foundation-level metaphysical residue (13.2) and is *not* identified with God, the universe, the substrate, or any external referent. See also: foundation-level metaphysical residue.

**Primal not-I dependence** — *Open problem.* The metaphysical question of whether and how the indexed modeling I depends on the primal not-I. Primal not-I dependence is a foundation-level metaphysical residue (13.2) the architecture does not address; the architecture takes the indexed modeling I as primitive and treats the question of its dependence on the primal not-I as outside its scope. (Section 6.2)

**Principled limit** — *Open problem.* A structural commitment the architecture identifies as outside its scope on principled grounds rather than as a problem to be eventually solved. Principled limits include foundation-level metaphysical residue (13.2), theological resonance under translation-not-derivation discipline (13.3), normative sufficiency, and several research-program problems whose resolution would require apparatus the architecture does not develop. The principled-limit framing is *not* defeatism; it preserves epistemic humility against overclaim. (Sections 13.2, 13.20)

**Profile-indexed attribution** — *Attribution.* The architecture's stance on attribution: attribution is computed from a structural profile $AP_{m,t;a}$ rather than delivered as a binary verdict, and the profile surfaces what is true about a particular configuration rather than collapsing the configuration to free / unfree, responsible / not-responsible, blameworthy / not-blameworthy. Profile-indexed attribution rejects responsibility-as-conserved-quantity (responsibility is not transferred wholesale between agents) and zero-sum responsibility error. See also: $AP_{m,t;a}$; zero-sum responsibility error. (Sections 9.4, 9.12)

**PtCns (prerogative of consistency)** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The foundation-level structural commitment to internal consistency. Inherited from Foundation v5.3.3.

**Reasons-responsiveness** — *Translation.* The compatibilist family of free-will views that ground freedom in the agent's responsiveness to reasons. The architecture translates reasons-responsiveness as part of the broader compatibilist-coherent stance: the architecture's evaluative engagement (Condition 2) and Mode B reflective availability (Condition 5) overlap substantially with reasons-responsiveness conditions, but the architecture's six-condition account is broader and more structurally specified. (Section 5.4)

**Refused reduction** — *Translation.* The architecture's stance toward translation: an alternative framework can be translated by the architecture without being reduced to it. Refused reduction is the central discipline of the framework translation profile $FTP_f$: each framework is engaged at the level of its central insight without claim of refutation, absorption, or synthesis. The architecture preserves what it can translate from each framework; what it cannot translate is acknowledged as residual incommensurability. See also: translation; $FTP_f$. (Section 5.3)

**Repair** — *Attribution.* A repair-mode of accountability: structural addressability for action that restores or compensates for cascade-effects. Repair may obtain even where original-act responsibility is reduced or unclear; repair-availability is a profile-component in $AP_{m,t;a}$. See also: mitigation; repair-accountability. (Section 9.6)

**Repair-accountability** — *Attribution.* The form of accountability that addresses repair, mitigation, or restoration of cascade-effects rather than blame, punishment, or moral verdict for the original act. Repair-accountability can obtain even when ownership is intact but sourcehood is compromised, when responsibility is partial, or when the original-act attribution is unclear. The architecture treats repair-accountability as a distinct attribution-mode rather than collapsing accountability to blame. (Section 9.6)

**Resistance** — *Agency.* The structural difficulty an action poses given current toolkit, configuration, and substrate state. Resistance is what effort tracks; high resistance can be managed through high-effort direct willing, configurational preparation, or slingshot strategies, depending on the action and the agent's profile. Resistance is *not* a measure of agency-quality. See also: effort; effort-economy. (Section 6.8)

**Responsibility** — *Attribution.* The structural attribution of an action or cascade to an agent for evaluative purposes. Responsibility is *not* identical to ownership (which concerns structural integration), accountability (which concerns addressability for response), or blame (which adds a normative-evaluative judgment the architecture does not deliver). Responsibility is computed from the attribution profile $AP_{m,t;a}$ as profile-indexed rather than binary, and is *not* a conserved quantity transferred wholesale between agents. See also: ownership; accountability; profile-indexed attribution; zero-sum responsibility error. (Sections 9.4, 9.12)

**Role-indexed attribution-input** — *Attribution.* A profile-component in $AP_{m,t;a}$ that captures how an agent's role-position in a system structures the attribution-inputs for an action or cascade. Role-indexed attribution-inputs allow distributed and collective attribution without group-mind reification; they are how the architecture handles institutional, organizational, and collaborative attribution-cases. See also: group-agency without group-mind. (Sections 9.13, 9.14)

**Second-order willing** — *Agency.* See order-indexed willing; second-order willing operates over the foundational order of thought.

**Section-level structural objects** (introduced in this paper):

- $AP_{m,t;a}$ — *attribution profile* indexed to agent / modeling I $t$, moment $m$, and act / action or cascade $a$ (Section 9).
- $EIP_p$ — *empirical interpretation profile* for a paradigm $p$ (Section 10).
- $PP_i$ — *prediction profile* for a prediction $i$ (Section 11).
- $FTP_f$ — *framework translation profile* for a framework $f$ (Section 5).

The four section-level objects perform distinct analytical tasks and are not interchangeable; the architecture's analytical leverage depends on keeping them distinct.

## Alphabetical glossary

**Six conditions of free choice** — *Agency.* The six interdependent structural conditions that define free choice proper: (1) contrastive live-option construction by the verification-toolkit; (2) evaluative engagement; (3) operator-bundle selection; (4) non-preemptive substrate coupling; (5) non-confabulatory Mode B reflective availability; (6) ownership-integration. The conditions are interdependent: failure of any one constitutes a structural variation in the agency-configuration (see Section 8 for the seventeen variation-families). The conditions hold across order-indexed willing domains. (Section 3.2)

**Slingshot willing** — *Agency.* A willing-strategy in which the agent uses a sequence of intermediate actions to launch a target action, often by configuring substrate or toolkit through preparatory steps that release the target action with low marginal effort. Slingshot willing is one of three principal willing-strategies (alongside direct and configurational); it is appropriate for high-resistance actions where direct willing is effort-inefficient and configurational willing requires more time than slingshot allows. (Section 6.7)

**Source-pathway access ($\phi$)** — *Foundation.* See Notation and formal objects above. The foundation coordinate by which the I has access to the source-status and verification-pathway of a content. *Not* the same as Mode B reflective object-access: $\phi$ concerns source-pathway access; Mode B concerns reflective object-access. The two are dissociable. (Foundation v5.3.3)

**Source-status** — *Agency / Attribution.* The structural status of the option-space, evaluative pathway, or shaping pathway from which an agency event emerges. The architecture distinguishes source-statuses including source-clean (no upstream shaping by another agent), scaffolded (shaped within developmentally-appropriate guidance), osmotically shaped (gradually integrated through low-level contact), source-compromised (upstream-shaped in ways that compromise sourcehood), source-poisoned (upstream-shaped to defeat the agent's own evaluative profile), coerced (shaped by force or threat), and manipulated (shaped without the agent's awareness or consent). Source-status is a profile-component in $AP_{m,t;a}$ and is dissociable from ownership-integration. See also: sourcehood; manipulation; ownership / sourcehood dissociation. (Sections 5.9, 9.4)

**Sourcehood** — *Attribution.* The structural assessment of the source-status of the option-space, evaluative pathway, or shaping pathway from which an action emerges. Sourcehood is *not* the same as ownership: ownership concerns the agent's structural integration of the action with their own evaluative profile and indexed modeling I; sourcehood concerns where the option-space, evaluation, or shaping came from. The ownership / sourcehood dissociation runs through Sections 8 and 9 and is one of the architecture's backbone predictions (P5: ownership / sourcehood dissociation under manipulation conditions). See also: ownership; source-status; manipulation; ultimate sourcehood. (Sections 9.4, 9.5)

**Substrate** — *Foundation.* In Foundation v5.3.3, the physical, biological, or implementation-level system on which the indexed modeling I and its operations run. The agency paper inherits substrate as a background commitment and is implementation-neutral on substrate-metaphysics within the lawful-frame qualifier. See also: lawful-frame qualifier; substrate coupling.

**Substrate coupling** — *Agency.* Condition 4 of free choice: the structural coupling of the operator-bundle selection to substrate-level implementation, in a way that does not preempt the selection (substrate-level pre-determination of action without operator-bundle selection), bypass it (substrate-level action without operator-bundle engagement), or capture it (substrate-level domination of the operator-bundle by drive, urge, or compulsion). Substrate coupling can fail through preemption, bypass, or capture; the failure modes correspond to different variation-families (compulsion, automaticity, dissociation, etc.). (Sections 3.2, 6.4)

**Theological identification** — *Open problem.* The identification of architectural elements (the indexed modeling I, primal not-I, identity-correlation operations, etc.) with specific theological referents (God, soul, grace, providence, etc.). The architecture does *not* license theological identification: theological resonance permits translation into theological motifs, but identification is excluded under the translation-not-derivation discipline. See also: theological resonance; theological translation. (Section 6.3)

**Theological resonance** — *Open problem.* The structural availability of the architecture for translation into theological motifs (grace-like repair, repentance-like reconfiguration, providence-like lawfulness, sourcehood-residue and ultimate-sourcehood concerns, etc.) without the architecture deriving, proving, identifying, or grounding theological claims. Theological resonance preserves the translation-not-derivation discipline: the architecture permits translation but does not license theological identification, primal-not-I-as-God identification, grace-as-architectural-primitive claims, or theological proof. Theological resonance is one of the seventeen-entry open-problem catalogue entries (13.3) and is treated as a principled limit. See also: theological identification; theological translation; principled limit. (Section 6.3)

**Theological translation** — *Open problem.* The translation of theological motifs into the architecture's structural vocabulary, or of architectural elements into theological vocabulary, under the translation-not-derivation discipline. Theological translation is permitted where it preserves the architecture's commitments and does not require theological identification. Where translation requires identification or derivation, the architecture treats the case as a principled limit. (Section 6.3)

**Third-order willing** — *Agency.* See order-indexed willing; third-order willing operates over the foundational order of substrate-mediated action.

**Timing-marker non-identity** — *Empirical.* The structural distinction between empirical timing-markers (readiness-potential onset, motor-cortex activation, action onset, subjective awareness of decision, ownership report, etc.) and the architectural objects they probe (preparation events, selection events, decision-events, ownership-recognition events). Timing-marker non-identity is the architecture's central discipline against the empirical-proxy / architectural-object collapse: an empirical paradigm measuring readiness-potential is not measuring decision-event timing, free will, or agency-as-such. See also: empirical proxy; $EIP_p$. (Section 10.6)

**Toolkit-development** — *Agency.* The structural process by which the verification-toolkit develops new operations, new order-engagements, and new mastery in target-domains over time. Toolkit-development is the mechanism by which constructed orders become available beyond the three foundational orders, and is the source of the order-asymmetry in agency configurations. See also: verification-toolkit; mastery; constructed order. (Sections 5.5, 5.8)

**Trained expectation** — *Agency.* A condition in which the verification-toolkit's live-option construction is shaped by prior training such that certain options appear as default candidates and others appear as non-candidates, even when both are structurally live. Trained expectation can compromise Condition 1 (contrastive live-option space) when training has narrowed the option-space to artificial defaults, but trained expectation is also how mastery and skilled flow operate (high-quality narrowing). The mastery vs capture distinction applies. (Sections 5.4, 8.6)

**Translation** — *Translation.* The architecture's mode of engagement with alternative frameworks: structural mapping that preserves the framework's central insight where it can be translated into the architecture's vocabulary, identifies what the architecture can and cannot translate, and acknowledges residual incommensurabilities. Translation is *not* refutation, absorption, or synthesis. The framework translation profile $FTP_f$ organizes translation systematically. See also: refused reduction; $FTP_f$. (Section 5.3)

**Trying** — *Agency.* The structural attempt to execute an action through operator-bundle selection and substrate coupling. Trying is *not* identical to outcome-success: an agent can try and fail (substrate resistance dominates), try with low effort and succeed (mastery), or try with high effort and partially succeed (effortful action). The deliberation / decision / trying / action / outcome separation (7.10) preserves these distinctions. (Section 7.10)

**Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture** — *Foundation.* The book-projection foundational architecture established in Foundation v5.3.3, comprising the inside-perspective discipline anchored in the indexed modeling I, the identity-correlation $\rho$ as central relation, the verification-toolkit, the foundation coordinates ($\kappa$, $\phi$, $\rho$, $\Pi$), the phenomenal-availability status predicate $A_{m,t}(x)$, the prerogatives ($PtCns$, $PCE$), the modes of I-development (A, B, C), and the layer-structure (intrinsic, asymptotic). The agency paper is the third application paper (Layer 8) in the projected book series, building on Foundation v5.3.3, *Temporal Phenomenology*, and *Differentiated Affect* without new foundation-level primitives. (Section 2.2)

**Ultimate sourcehood** — *Open problem.* The metaphysical question of whether and how an agent can be the ultimate source of their own evaluative profile, free of dependence on prior causes the agent did not originate. Ultimate sourcehood is a foundation-level metaphysical residue (13.2) the architecture does not address; the architecture's compatibilist-coherent stance is consistent with ultimate sourcehood being either preserved or unattainable. A reader committed to ultimate-sourcehood demands as a precondition of free will may legitimately decline the architecture's free-choice-proper account; the architecture operates at a level where its commitments hold under both ultimate-sourcehood-preserving and ultimate-sourcehood-denying assumptions. See also: hard incompatibilism; foundation-level metaphysical residue. (Sections 13.2, 12.5)

**Verification-toolkit** — *Foundation / Agency.* The application-level coordinated set of $\phi$-bearing operations through which the indexed modeling I constructs, evaluates, and inhabits live options at each order of willing. The verification-toolkit is inherited from Foundation v5.3.3 and specialized for the agency domain in Section 5: it is the mechanism of contrastive live-option construction, evaluative engagement, and Mode B reflective availability. Toolkit-development extends the verification-toolkit through mastery and constructed orders. See also: live-option space; toolkit-development; six conditions of free choice. (Section 5.1)

**Wholehearted free choice** — *Agency.* The deepest tier of the three-tier configuration: an agency event satisfying all six conditions of free choice with deeper integration across the agent's evaluative profile, indexed modeling I, and order-engagements. Wholehearted free choice is *not* the same as free choice proper (which satisfies all six conditions but at less deep integration) or automatic responsibility-attribution; an agent acting wholeheartedly can still be source-compromised (the manipulation case in 2.1 demonstrates this), and ownership-integration in wholehearted free choice does not entail full responsibility-attribution. The three tiers (free selection / free choice proper / wholehearted free choice) are *not* normatively ranked; they are structural configurations that occur in different domains, conditions, and developmental windows. See also: free choice proper; free selection. (Section 3.4)

**Willing-strategies** — *Agency.* The umbrella term for the principal strategies through which agents implement actions: direct willing (will the action itself), configurational willing (configure substrate to support the action), and slingshot willing (use intermediate actions to launch the target action). Additional willing-strategies (receptive, distributed, counter-cyclical) operate in specific configurations. Willing-strategies are not normatively ranked: each strategy is appropriate for different effort-economies, resistance levels, and toolkit-configurations. See also: direct willing; configurational willing; slingshot willing; effort-economy. (Section 6.7)

**Zero-sum responsibility error** — *Attribution.* The structural error of treating responsibility as a conserved quantity such that responsibility attributed to one agent must be subtracted from another. The architecture explicitly rejects zero-sum responsibility error: profile-indexed attribution allows responsibility to be partial, distributed, or absent without redistribution constraint, and manipulation cases can leave both the manipulator and the manipulated agent with their own profile-indexed attributions without zero-sum trade-off. See also: profile-indexed attribution; responsibility. (§4.3)
