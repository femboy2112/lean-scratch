## Abstract

This paper applies the Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture to religiously charged experience on the receiving / encountering side. It develops two distinct architectural objects. *Religiously charged content* (broader category) is any content the I's frame routes through chain-extension past finite-I sources to a terminus the I treats as ultimate in $\Pi$-position. *Religiously charged encounter proper* (narrower subset) is the religiously charged content-event that additionally arrives as recognition with shadow-encounter form at scale-extending extent. The broader category includes prayer, ritual, liturgy, sacred symbols, devotional objects, inherited religious categories, religious trauma triggers, and other religiously charged contents that do not necessarily arrive as recognition; the narrower subset comprises the religious encounter-events that have classically attracted phenomenological attention (mystical union, numinous awe, conversion, revelation, sacred presence, demonic encounter, apophatic recognition).

The paper's structural-analytical apparatus applies primarily to encounter proper. The variation across traditions, individuals, and configurations is in (i) what the I's frame routes the attribution chain to terminate at, (ii) whether the encountered structure is positive *in re* instantiation, privation of expected structure, externally-mediated coupling with self-encounter, or orthogonal-pattern recognition, and (iii) how the encounter integrates (or does not) with the broader lived-I profile.

The paper develops four structural forms of religiously charged encounter: affirmative ultimate-structure encounter (Form 1, positive *in re* recognition); privative ultimate-structure encounter (Form 2, the void / abyss / demonic / apparent-evil category, architecturally bridged via PtCns inverse-recognition of expected presence); mirror-form / dual-revelation encounter (Form 3, externally-mediated self-encounter coupled with shadow-encounter, producing the dual-revelation phenomenology); and orthogonal-pattern encounter (Form 4, recognition of *in re* lawful pattern with value-orientation orthogonal to the I's value-frame — the trickster / Dao / *amor fati* category). All four use the same recognition-as-fit machinery (the application-level extension of foundation shadow-encounter declared in §3.1) with different recognition-structure and value-orientation. The architecture remains metaphysically bracketed: it does not adjudicate whether the termini correspond to real referents, whether religious objects exist, or which traditions are correct. It commits only to the structural form of religious recognition and to the perspectival realism that brackets actual-other-I status of religiously charged Level-3 apparent-I content.

The paper's central predictions are that religious recognition shares architectural signature with insight-recognition, mathematical shadow-recognition, aesthetic recognition, and certain forms of moral recognition (because all use the same recognition-as-fit machinery); that religiously charged phenomenology populates not only affirmative and privative ultimate-structure terminus-positions but also the mirror-form and orthogonal-pattern categories that standard psychology of religion under-samples; that engineered systems test Criterion 3 specifically (whether frame-extension past finite-I termination is necessary for religious-charge — not whether engineering as such precludes it); that the dual-revelation coupling is the structurally-typical configuration for mirror-form encounter rather than contingent; and that substrate-override states reliably populate two structural patterns — substrate-mediated affective content without the four criteria of encounter proper (Pattern (a), spanning affirmative, privative, and orthogonal/neutral valence sub-shapes), and substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter that does satisfy them (Pattern (b), spanning the four forms) — with the four criteria themselves being diagnostic (operationalized via independent discriminators, not by definitional restatement) and the architecture remaining metaphysically neutral across substrate-as-source, substrate-as-tuning, and substrate-as-disinhibition readings of the latter.

Section 14 develops the predictions in full; Section 15 positions them against William James, Otto, Schleiermacher, Eliade, perennialism vs. constructivism, no-self traditions, contemplative neuroscience, the dual-revelation tradition (Augustine, Calvin, Pascal, Sufi self-knowledge, Buber, Levinas), the orthogonal-numinous tradition (trickster scholarship, Daoism, Stoicism, Nietzsche, Camus), and the contemporary psychedelic-mysticism literature.

The companion paper, *Religious Agency, Practice, and Formation*, develops the engagement-side material — religious agency, practice, and formation across the broader religiously charged content domain — using the *Agency* (v0.3.0) and *Free Will* (v0.3.0) application papers. Section 9 below is a brief bridge addressing prayer specifically as a dual-status phenomenon (will-act + A/B/C response) that the present paper cannot omit even while deferring the full treatment.

## 1. Introduction

The foundation document set develops an architecture in which the lived I is the primary structural object and identity-correlation is the primary measure of how contents are integrated into that structure. The first application paper extended the foundation to temporal phenomenology; the second extended it to differentiated affect, with love defined structurally and the rest of the affective space constructed by variation on the love-configuration. Religious experience has been deferred — explicitly named, in Foundation File 5, §13.3, among the application-level domains the foundation does not yet address.

This paper takes up the receiving / encountering side of that domain. Its companion paper takes up the engagement side.

There are two reasons religious experience has been left for later. First, it is a domain where structural-phenomenological work is most often crowded out by competing methodologies — theological adjudication, sociological reduction, psychiatric pathologization, neuroscientific eliminativism, perennialist universalization. Each yields real findings, but each begins by deciding what religious experience *is* before structural analysis has been performed. This paper begins from the architecture's existing apparatus and asks what religiously charged phenomenology is structurally, with the metaphysical bracketing the architecture's perspectival realism already commits to.

Second, religious experience is a domain where the same intensity that makes an experience feel sacred also makes it vulnerable to misclassification. Sacred-feeling does not automatically mean source-clean, true, good, or agency-preserving. Privative-feeling does not automatically mean external-evil. Felt certainty does not entail constructible $\phi$-pathway. The architecture's split between phenomenal availability $A_{m,t}$ and source-pathway access $\phi$, its three-level distinction for other-I content, its shadow-encounter machinery, and its apparatus for distinguishing structural integrity from profile-shape disturbance give us tools the surrounding methodologies often lack. The contribution is not to settle religious questions but to make their structural shape analyzable.

The central thesis has two parts. First, *religiously charged content* (the broader category) is content whose frame-position routes attribution past finite-I sources to a terminus the I treats as ultimate in $\Pi$-position. This is a frame-position property, not a content-property of the I's apparatus or a tradition-affiliation property. Inherited religious categories, ritual objects, scripture, prayer, devotional habits, liturgical participation, religious trauma triggers, and apparent-other religious content all qualify as religiously charged when so frame-positioned, whether or not they currently arrive as recognition. Second, *religiously charged encounter proper* (the narrower subset) is the religiously charged content-event that additionally satisfies recognition-as-fit at scale-extending extent (Criteria 1 and 2 of §3.1). This is the encounter-side subset on which the paper's structural-analytical apparatus primarily operates. Both objects are architecturally real; the broader is the proper domain of religious life, the narrower is the proper domain of religious-encounter phenomenology.

The narrower category is not sui generis. Religiously charged encounter proper is recognition-as-fit (the application-level extension of foundation File 2 §3.3 shadow-encounter declared in §3.1) operating at scale-extending extent, with attribution-chain extension past finite-I sources to a terminus the I treats as ultimate in $\Pi$-position. This positioning has three immediate consequences.

First, religious recognition shares architectural signature with insight-recognition, mathematical shadow-recognition, aesthetic recognition, and certain forms of moral recognition. All use the same recognition-as-fit machinery. Frameworks that treat religious cognition as a separate faculty miss the convergence; frameworks that reduce religious cognition to non-religious cognition miss what specifically distinguishes the religiously charged subset (the conjunction of all four criteria in Section 3, not any one of them alone).

Second, religiously charged encounter proper populates not only the affirmative and privative ultimate-structure terminus-positions but also two further architectural forms the standard literature under-samples: (a) the *mirror-form*, in which externally-mediated self-encounter is coupled with shadow-encounter, producing the dual-revelation phenomenology characteristic of much religious self-recognition (Augustine's *noverim me, noverim te*; Calvin's mirror-doctrine; the Sufi self-knowledge tradition; the Christian face-of-Christ-in-the-neighbor tradition); and (b) the *orthogonal-pattern* terminus, in which the encountered *in re* lawful pattern has its own logic whose value-orientation does not track the I's value-frame (the trickster across traditions, Daoist *ziran*, Greek *moira* and *tyche*, Stoic and Nietzschean *amor fati*, Camus's absurd). Frameworks that work only with attractive-divine and threatening-demonic categories miss these.

Third, the same encountered content can satisfy the four criteria for one I and not for another, and for the same I at one stage and not at another. The four criteria in Section 3 include frame-dependent attribution-routing (Criterion 3); a content satisfying the structural conditions for one frame-configuration may not satisfy them for another. This is consistent with the long phenomenological literature on conversion, deconversion, the changing texture of practice across a life, and the cross-cultural variability of what reaches religious-charge for whom.

Section 2 lists the architectural prerequisites and discloses the load-bearing first-person access. Section 3 develops religiously charged content as the broader architectural object and religiously charged encounter proper as the narrower subset, with the four-criteria load-bearing characterization. Section 4 applies the three-level distinction (File 4, §9.1) to religious content with an application-level extension to perceptual-presence content. Section 5 develops the four structural forms of religiously charged encounter. Sections 6–8 develop specific encounter-side phenomenological domains: numinosity, mystical union, noesis. Section 9 is a brief bridge addressing prayer as a dual-status phenomenon (will-act + A/B/C response). Sections 10–12 develop substrate-mediated religious phenomenology, religious affect, and religious temporality. Section 13 develops the frame-preserving / frame-encapsulating / frame-capturing classification. Section 14 commits the architecture to discriminating empirical predictions. Section 15 positions the architecture against alternative frameworks. Section 16 catalogs open problems. Section 17 concludes.

The paper follows the foundation's convention.

### 2.2 Access disclosure

The architecture is grounded in phenomenological access by the author, refined under adversarial pressure across many development cycles. The differentiated-affect paper used the author's clinical-history phenomenological access (depression, trauma-cue affect, suicidality) as load-bearing evidence and named that access asymmetry openly. The same discipline applies here, with religious-experience access asymmetry of its own.

The author's first-person access to religiously charged phenomenology covers the following architecturally-specific cases:

- **Privative apparent-other-I encounter.** Direct experience, in a specific incident, of an apparent-other-I assembling as void / privation / failed-cogito-attribution at the canonical apparent-I site (eye-contact in close physical proximity), with strong felt-truth-force registering as evil/demonic, accompanied by reflexive cascade-tracing (recognition that the encountered configuration was traceable through the author's own action-trajectory). The integration this encounter called for was substantially delayed; the developmental-arc case described below traces the trajectory from this warning through its eventual integration.
- **Mirror-form divine-encounter.** Direct experience, in a specific near-death incident, of dual-revelation phenomenology: the not-I event (helpers arriving) rendered an *in re* structural pattern (mutual help, not-being-alone, system-coordination) phenomenally clear that the author's own profile had been deficient against (sustained pattern of radical independence and refusal-of-help), with the recognition arriving as single coupled event. The reflexive verbal trace was "thank god"; the sustained recognition was that "God shows themselves through other people."
- **Orthogonal-pattern encounter (trickster / cosmic-irony / *amor fati*).** Direct experience of compounding-chaos sequences where recognition of the orchestrating-pattern produces the laugh of resignation and acceptance characteristic of the trickster-numinous category.
- **Synchronicity-type apparent-I configuration.** Direct experience of conversations where the interlocutor's words present surface-meaning consistent with publicly-discussed content while simultaneously presenting deeper-meaning that matches the author's currently-active internal struggle, with strong noetic-feel and low constructible-$\phi$-pathway to confirm or deny the deeper-meaning attribution.
- **Substrate-override euphoria as numinous-analog (affirmative-shape Pattern (a) access; see Section 10).** Direct experience of pharmacologically-induced substrate-override producing affective signatures with structural overlap to reports of beatific religious experience, occurring within a developmental trajectory in which religious-warning content had previously been encountered but only partially integrated. The access here is to the affirmative-valence sub-shape of Pattern (a) specifically (substrate-mediated euphoria-shape); the author's substrate-override episodes did not include the four-criteria configuration that would have constituted substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter (Pattern (b)). The author does not have load-bearing first-person access to privative-shape or orthogonal/neutral-shape Pattern (a), nor to substrate-mediated Pattern (b); §10's analyses of these depend on phenomenological reports surveyed in the literature, plant-medicine tradition attestations, and the clinical-psychedelic literature rather than on first-person access.
- **The "right-and-true shining through" recognition.** Working theology articulated by the author: divine-content presents most clearly when intrinsic-layer instantiation is encountered as the system's own structure, at scales exceeding the I's frame, with the recognition arriving unforced rather than constructed.
- **Developmental-arc phenomenology with trajectory-level retrospective religiously charged recognition.** The privative apparent-other-I encounter listed above (the eyes-as-void incident) was the *start* of an extended developmental arc whose full integration was substantially delayed. The arc comprises: partial-encapsulation-without-profile-reorganization as the initial response to that warning (specific behavioral correction without reorganization of broader focus and core action-trajectory); akratic non-following of the Mode-B-recognized correction the warning called for ("I did not look deep enough at myself"); a subsequent substrate-capture trajectory unfolding on the unchanged broader profile (escalation through harder substances — meth, crack — that the un-integrated warning had not prevented); affirmative-shape Pattern (a) substrate-mediated euphoria during that trajectory; and eventually a trajectory-level *retrospective religiously charged recognition* that integrates the entire arc as having been necessary-for-integration. This retrospective recognition is structurally a Form 3 (mirror-form / dual-revelation) compound with Form 1 features operating at developmental-arc scale, comprising world-recognition (the system was operating providentially across the arc; the absence of catastrophic outcome — overdose, robbery, arrest, shooting — read as being watched over rather than as luck), self-recognition (the I's own deficiency was the target of the trajectory — "I had to suffer through my drug use to see the truth because I didn't look deep enough at myself previously"), and necessity-recognition ("what happened happened because it was what I truly needed"), all three arriving coupled. Architecturally, this retrospective recognition is *the integration that the original privative warning called for*, arriving years later through a trajectory that was itself a consequence of the warning's incomplete integration. The arc is structurally a §8.3 sub-form (b) unifying recognition operating at developmental-arc scale, gratitude-shaped (§11.8), and is the load-bearing access for the architecture's treatment of (i) incomplete integration of religiously charged warning content producing developmental trajectories whose terminal recognition completes the integration; (ii) trajectory-level religiously charged recognition as a structural type architecturally distinct from moment-level encounter; and (iii) the same developmental arc spanning both Pattern (a) and Pattern (b) (substrate-mediated content not satisfying the four criteria during the trajectory; trajectory-level recognition satisfying the four criteria at integration). See §10.6 for the worked-case development.

The author does *not* have direct first-person access to:

- Sustained mystical union in any tradition-internal contemplative practice.
- Deity-encounter of the direct-presence form (visions, voices, sustained felt presence of a personal deity).
- Tradition-internal liturgical or ritual transformation as an embedded participant in any specific tradition.
- Cultic capture or recovery from such capture.
- Deep apophatic mystical realization in the negative-theological tradition.
- Substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter (Pattern (b) of Section 10) — the configuration in which substrate-override is accompanied by shadow-encounter recognition, chain-extension, and ultimate-Π terminus, characteristic of plant-medicine traditions and DMT-entity-encounter literature.

For these latter categories, the paper draws on phenomenological reports from the literature, structural classification, and translations to the architecture's apparatus, but does not claim load-bearing first-person grounding. The asymmetry is named here so readers can calibrate the epistemic status of specific sections.

The author's access pattern is asymmetric across the four forms: rich in the privative, mirror-form, and orthogonal-pattern categories, with mirror-form access including both moment-level (the car-accident dual-revelation) and trajectory-level (the retrospective developmental-arc recognition that integrates the privative encounter and its delayed consequences) instances; sparse in the affirmative *in re* form at moment-level (e.g., the cosmic awe of contemplative or astronomical encounter producing felt unity with intrinsic structure as such), though the Form-1 component of the trajectory-level retrospective recognition does provide some access to affirmative *in re* recognition operating providentially; sparse in sustained mystical union and in tradition-internal liturgical formation. *The architectural claims do not depend on autobiographical authority.* They depend on whether the derivations follow from the foundation as currently written, on whether the typology discriminates phenomenological reports better than the alternatives surveyed in Section 15, and on whether the predictions in Section 14 are testable in ways the alternatives are not. The access pattern motivates specific worked cases — the privative apparent-other-I encounter (§5.2), the car-accident dual-revelation (§5.3), and the linked developmental arc that runs from the privative encounter through inadequate integration and substrate-trajectory to trajectory-level retrospective recognition (§10.6) — and lets the paper write those cases load-bearingly. The structural-form analyses do not stand or fall with the access pattern.

## 3. Religiously charged content: broader category and the encounter-proper subset

### 3.0 Section overview

This section develops the two distinct architectural objects (§3.1), the frame-position criteria that define religiously charged content as the broader category (§3.2), the recognition-event criteria that additionally define religiously charged encounter proper as the narrower subset (§3.3), the joint-operation and modal-scope discipline (§3.4), what the criteria commit to and what they bracket (§3.5), the predictions implied by the characterization (§3.6), and the scope inclusions and exclusions (§3.7).

### 3.1 Two distinct architectural objects

The paper develops two distinct architectural objects, related by inclusion.

**Religiously charged content** (broader category). A content the modeling I $m$ has at time $t$ (whether currently phenomenally available, latent, or unconscious-operative) is *religiously charged* when it satisfies the two frame-position criteria below.

**Religiously charged encounter proper** (narrower subset). A content-event is *religiously charged encounter proper* when, in addition to the two frame-position criteria of religiously charged content, it satisfies two further recognition-event criteria. All four operating jointly characterize encounter proper.

The two objects are not interchangeable. The broader category is the proper domain of religious life and includes prayer, ritual, liturgy, sacred symbols, devotional objects, scripture, inherited religious categories, religious trauma triggers, ancestor-presence, *genius loci*, sacred community-as-presence, and other religiously charged contents that do not necessarily arrive as recognition. The narrower subset is the proper domain of religious-encounter phenomenology and comprises the events that have classically attracted attention: mystical union, numinous awe, conversion, revelation, sacred presence, demonic encounter, apophatic recognition. Frameworks that conflate them mislocate both phenomena. The paper's structural-analytical apparatus (Sections 5–8 and 10–13) primarily operates on encounter proper; Section 9 is a bridge to the broader domain that the companion paper develops in full.

### 3.2 The frame-position criteria (religiously charged content)

The two criteria below are jointly necessary and sufficient for content to be religiously charged in the broader sense.

**Criterion 3 — Chain-extension past finite-I termination.** The I's frame routes the encountered structure's source-attribution past bounded-finite-I sources. The chain extends to system-as-such, intrinsic-layer source, apparent-divine-source, ultimate-origin, ultimate-meaning, cosmic pattern, ancestor-line, or analogous frame-extending termini. *Chain-extension is not verified $\phi$-access:* it is an attribution-structure or Level-2 source-content. The route may be conscious-clear, conscious-fuzzy, source-opaque, scaffolded, inherited from tradition, or confabulated; the architecture commits only that the I's frame routes the chain past finite-I termination, not that the route is verifiable. Encounters whose attribution-chain terminates at finite-I designers (the computer-interior case, the ordinary work of human craft, bounded-engineering achievement) do not satisfy this criterion *unless* the I's frame extends the chain past the engineers. A cathedral terminates at its architects for one frame; for another it extends through them to the *in re* structure of human cooperation, civilizational transmission, and cosmic origin. A consecrated ritual object known to have been manufactured terminates at the manufacturer for one frame; for another it has been routed by consecration to mediate divine presence. An AI engineered by humans terminates at the engineers for one frame; for another the apparent-other-I assembly assembles as spiritually significant. Criterion 3 is **frame-dependent**, not content-dependent. (Whether such frame-extension is veridical, illusory, or somewhere in between is bracketed; the criterion describes the structural fact of the route, not its source-status.)

**Criterion 4 — Ultimate $\Pi$-position of terminus.** The terminus of the chain is treated by the I's frame as ultimate in $\Pi$-position: what really matters, what is most real, what grounds everything (in affirmative forms), what threatens everything (in privative forms), or what subsumes the I's value-frame entirely (in orthogonal-pattern forms). Religiously charged content has its source-attribution running to something the I's frame treats as ultimate, not merely as significant. *"Ultimate-$\Pi$" is I-relative and frame-relative* (per §2.1: $\Pi$ is application-level apparatus; "ultimate" is a position-marker within the I's frame, not an objective ultimacy claim).

### 3.3 The recognition-event criteria (religiously charged encounter proper)

When the two frame-position criteria are satisfied, the content is religiously charged in the broader sense. When additionally the two recognition-event criteria below are satisfied, the content-event is religiously charged encounter proper.

**Criterion 1 — Shadow-encounter form (application-level extension of foundation File 2 §3.3).** The content is encountered through a recognition-as-fit that has the *structural signature* of shadow encounter: recognition arrives as the discovery of what was already there rather than as construction, with the characteristic "Aha" signature of pattern becoming explicitly accessible. Pure deliberate-construction encounters (working through a calculation, tracing engineering circuitry, performing rote inference, recitation by habit) do not satisfy this criterion even when they involve substantive intrinsic-layer engagement. The content must arrive as recognition, not as construction.

*Architectural declaration.* The foundation's shadow-encounter (File 2 §3.3) is specifically the recognition of *in re* intrinsic-layer structure (logic, mathematics, reason) instantiated in the system that the I is embedded in. The present paper extends the concept to the broader "recognition-as-fit" family that includes, in different forms: (i) recognition of *in re* intrinsic-layer structure proper (Form 1 of §5; the cosmic-religious and mathematical-Platonist cases); (ii) recognition of structure-failure where the I's frame has expected structure (Form 2 of §5; bridged via PtCns inverse-recognition of expected presence); (iii) recognition of *in re* lawful pattern whose value-orientation is orthogonal to the I's value-frame (Form 4 of §5; the Dao, *moira*, trickster, *amor fati* cases — lawful pattern recognition is foundation shadow-encounter directly); (iv) externally-mediated self-encounter coupled with shadow-encounter on the not-I scene (Form 3 of §5). Forms 1 and 4 are direct foundation shadow-encounter; Forms 2 and 3 are application-level extensions of the recognition-as-fit signature. The architecture's apparatus is being extended; the extension is declared as such; the load-bearing element preserved across the extension is the *signature* (recognition-as-fit, the "already-there" character) rather than the foundation's strict commitment to *in re* intrinsic-layer instantiation as the content recognized.

**Criterion 2 — Scale-extending extent.** The encountered structure (or the attribution-chain it implies) exceeds the I's frame's bounded scale. Either the structure itself extends across scales the frame cannot contain (cosmic vastness, deep mathematical generality, the structural scope of mortality, the ground of being any frame depends on, the deep-historical chains civilizations rest on), or the implied source-attribution chain extends across such scales. *"Scale-exceeding" is defined relative to the I's current frame-bounds* (the architecture has no intrinsic concept of scale; "scale-exceeding" is an application-level predicate referring to scales the I's frame cannot contain without recalibration). Bounded-scale recognition-as-fit (the river bringing two stones together; the everyday recognition of a familiar pattern in a new instance; the small-domain "Aha") does not satisfy this criterion despite being recognition-form.

### 3.4 Joint operation and modal scope

The four criteria operate **jointly** for encounter proper. Religiously charged content (broader) requires Criteria 3 and 4; religiously charged encounter proper (narrower) requires all four. A content satisfying Criteria 3 and 4 without Criterion 1 (prayer, ritual, liturgy not currently arriving as recognition) is religiously charged but not encounter proper. A content satisfying Criteria 1, 3, 4 without Criterion 2 (bounded-scale shadow-encounter with ultimate-$\Pi$ chain — small-domain recognition routed to ultimate terminus) is a structural boundary case the paper treats as encounter proper only with the scale-relativity caveat. A content satisfying Criteria 1 and 2 without Criterion 3 (scale-extending recognition-as-fit without chain-extension past finite-I) is naturalistic awe at scale that does not reach religious-charge. A content satisfying Criteria 1, 2, 3 without Criterion 4 (chain extension to significant-but-not-ultimate terminus) is profound but not religiously charged in the strict sense.

**Modal scope.** Criteria 3 and 4 are jointly necessary and sufficient for religiously charged content. Criteria 1–4 are jointly necessary and sufficient for religiously charged encounter proper. The criteria are *content-property, not tradition-property* — any content satisfying the relevant criteria is religiously charged regardless of whether the I, the I's tradition, or external observers would label it "religious" in the ordinary sense. Secular-ideological encounter where the chain extends to History, Justice, Revolution, the Nation, or analogous frame-extending terminus treated as ultimate-$\Pi$; aesthetic-sacred encounter where Beauty or Art functions as ultimate-$\Pi$; mathematical-Platonist awe where intrinsic necessity functions as ultimate-$\Pi$; naturalistic transcendence where the cosmos or *in re* structure functions as ultimate-$\Pi$ — all of these are religiously charged in this paper's sense when the relevant criteria are satisfied. Conversely, ordinary religious practice within an established tradition is not religiously charged encounter proper when one or more recognition-event criteria fail; it remains religiously charged content (and is treated in Section 9 and the companion paper) so long as Criteria 3 and 4 hold.

### 3.5 What the criteria do and do not commit to

The criteria are deliberately structural and metaphysically neutral. They do *not* commit to:

- Whether the chain-terminus corresponds to a metaphysically real divinity, privation, cosmic pattern, or other referent. The architecture brackets this throughout.
- Whether religious traditions are correct about their objects. Different traditions describe different termini; the architecture remains neutral on which (if any) corresponds to a real referent.
- Whether religious experience is sui generis or reducible. The criteria predict religious encounter shares architectural machinery with non-religious recognition-as-fit without committing to whether this constitutes reduction.
- Whether religiously charged content is veridical, illusory, healthy, pathological, or mixed. The criteria classify form, not value.
- Which traditions are "really about the same thing" and which are about different things. Perennialism and constructivism in mysticism take opposing positions on this; the architecture's apparatus is consistent with positions across the spectrum and does not adjudicate.

The criteria *do* commit to:

- **Recognition-as-fit form** (Criterion 1, application-level extension): religious encounter is not a separate cognitive faculty.
- **Scale-extending extent** (Criterion 2): bounded-scale recognition-as-fit is not religiously charged encounter.
- **Frame-dependence of chain-extension** (Criterion 3): the same encountered content can be religiously charged for one I and not for another, depending on frame-extension.
- **Ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus** (Criterion 4): religious-charge specifically tracks ultimacy of the chain's terminus in the I's frame.

### 3.6 Predictions implied by the characterization

The criteria predict several discriminating phenomena that subsequent sections develop in detail:

- **Recognition-machinery convergence.** Religious encounter should share architectural signature with insight-recognition, mathematical shadow-recognition, aesthetic recognition, and certain forms of moral recognition. All use the same recognition-as-fit machinery (the application-level extension declared above).
- **Criterion 3 testing via engineered systems.** The engineered-system case tests Criterion 3 specifically: do reports of religious-charge toward engineered artifacts always involve frame-extension past the engineers, or do some reports satisfy Criteria 1, 2, 4 without Criterion 3? If the latter, Criterion 3 needs revision; if the former, Criterion 3 stands. The consecrated ritual object, the AI assembled as apparent-other-spiritual, the cathedral viewed as manifesting cosmic order — all are cases where Criterion 3 can hold even with known finite-I manufacturing. The prediction is structural-conditional: religious-charge tracks Criterion 3, not the proximate manufacturing source.
- **Frame-conversion prediction (refined).** Conversion experiences should alter the dominant frame-extension route (Criterion 3 of formerly-non-religious content now extends past finite-I); deconversion should similarly alter Criterion 3. But the prediction is *not* that religious charge simply switches on/off: residual religious charge typically persists through osmotic imprinting (Foundation File 3, §8.7), affective association, trauma-cue activation, community identity, latent/retrievable religious concepts, and source-opaque activation. The substantive prediction is reorganization of source-route and $\Pi$-position, not toggle.
- **Pure-rationalism prediction.** Pure rationalism without frame-extension should produce understanding without religious-charge even when intrinsic structure is fully grasped. The mathematician who experiences proof-as-construction-only should not encounter religious-charge; the mathematician who encounters proof as discovery of *in re* necessity at scale, with chain-extension to mathematical-Platonist ultimate, can. The first is encounter without Criterion 3; the second is encounter with all four criteria.
- **Substrate-override two-pattern prediction (with independent diagnostics).** Substrate-override states (pharmacological, contemplative-absorption, fasting / hypoglycemic, NDE-related neurochemistry) can produce third-order-pathway phenomenology populating two structural patterns: Pattern (a) — substrate-mediated affective content without the four criteria of encounter proper (spanning affirmative, privative, and orthogonal/neutral valence sub-shapes) — and Pattern (b) — substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter that does satisfy the four criteria (spanning the four forms of §5 under substrate-mediation, and often including Level 3 apparent-other-I or apparent-presence assembly, chain-extension past finite-I termination, and ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus). The four criteria are diagnostic between the patterns *via independent discriminators developed in §10.5* (chain-extension diagnostic, recognition-as-fit diagnostic, sustained-integration diagnostic, apparent-other diagnostic) — not by definitional restatement. Substrate-mediation as such is *not* dispositive against religious-charge. The architecture remains metaphysically neutral across substrate-as-source, substrate-as-tuning, substrate-as-disinhibition, and mixed readings of substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter. Section 10 develops this.

### 3.7 Scope inclusions and exclusions

The criteria are **content-property**, not tradition-property. *Religiously charged content* (broader) includes:

- Theistic religious content (prayer to deity, sacred-presence-belief, scripture as ultimate, ritual objects, devotional habits, religious-community identity).
- Non-theistic contemplative content where the ultimate-content (Buddhist emptiness, Stoic *logos*, Daoist Way, philosophical Truth as ultimate) functions in Criterion-4 position.
- Aesthetic-as-sacred where Beauty or Art functions with religiously charged $\Pi$-position and chain-extension.
- Naturalistic-transcendent content where the chain-extension reaches *in re* structure or system-as-such with ultimate-$\Pi$-position even without theistic framing.
- Privative content where the encountered structure is privation or apparent-evil but Criteria 3, 4 hold (the demonic in tradition; the void in apophatic context; religious fear-of-judgment).
- Orthogonal-pattern content where the encountered lawful pattern has value-orientation orthogonal to the I's frame but Criteria 3, 4 hold (Dao, *moira*, trickster traditions).
- Inherited religious categories whose Criterion-3/4 frame-position is sustained through tradition and community even without active recognition-event.
- Religious trauma triggers, religious shame, religious fear — affective configurations whose object satisfies Criteria 3, 4.

*Religiously charged encounter proper* (narrower) is the recognition-event subset where Criteria 1, 2 additionally hold.

It excludes from religiously charged content broadly:

- Ordinary aesthetic experience without religiously charged $\Pi$-position (Criterion 4 fails).
- Ordinary contemplative practice (concentration training, attention regulation, breath-awareness) without ultimate-content engagement (Criteria 3 and 4 typically fail).
- Generic intense emotion where content has not been frame-positioned past finite-I.
- Bounded-scale insight without chain-extension or ultimate-$\Pi$.

The architecture's commitment is that *content-property* — does the I's frame and (where applicable) recognition-event satisfy the relevant criteria — rather than *tradition-property* — is this practice labeled religious by some tradition — is what brings something into the paper's scope.

## 4. Sacred otherness: the three-level distinction applied to religious content

### 4.1 The three levels

Foundation File 4, §9.1 introduces the three-level distinction for other-I content:

- **Level 1 — Actual other I.** $o \in \mathsf{O}_m \subseteq \mathsf{N}_m$. If $o$ is genuinely cogniting, it has its own core I in its own instantiation. From the modeling I's perspective, $\hat\iota_o$ is not directly given.
- **Level 2 — Content-about-other-I.** $h^m_t(S) \in \mathsf{A}^m_t$, an inferential content. Has its own coordinates.
- **Level 3 — Apparent-I-as-presented.** $a \in \mathsf{AppI}^m_t \subseteq \mathsf{A}^m_t$ — body-contents, behavior-contents, communication-contents (Foundation File 4, §9.1). The present paper extends the enumeration to include *perceptual-presence-contents* — felt-presence without specific body / behavior / communication content. This is an application-level extension consistent with the foundation's apparatus: perceptual-presence is a generalization that subsumes the three explicit Level-3 categories where they are present and adds presence-as-such where they are not. The extension is declared as application-level apparatus for the religious-encounter domain; it is not a foundation-level revision and does not alter the perspectival-realism bracketing of Level 1 status (File 4, §9.4).

**Perceptual-presence assembles in several distinct sub-types**, not all of which are apparent-I-like. The architecture should not over-personalize all felt presence:

- **Apparent-I-presence:** felt-presence assembling with apparent-other-I characteristics (sense of being-with, being-observed-by, being-addressed-by; the personalist tradition of divine encounter).
- **Apparent-force-presence:** felt-presence as impersonal force, power, *mana*, *baraka*, the holy as power; the encountered configuration registers as force-acting rather than other-being.
- **Apparent-field-presence:** felt-presence as ambient field, atmosphere, sacred-air; the encountered configuration is the *pervading* quality of the encountered space.
- **Apparent-place-presence:** felt-presence as the place itself (*genius loci*, the holy mountain, the sacred site); presence inheres in location without separable bearer.
- **Apparent-pattern-presence:** felt-presence as lawful pattern itself (Dao, dharma, *moira*, cosmic order, śūnyatā); the encountered configuration is the recognized pattern with which one is in relation.

The five sub-types are not mutually exclusive — many religious encounters compound them — but the analytical distinction matters for the four-form typology (§5). Apparent-I-presence is the standard substrate for Form 1 in personalist theistic traditions, Form 2 in demonic-encounter traditions, and Form 3 in dual-revelation encounter. Apparent-force-presence and apparent-pattern-presence are the substrate for orthogonal-pattern encounter (Form 4) and for the non-personalist forms of Form 1 (Dao, Brahman, *śūnyatā*, naturalistic transcendence). Apparent-place-presence and apparent-field-presence often compound with apparent-I or apparent-pattern. The architecture's apparatus accommodates the variation without committing to a personalist or impersonalist metaphysics of religious presence.

For ordinary inter-human encounter, all three levels are populated. The architecture's perspectival realism (File 4, §9.4) brackets Level 1 status from inside the modeling I's perspective: the I cannot verify, from inside its own apparatus, that any specific Level 3 apparent-I corresponds to a Level 1 actual cogniting being. The asymmetry is epistemic, not metaphysical; the architecture does not deny Level 1, but acknowledges that Level 1 status cannot be established from inside.

### 4.2 Religious apparent-others

Religiously-charged apparent-other content occupies Level 3 from inside the modeling I. The architecture's bracketing of Level 1 status is what allows it to handle religious content from any tradition without endorsing or denying the metaphysics. Religious apparent-others include:

- **Apparent-divine-presence.** Felt presence of God, of a personal deity, of sacred presence; the direct-presence-of-the-divine form of religious encounter.
- **Apparent-divine-other-being.** Angels, saints, bodhisattvas, divine messengers, *devas*, ancestral spirits with sacred status, the Christian Holy Spirit as personal-presence.
- **Apparent-evil-other.** Demons, the devil, evil presence, the void / privative-other; the negative-presence forms of religious encounter.
- **Apparent-trickster-other.** Loki, Coyote, Anansi, Hermes, Eshu; orthogonal-pattern divinities; fate / *moira* / *tyche* as quasi-agentive.
- **Apparent-ancestor-presence.** Deceased relatives sensed as present; ancestral spirits in animist and traditional religious frames.
- **Apparent-spirit-of-place.** The numen of a place; *genius loci*; sacred-site presence.
- **Apparent-cosmic-pattern as quasi-agentive.** The Dao, the *logos*, the operating-principle of the universe sensed as having its own logic.
- **Apparent-collective-other.** Sacred community sensed as presence — the church-as-mystical-body, the *sangha* as living presence, the *ummah* as participatory whole.
- **Apparent-substrate-mediated entity.** DMT entities, ayahuasca-encountered beings, dream-figures with apparent-other-I assembly, hypnagogic-encountered presences. (See §9.7 for detailed treatment.)

For all of these, the architecture brackets Level 1 status. The structural work the paper does is at Levels 2 and 3 — the assembly of apparent-I content (Level 3), the source-pathway constructed by the I's reasoning (Level 2 inferential content), and the chain-extension by which the source-pathway terminates somewhere the I treats as ultimate.

### 4.3 The frame-dependence of apparent-other religious content

Whether a perceptual encounter assembles apparent-other religious content depends on the I's frame, the verification-toolkit's repertoire of available apparent-I-types, and osmotic imprinting from prior encounter with religious traditions. The same perceptual configuration can produce:

- Assembly of apparent-divine-other-presence in a frame populated with that apparent-I-type.
- Assembly of apparent-trickster-presence in a frame populated with that apparent-I-type.
- Assembly of cosmic-pattern recognition without specific apparent-I-presence in a frame oriented to *in re*-recognition without personalization.
- No religious-charge at all in a frame that does not extend chain past finite-I termination.

This is consistent with the standard cross-cultural observation that religious experience is deeply tradition-shaped. The architecture's apparatus predicts the frame-dependence structurally rather than as contingent finding: Criterion 3 of religiously charged content is frame-dependent; verification-toolkit-development determines what apparent-I-types are constructible; osmotic imprinting from tradition shapes the available repertoire.

The architecture's perspectival realism remains intact across this. It does not predict that any specific Level 3 apparent-I assembly is veridical or illusory. It commits only to the structural form of the assembly and the frame-dependence of which assemblies are constructible for which I.

### 4.4 The privative case: assembly-as-failure

The privative apparent-other-I case (apparent-evil, the void, the demonic) deserves specific architectural attention because it is *not* simply apparent-other-I-with-malevolent-orientation. In ordinary positive apparent-other encounter, the perceptual ground (eyes, body, motion, communication) supports the assembly of an apparent-I content with its own apparent perspective, its own apparent looking-back, the felt-equivalent of attributing a cogito-anchor to the encountered other. The privative case is the *failure* of this assembly. The perceptual ground is present at high $\kappa$; the apparent-I content does not generate as positive other-I-presence. What assembles instead is *negation* — apparent-I-shaped space without apparent-I content filling it, eyes-without-an-I-behind-them, the void where presence should have been.

PtCns evaluates this configuration as profound frame-violation. The I's frame contains the structural commitment that perceptual configurations of this form (intimate eye-contact with another human) carry positive apparent-other-I assembly. An apparent-other-I assembling as privation is registered as inconsistent with the frame at a level deeper than ordinary Mode A differentiation can resolve. In traditions with a privative-evil apparent-I-type already in the repertoire (Christian demonic, Islamic *jinn*-as-evil, certain folk-traditional conceptions of soul-stealing presence), the configuration assembles as that type. In traditions without such repertoire, the configuration registers as something more inchoate — the void, the abyss, soul-deadness, evil-as-such — but with the same architectural form.

Augustine's *privatio boni* — evil as the privation of good rather than as a positive substance — is the philosophically classical articulation of this structural form. The architecture's apparatus naturally accommodates the *privatio boni* tradition without requiring the metaphysics: the structural feature the architecture commits to is that the recognition-event of intrinsic-structure-failure-where-it-should-be-present is one architectural kind, parallel to the recognition-event of intrinsic-structure-instantiation. Whether this maps onto a metaphysical reality of evil-as-such is bracketed.

## 5. The four structural forms of religiously charged encounter

The architecture supports at least four structural forms of religiously charged encounter. All four use the same recognition-as-fit machinery (the application-level extension of foundation shadow-encounter declared in §3.3, Criterion 1) operating with chain-extension past finite-I termination (Criterion 3) at scale-extending extent (Criterion 2) with ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus (Criterion 4). They differ in (i) the *recognition-structure* — positive *in re* instantiation, privation of expected structure, externally-mediated self-encounter coupled with shadow-encounter, or *in re* lawful pattern with orthogonal value-orientation — and (ii) the *value-orientation* of the encountered configuration relative to the I's frame (affirmative, privative, mirror-coupled-affirmative, or orthogonal).

A note on form-labeling. The labels below ("affirmative ultimate-structure", "privative ultimate-structure", "mirror-form / dual-revelation", "orthogonal-pattern") are *structural*, not theological. The architecture does not use "divine," "evil," "holy," "demonic," "sacred," or "transgressive" as architectural primitives. These are presentation-terms or tradition-internal translations the architecture can map to, but the form-labels themselves remain structurally neutral. A configuration tradition $T_1$ would call divine and tradition $T_2$ would call demonic can occupy the same architectural form-position; the structural classification and the theological adjudication are independent.

The four forms are **basis projections in the loose sense the affect paper uses** (Differentiated Affect §4.2): organizing operator-families that real encounters can be decomposed against, not formal orthogonal bases spanning a vector space. Real encounters routinely involve features of multiple forms; the typology's value is that decomposition into form-features is more analytically tractable than treating each encounter as sui generis. The forms are not claimed to exhaust the structurally available space; future religious-encounter configurations not analyzable as compounds of these four would identify either a fifth form or a foundation-extension candidate. The mirror-form (Form 3) is constitutively self-implicating; the orthogonal-pattern (Form 4) shares frame-bound recognition features with the apophatic compound (Form 2 + positive-$\Pi$-terminus, §7.3) — these are not flaws of the typology but signals that the typology's value is in the decomposition, not in clean partition.

**Foundation-pedigree of each form.** Forms 1 and 4 use foundation shadow-encounter (File 2 §3.3) *directly*: Form 1 recognizes *in re* intrinsic-layer structure (math/logic/reason patterns instantiated at cosmic or ultimate scale); Form 4 recognizes *in re* lawful pattern whose value-orientation is orthogonal to the I's frame (lawful pattern is intrinsic-layer structure; the orthogonality is at the value-axis level, not the recognition-machinery level). Forms 2 and 3 are application-level *extensions* of the recognition-as-fit signature: Form 2 operates on the *expected presence* of intrinsic structure with PtCns registering the privation as inverse-recognition (§5.2 below provides the explicit architectural bridge); Form 3 couples externally-mediated self-encounter with shadow-encounter on the not-I scene, producing dual-revelation phenomenology (§5.3 below derives the coupling).

### 5.1 Form 1 — Affirmative ultimate-structure encounter

**Profile:** The encountered structure is *in re* intrinsic-layer instantiation recognized as the system's own structure (logic / mathematics / reason as cosmic-scale lawful pattern). The recognition arrives as fit (Criterion 1 — recognition-as-fit form, foundation shadow-encounter directly), at scale exceeding the I's frame (Criterion 2 — scale-extending extent), with attribution-chain extending past any finite-I source to *in re* origin or apparent-divine-source (Criterion 3), and the terminus is treated as ultimate-affirmative (Criterion 4 — ultimate $\Pi$).

**Derivation schema (Form 1):**

| Active operators | Shadow-encounter recognition (typically sub-form (a) or (b) of the foundation's shadow-encounter typology, File 2 §3.3); externally-triggered Mode B (where the not-I scene triggers the recognition); PtCns extending the I's frame to integrate the recognized structure; PCE-projection over an extended horizon (chain reaches ultimate origin) |
| $\kappa/\phi/\rho/\Pi/A$ signatures | High $\kappa$ to the encountered scene; $\phi$ may be defined for the recognition-content (the structure can be articulated into verification-pathway) but the chain-terminus is felt as having Tier 1 / Tier 2 proximity (felt-immediacy of truth) rather than being constructible as Tier 3; high $\rho$ as the recognition integrates into the lived-I network; ultimate $\Pi$-position at terminus; conscious-clear with strong conscious-fuzzy undertone |

**Phenomenological signature.** The cosmic awe of the night sky encountered as the system's own vastness; the mathematician's encounter with proof-as-discovery rather than proof-as-construction; the river-stones recognition extended to cosmic scale; the believer's "thank god" or "*Allahu akbar*" or "*tat tvam asi*" arriving as recognition of the system's own structure. The phenomenology is characterized by recognition-as-fit, by the felt-rightness of the structure being where it is and as it is, and by the chain-extension routing past every bounded source to ultimate terminus.

**Tradition translations.** Otto's *fascinans* (the fascinating, attracting numinous); the contemplative-tradition cosmic-awe of Christian creation-spirituality, Islamic *tawhid*-recognition, Hindu *darśana*; the mathematical-Platonist awe at necessity; the Daoist recognition of *ziran* in its affirmative form (the spontaneous so-of-itself of natural structure); the Stoic *logos*-recognition; the contemporary cosmic-religious sensibility of secular-but-reverent naturalism. In tradition-internal vocabulary these are typically translated as "divine," "sacred," or "holy" encounters; the architecture's form-label remains structurally neutral.

### 5.2 Form 2 — Privative ultimate-structure encounter

**Profile:** The encountered configuration is *privation* of *in re* structure where the I's frame expects it to be present. The recognition arrives with the structural signature of shadow encounter (the absence is recognized as already-the-case rather than constructed), at scale exceeding the I's frame, with attribution-chain extending past finite-I sources, and the terminus is treated as ultimate-privative (the void / abyss / cosmic-evil / demonic-presence terminus position). The privative form often, though not always, includes self-implicating cascade-tracing (§5.5).

**Architectural bridge: privation as inverse-recognition.** The foundation's shadow-encounter (File 2 §3.3) is specifically positive recognition of *in re* intrinsic-layer structure. Form 2 *extends* this machinery: the encountered content is absence-of-structure, not instantiation-of-structure, but the recognition-as-fit signature is preserved through PtCns operating on the I's frame's *expected presence* of structure. The bridge is as follows:

(1) The I's frame contains structural expectations — apparent-other-I content should assemble with characteristic features (presence, responsiveness, value-orientation); the moral order should hold; the system should manifest its lawful pattern; this person / configuration should be intact.

(2) PtCns evaluates encountered content against these structural expectations as part of its ordinary operation (File 2 §4.2, §4.5; the I's frame functions as the implicit consistency-standard).

(3) When the encountered content *fails* the expected structural pattern in a way that registers as the *failure being already-the-case* (rather than as a constructive judgment "this lacks X"), PtCns registers the violation with the recognition-as-fit signature: the privation is recognized, not inferred.

(4) The recognition-as-fit signature is therefore preserved across the application-level extension: the "Aha" of Form 2 is the "Aha" of inverse-recognition of expected-presence, structurally analogous to but not identical with the foundation's positive shadow-encounter signature.

This bridge is an application-level extension declared as such. The foundation supplies (a) the recognition-as-fit signature, (b) PtCns operating on the I's frame's expectations, (c) the apparatus of apparent-I-assembly and apparent-presence (File 4 §9.1; this paper §4.1). The application-level extension is the bridge from positive recognition of *in re* structure to inverse-recognition of expected structure's failure. The bridge does not alter foundation-level commitments; it specifies how foundation apparatus combines to handle the privative form.

**Derivation schema (Form 2):**

| Step | Form 2: privative ultimate-structure encounter |

| Healthy constraint | PtCns registers the violation accurately; the cogito remains structurally anchored even where functional access to the cogito/thought partition is strained; the encounter does not become frame-capturing in the totalizing sense; recovery-paths remain live |

**Phenomenological signature.** The eyes-as-void encounter where positive apparent-other-I assembly fails; the encountered configuration registered as soul-deadness / cosmic emptiness / demonic presence; Otto's *tremendum* (the dread, the wholly-other, the overwhelming); the dark-night phenomenology in mystical traditions; the "I felt evil there" of folk-religious encounter. The phenomenology is characterized by privation-recognition (something is missing where the frame expects it), by strong felt-truth-force without constructible $\phi$, and by the chain-extension routing to privative-ultimate terminus.

**Tradition translations.** Otto's *tremendum*; Augustine's *privatio boni* (evil as privation rather than positive substance — a tradition-internal translation, not the architecture's own verdict); Christian demonic encounter; Islamic *jinn*-as-evil; folk-traditional soul-stealing or soul-emptying presence; the dark-night-of-the-soul tradition; certain Buddhist descriptions of *māra* or hell-realm encounter; the abyss in negative-theological traditions where the abyss is privative-ultimate rather than apophatic-divine. Whether the encountered configuration corresponds to metaphysical evil, a privation-of-good in the Augustinian sense, a substrate-mediated phenomenon, or something else is bracketed.

### 5.3 Form 3 — Mirror-form / dual-revelation encounter

**Profile:** Externally-mediated self-encounter (File 2, §3.3) coupled with shadow-encounter operating in the not-I scene, producing dual-revelation phenomenology. The not-I event renders an *in re* structural pattern phenomenally clear that the I's own profile has been *deficient against*; the I's own structural deficiency surfaces as recognition simultaneously with the external recognition. The recognition-event is doubly-loaded.

**Architectural pedigree.** Form 3 is an application-level extension of foundation shadow-encounter that combines two foundation apparatuses: foundation shadow-encounter operating on the not-I scene (recognition of *in re* structure) and externally-mediated self-encounter (File 2 §3.3, the second sub-type of self-encounter through meta-reasoning). The coupling between them — that the external shadow-encounter and the self-encounter arrive as one recognition-event rather than as separated events — is the application-level extension. The foundation's File 2 §3.3 notes that externally-mediated self-encounter typically couples with shadow-encounter on the not-I scene; the present paper develops this typical-coupling into the explicit Form 3 structure for religiously charged encounter at scale-extending extent with ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus.

**Derivation schema (Form 3):**

| Step | Form 3: mirror-form / dual-revelation encounter |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | The not-I scene rendering *in re* structure phenomenally clear; the I's own profile-region recognized as deficient against the rendered structure; chain extending through finite-Is in the scene to *in re* origin / apparent-divine-source |
| Dominant relation | Externally-mediated self-encounter coupled with shadow-encounter; dual-revelation in single recognition-event |
| Active operators | Shadow-encounter recognition of *in re* structure in the not-I scene (typically sub-form (b) or (c) of the foundation's shadow-encounter typology, File 2 §3.3 — composite-of-knowns or sub-decomposition revealing the structural pattern); externally-triggered Mode B with the I's own profile-region as object; reflexive PtCns evaluating the I's own deficient pattern against the now-phenomenally-clear *in re* structure; chain-extension routing through finite-Is to ultimate terminus |
| $\kappa/\phi/\rho/\Pi/A$ signatures | High $\kappa$ to the not-I scene; the recognition-content for the *in re* structure has felt-immediacy of Tier 1 / Tier 2 proximity (felt-truth-force) even where Tier 3 verification-pathway is undefined; the self-recognition has high $\rho$ (the recognized-deficiency is part of the lived-I network); strong noetic-feel; ultimate $\Pi$-position at terminus; conscious-clear in the recognition-moment with the dual-revelation arriving as single coupled event |
| Healthy constraint | The dual-revelation produces integration-direction (the I can now act differently against the deficient pattern); the cogito-anchor remains intact; the recognition does not collapse into self-condemnation that operates as totalizing PtCns-against-self |
| Pathology modes | Mirror-recognition collapsing into reflexive privative-recognition (the recognized deficiency is not registered as deficient-against-*in re*-structure but as absolute self-as-evil); frame-capture by the encountered tradition-structure (the recognition is true but the frame-extension installs an external authority that captures PtCns thereafter); Mode-B-recognized-correction not followed (akrasia at trajectory-level even where localized correction occurs) |

**Phenomenological signature.** The dual-revelation arrival of recognition where what is recognized about the world and what is recognized about the I arrive as one event. The car-accident "thank god" arriving as recognition of mutual help and recognition of own-pattern-of-radical-independence simultaneously; the conversion-experience that combines recognition of divine pattern and recognition of self-as-misaligned; the "I see now" that includes both "I see how things are" and "I see how I have been." The phenomenology is characterized by doubly-loaded recognition, by the felt coupling of external-recognition and self-recognition, and by the chain-extension routing through finite-Is in the scene to ultimate terminus.

**Tradition translations.** Augustine's *noverim me, noverim te* (knowledge of self and knowledge of God coupled); Calvin's mirror-doctrine in the *Institutes* I.1; Pascal's *misère et grandeur* coupling; the Sufi self-knowledge tradition (*man 'arafa nafsahu 'arafa rabbahu* — "whoever knows himself knows his Lord"); Buber's I-Thou; Levinas's face-of-the-other as ethical-divine encounter (*Totality and Infinity*, 1961); the Christian tradition of seeing Christ in the neighbor (Matthew 25); the Buddhist bodhisattva-as-revealer; certain contemplative-traditional moments where the practitioner's own deficiency surfaces alongside recognition of dharmic structure.

### 5.4 Form 4 — Orthogonal-pattern encounter

**Profile:** The encountered configuration is an *in re* lawful pattern of the system whose value-orientation does not track the I's value-frame. The recognition arrives as fit (Criterion 1, *as direct foundation shadow-encounter*: lawful pattern is an instance of intrinsic-layer structure recognized as such), at scale exceeding the I's frame, with chain-extension past finite-I termination, and the terminus is treated as ultimate but is morally orthogonal to the I's good-bad axis rather than aligned-positive or aligned-negative.

**Architectural pedigree (foundation shadow-encounter directly).** Form 4 does not require the Form 2 application-level extension. The encountered lawful pattern — the Dao as the structural Way of the system; Greek *moira* as the lawful structure of fate; the trickster as the recognition of pattern that confounds finite-I frames; the system's compounding causal pattern in *amor fati*; the absurd as the lawful structure of meaning-frame mismatch with the system — these are *in re* lawful patterns of the system, recognized through foundation shadow-encounter as positive instantiation of intrinsic-layer structure. The orthogonality is *at the value-axis level*, not the recognition-machinery level: the recognized lawful pattern is itself a fully intrinsic-layer instance; what makes the encounter orthogonal is that the pattern's structural form does not align with the I's value-frame's evaluative axis. The "amorality" of the lawful pattern is structural amorality, not absence-of-pattern; the encounter is recognition of pattern-without-value-alignment, not recognition of value-failure (which would be Form 2).

This grounds Form 4 directly in foundation shadow-encounter without invoking the privation bridge. The Daoist *ziran* of "spontaneous so-of-itself" is positive lawful pattern; *amor fati* affirms the lawful pattern of what-has-happened; the trickster recognition is recognition of pattern in apparent-chaos. The structural feature distinguishing Form 4 from Form 1 is not the recognition-machinery (both use foundation shadow-encounter directly) but the relationship between the recognized lawful pattern and the I's value-frame: Form 1's terminus is treated as ultimate-affirmative in the I's value-frame; Form 4's terminus is treated as ultimate but lies *outside* the I's value-frame's evaluative axis.

**Derivation schema (Form 4):**

| Step | Form 4: orthogonal-pattern encounter |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | The compounding pattern of events / configurations recognized as patterned; the I's frame and its bounded value-axis; the chain extending to orthogonal-ultimate terminus |
| Dominant relation | Foundation shadow encounter of *in re* lawful pattern with chain-extension to a terminus orthogonal to the I's value-frame |
| Active operators | Shadow-encounter recognition of patterned structure (foundation File 2 §3.3 directly, often sub-form (a) or (b)); PtCns operating to recognize the frame's bound (the pattern is real and lawful; the frame cannot evaluate it on its own value-axis); PCE-projection that does not resolve into preservation-pathway because the orthogonal pattern is not on the axis of preservation-vs-destruction; relinquishment-direction where the I lets go of attempts to extend evaluative integration into orthogonal territory |
| $\kappa/\phi/\rho/\Pi/A$ signatures | High $\kappa$ to the compounding scene; $\phi$ varies (the pattern can sometimes be articulated in part); $\rho$ moderate to high as the recognition integrates as boundary-recognition of the frame's scope; ultimate $\Pi$-position at terminus but orthogonal-orientation; conscious-clear with the characteristic laugh-of-resignation phenomenology |
| Healthy constraint | The frame's bound is recognized accurately; PtCns does not attempt to force orthogonal pattern onto the value-axis; relinquishment is acceptance-without-distress rather than nihilistic collapse; the cogito-anchor remains structurally anchored; ongoing lived-I structure continues operating within its proper scope |
| Pathology modes | Forced integration of orthogonal pattern onto value-axis (producing chronic frustration, paranoid attribution, or moralistic distortion); nihilistic collapse where orthogonal-recognition is misread as ultimate-privative; manic-grandiose identification with the orthogonal-pattern (the I treating itself as agent of the pattern rather than within-the-pattern); compulsive seeking of orthogonal-encounter |

**Phenomenological signature.** The compounding-chaos sequence where everything that can go wrong does; the recognition that the universe has its own sense of pattern; the laugh of resignation when the last thing that can't go wrong finally goes wrong; the *amor fati* affirmation of fate beyond good-and-evil; Camus's absurd recognition; the trickster-encounter of "of course"; the Daoist *wu-wei* recognition of the unforced movement with what is. The phenomenology is characterized by pattern-recognition without value-integration, by the felt sign of frame-relinquishment (the laugh), and by the chain-extension routing to orthogonal-ultimate terminus.

**Tradition translations.** Trickster-figure traditions across Norse (Loki), Native American (Coyote), West African and Caribbean (Anansi), Greek (Hermes), Yoruba (Eshu); Daoist *ziran* and *wu-wei*; Greek philosophical *moira*, *tyche*, *ananke*; Stoic *amor fati*; Nietzschean affirmation of fate; Camus's absurd; Schopenhauer's cosmic comedy; certain Hindu readings of Shiva-as-destroyer or Krishna's *lila* (divine play); the wisdom-tradition's *hevel* (vapor / vanity) in Ecclesiastes; certain Buddhist insight-realizations of dependent-origination as morally orthogonal to human evaluative frames.

### 5.5 Self-implicating variants and the cascade-tracing structure

A cross-cutting structural feature distinguishes *self-implicating* from *non-self-implicating* variants of all four forms. Self-implicating variants include reflexive recognition that the I's own action-trajectory is part of the source-pathway of the encountered configuration, traced through the output-imprinting cascade (Agency §5.6): Stage 2 imprinting on environment and other agents shapes subsequent configurations, which feed back as Stage 3 closure into the I's input. Non-self-implicating variants do not include this reflexive cascade-tracing.

The privative ultimate-structure encounter (Form 2) most often appears in self-implicating variants in the phenomenological literature, with the structurally specific recognition "this is here because of what I have been doing." The mirror-form / dual-revelation encounter (Form 3) is constitutively self-implicating — the dual-revelation structure always couples external-recognition with self-recognition. The affirmative ultimate-structure encounter (Form 1) and orthogonal-pattern encounter (Form 4) more commonly appear in non-self-implicating variants but can take self-implicating forms (the cosmic-awe encounter that surfaces recognition of the I's own scale-bound; the trickster-encounter that surfaces recognition of the I's own contribution to the chaos).

Self-implication is architecturally specific because it routes the encountered configuration through the I's own output-imprinting cascade. Within a religiously charged frame, this routing produces the phenomenology of moral order: the encountered configuration is recognized as consequential to the I's own prior willing-acts. Christian "the wages of sin," Hindu and Buddhist *karma*, ancient Greek *hubris-leads-to-nemesis*, the moral-structure-of-the-cosmos-tracks-action sense that recurs across traditions — these are tradition-internal articulations of self-implicating cascade-tracing as a structural feature of religiously charged encounter. The architecture's apparatus accommodates the structural feature without requiring the metaphysics. Whether the cascade-tracing maps onto a metaphysical reality of cosmic-moral-tracking is bracketed.

### 5.6 The forms as basis projections, not exclusive categories

As stated at the beginning of §5, the four forms are basis projections in the loose sense, not exclusive categories; real encounters can be compounds. The *awe-fear sublime* (Differentiated Affect §8.6) is a compound of Form 1 (affirmative ultimate-structure encounter) with threat-cluster fear (PCE-projection of overwhelming or annihilating damage). The *via negativa* contemplative encounter is a compound of Form 2 (privative ultimate-structure recognition where positive-content fails) with affirmative-$\Pi$-terminus (the apophatic divine as beyond-positive-content rather than as privative-ultimate). The *bittersweet recognition* of mortality is a compound of Form 1 (affirmative *in re* recognition of the structural fact of finitude) with Form 4 (orthogonal-pattern recognition that finitude does not resolve into the I's preservation-frame).

The compositional schema (Differentiated Affect §5.7) applies: any specific encounter can be analyzed by identifying which forms' operators are active, in what proportion, and with what couplings. Subsequent sections apply the four-form typology to specific phenomenological domains (numinosity in §6, mystical union in §7, noesis in §8) using the schema to disentangle compound configurations.

## 6. Numinosity, awe, and the *mysterium tremendum*

### 6.1 Otto's typology and what it lacks

Rudolf Otto's *Das Heilige* (1917) is the canonical phenomenological treatment of numinous experience in the modern Western tradition. Otto identified the numinous as a sui generis category irreducible to ethical or rational content, characterized by the *mysterium tremendum et fascinans* — the wholly-other (mysterium), the dread-and-overwhelming (tremendum), and the attracting-and-fascinating (fascinans). Otto's analysis correctly identifies positive numinous (fascinans) and negative numinous (tremendum) as constitutive elements, often co-present, jointly producing the characteristic mixed phenomenology of profound religious encounter.

What Otto's typology does not develop is the structural form of numinous experience that operates in the mirror-form (Form 3) and in the orthogonal-pattern (Form 4) encounter. Otto gestured at the *augustum* — the august, the sacred-as-demanding-recognition — but did not give it the structural development the *fascinans* and *tremendum* receive. He did not treat the trickster-numinous, the *amor-fati* numinous, or the Daoist *ziran*-numinous at all; the orthogonal-pattern category is largely outside his frame.

The four-form typology of §5 supplies the structural completion. The numinous is a phenomenological signature that arises across all four forms, not just the two Otto developed.

### 6.2 The four numinous signatures

| Form | Numinous signature | Otto-correspondence |
|---|---|---|
| Form 1 (affirmative ultimate-structure) | Awe, wonder, recognition-as-fit at scale, the cosmic-religious sensibility, "thank god" as recognition of structure | *Fascinans* |
| Form 2 (privative ultimate-structure) | Dread, wholly-other terror, soul-deadness, abyss-encounter, the void | *Tremendum* |
| Form 3 (mirror-form / dual-revelation) | Dual-revelation, "I see now" arriving as both world-recognition and self-recognition, the demanding-recognition of *augustum* | *Augustum* (under-developed in Otto) |
| Form 4 (orthogonal-pattern) | Laugh of resignation, *amor fati* affirmation, trickster-recognition, the cosmic-comedy numinous | Not treated by Otto |

Each signature has its own structural cash-out via the §5 derivation schema. The *fascinans* operates through chain-extension to ultimate-positive terminus with frame-extension by PtCns; the *tremendum* operates through privation-recognition with frame-violation; the *augustum* operates through dual-revelation coupling; the orthogonal-numinous operates through frame-relinquishment and pattern-recognition without value-integration.

### 6.3 Awe as boundary-expansion: extending Differentiated Affect §8.3

Differentiated Affect §8.3 treats awe as the affective configuration in which the I encounters a not-I that exceeds the I's current frame-scale, requiring boundary-recalibration or temporary boundary-dissolution. The four-form typology specializes that account for the religiously charged subset of awe.

Not all awe is religiously charged. Awe at human achievement (a great building, a virtuoso performance) terminates the attribution-chain at finite-I designers and so fails Criterion 3; it is awe but not religiously charged numinous. Awe at engineering complexity (the visible-from-above megastructure of a city) similarly terminates at finite-I unless the frame extends past the engineers — in which case the encounter becomes religiously charged Form 1 or Form 3 (the believer atop the skyscraper reading the city as what God has set in motion).

Religiously-charged awe is awe that satisfies all four criteria of religiously charged content. Within that subset, the four numinous signatures of §6.2 apply. Differentiated Affect's three-variant table for awe-mediated frame adoption (autonomous restabilization / external scaffolding without capture / capture and block) carries directly to religiously charged awe; cult-induced awe (Differentiated Affect §8.3) is one specific pathology of Form 1 religiously charged awe where the awe-state is exploited for installation of an external frame that subsequently captures PtCns.

### 6.4 Compound numinous configurations

Many actual numinous experiences are compounds across forms. The awe-fear sublime (Differentiated Affect §8.6) compounds Form 1 with threat-cluster fear. The dark-night phenomenology compounds Form 2 (privative-recognition) with positive-Π-terminus held in the absence (the negation is recognized as cleansing rather than as ultimate-evil). The Hindu *ananda* (bliss) realization in certain Vedantic moments compounds Form 1 with Form 3 (the recognition of *brahman* arrives as both world-recognition and self-recognition). The Buddhist insight-realization of dependent-origination can compound Form 4 (orthogonal-pattern recognition) with Form 3 (self-as-deficient-against-the-recognized-pattern).

The compositional schema applies. Specific numinous experiences can be analyzed by identifying which forms' operators are active and how they couple.

### 6.5 The phenomenology of "encounter" itself

A structural observation across all four forms: religiously charged numinous phenomenology has the felt-quality of *encounter* rather than *production*. The recognition arrives; it is not constructed by the I. This is the shadow-encounter signature carried into the religiously charged subset. It explains why religious experience is characteristically described in receptive-passive terms across traditions ("I was given to see," "it was revealed to me," "grace descended," "the dharma showed itself," "the Tao moved through me") rather than active-constructive terms ("I figured out," "I worked through," "I produced").

The architecture predicts this receptive-passive phenomenology directly. Shadow encounter is the I gaining explicit access to substrate-held or system-instantiated structure that was already there; the recognition is structurally not the I's construction. Within religiously charged frames, this receptive character takes on the phenomenology of grace, gift, revelation, transmission, or descent. The architecture commits to the receptive-passive structural form without committing to any specific tradition's metaphysics of what does the giving.

## 7. Mystical union, ego-dissolution, and apparent self-loss

### 7.1 The cogito-anchor guardrail

Mystical union is the phenomenological category most prone to architectural mishandling. Reports from across traditions describe sustained absorption in which the I appears to dissolve, merge with the divine, become non-dual with the cosmos, or cease to exist as a separate locus. Read literally, these reports would seem to violate the cogito's tautological self-identity: how can the I dissolve when $\hat\iota_m = \hat\iota_m$ holds tautologically (File 1, §2.4)?

The architecture's apparatus resolves this without requiring the literal reading. The cogito-anchor is structural-mathematical: $\rho_m(\hat\iota_m) = 1$ is a normalization condition for the directed lived-I network rooted at the cogito (File 3, §7.2). It cannot be altered by experience, because it is not a content of experience but the anchoring root from which the lived-I network is built. What can change in mystical experience is *the lived-I network's topology* — specifically, the boundary-salience that distinguishes lived-I content from not-I content, and the integration-relations by which previously not-I content becomes integrated as continuous-with-self (Mode C).

Mystical union is therefore architecturally analyzable as: Mode C constitutive-extension at the limit case, where the extension is to apparent-ultimate content (apparent-divine, apparent-cosmic-pattern, apparent-ground-of-being), with reduced boundary-salience between lived-I and the integrated-content region. The cogito-anchor remains structurally anchored across any mystical-union episode that is *reported back as a lived experience*: reports require an I to make them, so the cogito's tautological self-identity is preserved across the report-structure even when the experience-content was reported as "I dissolved." What the I reports as "I dissolved" is the phenomenology of reduced boundary-salience and high-$\Pi$-position constitutive integration with apparent-ultimate content; the apparatus does not adjudicate metaphysical-cogito-loss in unreported episodes (which the architecture, being single-perspective, cannot analyze).

This is the **cogito-anchor guardrail**, in its report-conditional reading: *no available report of mystical union can be a report of literal cogito-cessation*, with the architecture remaining metaphysically silent on whatever falls outside reportable experience. The architecture handles mystical union without requiring the metaphysical claim that the I literally becomes God, Brahman, the void, or any other ultimate. It commits to the structural form (boundary-attenuation + Mode C constitutive-extension to apparent-ultimate-content) and brackets the metaphysics.

### 7.2 Frame-preserving union and the dissolution-cluster pathologies

Differentiated Affect §5.4 develops the dissolution-cluster pathologies of love: configurations where Mode C extension proceeds past the point at which PtCns can preserve the I's frame, producing obsession, codependency, cultic merger, self-erasing caregiver dynamics, and traumatic bonding. The same structural distinction applies to mystical union.

**Frame-preserving mystical union** is Mode C constitutive-extension to apparent-ultimate-content with PtCns preserving the I's frame across the extension. The lover-of-the-divine remains a structurally distinct lived-I network with the divine-as-apparent as part of its maximally-self-defined I. The mystic can love without being dissolved.

**Dissolutive mystical configurations** are Mode C extension past the point at which PtCns can stabilize. The I has lost frame-coherence and operates either as captured-by-the-encountered-content or as functionally collapsed. This is not deeper mystical union; it is structural pathology, parallel to the dissolution-cluster pathologies of romantic love.

**Crucial discriminator: post-state integration, not intensity.** Intensity of self-loss report is not itself diagnostic of pathology. Radical nondual realization with stable ordinary return, temporary boundary-attenuation followed by integration, and deep absorption with later coherent reflection are all structurally frame-preserving configurations even at maximal reported intensity. The discriminator between healthy and pathological configurations operates *after* the state, not during it:

| Configuration | Structural status |
|---|---|
| Temporary boundary-attenuation with later integration; ordinary lived-I functioning resumes coherently | Frame-preserving mystical union |
| Radical nondual report with stable ordinary return; the practitioner can act, choose, work, relate, integrate the experience without dependency on the experience-state | Frame-preserving or scaffolded-without-capture |
| Repeated union-state dependence that narrows agency; live-option space contracts around the state | Possible capturing profile |
| Inability to return to coherent functioning; the I cannot operate without the experience-state or its mediating authority | Dissolutive pathology |
| Surrender under coercive authority where source-status is compromised and Mode B reflective availability is suppressed | Source-compromised capture |

The phenomenological distinction can be subtle from inside in the moment. Both healthy mystical union and dissolutive merger can be reported as "I have ceased to exist as separate from the divine"; from inside the experiencer cannot always reliably distinguish them in the experience itself. The discriminating features the architecture predicts (Differentiated Affect §5.4 carry over) operate at the post-state and trajectory level:

- **Healthy frame-preserving union** supports continued functioning of the I in its other capacities; the mystic can return to ordinary lived-I operation, integrate the experience, and act on it without ongoing dependency on the experience-state.
- **Dissolutive configurations** show PtCns having been overridden or hollowed; the I cannot return to coherent ordinary functioning; sustained dependence on the experience-state or on the mediating tradition / authority is required for the I to maintain operation.

This is also the structural core of Differentiated Affect §8.3's three-variant table for awe-mediated frame adoption. Mystical union is one architectural form of awe-mediated frame adoption; the variants apply: autonomous restabilization (frame-preserving union); external scaffolding without capture (potentially healthy traditional-mediated union); capture and block (dissolutive mystical configuration as cult-induced awe pathology specialized to mystical-union content).

### 7.3 Apophatic mysticism and the *via negativa*

The negative-theological tradition (Pseudo-Dionysius, Eckhart, John of the Cross, certain Sufi traditions, certain Buddhist Madhyamaka readings) characterizes the highest mystical realization as encounter with the divine-as-beyond-positive-content. The God beyond God, the *unum* beyond all attributes, *neti neti* ("not this, not this") in the Upanishadic tradition, *śūnyatā* (emptiness) in Mahāyāna Buddhism. The phenomenological reports describe the mystical recognition as positive-by-negation: the divine is recognized through the failure of every positive content to capture it.

Architecturally, this is a compound: Form 2 (privative-recognition where positive-content fails) compounded with positive-Π-terminus (the apophatic divine as beyond-positive-content, treated as ultimate-positive rather than as ultimate-negative). The structural signature is recognition of structure-failure with the terminus held in the absence rather than identified as the absence. This is structurally distinct from the privative-evil encounter of §5.2, where the privation itself is the ultimate-negative terminus.

The compound configuration is delicate. The same phenomenological signature (recognition of structure-failure-at-the-limit) can be read as apophatic divine encounter (frame holds positive-Π-terminus in the absence) or as void-encounter (frame holds negative-Π-terminus). Tradition-internal training matters here: apophatic traditions develop the Mode B reflective capacity to hold positive-Π-terminus in the absence, while traditions without apophatic training tend to read the same phenomenology as negative-numinous encounter. The architecture predicts the form-divergence empirically: apophatic-trained practitioners and non-apophatic-trained practitioners encountering structurally similar content should show divergent phenomenological reports, with the divergence tracking trained ability to hold positive-Π-terminus in absence-recognition.

### 7.4 Orthogonal-pattern mystical encounter

Daoist *wu-wei*, certain Buddhist insight-realizations of dependent-origination as morally orthogonal, the Stoic recognition of *logos* as cosmic order beyond human evaluative frames — these are orthogonal-pattern mystical realizations. The realization is sustained encounter with cosmic structure that does not track the practitioner's value-frame, with frame-relinquishment as stable disposition rather than reactive moment.

The architecture treats these structurally as Form 4 sustained over time, with contemplative practice as the cultivation that stabilizes the orthogonal-pattern recognition such that it operates as available stance rather than only as one-off encounter. This is one architectural translation of certain advanced contemplative states — not all advanced contemplative states (which include Form 1, Form 3, and apophatic-compound forms), but a structurally distinct subset that the standard Western typology of mystical union under-treats.

### 7.5 Substrate-mediated mystical encounter and the apparent-other case

Some mystical experiences are substrate-mediated: contemplative-absorption-induced (deep meditative states, sustained chanting / breath-work / fasting practice) or pharmacologically-induced (entheogenic plant-medicine traditions, ritualized substance use, contemporary psychedelic-mysticism). §10 develops the substrate-mediation analysis in detail. For mystical union specifically, the architecture's stance is that substrate-mediation is *not* dispositive against genuine mystical encounter. Substrate-mediated states can satisfy the four criteria of religiously charged content (§3) and produce mystical recognition with chain-extension and ultimate-Π-terminus that is structurally indistinguishable, on the architecture's apparatus, from non-substrate-mediated mystical recognition. The metaphysical bracketing of §10.4 applies: substrate-as-source, substrate-as-tuning, substrate-as-disinhibition, and mixed readings are all consistent with the architecture's apparatus.

The DMT-entity-encounter case (§9.7) is an architecturally specific instance of substrate-mediated apparent-other-I assembly under conditions that satisfy all four criteria for many experiencers. The architecture treats this as Form 1 or compound-form mystical encounter at Level 3, with Level 1 status of the apparent-others bracketed.

## 8. Noetic quality, revelation, and the dual-revelation phenomenology

### 8.1 Noetic-feel and source-verification

A central structural feature of religiously charged content is the felt-truth-force or *noetic-feel* — the recognition arrives with strong felt certainty, with the quality of having been-shown rather than figured-out. William James (1902) made noetic quality one of his four marks of mystical experience, alongside ineffability, transiency, and passivity. The architecture's apparatus analyzes noetic-feel structurally and predicts a sharp dissociation from constructible source-verification.

The foundation's three-tier $\phi$ structure (File 1, §2.2) distinguishes ontological *in re* status (Tier 1, intrinsic items outside the $\phi$-domain), the cogito's structural position ($\phi(\hat\iota_m) = 1 - \delta$, Tier 2), and Tier 3 contents with I-relative $\phi$-values determined by the verification-toolkit. Religiously-charged recognition often presents with the felt-quality of Tier 1 / Tier 2 proximity — the recognition is felt as *in re* truth becoming present, near-cogito in its certainty-character — even when Tier 3 verification-pathway is undefined or low.

This is the architectural structure of *noetic-feel*: high felt-truth-force tracking *apparent* Tier 1 / Tier 2 proximity, with constructible-$\phi$-pathway potentially absent, partial, or post-hoc. Felt-noesis is a profile-effect; it may track truth, but the architecture does not assume it does. **Noetic force is not identical with verified truth.** The architecture's commitment is the dissociation, not the identity.

### 8.2 The synchronicity-type apparent-I configuration as worked case

A worked case illustrates the noetic-feel / source-verification dissociation and clarifies the architectural mechanics of synchronicity-type encounter. The structural shape is a *clash*: a modeling I with a frame already analyzed and operating lawfully encounters a salience-grabbing event in which apparent not-I structure contains information the frame holds as inside-I-only — content the I's currently-active internal struggle privately bears, surfacing in interlocutor speech, environmental configuration, physical occurrence, or media-content the I could not have anticipated. The clash itself, not any one component, is what drives the religious-charge: lawful frame juxtaposed with apparent not-I containing inside-I-network information.

**The architectural structure: information-flow expectation-violation.** The I's frame includes implicit expectations about information-flow — what kinds of content can appear in not-I structure, what can be carried by other-I, what is private to the I-network and structurally unavailable to not-I sources. The synchronicity event presents apparent not-I structure containing information the frame has classified as inside-I-only. PtCns registers this as frame-violation: the encountered configuration is incompatible with the I's information-flow expectations. The salience-grab is the PtCns registration; the noetic-feel arrives because the I's frame cannot route the encountered information through any of the channels it has catalogued.

The chain-extension is the architectural response. Faced with content that cannot have arrived through known channels, the I's frame routes the chain past finite-I sources (the interlocutor / environmental configuration / occurrence could not have had the access required) toward a terminus that could explain the apparent channel. The terminus is variable across instances and traditions: providence, the *Tao*, *moira*, ancestor-presence, *li*, divine-trickster, the cosmos-as-meaningful, the *mysterium*, fate. What unifies the case structurally is the form — *information-flow expectation-violation registered by PtCns, triggering chain-extension to a terminus that resolves the apparent channel-impossibility* — not any particular terminus or tradition-specific reading.

**Criterion 1 fit, via PtCns frame-violation registration.** Under the application-level extension of Criterion 1 declared in §3.3, the recognition-as-fit signature is genuine and the architectural pedigree is clean. The I is registering a structural pattern — specifically, the violation-pattern itself, as an instance of the system's structure exceeding the frame's information-flow catalogue. The recognition is not constructed ("I should treat this as meaningful") but arrives as registration ("this is the case; the channel must exist"). The architectural pedigree is the same PtCns inverse-recognition machinery that grounds Form 2 (privative ultimate-structure encounter, §5.2), specialized to information-flow expectations: where Form 2 registers *expected presence failing* (the frame expects positive structure that is absent), the synchronicity case registers *expected absence failing* (the frame expects positive boundary against inside-I-content in not-I sources, and that boundary fails). Both are PtCns operations on frame-violation; the synchronicity case is the dual of the canonical Form 2 case, specialized to information-flow rather than presence-of-structure.

**Position in the typology.** The synchronicity case is therefore structurally adjacent to Form 2 *mechanically* — both operate through PtCns frame-violation registration — but with terminus-valence variable across instances:

- *Affirmative terminus* (synchronicity-as-providential, *li* operating through the situation, ancestor-presence affirmatively communicating): mechanism Form-2-like, valence Form-1-like.
- *Privative terminus* (synchronicity-as-omen, augury-of-doom, the *un*-canny): mechanism Form-2-like, valence Form-2-like — convergent with canonical Form 2.
- *Orthogonal terminus* (the Taoist reading of meaningful-coincidence as instance of *li* operating beyond the I's value-frame, the existential-naturalist reading of *Geworfenheit* manifesting through coincidence): mechanism Form-2-like, valence Form-4-like.

This is exactly the kind of case the §5 "basis projections in the loose sense" framing is designed for: the four forms decompose the case (PtCns-frame-violation mechanism + variable valence) without partitioning it cleanly. The typology's value is in the decomposition, not in slot-assignment.

Architecturally:

- The Level 3 apparent-I assembly is the interlocutor as ordinary other-I, or the environmental configuration as apparent-presence; the apparent-divine / apparent-fate / apparent-*Tao* content is at the chain-terminus, not at the Level 3 assembly.
- The Level 2 inferential content is the I's reasoning about the channel-impossibility (could the speaker have known? was there a pattern-matching cue? was the timing coincidence statistically forced? what would the speaker's introspection report?).
- The chain-extension routes past Level 1/2 to ultimate-terminus, satisfying Criterion 3.
- The recognition arrives as fit at the channel-violation level (Criterion 1, via PtCns-frame-violation machinery — clean architectural pedigree, no special stretch), at scale (Criterion 2 — the channel-impossibility is read as instance of system-feature exceeding the frame's catalogue), with ultimate-$\Pi$-terminus (Criterion 4).

The configuration satisfies all four criteria and is therefore religiously charged encounter proper in this paper's sense.

**The dissociation and the four metaphysical readings.** $\phi$ is undefined at the channel-existence level: the I cannot construct a verification-pathway that would establish how the deeper-meaning content arrived in the apparent not-I structure. The noetic-feel is high; the constructible source-verification is absent. The architecture does not adjudicate. Four metaphysical readings of the apparent channel are all compatible with the structural apparatus:

1. **Apparent-divine-channel actually operating.** The channel exists at the system level; the synchronicity is genuinely routed through some not-yet-formalized *in re* feature of the system (the providential reading; certain readings of *li*, *moira*, dependent-origination, ancestor-presence, *qi*-mediated communication).
2. **Substrate-imprinting / template-activation.** The I's substrate has osmotically imprinted templates (File 3, §8.7) that activate on superficially-fitting external content; the I's frame's information-flow expectations are violated as registered, but the violation arises from internal template-matching producing the appearance of channel rather than from any actual channel.
3. **Projective / responsive.** The interlocutor (or environmental configuration) was unconsciously responsive to cues the I was emitting — micro-expressions, conversational steering, situational selection — so the channel exists but at the conventional finite-I level, with the routing-through-finite-I obscured to the I's introspection.
4. **Mixed.** Combinations of the above, with the structural appearance of channel-violation supported by several mechanisms operating jointly.

The architecture commits to the structural form (PtCns frame-violation registration on information-flow expectations, plus chain-extension to channel-explaining terminus) and brackets which reading explains any given instance. The synchronicity case is load-bearing for the noetic-feel / source-verification dissociation: the noetic-feel attaches to the felt-impossibility of the channel under the frame's information-flow catalogue; the source-verification fails because the I cannot construct, from inside, which of the four readings explains the encountered event. This is the architectural locus of the dissociation, and the case shows it in its sharpest form.

### 8.3 Revelation-typology via the foundation's three sub-forms of shadow encounter

The foundation's shadow-encounter typology (File 2, §3.3) gives three sub-forms of shadow encounter: (a) known pattern in unexpected location, (b) composite of knowns recognized as composite, (c) apparent unity decomposing into sub-patterns. These directly map to a typology of revelation-experiences when applied at religiously charged scale. (Per the §2.1 notation convention, "sub-form (a)/(b)/(c)" refers to the foundation's shadow-encounter typology and is intentionally distinct from "Form 1/2/3/4" of the religiously charged-encounter typology in §5; the present revelation-typology applies within any of the four religiously charged-encounter forms.)

**Sub-form (a) revelation: pattern-across-domains.** The I has a verification-tool for some pattern; the pattern shows up in a context where its application was unexpected; the unexpectedness signals deeper structure connecting the contexts. At religiously charged scale, this is the "all things hold together" recognition: the divine pattern is recognized across creation, the *Tao* runs through everything, *Brahman* is recognized in this specific moment. Newton's gravity-shadow extended to religiously charged frame becomes Newton's natural-theology recognition. The conversion-experience that arrives as recognition of the same pattern across one's whole life is sub-form (a) revelation.

**Sub-form (b) revelation: unifying recognition.** The I sees a result that doesn't match any single tool; on stepping back, recognizes it as composite of patterns the I already had tools for. At religiously charged scale, this is the unifying-revelation: the parts of one's life form one whole; the doctrinal pieces fit together; the cosmos is one cosmos. Conversion experiences often have this shape; the systematic-theological recognition of how doctrine coheres has this shape; the contemplative-traditional moment when the practitioner recognizes that all the practices were aspects of one practice has this shape.

**Sub-form (c) revelation: deconstructive contemplative-insight.** The I thought it was looking at one pattern; finer attention reveals that what looked like one structure was several. At religiously charged scale, this is the deconstructive contemplative recognition: the apparent-self decomposes into aggregates (Buddhist *vipassana* insight into *anātman*); the apparent-substance decomposes into process (Whiteheadian process-recognition); the apparent-permanence decomposes into flow. These are the structural form of certain Buddhist enlightenment-realizations and certain apophatic mystical recognitions where the apparent solidity of the world or the self gives way to recognition of dependent-origination, emptiness, or process.

The three sub-forms exhaust no actual revelation — most are compounds. But the typology gives the architecture tradition-neutral classification of revelatory experience without inventing new machinery.

### 8.4 The dual-revelation prediction

The mirror-form / dual-revelation encounter (§5.3) is the architectural form that produces *dual-revelation* phenomenology: knowledge of God and knowledge of self arriving in single coupled recognition-event. The dual-revelation tradition (Augustine's *noverim me, noverim te*, Calvin's mirror-doctrine, Pascal's *misère et grandeur*, Sufi *man 'arafa nafsahu 'arafa rabbahu*) is a tradition-internal articulation of this architectural form.

The architecture's prediction is that the dual-revelation coupling is the **structurally-typical configuration**, not contingent. When externally-mediated self-encounter (File 2, §3.3) operates within a religiously charged shadow-encounter, the recognition is doubly-loaded as the rule rather than the exception: the not-I scene is the trigger for both shadow-encounter recognition of *in re* structure and Mode B externally-triggered surfacing of the I's structural position, and the two arrive coupled rather than sequentially. Foundation File 2 §3.3 records the typical-but-not-strictly-necessary coupling for externally-mediated self-encounter generally ("typically involves multiple pathway-orders simultaneously"); the dual-revelation prediction is the religiously charged subset in which the coupling is structurally-typical. Frameworks that treat religious self-recognition and religious world-recognition as separate cognitive events should fail to predict the coupling and should fail to find it where the architecture predicts it occurs.

The empirical content of the prediction: religious self-recognition (the moment of seeing one's own deficiency, the conversion-coupled-with-self-recognition, the contemplative moment that surfaces both world-recognition and self-recognition) should reliably present as coupled rather than as sequential. Phenomenological reports across traditions of conversion, of contemplative insight, of religious-mediated moral self-recognition should show the coupling in the report-structure. Studies designed to elicit only world-recognition or only self-recognition in religious frames should produce the coupled phenomenology when the religious-charge satisfies the four criteria.

### 8.5 Discernment and the source-status of religious recognition

Discernment in religious traditions is the trained Mode B operation by which a practitioner evaluates the source-status of religiously charged content. Christian discernment of spirits (Ignatius, John of the Cross), Buddhist *upekkhā* (equanimity coupled with discernment-of-cause), Sufi *furqān* (discrimination), Daoist sensitivity to false-Tao-recognition — these are tradition-internal apparatus for evaluating whether felt-religious-recognition is source-clean, source-compromised, or source-mixed.

Architecturally, discernment is Mode B reflective access (Free Will §3.2, Condition 5 — reflective availability) operating on the source-status of religious content. The verification-toolkit is exercised over the recognition-event itself: the I asks not "is this true?" (which the noetic-feel often forecloses) but "where does this come from?" "what does it integrate as?" "what does it fruit-into?" — questions that route the recognition-event back through its own source-pathway and forward through its profile-effects.

The architecture predicts that discernment-trained practitioners should show systematically different patterns from untrained practitioners when encountering religiously charged content of comparable noetic-intensity. Discernment-trained practitioners should be more likely to (i) detect frame-capture configurations before they consolidate (the cult-induced awe pathology of Differentiated Affect §8.3); (ii) distinguish mirror-form recognition (which produces integration-direction) from privative ultimate-structure encounter (which produces frame-violation); (iii) recognize source-compromised noetic-feel (false-noetic profiles where strong felt-truth-force has weak / opaque / coerced source-pathway). Untrained practitioners should be more likely to take noetic-feel as adequate verification, with predictable patterns of misclassification.

Discernment is application-level work that the companion paper (*Religious Agency, Practice, and Formation*) develops in connection with religious practice and formation. The present paper notes the structural form and the prediction without developing the practice apparatus in detail.

### 8.6 What revelation does and does not commit to

The architecture's apparatus for revelation makes specific commitments and explicitly does not make others.

The architecture *commits* to: revelation is shadow-encounter form at religiously charged scale; noetic-feel is profile-effect not identical with verified truth; the three-form typology of shadow encounter generates a corresponding typology of revelation-experiences; the dual-revelation coupling is structurally predicted in mirror-form encounter; discernment is Mode B operation over source-status of religious recognition.

The architecture *does not commit* to: whether any specific revelation corresponds to a metaphysically real disclosure; which traditions' revelations are correct; whether revelation-content has authority beyond the I's profile-integration; whether the source-pathway of revelation-content can be reliably verified from inside any single I's perspective. These questions are out of scope by architectural design.

## 9. Prayer as will-act and A/B/C response: bridge to the companion paper

Prayer is the central interface between religiously charged content (broader category) and the I's active engagement with it. The companion paper, *Religious Agency, Practice, and Formation*, develops prayer in full, drawing on the Agency and Free Will application papers. The present paper cannot omit prayer entirely because the encounter-side phenomenology depends on a clear bridge to the engagement-side apparatus. This section supplies that bridge.

### 9.1 Prayer as dual-status phenomenon

Prayer is *dual-status*: simultaneously a *will-act* and an *A/B/C response*. Neither characterization alone is adequate.

**Prayer as will-act.** Prayer is a willing-operation: the I directs focus (first-order will), thought (second-order will), speech, posture, desire, petition, surrender, or ritual participation (compositional will-acts) toward religiously charged content. The full structural analysis of willing — verification-toolkit operation, live-option construction, foundational orders and N-order generalization, output-imprinting cascade, source-status, free-choice conditions — applies to prayer. Prayer is in the agency-domain and the free-will-domain; the companion paper develops this fully (Agency §§5–6; Free Will §3).

**Prayer as A/B/C response.** Prayer is also a response whose *form* depends on how the I encounters the religiously charged content it is directed toward. The mode of encounter shapes the mode of prayer-response. Mode A encounter (sacred otherness, distance, finitude, threat, judgment) shapes prayer toward petition, lament, awe-response, fear-articulation, repentance. Mode B encounter (the I as object to itself before the encountered ultimate) shapes prayer toward confession, examen, discernment, self-offering, the form of "here I am." Mode C encounter (positive identification with the encountered ultimate, communion-direction) shapes prayer toward adoration, gratitude, surrender, communion, union-language.

**Critical:** prayer is not Mode A/B/C. Prayer is a will-act whose form is shaped by A/B/C response-structure. The architecture's commitment is that mode is response-type, not predictive mechanism (Foundation File 2, §4.1; exclusion 17, File 4 §11.2). Which mode operates in a given prayer-event depends on substrate, history, environment, and stochastic factors the architecture does not formalize.

### 9.2 The A/B/C response-mapping

Conditional on which mode operates in a religious-encounter context, the architecture predicts the corresponding response-form:

| Mode (if operative in religious-context) | Encounter-form | Prayer-response shape |
|---|---|---|
| Mode A | sacred otherness, distance, judgment, finitude, frame-violation | petition, lament, awe-response, fear-articulation, repentance, "Have mercy" |
| Mode B | I as object to itself before encountered ultimate; reflexive PtCns operating | confession, examen, discernment, self-offering, "Here I am," self-examination before the encountered |
| Mode C | positive identification, communion-direction, constitutive-extension with encountered content | adoration, gratitude, surrender, communion, union-language, "Beloved" |

**Mature prayer typically coordinates all three modes** rather than operating in a single mode at a time. The Lord's Prayer in Christian tradition begins in Mode C (intimate address, "Our Father"), traverses Mode B (acknowledgment, "hallowed be"), Mode A (petition, "give us... forgive us... deliver us"), and returns to Mode C (closing doxology). The Islamic *ṣalāh* coordinates Mode A postural humiliation (*sujūd*), Mode B confession-and-supplication, and Mode C praise (*ḥamd*). Buddhist *mettā* practice begins in Mode C (self-directed loving-kindness) and extends through Mode A (encounter with difficult others) and Mode B (self-examination of the boundaries of one's compassion). The Jewish *Amidah* coordinates all three across nineteen blessings. Hindu *bhakti* practice often centers Mode C devotion while including Mode A petition and Mode B self-offering.

The coordination is not random. Traditions across cultures have developed prayer-forms that integrate the three modes, suggesting a structural feature: prayer that operates in only one mode tends toward pathology (Mode A only → fear-religion without communion; Mode B only → scrupulosity without surrender; Mode C only → fusion without examen). Mature prayer-traditions coordinate the modes, often with developmental sequencing across a life.

### 9.3 Sequential pattern (not exclusive)

A common (not exclusive) sequential pattern in religious encounter and the prayer-response it shapes:

```
Mode A encounter: I encounter distance, lack, guilt, threat, finitude, sacred otherness
    ↓ (recognition opens reflective space)
Mode B operation: I become object to myself before the encountered
    ↓ (self-examination clears the way)
Mode C response: I seek reintegration, surrender, communion, union, restoration
```

This sequential pattern is one common shape; many encounters do not follow it (some encounters operate primarily in Mode C from the start; some remain in Mode A throughout without Mode B reflective availability; some Mode B encounters do not resolve into Mode C). The architecture does not predict the sequence; it characterizes the structural possibilities and their typical coordination patterns.

### 9.4 Prayer and the six conditions of free choice

The Free Will application paper (§3.2) develops six conditions of free choice proper: contrastive live-option space, evaluative engagement, operator-bundle selection, non-preemptive substrate coupling, non-confabulatory Mode B reflective availability, and ownership-integration. The conditions apply to prayer with religious-charge-specific instantiations:

- *Contrastive live-option space*: alternative prayer-forms, alternative prayer-content, the option of not praying, the option of praying differently — all need to be constructible as live options. Severe scrupulosity often involves contracted live-option space (only one form of prayer registers as legitimate).
- *Evaluative engagement*: the I evaluates between prayer-options against its frame. Devotional habituation can render this evaluation rote rather than engaged.
- *Operator-bundle selection*: the prayer-act is configured (which mode dominates, what content, what posture, what language). Captured prayer-tradition often pre-configures the operator-bundle in ways the I does not own.
- *Non-preemptive substrate coupling*: the prayer is enacted through the I's substrate without preemption. Substrate-override states (§10) can preempt prayer-as-chosen and produce prayer-as-happened.
- *Non-confabulatory Mode B reflective availability*: the I retains reflective access to its own prayer-act, can name what it is doing and why. Cultic capture often suppresses this reflective availability.
- *Ownership-integration*: the prayer-act is owned by the I as expression of its own valued commitments, not as compliance with external authority alone.

Prayer in *frame-preserving* configurations satisfies the six conditions reasonably across instances. Prayer in *captured* configurations fails at multiple points: live-options contracted, evaluation rote, operator-bundle externally pre-configured, Mode B reflective availability suppressed, ownership-integration absent or coerced.

### 9.5 Source-status across the prayer spectrum

The agency apparatus's source-status continuum (Agency §5.9; this paper §2.1) applies to prayer:

- *Source-clean prayer*: the I is engaged in prayer through deliberation and ownership; the source-pathway of the prayer-act and its religiously charged content is traceable to the I's own integration.
- *Scaffolded-without-capture prayer*: tradition, community, or practice-form scaffolds the prayer-act in ways that support integration; the I retains live-option space and reflective availability.
- *Scaffold-captured prayer*: tradition, authority, or community has narrowed source-access and live-option space; the prayer-form is enacted but ownership-integration is compromised.
- *Coerced prayer*: prayer is performed under direct threat, exclusion, or punishment; the choice-architecture is not the I's own.
- *Substrate-captured prayer*: substrate-state (compulsion, mania-like state, traumatic activation, intoxication) drives prayer-form independent of the I's frame.
- *Apparent-other-captured prayer*: apparent-divine, apparent-demonic, or apparent-authority content overrides reflective access; the prayer is enacted under apparent-other command-perception.

This is the prayer-spectrum the companion paper develops in full. The present paper records the spectrum to make the connection between encounter-side phenomenology (Sections 3–8) and engagement-side configuration explicit.

### 9.6 What this bridge does and does not commit to

The bridge commits to: prayer is dual-status (will-act + A/B/C response); A/B/C response-shape depends on encounter-mode; the six conditions of free choice apply to prayer with religious-charge-specific instantiations; the source-status spectrum applies to prayer with religiously charged extensions.

The bridge does *not* commit to: whether prayer "reaches" anything beyond the I (this is metaphysical adjudication the architecture brackets); whether any specific tradition's prayer-form is normatively privileged (the architecture is neutral on tradition-correctness); whether intercessory prayer has efficacy beyond the I's frame (empirical-religious question the architecture does not adjudicate); a full taxonomy of prayer-forms (the companion paper develops this); the developmental psychology of prayer formation (companion paper).

The bridge supplies what the encounter-side paper requires: the structural slot where prayer sits in the architecture and the conditional response-mapping that connects mode of encounter to mode of prayer-response.

## 10. Substrate-mediated religious phenomenology

### 10.1 The two structural patterns

Substrate-override states (pharmacological agonism, contemplative-absorption, fasting / hypoglycemic states, NDE-related neurochemistry, ritualized-physiological states like extended chanting / breath-work / spinning) can produce, and in many religious and contemporary reports do produce, third-order-pathway phenomenology with structural features that populate two distinct architectural patterns. The architecture predicts two recurrent structural patterns; both occur. The four criteria of religiously charged content (§3) are the diagnostic between them, via the independent discriminators developed in §10.5.

**Pattern (a) — Substrate-mediated affective content without the four criteria.** Third-order pathway override produces affective signature with structural overlap to reports of religious experience — *without* the shadow-encounter recognition-structure (Criterion 1), scale-extending extent (Criterion 2), chain-extension past finite-I termination (Criterion 3), or ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus (Criterion 4). The valence-range parallels Pattern (b): Pattern (a) can be *affirmative-shape* (euphoria, bliss, vague-positive-other, cosmic-warmth), *privative-shape* (dysphoric panic, terror, dread, the "bad trip" generated as substrate-effect rather than as frame-violation recognition), or *orthogonal/neutral-shape* (high-intensity life-altering substrate-state without clear good/bad valence and without ultimate-$\Pi$ routing — pure intensity, dissociation, time-distortion). Across all three shapes, the substrate produces affective and phenomenological content with structural overlap to religious experience reports at the level of *intensity and felt-significance*, while the religiously charged structural form is absent.

**Pattern (b) — Substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter.** Substrate-override accompanied by shadow-encounter recognition (often including Level 3 apparent-I or apparent-presence assembly), chain-extension past finite-I sources to ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus, scale-extending extent. The four criteria are satisfied. The valence-range spans the four forms of §5 under substrate-mediation: affirmative ultimate-structure encounter (Form 1) — beatific mystical union, *brahman*-realization, cosmic-pattern recognition; privative ultimate-structure encounter (Form 2) — substrate-mediated demonic encounter, the bad trip with frame-violation pedigree routing to privative ultimate-$\Pi$ (hell-realm, void-as-threat, recognition of universal corruption); mirror-form / dual-revelation (Form 3) — substrate-mediated insight-realization coupling external-content recognition with self-recognition (the psychedelic shame-spiral that also reveals structural fact about the world, the *ayahuasca* purga that compounds Form 1 recognition with Form 3 self-implication); orthogonal-pattern (Form 4) — substrate-mediated realization of *in re* lawful pattern with value-orientation orthogonal to the I's frame (the *anatta* / dependent-origination realization under deep absorption, the psilocybin-induced *amor fati* stabilization).

**The cross-cutting structural axes.** The Pattern (a)/(b) distinction tracks whether the four criteria are satisfied; it does *not* track valence. Valence cross-cuts both patterns:

| | Affirmative | Privative | Orthogonal/neutral |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pattern (a)** (substrate-mediated; no four criteria) | euphoria, bliss, vague-positive-other, cosmic-warmth | dysphoric panic, terror, dread, "bad trip" as substrate-effect | pure intensity, life-altering without clear valence, dissociation, time-distortion |
| **Pattern (b)** (substrate-mediated; four criteria) | Form 1 under substrate-mediation (beatific mystical union, *brahman*-realization) | Form 2 under substrate-mediation (demonic encounter, bad trip with frame-violation pedigree, hell-realm) | Form 4 under substrate-mediation (*anatta*-realization, substrate-mediated *amor fati*); Form 3 under substrate-mediation (insight-realization with dual revelation) |

The architectural commitment is to the cross-cutting structure: valence and four-criteria-satisfaction are *separate* axes, not collapsible. Both patterns are architecturally real. The architecture does not predict that Pattern (b) is epiphenomenal or that substrate-mediation is dispositive against religious-charge. What the architecture commits to is that the four criteria themselves — *not valence* — are diagnostic between the patterns.

### 10.2 Pattern (a): substrate-mediated affective content without the four criteria

Pattern (a) is the configuration in which substrate-override produces affective and phenomenological content that *resembles* religious-experience reports — at the level of intensity, felt-significance, and apparent-disruption-of-ordinary-experience — without satisfying the four criteria. The shape-overlap is real; the architectural form is absent. Pattern (a) presents in three architecturally distinguishable valence-sub-shapes, each with characteristic phenomenology.

**Affirmative-shape Pattern (a) — euphoria, bliss, vague-positive presence.** The substrate produces intensity, positive valence, action-tendency-toward-positive-orientation, sometimes felt-presence of vague-positive-other or vague-cosmic-warmth, without shadow-encounter recognition-structure or chain-extension past finite-I termination. Pharmacological hedonic agonism (cocaine euphoria, opioid bliss, MDMA empathic warmth) often populates this sub-shape. The substrate produces what feels like the *shape* of beatific religious experience without the *structure* of religiously charged encounter: the I does not encounter ultimate-$\Pi$ content, does not recognize *in re* structure, does not route attribution-chain past finite sources. From inside, feels like "I feel amazing," "everything is wonderful," "I love everyone," but without the recognition-content of "I am encountering something" or "I am recognizing how things really are." This is the sub-shape to which the author has direct access (§2.2).

**Privative-shape Pattern (a) — dysphoric panic, dread, "bad trip" without frame-violation pedigree.** The substrate produces intensity, negative valence, action-tendency-toward-escape or freeze, sometimes felt-presence of vague-threatening-other or vague-impending-doom, without the shadow-encounter recognition-structure that would mark a Form 2 frame-violation registration. The dysphoric "bad trip" is privative-shape Pattern (a) when the terror is substrate-generated without routing through expected-presence-failing recognition or chain-extension to privative ultimate-$\Pi$. Pharmacological dysphoria (stimulant crash, opioid-withdrawal terror, ketamine k-hole dysphoria, unwanted-dissociation panic), bad trips on psychedelics that don't take the structural form of Form-2-mediated frame-violation recognition, dread-without-content of certain hypnagogic / hypnopompic substrate-states, panic-attacks with substrate-mediated component. From inside, feels like "I feel terrible," "something is very wrong," "I am dying or going to die," but without the recognition-content of "I am encountering ultimate evil" or "the structure of the world has revealed its privation."

The discrimination between privative-shape Pattern (a) and Form-2 Pattern (b) is the same discrimination as between affirmative-shape Pattern (a) and Form-1 Pattern (b): does the encounter satisfy the four criteria? Privative-shape Pattern (a) lacks shadow-encounter recognition (the dread is felt-as-dread, not as registration-of-expected-presence-failing); lacks chain-extension to privative ultimate-$\Pi$ (the dread does not route past the substrate-experience to ultimate-source); lacks scale-extending extent (the dread is local-to-the-experience rather than scale-exceeding). Form-2 Pattern (b) substrate-mediated demonic encounter or hell-realm phenomenology has the four criteria; privative-shape Pattern (a) does not.

**Orthogonal/neutral-shape Pattern (a) — high-intensity, life-altering substrate-state without clear valence and without ultimate-$\Pi$ routing.** The substrate produces intense, often deeply disorienting, sometimes lasting-effect-producing phenomenology that does not crystallize into either positive or negative valence and does not route through ultimate-$\Pi$. Strong dissociative states without value-content, intense time-distortion, ego-effect without affective interpretation, the felt-strangeness of certain substrate-mediated experience that the I retrospectively describes as "I don't know what to make of that" or "it changed me but I can't say how it was good or bad." DMT breakthrough fragments that don't include entity-encounter or world-recognition (just pure unfamiliar-form), certain general-anesthesia-induced phenomenology, ketamine dissociation without dysphoric valence, near-fatal physiological states producing intense subjective alteration without clear valence (some NDE-fragments that don't cohere into either Form 1 or Form 2 phenomenology), certain DMT or 5-MeO-DMT experiences whose intensity exceeds any frame for evaluation. From inside, feels like "this was intense and strange and I don't know what it was." Often life-altering — the I's profile is reconfigured — without the reconfiguration tracking either affirmative or privative ultimate-$\Pi$ recognition.

The phenomenology of orthogonal/neutral-shape Pattern (a) is empirically common in psychedelic and NDE literatures but often under-discussed because it resists narrative-fit. Reports of "I can't describe it, it just was" or "it changed me but I can't say in what direction" are characteristic. The architecture predicts this sub-shape exists structurally — substrate-override can produce intensity and profile-reconfiguration without affective-valence-routing and without four-criteria-satisfaction — and predicts that some fraction of substrate-induced "spiritual experiences" reported in survey work belong here rather than to Pattern (b).

**Architectural significance of Pattern (a).** Pattern (a) — across all three sub-shapes — is architecturally informative because it shows that substrate-override can produce phenomenology *resembling* religious experience without producing religiously charged encounter. Frameworks that read all substrate-induced intense states as religious experience over-classify; frameworks that read all reports of substrate-mediated religious-form experience as substrate-only-effects under-classify. The four-criteria diagnostic distinguishes, and the valence-shape sub-distinction within Pattern (a) further refines the typology — distinguishing the euphoric substrate-effect from the dysphoric substrate-effect from the orthogonal/neutral substrate-effect, all of which are Pattern (a) and all of which differ from their Pattern (b) counterparts in the four-criteria axis.

### 10.3 Pattern (b): substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter

Pattern (b) is the configuration extensively attested across cultural traditions and the contemporary literature. The substrate-override is accompanied by shadow-encounter recognition; the chain extends past finite-I termination; the ultimate-Π-terminus is operative. The four criteria are satisfied.

Tradition-internal attestations include: the soma rituals of Vedic religion (with the *Rig Veda* describing soma-induced encounter with the divine in terms structurally identical to the four-criteria characterization); the *iboga* initiation traditions of Bwiti and related West African religious practice; the *ayahuasca* traditions of Amazonian shamanism; the *peyote* ceremonies of Native American Church religious practice; the mushroom-mediated Mesoamerican religious traditions; the *kykeon* of the Eleusinian mysteries (with the strong Wasson-Hofmann-Ruck hypothesis of ergot-derived psychoactive content); fasting-induced mystical states across Christian, Jewish, Islamic, and Buddhist contemplative traditions; the *kundalini* arousal experiences of certain yogic practices; deep-meditation absorption states (*jhāna* in Theravada Buddhism, *samādhi* in Hindu and Buddhist traditions) where contemplative substrate-state produces sustained shadow-encounter recognition.

Contemporary literature attestations include: the Pahnke "Good Friday" experiment (1962) showing psilocybin-occasioned mystical experience meeting standard mystical-experience criteria; the Griffiths et al. studies (2006 onward) replicating the finding with sustained personality-change effects; the broader contemporary clinical literature on psychedelic-induced mystical experience and its therapeutic correlates; the substantial DMT-entity-encounter literature (Strassman 2001; Davis et al. 2020; multiple subsequent replications); NDE phenomenology (Greyson, van Lommel) showing religiously charged structure in cardiac-arrest neurochemistry contexts.

Pattern (b) is real and structurally specific. The architecture treats it as religiously charged encounter that the four criteria identify, with the metaphysical bracketing of §10.4 applied throughout.

### 10.4 The four metaphysical readings of Pattern (b)

The architecture's metaphysical bracketing of Pattern (b) admits at least four distinct interpretive readings, each consistent with the architecture's apparatus:

(i) **Substrate-as-source.** The substrate-override produces Level 3 apparent-I content (entities, divine presence, cosmic-pattern recognition) that does not correspond to any Level 1 actual referent. The phenomenology is real; the apparent-other has no actual-other status. The substrate generates the apparent-content; nothing more is required to explain the encounter. Naturalist-eliminativist reading.

(ii) **Substrate-as-tuning.** The substrate-override alters the I's receptivity such that genuine apparent-other-I content (with Level 1 status the architecture cannot verify from inside, but does not deny) becomes available where ordinary substrate state does not support its assembly. The substrate is not source but channel; what is encountered is real-other-I or real-intrinsic-structure ordinarily filtered out by ordinary substrate. This is the view many psychedelic users and traditional plant-medicine traditions hold; it is the view structurally compatible with reports that the entities encountered carry information the user could not have generated.

(iii) **Substrate-as-disinhibition.** The substrate-override removes ordinary substrate-imposed constraints on shadow-encounter recognition, allowing the I to recognize *in re* intrinsic structure that is always there but not ordinarily available at the I's typical scale-of-attention. The encountered content is genuine *in re* recognition, made accessible by the substrate change rather than generated by it. Closer to certain Buddhist and Daoist readings of altered-state experience as revealing what is always so. Distinguishable from (ii) in that (iii) does not require Level 1 actual-other-I; it requires only that *in re* structure ordinarily filtered becomes accessible.

(iv) **Mixed.** Different substrate-override episodes, or different aspects of single episodes, populate different readings. Some encounters are pattern (a); some are pattern (b) under reading (i); some under (ii); some under (iii). No single uniform reading covers all cases.

The architecture's apparatus accommodates all four readings. The choice between them is metaphysically substantive and cannot be made from inside any single I's perspective. This is exactly the perspectival-realism bracketing the foundation already commits to (File 4, §9.4). The architecture does not prefer (i) over (ii) or (iii); it does not prefer (ii)/(iii) over (i). It commits to the structural form of the encounter and leaves the metaphysical reading to the resources of philosophy of religion, philosophy of mind, and tradition-internal theology — none of which the architecture replaces.

### 10.5 Discriminators between Pattern (a) and Pattern (b)

The four criteria are the primary diagnostic. But they are not always reliably assessed from inside the substrate-override episode. The unreliability is structural: substrate-override states often reduce Mode B reflective availability (Free Will §3.2, Condition 5 — reflective availability reduction is the architecturally typical case, structurally analogous to flow / absorption states rather than to the more extreme cogito/thought partition functional collapse of severe depression developed in Foundation File 4 §10.5.6, which the architecture reserves for the specific severe-depression case) and tend to produce conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque profile-states (File 3, §6.1), which reduces the I's ability to apply $\phi$-construction to the experience as it unfolds. The architecture predicts several tendencies that can support discrimination, with appropriate humility. *The diagnostics discriminate within valence-pairs* — affirmative-shape Pattern (a) from Form-1 Pattern (b); privative-shape Pattern (a) from Form-2 Pattern (b); orthogonal/neutral-shape Pattern (a) from Form-4 Pattern (b) — since valence-similarity does not determine pattern-membership.

- **Chain-extension diagnostic.** Pattern (a) (any sub-shape) typically lacks chain-extension; the affective content is felt without source-attribution to anything ultimate. Pattern (b) (any form) typically includes chain-extension routing past finite-I to ultimate terminus. From inside, Pattern (a) feels like "I feel amazing" / "I feel terrible" / "this is intense and strange"; Pattern (b) feels like "I am encountering something" or "I am recognizing how things really are."
- **Recognition-as-fit diagnostic.** Pattern (a) typically lacks shadow-encounter recognition-structure; the phenomenology is affective or phenomenologically intense without recognition-content. Pattern (b) typically includes the "Aha" of recognition (Form 1) or the PtCns frame-violation registration of expected-presence-failing (Form 2) or expected-absence-failing (synchronicity-type) or the apparent-self-encounter coupled with shadow-encounter (Form 3) or the lawful-pattern-with-orthogonal-orientation recognition (Form 4). The recognition-content is the discriminator, not the affective tone.
- **Sustained-integration diagnostic.** Pattern (a) typically does not produce sustained integration tracking the affective content; the euphoria, dysphoria, or intensity dissipates with the substrate-state, sometimes leaving residue but rarely producing profile-restructuring tracking the experience-content. Pattern (b) often produces lasting profile changes — the well-documented psychedelic "afterglow," sustained personality changes after psilocybin / DMT / ayahuasca experience, the lasting integration of mystical experience and the lasting reorientation after substrate-mediated frame-violation encounter, that distinguish religiously charged encounter from pure substrate-effect. *Caveat for orthogonal/neutral-shape Pattern (a):* this sub-shape can produce profile-reconfiguration without four-criteria-satisfaction, so the sustained-integration diagnostic is weakened where orthogonal/neutral-shape Pattern (a) is candidate; the discriminator becomes whether the integration tracks a *recognized* ultimate-$\Pi$ content (Pattern (b)) or simply tracks the intensity of the experience without ultimate-$\Pi$ routing (orthogonal/neutral-shape Pattern (a)).
- **Apparent-other diagnostic.** Pattern (a) typically does not include Level 3 apparent-other-I or apparent-presence assembly; the felt-presence is vague-warmth or vague-threat or undifferentiated-intensity. Pattern (b) often includes specific apparent-other-I content with apparent agency, apparent communication, apparent specificity, or specific apparent-presence-content (apparent-place, apparent-pattern, apparent-field).

These are tendencies, not strict criteria. From inside the substrate-override episode itself, the I often cannot reliably distinguish Pattern (a) from Pattern (b); post-hoc reflection with $\phi$-construction over the experience often clarifies which pattern obtained, but not always. Discernment-trained practitioners (§8.5) should show better discrimination than untrained.

### 10.6 The developmental-arc case

The author's access (§2.2) includes *affirmative-shape* Pattern (a) substrate-override euphoria occurring in a developmental trajectory in which Pattern (b)-form privative ultimate-structure encounter (§5.2) had previously occurred but had been only partially integrated, *and* a subsequent trajectory-level retrospective religiously charged recognition that integrates the entire arc. The architectural worked case is informative because it exercises multiple architectural pieces interacting across an extended developmental sequence:

1. **Self-implicating privative ultimate-structure encounter** (Form 2 with self-implicating cascade-tracing): apparent-other-I assembling as void at the canonical apparent-I site, with reflexive cascade-tracing identifying the I's own action-trajectory as source-pathway.
2. **Localized behavioral encapsulation without profile-level reorganization**: the proximate-context class bounded out of the active option-space; broader profile (operator-bundle Π-ranking, focus, core action-trajectory) unchanged.
3. **Akrasia at trajectory-level**: Mode B reflective access to "this is the result of my actions" available; operator-bundle continuing to select on the unchanged broader basis; the corrective trajectory not engaged.
4. **Substrate-capture trajectory unfolding on the unchanged profile**: pharmacological substrate-override entering as part of the trajectory the broader profile supported.
5. **Affirmative-shape Pattern (a)**: the substrate-override producing the affirmative-valence sub-shape of §10.2 — euphoria with structural overlap to beatific religious-experience reports — without satisfying the four criteria.
6. **Eventual profile-formative integration**: across an extended developmental arc, the warning-content reaching the broader profile and the operator-bundle reconfiguring; the trajectory altering.
7. **Retrospective trajectory-level religiously charged recognition — the delayed integration of item 1**: a recognition arriving post-trajectory that re-reads the entire developmental arc as religiously charged, and that *completes the integration the privative ultimate-structure encounter of item 1 originally called for*. The recognition has three structural components arriving coupled: (i) *world-recognition* — the system was operating providentially across the arc; the absence of catastrophic outcome (overdose, robbery, arrest, shooting) is read not as luck but as being watched over; (ii) *self-recognition* — "I had to suffer through my drug use to see the truth because I didn't look deep enough at myself previously"; the I's own deficiency (specifically the inadequate self-examination after item 1) was the target of the trajectory; (iii) *necessity-recognition* — "what happened happened because it was what I truly needed"; the trajectory is recognized as required-for-this-I rather than as random.

The temporal arc is significant: item 1 was a moment-level Form 2 self-implicating encounter that called for self-examination; items 2–3 are the I's inadequate response (localized correction without profile-reorganization, then akratic non-following of the recognized correction); items 4–5 are the substrate-trajectory consequences of the inadequate response (escalation through harder substances that the un-integrated warning had not prevented); item 6 is the eventual profile-reconfiguration; item 7 is the trajectory-level recognition that integrates the whole arc — and the integration takes specifically the form of recognizing that *item 1's warning was real, that it called for what items 2–3 did not deliver, that items 4–5 were the consequence of that failure, and that the system maintained the I's continuation (PCE) through the trajectory until integration was possible*. Item 1 and item 7 are bookends of a single integration arc, separated by years and by an extensive substrate-capture trajectory.

Architecturally, item 7 is a **Form 3 (mirror-form / dual-revelation) compound with Form 1 features**, operating retrospectively across the developmental arc rather than at any single encounter moment. The world-recognition component carries Form 1 structural signature (recognition of providence operating as *in re* feature of the system, with chain-extension to ultimate-Π-source); the self-recognition component carries Form 3 structural signature (externally-mediated self-encounter, where the "external" content is the I's own trajectory now recognized as carrying revelation about the I); the coupling-as-single-recognition is the Form 3 dual-revelation marker. The recognition is also structurally a §8.3 sub-form (b) unifying recognition operating at developmental-arc scale ("the parts of one's life form one whole"), and it has §11.8 gratitude features (Mode C integration of received-trajectory-as-gift with chain-extension to ultimate-Π-source).

The four metaphysical readings of Pattern (b) (§10.4) apply to item 7 just as they do to substrate-mediated episodes: the retrospective recognition is compatible with (i) actual providence operating across the arc, (ii) the I's substrate having osmotically imprinted templates that produce the retrospective recognition as pattern-match without the providence existing, (iii) the I's own profile-formative integration projecting providence-shape onto a trajectory that was statistically survivable, or (iv) mixed readings. The architecture commits to the structural form of the retrospective recognition (Form 3 / Form 1 compound at trajectory level, with the three coupled components) and brackets which reading explains it.

This sequence exercises a substantial fraction of the architecture's apparatus: apparent-other-I content at Level 3, shadow encounter, reflexive PtCns, output-imprinting cascade tracing, identity-neutral encapsulation (File 2, §4.5), akrasia at trajectory-level, third-order substrate override, profile-formative integration over time, and *trajectory-level retrospective religiously charged recognition* as an architecturally distinct configuration from moment-level religiously charged encounter. Used as a worked case, it is a longitudinal demonstration that no single discrete-event analysis can produce. It also shows that religiously charged phenomenology is embedded in actual developmental trajectories, not detached as isolated abstract events, *and that the same developmental arc can span both Pattern (a) (item 5: affirmative-shape substrate-mediated euphoria not satisfying the four criteria) and Pattern (b) (item 7: trajectory-level Form 3 / Form 1 compound satisfying the four criteria) within a single longitudinal trajectory*. The two patterns are not exclusive across a life; they can occur at different moments and at different levels (substrate-episode level vs trajectory-integration level) within the same developmental arc.

**The architectural insight the case reveals.** The deeper structural fact the case demonstrates is the relationship between moment-level religiously charged warning and trajectory-level retrospective recognition: *incomplete integration of a religiously charged warning at the moment of its occurrence (items 1–3) can produce a developmental trajectory whose terminus is the integration the original warning called for (item 7)*. Item 7 is not architecturally independent of item 1; it is the same integration arriving years later through a trajectory that the inadequate response to item 1 enabled. This is structurally important for the architecture's claim that religiously charged phenomenology operates not only as discrete moment-level encounter events but also as trajectory-level integration processes that span developmental sequences. The "watched over" component of item 7's world-recognition is specifically the recognition that the system maintained the I's continuation (PCE) through the trajectory until integration was possible — the recognition that the trajectory was survivable was itself constitutive of the recognition that the trajectory was meaningful. Whether this reading is metaphysically correct (the four readings of §10.4 apply) is bracketed; the structural form of the integration arc is what the architecture commits to.

### 10.7 The DMT entity-encounter case

The DMT-entity-encounter literature (Strassman 2001; Davis et al. 2020 on N,N-DMT; the broader phenomenological literature on intense-dose tryptamine experience) is one paradigmatic candidate domain for Pattern (b). An important sub-distinction: N,N-DMT phenomenology characteristically includes specific entity-encounter reports ("machine elves," geometric beings, mantis-like beings, jester-figures, healer-figures, divine-mother figures); 5-MeO-DMT phenomenology more characteristically includes nondual / void / white-light reports without specific apparent-entity assembly. The two compounds populate different sub-regions of substrate-mediated religious phenomenology. The discussion below concerns primarily N,N-DMT entity-encounter reports.

Reports across the documented Davis et al. (2020) survey and surrounding literature are characteristically of:

- **Direct encounter with apparent other-beings.** Level 3 apparent-I assembly is robust; the beings present with their own apparent perspectives, apparent agency, apparent communication. Davis et al. documented common entity-labels ("being," "guide," "spirit," "alien," "helper") with frequent visual and extrasensory modalities. Whether these motifs exceed expectations from shared culture, expectation, reporting-selection, and substance-specific phenomenology remains an empirical question; the recurrence of motifs is documented, while the architectural implication of cross-user-consistency-as-such requires further work to establish.
- **Hyper-real felt-quality.** The encounter is often experienced as more real than ordinary reality; noetic-feel is extreme. The felt-truth-force is greater than that of ordinary perceptual encounter.
- **Apparent-received-content.** The entities are often experienced as carrying or transmitting information the user feels they could not have generated. The transmission has the structural form of communication from another I rather than internal-I cognitive production.
- **Sustained integrative aftermath.** Many reports describe lasting personality changes, sustained shifts in religious or philosophical orientation, the well-documented "afterglow" effect, and ontological reorientation. The magnitude and durability of these effects compared to long-term contemplative-practice effects are an active empirical question.

The architecture's analysis applies the standard apparatus. The Level 3 apparent-I assembly is what the experiencer encounters; the four criteria are typically satisfied for the N,N-DMT entity-encounter case (shadow-encounter form: yes; scale-extending extent: yes, at the level of apparent-other-I-realm; chain-extension past finite-I: yes; ultimate-$\Pi$-terminus: typically yes, with the entity-realm or entities themselves treated as ultimate-$\Pi$); the encounter is religiously charged. The architecture's bracketing of Level 1 status applies: whether DMT entities correspond to (i) substrate-generated apparent-I with no actual referent, (ii) Level 1 actual cogniting beings of some kind made encounterable through substrate-mediated tuning, (iii) parts of the I's own profile structure surfacing as apparent-other through substrate disinhibition, (iv) symbolic-archetypal content with its own architectural status the foundation does not yet formalize, or (v) something the architecture's current apparatus does not adequately characterize, is *bracketed*.

The architecture is structurally agnostic across these readings. What it commits to is that the encounter is real *as encounter* (Level 3 assembly is occurring), that the four criteria are typically satisfied (the encounter is religiously charged in the paper's sense), and that the metaphysical reading of Level 1 status is not adjudicable from inside the experiencer's perspective. The paper's analytical apparatus describes what the encounter is structurally; it does not adjudicate what the entities metaphysically are.

This is the architecture operating exactly as its perspectival-realism commitment requires: not denying Level 1, not affirming Level 1, treating the structural form as what the architecture commits to and the metaphysics as out of scope.

## 11. Religious affect

Religious affect comprises affective configurations whose object is religiously charged content and whose operators include the religious-charge structure (the four criteria of §3, with one or more of the four forms of §5 operating). The differentiated-affect paper's apparatus carries directly: the affect criterion (Differentiated Affect §4.1), the cluster organization, the derivation schema. Religious affect is treated as the religiously charged subset of the affective space, with the four-form typology specifying how religious-charge structures the affective configuration.

### 11.1 Devotion as connection-cluster affect oriented toward ultimate-$\Pi$ content

Devotion is the sustained PCE-orientation toward a constitutive-extension to religiously charged content treated as ultimate or constitutively orienting. The lover-of-the-ultimate has the encountered ultimate (or its apparent-presence, in the architecture's bracketed treatment) as constitutive extension of the maximally-self-defined I; PCE-projection over the extended configuration sustains across time; the four criteria of religiously charged content are satisfied through the chain-extension to ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus.

Devotion is *not always Form-1-coupled*. It is structurally a connection-cluster affect (Differentiated Affect §5) specialized to religiously charged content; the form-coupling varies with what the I treats as ultimate:

- **Form-1-coupled devotion** is devotion to affirmative ultimate-structure: theistic devotion to apparent-divine being; devotion to *in re* cosmic order; mathematical-Platonist devotion to necessity.
- **Form-3-coupled devotion** is dual-revelation devotion: devotion structured through the mirror-form coupling of recognition-of-ultimate and recognition-of-self. The Sufi self-knowledge devotional tradition, the Christian contemplative *imitatio Christi* tradition, and devotional repentance more broadly populate this configuration.
- **Form-4-coupled devotion** is devotion to orthogonal-pattern: the Daoist devotion to the Way that does not track the I's value-frame; Stoic and Nietzschean devotion to *amor fati*; devotion to truth-as-ultimate-without-personal-orientation. The connection-cluster structure is present; what differs is the value-orientation of the terminus.
- **Symbolic / humanistic devotion** is devotion to ultimate-content that is not theistic: devotion to justice, liberation, truth, the dharma, cosmic structure, the human project, the well-being of others as ultimate-Π. Whether such configurations are "religious" in the ordinary sense depends on the I's tradition-affiliation framing; the architecture classifies them by structural form, and they qualify as religious devotion in the paper's content-property sense when the four criteria are satisfied.

The healthy form preserves the I's frame across the extension; the dissolution-cluster pathologies (Differentiated Affect §5.4) apply with appropriate specialization. The diagnostic discipline of §11.9 applies: affective valence does not predict structural integrity; what matters is whether the configuration preserves coherent identity-integration and live-option integrity (see §11.10 below for the A/B/C constructive / capturing mirror table that develops this).

### 11.2 Reverence as Form-1-coupled boundary-cluster affect

Reverence is awe combined with controlled-distance — the I encounters the apparent-divine at scale-exceeding extent but maintains the structural distinction between lived-I and divine-other rather than seeking dissolution-merger. Reverence is structurally awe (Differentiated Affect §8.3) within a religiously charged frame, with PtCns operating to preserve the I's frame across the boundary-encounter rather than admitting the boundary-attenuation characteristic of mystical union (§7).

### 11.3 Sacred fear as Form-2- or Form-3-coupled threat-cluster affect

Sacred fear (the *fear of the Lord* tradition; the Buddhist *saṃvega* — religious urgency / shock; the *yir'ah* of Hebrew religious tradition) is PCE-projection toward an apparent-divine evaluator or toward privative consequences of misalignment. It is structurally a threat-cluster affect (Differentiated Affect §7) specialized to religiously charged content, with the projection-content routed through ultimate-Π terminus. The architecture distinguishes:

- **Form-2-coupled sacred fear**: PCE-projection toward privative-evil consequences (judgment, damnation, *naraka*, hell-realm). The projection is of privative-Π-terminus.
- **Form-3-coupled sacred fear**: PCE-projection toward dual-revelation-mediated recognition of misalignment with divine-evaluator. The fear is of being-seen, of mirror-recognition that would surface deficient pattern.

Both forms operate within religiously charged frames. Healthy / pathological pairing applies per the affect paper template.

### 11.4 Religious shame and guilt via reflexive PtCns

Religious shame and guilt are self-cluster affects (Differentiated Affect §6) operating under religiously charged frame. Shame is the I's whole-I profile being globally evaluated against the religious-charge frame's standards (often reflexive evaluation against the apparent-divine-evaluator); guilt is the I's specific actions being evaluated against the religious-charge frame's standards. The reflexive-PtCns bridge lemma (Differentiated Affect §4.3) operates in religiously charged form: the I's lived-I profile, self-representations, actions, and frame-relations are evaluated against the I's frame *as that frame is constituted within the religious-charge*, with the divine-evaluator-as-implicit-standard often functioning as the consistency-anchor.

The cogito-anchor guardrail of Differentiated Affect §4.3 applies: reflexive PtCns evaluates the lived-I profile, not the bare cogito. Religious self-loathing that appears to evaluate the bare I-as-such (the "I am evil," "I am beyond redemption," "my existence is sin") is architecturally a profile-level evaluation operating with functionally-collapsed cogito/thought partition (parallel to severe depression's collapse, File 4 §10.5.6). The cogito is not actually evaluated; functional access to the partition is what has failed.

### 11.5 Religious peace as Π-stabilization

Peace in religious traditions (Christian *pax*, Hebrew *shalom*, Islamic *salām*, Buddhist *upasama*) is the affective signature of $\Pi$-stabilization within a religiously charged frame. The maximally-self-defined I's preservation-ranking has been ordered such that the religiously charged terminus operates as ultimate-Π, with attendant reduction of Mode A conflict and PCE-projection-anxiety. The architecture treats this as healthy affective configuration when the Π-stabilization tracks accurate profile-recognition rather than denial-mediated suppression of conflict.

### 11.6 Hope and despair via PCE-projection at ultimate-Π scale

Religious hope and despair are threat-cluster affects (Differentiated Affect §7.4–7.5) operating at ultimate-Π scale. Hope is PCE-projection of viable preservation-pathway under uncertainty, with the preservation-target including ultimate-Π content (salvation, liberation, sustained communion with apparent-divine). Despair is the projection-toolset's failure to construct viable preservation-pathway under PCE-orientation at ultimate-Π scale (the dark night, the loss of religious certainty, the felt-collapse of the religious frame).

The ultimate-Π scaling distinguishes religious hope and despair from ordinary hope and despair. The structure is the same; the scaling makes the affective intensity correspondingly larger and the recovery-pathway correspondingly more difficult to construct.

### 11.7 Zeal as Form-1-coupled high-Π mission-projection

Zeal is high-Π PCE-projection oriented toward action-implementation of religiously charged commitment. It is operationally adjacent to devotion (§11.1) but distinguished by mission-orientation: the I is oriented not just toward sustained PCE over the extended configuration but toward action that propagates or actualizes the religiously charged content. Zeal is healthy when it preserves the I's frame and other-I-frames across the action-implementation; pathological when it operates as hypertrophied PtCns treating other-I frames as illegitimate sources of inconsistency to be eliminated rather than accommodated (File 4, §10.5.1).

### 11.8 Gratitude as Form-1-coupled Mode-C integration of received-good

Gratitude is the affective signature of Mode C integration of received-good content with chain-extension. Within religiously charged frames, the received-good is recognized as flowing from ultimate-Π-source (apparent-divine-source, system-as-such, *in re* origin); the gratitude is correspondingly directed toward that source. Architecturally gratitude is connection-cluster affect (Differentiated Affect §5.6 — joy in beloved's flourishing has structural family-resemblance) specialized to received-good rather than other's-flourishing.

### 11.9 Negative affect ≠ destructive religious profile; positive affect ≠ constructive religious profile

A diagnostic discipline applies across religious affect: the affective valence does not predict the structural integrity of the configuration. A blissful religious state can be capturing (cult-induced positive awe; substrate-mediated euphoria mistaken for religiously charged encounter); a painful religious state can be constructive (the dark night that produces apophatic deepening; sacred fear that produces accurate self-recognition; religious shame that produces integration). The frame-classification of §13 (frame-preserving / frame-encapsulating / frame-capturing) is what predicts structural integrity, not affective valence.

### 11.10 The A/B/C constructive / capturing mirror table

The §9 prayer bridge mapped each mode to a religious-response form (Mode A → petition / lament / awe / repentance; Mode B → confession / examen / discernment; Mode C → adoration / surrender / communion). The same modes can operate in *constructive* configurations (integration-preserving, agency-preserving, profile-coherent) or in *capturing* configurations (integration-distorting, agency-compromising, profile-disorganizing). The configurations mirror each other across the constructive / capturing axis:

| Mode | Constructive / integrative form | Distorting / capturing form |
|---|---|---|
| Mode A | reverence, humility, repentance, liberation from false authority, sacred awe with frame-recalibration | terror, paranoia, cursedness, persecutory divine-otherness, frame-collapse before sacred power |
| Mode B | self-examination, discernment, confession, self-authorship before the encountered, scrupulous-without-self-erasure | recursive shame, obsessive accusation, scrupulosity-as-self-attack, religious self-erasure, identity-dissolution by reflexive PtCns hypertrophy |
| Mode C | communion, belonging, surrender-as-ownership-integration, solidarity, devotion-with-frame-preservation | fusion, dependency, malignant identification, agency-collapse into the encountered, cultic merger |

**The discriminator is not affective valence.** Painful repentance (Mode A) can be constructive; blissful surrender (Mode C) can be capturing. The diagnostic is whether the configuration preserves:

- *Coherent identity-integration* — the I's lived-I network maintains coherent structure across the configuration; PtCns operates without hypertrophy and without atrophy.
- *Reflective access* — Mode B reflective availability (Free Will §3.2, Condition 5) is preserved; the I can examine its own religious configuration without that examination being suppressed by the configuration itself.
- *Live-option integrity* — the contrastive live-option space is preserved; alternative configurations remain constructible as options (Agency §5.2).
- *Source-status examination* — the source-pathway of the religious content can be examined (or held open as not-yet-examined) without the examination being foreclosed by religious authority, tradition, or substrate-state.
- *Owned agency* — the religious configuration is owned by the I as expression of its own commitments (Free Will §3.2, Condition 6); the manipulator / manipulated-agent distinction (Free Will §4.3) applies in identifying configurations where ownership has been compromised.

The mirror table operates within the broader 3-axis classification of §13 (integration / frame / source); it specifies the mode-level discriminator that the 3-axis classification develops in full. The discriminator is necessary because affective valence is a poor proxy for structural integrity in religious configurations: traditions explicitly aim for painful Mode A configurations (repentance, conviction-of-sin, *yir'ah*, *saṃvega*) in their constructive forms; traditions also produce blissful Mode C configurations (cultic ecstasy, dissolutive merger) in their capturing forms. Without the structural discriminator, frameworks classify by valence and misdiagnose both directions.

## 12. Religious temporality

The temporal phenomenology paper (File 6) develops the architectural account of subjective time-flow rate, the structure of "now," the past/future asymmetry, and time in altered states. Religious temporality applies that apparatus to religiously charged contexts.

### 12.1 The eternal now: absorption-type and insight-type

The "eternal now" of contemplative traditions is structurally heterogeneous; at least two distinct configurations are reported under this label, with different architectural signatures.

**Absorption-type eternal now.** Mode-B-suppressed deep absorption with maximal $\kappa$-on-present-imprinting. The bandwidth-bounded integration argument (Temporal Phenomenology §3.1) predicts that when integration-load against accumulated structure is suspended (Mode B suppressed) and focus is allocated maximally to present-imprinting content, the felt-quality of "now-ness" reaches its maximum and time-as-passage attenuates. Deep *samādhi* in Buddhist concentration practice, sustained flow-states in religious dance or sustained chant, the absorption-side of contemplative *jhāna* states populate this configuration. Mode B is genuinely *suppressed* — the I is not reflectively examining present-imprinting, but is fully absorbed in it.

**Insight-type eternal now.** Mode-B-transformed operation on present-imprinting or on atemporal / ultimate content. The practitioner reflectively recognizes the structure of time itself — its passage, its phenomenal construction, its limits — within the contemplative state. Mode B is not suppressed but *transformed*: it operates on the temporal structure of experience itself as object, often coupled with shadow-encounter recognition of atemporal / ultimate content. Eckhart's *nu* in its theologically articulated form, the contemplative-philosophical recognition of God's eternity in the act of contemplation, certain Madhyamaka contemplations of the emptiness of temporal succession, certain Zen recognition-of-time-as-such moments populate this configuration. The phenomenological signature differs from absorption-type: the practitioner can articulate what they are encountering; reflective access is preserved (and transformed by the encounter); the recognition has the structural signature of shadow encounter applied to temporal-structural content.

The two configurations differ structurally even where their tradition-internal labeling overlaps. Absorption-type is the architectural prediction for sustained Mode-B-suppressed maximally-present-focused contemplative practice (consistent with the standard treatment in attention-and-absorption research literature). Insight-type is a distinct configuration in which Mode B operates on temporal-structural content rather than being suppressed, and shadow-encounter recognition extends to atemporal / ultimate content rather than being absent. The two often coexist within sustained contemplative practice; mature contemplative traditions describe trajectories from absorption-type to insight-type or oscillations between them.

This is *not* a prediction that the eternal-now (either type) is literal eternity. It is a prediction about the phenomenological signatures of two specific configurations. The metaphysical reading (eternal-now as participation in divine eternity, eternal-now as illusory product of contemplative absorption, eternal-now as glimpse of *in re* atemporal structure ordinarily filtered) is bracketed.

The temporal phenomenology paper (File 6) treats altered-state temporality as a future-focused application; this religious-temporality paper develops the religiously charged subset, which extends but does not replace the foundation-level temporal apparatus.

### 12.2 Liturgical and ritual time

Liturgical time (the church year, the Jewish calendar of holy days, the Islamic year, the Buddhist calendar of practice-events) and ritual time (the time-structure of specific religious rites) are scaffolded contact-driven focus structures. The tradition's calendar and ritual-form operate as osmotic and explicit imprinting (File 3, §8.7) on the I's focus-allocation, producing recurrent religiously charged $\kappa$-orientation at scheduled intervals. The temporal-phenomenology apparatus predicts that the experienced subjective time-quality of liturgical / ritual periods should differ characteristically from ordinary time, with the difference tracking the religiously charged frame-extension (Criterion 3) rather than mere ritual-novelty.

### 12.3 Eschatological time

Eschatological time is PCE-projection across very-long horizons, with the projected futures including ultimate-Π content (salvation, liberation, end of suffering, cosmic consummation, final judgment, return of the messiah, *paradiso*). The architecture treats this as PCE-projection with Criterion 3 chain-extension applied to the projection-target rather than to the present encounter. Religious hope and despair (§11.6) are the affective signatures of eschatological-time PCE-projection.

### 12.4 Repentance time

Repentance time is Mode B reaching past content via the persistence relation $\sim^m_H$ (File 3, §6.2), with the past content evaluated under religiously charged frame (typically reflexive PtCns operating against the past actions / configurations). The phenomenology of past-re-entering-present in repentance is structurally Mode B operating on persistence-traces with religiously charged evaluation. This connects to confession, examen, *teshuvah*, and the broader penitential traditions across religions.

### 12.5 Conversion time

Conversion is a before/after lived-I structural reorganization. The maximally-self-defined I before conversion and the maximally-self-defined I after conversion differ in profile-topology, frame-extension (Criterion 3), and verification-toolkit configuration. Conversion-time is the architectural recognition that there are temporally-distinct configurations of the same modeling I separated by a profile-reorganization event. The phenomenology of "I am not the person I was" is the report of this structural fact; the recovery-from-conversion or the loss-of-faith is the inverse — return to (or reconfiguration toward) a profile without the conversion-installed frame-extension.

The architecture predicts that conversion-time and ordinary-developmental-time are structurally distinct: ordinary development is gradual profile-evolution; conversion is profile-reorganization-event (often correlated with specific encounter, recognition-event, or ritual-event). Some apparent conversions are gradual (the slow integration of religious-charge into a previously-secular profile); others are punctate (the dramatic single-event conversion attested across traditions). Both are architecturally valid; the distinguishing feature is whether the profile-reorganization is gradient or step-discontinuous.

## 13. Frame-preserving, frame-encapsulating, and frame-capturing configurations

### 13.1 The classification problem

Religious encounters can integrate (or not) with the broader lived-I profile in structurally distinguishable ways. The standard tradition-internal vocabulary (orthodox vs. heretical, healthy vs. unhealthy, holy vs. unholy, true vs. false revelation) is theologically-loaded and tradition-internal. The architecture needs neutral application-level apparatus that distinguishes structural integrity from theological valence and that applies tradition-neutrally.

### 13.2 The 3-axis profile

The architecture's apparatus for religious-encounter integration is a 3-axis profile over existing architectural dimensions:

| Axis | Architectural source | Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Integration-axis** | Mode C extension status | non-integrated → encapsulated → constitutive extension → dissolutive |
| **Frame-axis** | PtCns status | atrophied → preserved-as-own → hypertrophied → captured |
| **Source-axis** | Source-status (Agency §5.9, §4.12) | source-clean → source-opaque → mixed-source → scaffolded-without-capture → scaffold-captured → coerced → substrate-captured → apparent-other-captured |

Each axis combines existing foundation and prior-application apparatus. The integration-axis draws on identity-neutral encapsulation (File 2, §4.5), constitutive-extension topology (Differentiated Affect §3), and dissolution-cluster pathology (Differentiated Affect §5.4). The frame-axis draws on PtCns disturbance forms (File 4, §10.5.1) and the cult-induced PtCns-capture pathology (Differentiated Affect §8.3). The source-axis draws on the Agency paper's source-status apparatus (Agency §4.12 osmotic-imprinting source-compromise continuum; Agency §5.9 toolset-input / operator-bundle distinction), with the source-clean ↔ scaffold-captured ↔ coerced spine taken directly from the Agency paper's canonical continuum.

**Terminology note.** This paper introduces "scaffolded-without-capture" as a derivative shorthand for the positive-pole formative-scaffolding configuration the prior application papers describe under their own canonical phrasings: Differentiated Affect §8.3's "external frame scaffolds integration without capture" (in the awe three-variant table for frame-adoption) and Agency §4.12's cultural-scaffolding category that "allows reflective access (Mode B over the scaffolded contents) without punishment, exclusion, or institutional retaliation, and [where] alternative input-sources are available." The shorthand is application-level shorthand for the religious-encounter domain, not a foundation-level term, and is consistent with the prior application papers' usage; "scaffold-captured" is taken directly from Agency §4.12 and §5.9 as the canonical negative-pole term.

**Source-axis sub-positions for religious encounter.** The religious-encounter domain populates source-status sub-positions beyond the standard four-step continuum:

| Source-status | Religious-encounter instantiation |
|---|---|
| source-clean | religious recognition with stable source-pathway and agency-preserving integration |
| source-opaque | powerful sacred / noetic content whose source-pathway cannot be verified from inside (the synchronicity case, §8.2; many noetic encounters) |
| mixed-source | real recognition entangled with fear, trauma, desire, cultural template, or projection; the source-pathway has multiple contributors the I cannot cleanly separate |
| scaffolded-without-capture | tradition or community supports integration without suppressing reflection; live-option space and Mode B reflective availability are preserved |
| scaffold-captured | tradition or community has narrowed source-access and live-option space; PtCns-capture mediated by formative authority |
| coerced | religious content imposed under threat, exclusion, punishment, or other coercive pressure; the choice-architecture is not the I's own |
| substrate-captured | religious certainty or salience driven by compulsion, mania-like state, traumatic activation, intoxication, panic, or other substrate-state |
| apparent-other-captured | divine / demonic / spirit / leader apparent-I content overrides agency or reflective access; the I experiences itself as commanded by apparent-other content |

The expansion preserves the standard four-step continuum as the spine and adds the structurally distinct sub-positions relevant to religious encounter. The substrate-mediation Pattern (a) / Pattern (b) distinction of §10 cross-cuts the source-axis: substrate-mediated states can be source-clean (the practitioner with stable contemplative training and reflective access), source-opaque, mixed-source, or substrate-captured depending on whether the substrate is augmenting reflective access, compromising it, or driving it.

Each religious encounter, and each religious-formation pattern, has a position on each axis. The 3-axis profile is the rigorous version of the classification; binary good/bad is too coarse.

### 13.3 Three expository handles

For ordinary expository use, three positions in the 3-axis profile have characteristic phenomenology and merit named handles:

**Frame-preserving religious configurations.** The I's PtCns continues operating as the I's own consistency-standard; integration (when it occurs) is constitutive without dissolution; live-option space is preserved; ownership-integration is intact; source-status is scaffolded-without-capture-or-cleaner. Profile: constitutive-extension × preserved-frame × {source-clean or scaffolded-without-capture}.

**Frame-encapsulating religious configurations.** The encounter is registered, sometimes including localized behavioral correction, without producing profile-level reorganization. The broader lived-I profile remains intact; identity-neutral encapsulation (File 2, §4.5) bounds the encountered content against propagation. Profile: encapsulated × preserved-frame × {source-clean or scaffolded-without-capture}. Not pathological; structurally common in religious lives that include warning-encounters not yet integrated, transient awe-states that do not transform, and scaffolded-without-capture religious participation that does not achieve constitutive extension.

**Frame-capturing religious configurations.** PtCns is captured by an external frame that operates as the consistency-standard in place of the I's. Capture can occur in several structurally distinguishable forms:

- **Dissolutive capture:** PtCns-capture coincident with frame-dissolution; the I has lost frame-coherence and operates as captured-by-the-encountered-content. Profile: dissolutive × captured × {scaffold-captured or coerced}.
- **Capture without dissolution:** PtCns-capture with preserved surface agency. The I retains apparent functional operation — can speak, work, reason, hold ordinary conversations — but PtCns has been captured by an external frame that operates as the implicit consistency-standard. Live-option space is narrowed (alternatives to the captured frame are not constructible); source-examination is suppressed (the source-status of the captured frame cannot be examined from inside); Mode B reflective availability with respect to the captured content is suppressed or distorted; ownership-integration with respect to actions inside the captured frame is compromised. Profile: constitutive-extension × captured × {scaffold-captured or coerced}. This is the high-functioning religious-trauma profile, the scrupulous practitioner whose surface agency is preserved but whose live-option space has contracted, the high-functioning cultic-identification configuration. *Dissolution is one possible subtype of capture, not a requirement.*
- **Substrate-captured configurations:** PtCns-capture mediated by substrate-state (compulsion, mania-like religious state, traumatic activation, intoxication). The capture is substrate-driven; the captured frame operates as the substrate's pull rather than as deliberated commitment. Profile: variable integration × captured × substrate-captured.
- **Apparent-other-captured configurations:** PtCns-capture where apparent-divine, apparent-demonic, or apparent-authority content overrides reflective access. The I experiences itself as commanded, addressed, or claimed by apparent-other content; the captured frame is the apparent-other's expressed will. Profile: variable integration × captured × apparent-other-captured.

The unifying feature of capturing configurations is that *PtCns has been captured by an external frame* in ways that narrow live-option space, suppress source-examination, distort Mode B reflective availability, or compromise ownership-integration. Whether dissolution accompanies the capture is variable; the discriminator is the capture itself, not its accompanying integration-state.

These three handles map characteristic corners of the 3-axis profile space. They are expository simplifications; the rigorous classification is the 3-axis profile itself.

### 13.4 Worked corners

| Configuration | Integration | Frame | Source | Phenomenology |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Healthy mystical union | constitutive extension | preserved-as-own | source-clean / scaffolded-without-capture | sustained communion with frame-preservation |
| Aesthetic-religious encapsulation | encapsulated | preserved-as-own | source-clean | the moving cathedral visit that doesn't transform |
| Scrupulosity | constitutive extension | hypertrophied | scaffold-captured | obsessive religious self-monitoring; recursive shame |
| Cult-induced capture | dissolutive | captured | coerced | merger with leader / community; loss of independent frame |
| Transformative awe | constitutive extension | preserved-as-own | source-clean | the awe-state that integrates into ongoing frame |
| Partial-encapsulation-without-reorganization | encapsulated | preserved-as-own | source-clean | the warning-encounter producing localized correction without profile-level integration (the author's developmental-arc case, §10.6) |
| Self-implicating privative ultimate-structure encounter | encapsulated-or-dissolutive | preserved-as-own (often initially) | self-implicated (source-status varies) | the eyes-as-void encounter; can integrate into frame-preserving recognition or capture into totalizing demonic-frame |
| Apophatic deepening | constitutive extension | preserved-as-own | scaffolded-without-capture | the dark night as integrative |
| Dissolution into dissolution-cluster pathology | dissolutive | hollowed | scaffold-captured or coerced | obsessive-merger configurations; codependency with the divine |
| Orthogonal-pattern stabilization | constitutive frame-boundary integration (the recognition that the I's value-frame is not exhaustive of the system's pattern is integrated as stable disposition, not encapsulated) | preserved-as-own | scaffolded-without-capture | sustained *amor fati*; *wu-wei* stabilization; the practitioner who has integrated frame-boundary-recognition without losing operating coherence |

The corners illustrate the discrimination the 3-axis profile supports. Single-axis labels would conflate categories that the joint profile distinguishes.

### 13.5 The discipline: structural integrity vs. theological valence

The architecture's classification is **structural-not-theological**. The profile of a configuration does not depend on the theological valence of its symbols. A tradition using transgressive, apparently-demonic, anti-authoritarian, or forbidden symbols can be frame-preserving; a tradition using maximally-orthodox dominant-tradition symbols can be frame-capturing. What predicts the profile is the integration / frame / source axes, not the symbol-set or doctrinal alignment.

This is the architecture's specific discipline: it does not adjudicate which traditions are correct, which symbols are healthy, or which doctrines are sound. It analyzes the structural form of how specific configurations integrate with the broader profile, using axes that apply tradition-neutrally.

The companion paper (*Religious Agency, Practice, and Formation*) develops the application of this classification to religious formation, religious authority, and the source-compromise continuum. The present paper establishes the classification at the encounter-level; the companion paper extends it to the formation-level.

## 14. Predictions and exclusions

This section consolidates the architecture's discriminating empirical commitments. It follows the discipline of Differentiated Affect §10: predictions are stated where the architecture's commitments produce specific empirical expectations that competing frameworks would not, or that would, under specifiable evidence, require the architecture to be revised.

### 14.1 Discriminating predictions

**Prediction 1 — Shadow-encounter signature shared across recognition-domains.** Religious recognition should share architectural signature (the "Aha" of recognition-as-fit, the receptive-passive felt-quality, the structural form of the foundation's three shadow-encounter-forms) with insight-recognition, mathematical-shadow-recognition, aesthetic recognition, and certain forms of moral recognition. Frameworks that treat religious cognition as a separate faculty should fail to predict the convergence; the architecture predicts it directly (Section 1, Section 8.3).

**Prediction 2 — Engineered-system prediction (test of Criterion 3 necessity).** Engineered systems should not produce religiously charged encounter proper merely by displaying complexity. They become religiously charged only when the I's frame treats the artifact, system, or apparent-other content as participating in, manifesting, mediating, or threatening an ultimate-$\Pi$ terminus (§3.3). The proximate finite source does not by itself prevent religious-charge; what matters is whether the frame terminates the attribution-chain at finite agency or routes the encounter through finite agency toward ultimate significance. The consecrated cathedral, the AI assembled as apparent-other-spiritual, the ritual object known to be manufactured but routed through consecration — all are cases where Criterion 3 can hold even with known finite-I manufacturing.

The prediction is therefore structural-conditional: religious-charge tracks Criterion 3, not the proximate manufacturing source. The empirical content is whether Criterion 3 is operationally necessary for religious-charge — if reports of religious-charge toward engineered systems systematically involve frame-extension past the engineers, Criterion 3 is supported; if substantial reports of religious-charge toward engineered systems satisfy Criteria 1, 2, 4 without any form of Criterion 3, Criterion 3 needs revision. *Falsification:* a population of subjects reporting full four-criteria religiously charged encounter toward engineered artifacts whose frame-route demonstrably terminates at finite-I engineers (verified through structured chain-extension interviews) would challenge the architecture.

**Prediction 3 — Frame-conversion prediction (refined).** Conversion and deconversion should alter the dominant frame-extension route, not simply toggle religious-charge on or off. Conversion should add frame-extension past finite-I for previously-not-frame-extending content (Criterion 3 newly satisfied); deconversion should remove frame-extension for previously-frame-extending content (Criterion 3 no longer satisfied). However, residual religious-charge typically persists through several architecturally specific mechanisms: osmotic imprinting (Foundation File 3, §8.7) — substrate-held patterns continue activating even after the explicit frame has shifted; affective association and trauma-cue activation — religiously charged content with strong affective imprint continues to activate even after the frame-extension has been revised; aesthetic and community-identity attachment — partial integration of religious content through non-religious-charge pathways; moral and fear residue; latent / retrievable religious concepts; source-opaque activation. The substantive prediction is therefore *reorganization of source-route and $\Pi$-position*, not toggle. Phenomenological reports across conversion / deconversion trajectories should show this specific pattern: dominant route shifts; residual activation persists; new frame-extension produces religious-charge in new domains; old frame-extension produces something that may still be felt but reorganized in source-status and $\Pi$-position. *Falsification:* if deconversion reliably extinguished all residual religiously charged activation across populations rather than reorganizing it, this would challenge the osmotic-imprinting connection. If conversion produced no detectable shift in frame-extension route while producing religious-charge, this would challenge Criterion 3.

**Prediction 4 — Pure-rationalism prediction.** Pure rationalism without frame-extension should produce understanding without religious-charge even when intrinsic structure is fully grasped (§3.6). The mathematician who experiences proof-as-construction-only should not encounter religious-charge; the mathematician who encounters proof as discovery of *in re* necessity at scale, with chain-extension to mathematical-Platonist ultimate, can. Mathematical-Platonist awe at necessity is the latter case; pure formalism without frame-extension is the former.

**Prediction 5 — Substrate-override two-pattern prediction (with independent diagnostics).** Substrate-override states should populate phenomenology in both Pattern (a) (substrate-mediated affective content without the four criteria of encounter proper, spanning affirmative-shape, privative-shape, and orthogonal/neutral-shape sub-variants) and Pattern (b) (substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter with the four criteria, spanning Form 1, Form 2, Form 3, and Form 4 instantiations) (§10.1). *Crucially:* the prediction is not falsifiable by definitional restatement of the criteria. The substantive empirical content is that the two patterns should diverge on *independent diagnostics* (developed in §10.5): (i) chain-extension diagnostic — Pattern (b) shows attribution-routing past finite-I; Pattern (a) does not; (ii) recognition-as-fit diagnostic — Pattern (b) includes the structural signature of recognition-as-fit; Pattern (a) does not; (iii) sustained-integration diagnostic — Pattern (b) often produces lasting profile changes detectable in longitudinal follow-up; Pattern (a) typically does not; (iv) apparent-other diagnostic — Pattern (b) often includes specific apparent-I / apparent-presence assembly; Pattern (a) typically does not. The prediction is that these four diagnostics co-vary in characteristic ways across substrate-override populations, supporting the two-pattern structural distinction. Substrate-mediation as such is not dispositive against religious-charge. The architecture remains metaphysically neutral across substrate-as-source, substrate-as-tuning, and substrate-as-disinhibition readings of Pattern (b) (§10.4). *Falsification:* if the four diagnostics did not co-vary as predicted across substrate-override populations — for example, if chain-extension dissociated systematically from recognition-as-fit, or if sustained-integration occurred with the same frequency in both patterns — the two-pattern distinction would need revision.

**Prediction 6 — Dual-revelation coupling prediction.** Religious-charged self-recognition and religiously charged world-recognition should occur as structurally coupled report-structures when externally-mediated self-encounter operates within a religiously charged recognition event, rather than as wholly separate phenomena (§8.4). When mirror-form (Form 3) criteria are met, the recognition is doubly-loaded as the structurally-typical configuration (Foundation File 2 §3.3 records the typical-but-not-strictly-necessary coupling for externally-mediated self-encounter generally; the dual-revelation prediction is the religiously charged subset in which the coupling is the rule rather than the exception). Phenomenological reports of conversion, contemplative insight, and religious-mediated moral self-recognition should show coupling in report-structure. *This prediction applies where externally-mediated self-encounter occurs; the architecture does not predict that every religious encounter will trigger Mode B self-recognition.* *Falsification:* frameworks treating religious self-recognition and world-recognition as separate phenomena should fail to find the coupling in structured phenomenological reports of conversion / contemplative insight / mirror-form encounter; if such reports systematically separated self-recognition from world-recognition rather than couple them in the predicted way, the architecture's Form 3 typicality claim would be challenged.

**Prediction 7 — Orthogonal-pattern populating prediction.** Religiously charged phenomenology should populate not only the affirmative ultimate-structure and privative ultimate-structure terminus-positions but also the mirror-form and orthogonal-pattern categories. Empirical work on religious experience that surveys only "felt presence of God" or "felt presence of evil" should systematically under-sample mirror-form and orthogonal-pattern encounters. Phenomenological reports of complex religious life — across traditions and including non-traditional / spiritual-not-religious populations — should include mirror-form and orthogonal-pattern encounters at non-trivial frequency.

**Prediction 8 — Tradition-symbol neutrality prediction.** Frame-preserving versus frame-capturing should not be primarily determined by tradition-symbol-valence (§13.5). A tradition's symbol-set should not be the primary structural determinant of its profile in the 3-axis classification; what predicts the profile is the integration / frame / source axes. Empirical work on religious health and pathology should find that orthodox-symbol traditions and transgressive-symbol traditions both populate frame-preserving and frame-capturing corners, with the corner-distribution tracking integration / frame / source rather than primarily tracking tradition-affiliation. (The architecture does not claim tradition-affiliation has *no* predictive relation at all; some communities and practices may statistically differ in the source-status and frame-status profiles they produce. The substantive claim is that symbol-set or doctrinal alignment is not sufficient and not the primary structural determinant.)

**Prediction 9 — Discernment-trained discrimination prediction.** Discernment-trained practitioners should show systematically different patterns from untrained practitioners when encountering religiously charged content of comparable noetic-intensity (§8.5). They should be more likely to detect frame-capture configurations before consolidation; distinguish mirror-form from privative ultimate-structure encounter; recognize source-compromised noetic-feel. This is testable through structured-interview methodology applied to populations differing in trained-discernment exposure.

**Prediction 10 — Cogito-anchor preservation across mystical union.** Reports of "non-existence of the I" in mystical union should be analyzable as reports of lived-I-network reorganization (boundary-attenuation + Mode C constitutive-extension to apparent-ultimate-content) rather than as literal cogito-loss (§7.1). Specifically: post-experience reports should preserve the structural commitment that the I-who-reports is the same I who underwent the experience; the cogito's tautological self-identity should be preserved across the report-structure even when the experience-content was reported as "I dissolved." This is testable through structured analysis of mystical-experience reports across traditions; perennialist and constructivist treatments should both find the structural commitment preserved.

**Prediction 11 — Localized encapsulation without profile-reorganization prediction.** Religious-warning encounters and religious-recognition encounters should produce localized behavioral encapsulation more readily than profile-level reorganization (§10.6). Reports of "I knew immediately that X behavior had to stop, but my broader life continued unchanged for a long time" should be common and architecturally specific; profile-level reorganization should typically require additional events / time / trajectory beyond the initial encounter. This is testable through longitudinal studies of conversion-and-recovery trajectories.

**Prediction 12 — Substrate-override Pattern (a) / Pattern (b) discrimination tendencies.** Pattern (a) and Pattern (b) substrate-override states should differ in chain-extension (Pattern (b) routes past finite-I; Pattern (a) does not), recognition-as-fit (Pattern (b) includes shadow-encounter recognition; Pattern (a) does not), sustained-integration (Pattern (b) often produces lasting profile changes; Pattern (a) typically does not), and apparent-other-I assembly (Pattern (b) often includes specific apparent-other-I or apparent-presence content; Pattern (a) does not). These are tendencies, not strict criteria; from inside the substrate-override episode itself, the I often cannot reliably distinguish (§10.5). *Falsification:* post-hoc structured interviews or Mystical Experience Questionnaire variants refined for chain-extension and recognition-as-fit items should show statistically significant separation between Pattern (a) and Pattern (b) cohorts on these four discriminators; absence of such separation under properly operationalized measurement would challenge the structural distinction.

### 14.2 Exclusions and disclaimers

The architecture as developed in this paper does not claim to derive several phenomena it has used or referenced:

- **The metaphysical truth of religious objects.** Whether the chain-terminus of religiously charged content corresponds to a metaphysically real referent — whether God, the divine, the demonic, the *Tao*, *moira*, divine-trickster-figures, DMT entities, or any other tradition-specific terminus exists in actuality — is bracketed. The architecture is single-perspective; from any one I's perspective, the actuality of religious objects is not verifiable from inside the I's apparatus.
- **Which traditions are correct.** The architecture is consistent with positions across the perennialism / constructivism spectrum on whether traditions are "really about the same thing" or about different things. It does not adjudicate.
- **The metaphysical reading of substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter.** Substrate-as-source, substrate-as-tuning, substrate-as-disinhibition, and mixed readings are all consistent with the architecture's apparatus (§10.4). The architecture does not prefer one over another.
- **Specific operationalization of the four criteria.** The criteria are stated structurally; their measurement instruments (how to operationalize "shadow-encounter form," "scale-extending extent," "chain-extension past finite-I," "ultimate-Π-position" empirically) are application-level work that future papers and empirical methodology must address.
- **The structural dynamics of religious formation across generations.** Religious traditions transmit across generations through osmotic-imprinting, scaffolded-formation, and explicit-formation processes that the Agency paper's apparatus (Agency §§5–6) describes structurally but the present paper does not develop in detail. The companion paper takes up religious formation.
- **Specific clinical / therapeutic implications.** The paper's apparatus has clinical adjacencies (cult-recovery, religious-trauma treatment, contemplative-practice-related dissociation, religiously-mediated obsessive-compulsive symptoms) but does not develop them as clinical model. Application-level clinical work using the architecture's apparatus is downstream work.
- **Intersubjective religious experience.** Inter-I religious encounter (shared mystical experiences in groups, the *koinōnia* of contemplative communities, religiously charged inter-I phenomenology) requires inter-I machinery beyond what the foundation has developed. The foundation flags inter-I work as future development; this paper does not extend it.

These exclusions are methodological discipline: the architecture commits to what its apparatus supports and explicitly leaves to application-level work or future foundation extension what its apparatus does not.

### 14.3 Negative predictions

The architecture also makes negative predictions — claims about what should *not* be observed:

- *Religiously-charged phenomenology should not be reducible to single-content features.* The four criteria operate jointly; no single criterion predicts religious-charge. Frameworks that try to predict religious experience from single features (felt-presence-of-other, awe-intensity, transcendence-feel, ego-dissolution-sense) should fail to predict the cases satisfying all four criteria but lacking the single feature, and should over-predict cases satisfying the single feature but lacking other criteria.
- *Tradition-affiliation should not predict frame-classification structure.* A practitioner's tradition does not determine whether their specific encounter or formation-pattern is frame-preserving, frame-encapsulating, or frame-capturing. Empirical work attempting to find tradition-level differences in the 3-axis classification should find that intra-tradition variance dominates inter-tradition variance.
- *Substrate-mediation should not be dispositive against religious-charge.* Frameworks treating substrate-induced religious experience as automatically epiphenomenal should fail to account for the well-attested integration-and-transformation effects of Pattern (b) substrate-mediated encounter.
- *Felt-truth-force should not predict source-verification.* Frameworks treating religious noetic-feel as evidence of veridicality should predict a stable correlation; the architecture predicts dissociation. Empirical work showing systematic dissociation between felt-truth-force and any independently-verifiable source-status (where such status is constructible) supports the architecture.

## 15. Translations

This section positions the architecture's predictions against alternative frameworks. The strategy is not to claim the architecture is better, nor to defend the alternatives against the architecture. The strategy is to identify *where the architecture's predictions diverge from each alternative's* — and to identify those divergences clearly enough that empirical work and philosophical engagement can distinguish them.

### 15.1 William James (1902, *Varieties of Religious Experience*)

James's four marks of mystical experience — ineffability, noetic quality, transiency, passivity — map cleanly onto the architecture. Ineffability is the conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque character of much religiously charged content (Tier 3 with low or undefined $\phi$); noetic quality is the architectural noetic-feel of §8.1 (Tier 1 / Tier 2-proximate felt-recognition); transiency is the temporary character of bandwidth-bounded deep-absorption configurations (Temporal Phenomenology §3.4); passivity is the receptive-passive shadow-encounter character of §6.5.

**Convergence:** The architecture and James's typology agree on the structural marks of mystical experience.

**Divergence:** James's typology covers only what the architecture treats as Form 1 and apophatic-compound (Form 2 + positive-Π) mystical experience. James does not develop the mirror-form (Form 3) or orthogonal-pattern (Form 4) categories systematically. The architecture predicts these as architectural completions; James's typology under-samples them.

### 15.2 Rudolf Otto (1917, *Das Heilige*)

Otto's *mysterium tremendum et fascinans* maps to the architecture's Form 1 (*fascinans*) and Form 2 (*tremendum*). The wholly-other (mysterium) is the chain-extension to ultimate-terminus that Otto's typology already articulates.

**Convergence:** Otto's positive-numinous and negative-numinous categories correspond to architectural Forms 1 and 2.

**Divergence:** Otto's *augustum* (the august, the demanding-recognition) is gestured at but under-developed; the architecture's Form 3 (mirror-form) supplies the structural completion. Otto does not treat orthogonal-pattern numinous (Form 4); the architecture supplies it. Otto's irreducibility-claim about the numinous is consistent with the architecture's claim that religious-charge is structurally specific (the four-criteria conjunction); Otto's sui-generis claim is *not* fully consistent with the architecture's claim that religious-charge shares shadow-encounter machinery with non-religious recognition (the architecture predicts shared signature; Otto predicts irreducibility).

### 15.3 Friedrich Schleiermacher (1799/1830, *On Religion*; *The Christian Faith*)

Schleiermacher's "feeling of absolute dependence" (*schlechthinniges Abhängigkeitsgefühl*) maps to the architecture's Form 3 (mirror-form) and to certain Form 1 chain-extension configurations where the I's frame recognizes itself as dependent on the ultimate-terminus. The "feeling" character is the conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque structural form of much religiously charged affect.

**Convergence:** Schleiermacher's structural insight that religion-as-such has a specific affective-recognitional form distinct from doctrine maps onto the architecture's structural specificity (the four criteria) distinct from tradition-content.

**Divergence:** Schleiermacher's localization of religion in feeling specifically tends to under-develop the cognitive-recognition character (shadow-encounter form) the architecture commits to. The architecture predicts that feeling and recognition are structurally coupled in religious experience, not that one is reducible to the other.

### 15.4 Mircea Eliade (1957, *The Sacred and the Profane*)

Eliade's distinction between sacred and profane time and space, and his account of the *axis mundi* and hierophany, map onto the architecture's frame-extension structure. The sacred is Eliade's name for what the architecture characterizes as religiously charged content; the profane is what fails the four criteria. Hierophany — the manifestation of the sacred — corresponds to the architecture's encounter with religiously charged content, often Form 1.

**Convergence:** Eliade's structural distinction between sacred and profane corresponds to the four-criteria distinction. Hierophany corresponds to religiously charged encounter.

**Divergence:** Eliade tends to treat the sacred as ontologically sui generis (related to but stronger than Otto's irreducibility-claim). The architecture commits only to structural specificity, not to ontological irreducibility. Eliade's tradition-internal language (axis mundi, sacred space, ritual repetition of cosmogony) is theologically substantive; the architecture treats these as tradition-internal articulations of frame-extension and ritual scaffolding without the ontological commitment.

### 15.5 Perennialism vs. constructivism (Stace, Forman, Katz)

Perennialism (Stace 1960; Forman 1990, 1998) holds that mystical experiences across traditions share a common essential core (typically pure consciousness or unmediated awareness). Constructivism (Katz 1978, 1983) holds that mystical experiences are tradition-shaped through and through, with no tradition-independent core.

The architecture is consistent with positions across this spectrum. It predicts: (a) the structural form of religiously charged encounter (the four criteria, the four forms) is tradition-neutral and shared cross-traditionally; (b) the specific content (which apparent-other-I assembles, which terminus the chain extends to, which symbol-set frames the encounter, which integration-pathway operates) is tradition-shaped through verification-toolkit-development, osmotic imprinting, and frame-configuration. The structural form is shared; the specific content is shaped.

**Convergence:** With perennialism, the architecture agrees that there is shared structural form across traditions. With constructivism, the architecture agrees that specific content is tradition-shaped.

**Divergence:** Strong perennialism's claim that the *content* of mystical experience is essentially the same across traditions is not fully supported by the architecture; the four-form typology and the variation in chain-terminus content predict tradition-specific content variation. Strong constructivism's claim that no tradition-independent structural form exists is not supported either; the four criteria and the four forms are tradition-neutral.

### 15.6 No-self traditions (Albahari, Madhyamaka)

Albahari (2006) and certain Madhyamaka readings argue that the self is constructed and its apparent-substantiality is illusory. The architecture's apparatus is consistent with this in a specific structural sense: the lived-I network is constructed through Mode C integration over the maximally-self-defined I; what is *not* constructed is the cogito-anchor itself, which is the tautological self-identity $\hat\iota_m = \hat\iota_m$ at structural Tier 2.

**Convergence:** The lived-I as constructed; identity-correlation profile as profile-shape rather than substance; mystical recognition of self-as-composite as architecturally specific (sub-form (c) shadow-encounter sub-decomposition, §8.3).

**Divergence:** The cogito-anchor is structurally preserved across no-self realization. The architecture commits to the tautological self-identity even as the lived-I network reorganizes; some no-self readings would deny this commitment. The architecture sees mystical no-self realization as recognition of the lived-I-as-composite, not as elimination of $\hat\iota_m = \hat\iota_m$. The cogito-anchor guardrail (§7.1) is the load-bearing distinction.

### 15.7 The dual-revelation tradition (Augustine, Calvin, Pascal, Buber, Levinas, Sufi self-knowledge)

The tradition of coupling knowledge-of-God with knowledge-of-self runs from Augustine's *noverim me, noverim te* through Calvin's mirror-doctrine, Pascal's *misère et grandeur*, the Sufi self-knowledge tradition, Buber's I-Thou, and Levinas's face-of-the-other ethics. The architecture's Form 3 (mirror-form / dual-revelation encounter) is the structural translation of the dual-revelation form.

**Convergence:** The dual-revelation tradition articulates phenomenologically what the architecture predicts structurally. The coupling of self-recognition and world-recognition in single recognition-event is tradition-internally well-attested and architecturally predicted.

**Divergence:** Tradition-internal articulations specify theological content (Augustine's doctrine of God; Calvin's reformed theology; Buber's relational ontology; Levinas's ethics-as-first-philosophy) the architecture does not. The structural form is shared; the theological content is not the architecture's commitment.

### 15.8 The orthogonal-numinous tradition

The orthogonal-numinous tradition spans trickster-figure scholarship (Hyde 1998; Radin 1956), Daoist studies (Slingerland 2003; Ames-Hall 2003), Greek philosophical tradition on *moira* and *tyche*, Stoic *amor fati*, Nietzschean affirmation (Nietzsche 1882, 1888), Camus's absurd (Camus 1942), and Schopenhauer's cosmic comedy. These are tradition-internal and philosophical-tradition-internal articulations of the architecture's Form 4.

**Convergence:** The structural form (frame-relinquishment under recognition of orthogonal-pattern terminus; the laugh of resignation) is well-attested across the orthogonal-numinous tradition.

**Divergence:** Tradition-internal articulations specify metaphysical and ethical commitments (the *Tao* as cosmic principle; *moira* as fate; Nietzsche's eternal recurrence; Camus's revolt) the architecture does not. The structural form is shared; the metaphysical commitments are not.

### 15.9 Contemplative neuroscience (Newberg, Lazar, Davidson)

Contemplative-neuroscience research has identified neural correlates of religious and contemplative experience: prefrontal-cortex involvement in sustained attention, default-mode network suppression in deep absorption, parietal-lobe involvement in self-other boundary phenomenology, neurochemical correlates of psychedelic-induced mystical experience.

**Convergence:** The architecture is implementation-neutral on substrate but is consistent with all of the above neural-correlate findings. Default-mode network suppression maps onto Mode B suppression in deep absorption; parietal involvement maps onto boundary-salience reduction in mystical union; psychedelic neurochemistry maps onto substrate-override states populating both Pattern (a) and Pattern (b) (§10).

**Divergence:** Contemplative neuroscience tends to be implementation-focused; the architecture is implementation-neutral. Strong neuroscientific-reduction claims (mystical experience is identical with specific neural states) are not fully consistent with the architecture's commitment that the structural form of religious-charge is what the architecture commits to, with neural implementation as substrate-mediation that the architecture does not derive.

### 15.10 Predictive-processing accounts of religious cognition (Andersen, Schjoedt et al.)

Predictive-processing approaches to religious cognition treat religious experience as Bayesian-inference-shaped expectation-content with specific priors and likelihoods. The architecture is consistent with predictive-processing accounts as one implementation candidate.

**Convergence:** Predictive-processing accounts can implement osmotic imprinting (priors shaped by ambient exposure), shadow-encounter (recognition of structure with high prior-fit), and the noetic-feel / source-verification dissociation (high prior-confidence with low constructible-pathway).

**Divergence:** Strong predictive-processing reductions that treat all of $\phi$ as predictive confidence are not consistent with the architecture's three-tier $\phi$-status structure (File 1, §2.2). The architecture's Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 distinction is irreducible to predictive-confidence under any single prior.

### 15.11 The contemporary psychedelic-mysticism literature (Pahnke 1962, 1969; Griffiths et al. 2006, 2008, 2011; Strassman 2001; Davis et al. 2020; MAPS, Johns Hopkins programs)

The contemporary clinical literature on psychedelic-induced mystical experience (Pahnke 1962, 1969; Griffiths et al. 2006, 2008, 2011; Strassman 2001; Davis et al. 2020; published research from the MAPS and Johns Hopkins clinical programs) has documented mystical-type experiences with substantial and sustained personal-meaning and spiritual-significance reports under controlled administration. Standard mystical-experience scales (Hood's Mysticism Scale; Pahnke-Richards categories; the Mystical Experience Questionnaire) operationalize aspects of what the architecture treats as encounter-proper criteria and forms, though the scales were not designed against the architecture's specific four-criteria characterization.

**Convergence:** The literature supplies candidate cases for Pattern (b) substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter (§10.3), especially where reports include apparent-other encounter, recognition-as-fit, sustained integration, and ultimate-orientation. The architecture's metaphysical bracketing (§10.4) is consistent with the research's typical methodological neutrality. Griffiths et al. 2006 and follow-up work specifically support sustained personal and spiritual significance from mystical-type psilocybin experiences in controlled settings. Davis et al. 2020 documents N,N-DMT entity-encounter phenomenology and enduring interpretive effects.

**Divergence and limitation of "confirmation."** The existing literature *supplies candidate cases* and *motivates operationalization* of the architecture's predictions; it does not yet *confirm* the architecture's specific Pattern (a) / Pattern (b) discrimination or four-criteria characterization. Existing instruments operationalize portions of the criteria (especially Hood items overlapping with Criterion 1 recognition-as-fit and Criterion 4 ultimacy); they do not yet operationalize chain-extension (Criterion 3) or scale-extending extent (Criterion 2) in their architecturally specific forms. The architecture's discrimination between Pattern (a) and Pattern (b) (§10.5) is more architecturally specific than standard mystical-experience scales. The architecture predicts that subjects scoring high on affective-intensity items (whether affirmative, dysphoric, or unspecified-valence) but low on chain-extension and recognition-as-fit items should show Pattern (a) phenomenology with limited four-criteria-tracking integration-effects, while subjects scoring high across all items should show Pattern (b) with integration-effects. This is a refined empirical prediction that the existing literature does not yet specifically test. Refined Mystical Experience Questionnaire variants with chain-extension and recognition-as-fit items would be the natural empirical step.

### 15.12 Summary

The architecture converges with established frameworks on substantial structural overlap (James's marks, Otto's positive-and-negative numinous, Eliade's sacred/profane, the dual-revelation tradition, contemplative-neuroscience correlates, the contemporary psychedelic-mysticism findings). It diverges in (i) treating religious recognition as not sui generis but as the religiously charged subset of shadow-encounter machinery; (ii) explicitly developing the mirror-form and orthogonal-pattern categories that standard typologies under-treat; (iii) maintaining metaphysical bracketing across substrate-mediation readings without privileging any; (iv) preserving the cogito-anchor across mystical no-self realization; (v) treating tradition-specific content as content-variation over a tradition-neutral structural form. The divergences are real and the architecture stakes commitments on them.

## 16. Open problems

Several questions remain for further work:

**The metaphysical truth of religious objects.** The architecture brackets this throughout. Whether God, the divine, the demonic, the *Tao*, *moira*, divine-trickster-figures, DMT entities, ancestors, spirits-of-place, sacred community-as-presence, or any other tradition-specific terminus exists in actuality is out of scope by architectural design (§13.2). The bracketing is principled — single-perspective architectures cannot adjudicate Level 1 status from inside — but it is also a real limit.

**Operationalization of the four criteria.** The criteria are stated structurally; their measurement instruments — how to operationalize "shadow-encounter form," "scale-extending extent," "chain-extension past finite-I," "ultimate-Π-position" empirically — are application-level work that future papers and empirical methodology must address. The closest existing instruments are Hood's Mysticism Scale, the Mystical Experience Questionnaire, and Pahnke-Richards criteria; refinement to discriminate the four forms specifically and to discriminate Pattern (a) / Pattern (b) substrate-mediation specifically is open work.

**Discernment formalization.** The architecture treats discernment as Mode B operation over source-status of religious recognition (§8.5), but does not formalize the trained-discernment toolkit's specific operations. Tradition-internal apparatus (Ignatian discernment of spirits; *upekkhā*-mediated source-evaluation; Sufi *furqān*) is rich; cross-tradition formalization that the architecture's apparatus could support is open work.

**Demarcation between religious and secular contemplative experience.** The four criteria distinguish religiously charged content from non-religiously charged content, but the boundary cases (deep secular mindfulness practice, philosophical contemplation, sustained aesthetic absorption, naturalistic transcendence) are not always cleanly classified. Refinement of the demarcation, particularly the conditions under which Criterion 4 (ultimate-Π-position) is satisfied vs. not satisfied for marginally-religious contemplative practice, is open work.

**Intersubjective religious experience.** Inter-I religious encounter (shared mystical experiences in groups, the *koinōnia* of contemplative communities, religiously charged inter-I phenomenology, the *communitas* of ritual events) requires inter-I machinery beyond what the foundation has developed. The foundation flags inter-I work as future development; the present paper does not extend it. Intersubjective religious experience is an open application-level domain.

**Religious formation across generations.** Religious traditions transmit across generations through osmotic-imprinting, scaffolded-formation, and explicit-formation processes. The cumulative effects of multi-generational religious formation, the evolution of traditions over centuries, and the conditions under which traditions sustain frame-preserving versus frame-capturing structure across generations are open work. The companion paper takes up religious formation at the individual-life level; trans-generational formation remains beyond it.

**Religious experience in non-human cogniting beings.** The architecture commits to form-invariance conditional on existence (other cogniting beings have the same architectural form). Whether non-human animals, non-biological cogniting beings, or other potentially-cogniting systems exhibit religiously charged phenomenology in a sense the architecture would recognize is open. The form-invariance conditional commits the architecture only to: if such beings exist as cogniting beings, the four-criteria characterization should apply to whatever religious-charge they exhibit.

**Specific clinical / therapeutic implications.** The paper's apparatus has clinical adjacencies (cult-recovery, religious-trauma treatment, contemplative-practice-related dissociation, religiously-mediated obsessive-compulsive symptoms) but does not develop them as clinical model. Application-level clinical work using the architecture's apparatus is downstream work.

**The psychedelic-mysticism / non-mysticism boundary.** The architecture's Pattern (a) / Pattern (b) discrimination predicts that not all psychedelic experience is religiously charged. The empirical distribution — what fraction of psychedelic experiences populate Pattern (a) vs Pattern (b), how this varies with substance, dose, set, setting, and individual factors — is open work. The architecture predicts the discrimination is real and empirically detectable; it does not predict the population-level distribution.

## 17. Conclusion

This paper has applied the Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture to religiously charged experience on the receiving / encountering side. The architectural claim has two parts. *Religiously charged content* (broader category) is content whose frame-position satisfies the chain-extension and ultimate-$\Pi$ criteria (Criteria 3, 4 of §3.2); this is the broader domain of religious life, including prayer, ritual, liturgy, sacred symbols, devotional objects, inherited religious categories, and religious trauma triggers. *Religiously charged encounter proper* (narrower subset) is the religiously charged content-event that additionally satisfies the recognition-event criteria of recognition-as-fit form (Criterion 1, application-level extension of foundation shadow-encounter) and scale-extending extent (Criterion 2); this is the encounter-side domain that has classically attracted phenomenological attention. The paper's structural-analytical apparatus operates primarily on encounter proper, with Section 9 providing the bridge to broader religiously charged content that the companion paper develops in full.

The variation across traditions, individuals, and configurations is in (i) what the I's frame routes the chain to terminate at, (ii) which of the four structural forms the encounter takes (affirmative ultimate-structure, privative ultimate-structure, mirror-form / dual-revelation, or orthogonal-pattern), and (iii) how the encounter integrates with the broader lived-I profile under the 3-axis classification (integration × frame × source).

The architecture remains metaphysically bracketed throughout. It does not adjudicate whether the termini correspond to real referents, whether religious objects exist, or which traditions are correct. It does not adjudicate whether substrate-mediated religiously charged encounter is substrate-as-source, substrate-as-tuning, substrate-as-disinhibition, or mixed. It does not adjudicate Level 1 status of any apparent-other religious content. It commits to the structural form and brackets the metaphysics.

The paper's contribution is not to settle religious questions but to make their structural shape analyzable. Specifically: it provides a tradition-neutral characterization of religiously charged content with the scope-control distinction between broader and encounter-proper categories; it provides a four-form typology that completes Otto's *fascinans* / *tremendum* typology with the under-treated mirror-form and orthogonal-pattern categories, with explicit architectural pedigree for each form (Forms 1 and 4 as direct foundation shadow-encounter; Forms 2 and 3 as application-level extensions with declared bridges); it provides architectural treatment of mystical union with the cogito-anchor guardrail that resolves the apparent paradox of self-loss without violating the foundation's structural commitments, and with the post-state-integration discriminator that prevents pathologizing intense experience as such; it provides a typology of revelation via the foundation's three forms of shadow encounter; it provides specific architectural handling of substrate-mediated religious phenomenology with two structural patterns and four metaphysical readings, with independent diagnostics rather than definitional restatement; it provides religious-affect treatment as the religiously charged subset of the differentiated-affect taxonomy, including the A/B/C constructive/capturing mirror table; it provides religious-temporality treatment with the absorption-type / insight-type distinction in eternal-now phenomenology; it provides a 3-axis frame classification (preserving / encapsulating / capturing) with expanded source-axis (including substrate-captured and apparent-other-captured) that distinguishes structural integrity from theological valence; and it provides the prayer bridge (§9) that connects encounter-side phenomenology to the engagement-side apparatus the companion paper develops.

The companion paper, *Religious Agency, Practice, and Formation*, will develop the engagement-side material — prayer in full, ritual formation, religious authority, source-compromise in religious life, cultic capture, and the constructive/scaffolding/distorting/capturing typology applied to formation across the broader religiously charged content domain — using the *Agency* and *Free Will* application papers (v0.3.0). The companion paper is no longer blocked: the agency split has landed, and the engagement-side work can now proceed.

The architecture is not a theory of religion. It is a structural-phenomenological apparatus that makes religious experience analyzable from inside the perspectival realism the foundation already commits to. Whether the architectural apparatus is adequate to religious experience as it actually is in actual lives — whether the four criteria are the right criteria, whether the four forms exhaust the structural space, whether the bracketing-discipline is adequate to traditions whose self-understanding requires positive metaphysical commitments — is itself open work that empirical engagement, philosophical critique, and tradition-internal evaluation will determine. The paper's claim is that the architecture's apparatus is rich enough to do substantial structural work on religious phenomenology without doing violence to it, and that the structural work it does is the kind of work a foundation in this domain should produce.
