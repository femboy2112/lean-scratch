## Abstract

This paper develops a structural taxonomy of differentiated affect from a single definitional starting point: love. Using the apparatus of the Two-Layer Identity-Correlation Architecture (foundation document set v5.3.3) — the maximally-self-defined I, the four-class phenomenal-availability / source-pathway-access ($A$/φ) taxonomy, the preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$, identity-correlation $\rho$, three modes of I-development (A: differentiating, B: self-modeling, C: integrating), and the prerogatives of consistency (PtCns) and continued existence (PCE) — love is defined as the structural state in which the maximally-self-defined I has extended via Mode C to include another content at *constitutive-extension topology*, with PtCns preserving frame-coherence and the extension's $\Pi$-position invariant under non-reorganizing variation.

An *affect criterion* establishes affect-as-such as structural perturbation of salience, action-readiness, profile stability, PCE, PtCns, or focus, distinguishing affect from non-affective phenomenal content. Differentiated affect is then constructed across five clusters by variation on the love-configuration: connection (grief, devotion, jealousy, fear-for-beloved, joy-in-flourishing); self (pride, shame, guilt, self-loathing); threat (fear, dread, hope, despair); boundary (anger, disgust, awe, hate); and embodied (pain, pleasure, trauma-cue activation, diffuse somatic states). Each affect-type has healthy constraints and characteristic pathology modes, including five named apparatus-level *profile-shape disturbances* (dissolution, severe depression, learned helplessness, chronic boredom, anhedonia), each damaging different structural equipment.

Section 10 commits the architecture to fourteen discriminating empirical predictions, including bidirectional recognition-lag, Pattern A/B threshold dynamics, dominant-operator classification of ambiguous affect labels, action-or-resolution pathway parallelism across clusters, anhedonia / joy-in-flourishing dissociation, and toolset-mediated agency and recovery. Section 11 positions these predictions against seven alternative frameworks (basic emotion theory, appraisal theory, constructionism, affective neuroscience, phenomenology, interoceptive predictive coding, attachment theory) with prediction-by-prediction discriminators. Section 12 catalogs open problems pointing toward future application work and possible foundation extension: agency phenomenology, mode-selection mechanisms, $\Pi$-magnitude formalization, empathy and emotional contagion, cultural-linguistic affect categories, operationalization protocols, and others.

The paper is methodological exemplar rather than comprehensive enumeration. It claims to give the architectural skeleton from which differentiated affect can be classified, pathologies can be structurally distinguished, and empirical work can engage with the architecture's commitments. Affective life retains its complexity, felt depth, and phenomenal integrity; what changes is that we now have apparatus for analyzing it.

## 1. Introduction

The foundation document set develops an architecture of conscious experience in which the lived I is the primary structural object and identity-correlation is the primary measure of how contents are integrated into that structure. The foundation deliberately stops short of doing affect work directly. The four-class taxonomy of contents (Section 6.1, File 3) names the conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque region — phenomenally available contents with undefined or low source-level φ — and explicitly identifies panic, dread, somatic affect, and trauma-cue activation as paradigmatic inhabitants of that region, but the foundation does not work out what it means to be a panic-attack, a grief-state, or a love-configuration. Those are application-level questions. This paper takes them up.

Several aspects of the foundation make affect a particularly natural first application beyond the temporal phenomenology paper. First, the conscious-fuzzy region — perhaps the architecture's most distinctive innovation — is most populously inhabited by affect. The architecture's claim that conscious-fuzzy contents are "fully conscious but source-opaque, not 'almost unconscious'" matters most for those who have endured panic attacks, traumatic responses, depressive states, or the diffuse somatic affect of strong emotion. These experiences have been historically misclassified by frameworks that conflate consciousness with verifiability. The architecture's split between phenomenal availability ($A_{m,t}$) and source-pathway access (φ) gives them their proper home.

Second, the maximally-self-defined I — the action-relevant configuration of the lived-I network whose preservation counts as continuation of the I (Section 4.8, File 2) — is the structural object through which much affect operates. Affects that involve the self (pride, shame, self-loathing), affects that involve relations to others (love, grief, jealousy), affects that involve anticipated changes to one's life-configuration (fear, hope, despair), and affects that involve the I/not-I boundary (anger, disgust, awe) are all configurations of this object and its relations. Without the maximally-self-defined I as a structural primitive, affect-classification has to work from the outside in, mapping symptoms to categories. With it, affect-classification works from the inside out: each affect is a state of the maximally-self-defined I and its situational preservation-structure.

Third, the architecture's commitment to *coherent non-integration* (Section 4.5, File 2) and identity-neutral encapsulation gives affect a place even when it does not centrally restructure the lived-I network. Some affect is constitutive (love, deep grief); some is encapsulated (mild irritation that the I bounds and dismisses without restructuring). The taxonomy must cover both.

This paper proceeds constructively. It begins with the definition of love, builds the taxonomy of connection-affects (extensions of the maximally-self-defined I and their dynamics) from that definition, and then generalizes the methodology to the rest of the affective space. The choice of love as the starting point is not aesthetic or sentimental but architectural. Love is the affect for which the foundation's apparatus, taken at full strength and applied without metaphor, gives the most structurally fertile definition. Other affects can be understood as variations, threats, losses, or failures of the love-configuration. This is not a claim that love is metaphysically prior to other affects; it is a claim that beginning with love makes the architectural skeleton of the affective space most visible.

The paper does not claim to enumerate every distinguishable affective state. It claims to give the architectural skeleton on which such enumeration could proceed and to develop representative cases that demonstrate the methodology. It also does not claim that the architecture replaces existing frameworks. Basic emotion theory, constructionism, appraisal theory, affective neuroscience, phenomenology, and interoceptive predictive coding all carve genuine joints in the affective space. The architecture offers a different cut, and Section 11 articulates where the cuts agree, where they diverge, and what the architecture sees that the others do not.

A note on phenomenological access. Affect is a domain in which inside-perspective access varies sharply across individuals and across affect-types. The architecture is grounded in phenomenological access by the author, refined under adversarial pressure across many development cycles, and pressure-tested through dialectical engagement. For some affects covered in this paper, the author has unusually strong phenomenological access through clinical history (depression, trauma-cue affect, suicidality) and that access is used as load-bearing evidence rather than bracketed. For others, access is more diffuse and the architecture's contribution is structural-classification rather than phenomenological deepening. Where access-asymmetry is relevant, it is named.

## 2. Architecture in brief: what affect requires

This section is not a foundation summary. It surfaces only the architectural elements that the affect taxonomy uses centrally, with cross-references to the foundation for full development.

**The four-class taxonomy of phenomenal-availability and source-verification.** The foundation distinguishes (Section 6.1, File 3): conscious-clear contents (phenomenally available with defined φ — explicit reasoning, source-traceable memory), conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque contents (phenomenally available with low or undefined source-level φ — panic, dread, trauma-cue affect, somatic states, novel-but-not-yet-articulated content), latent / retrievable contents (not currently phenomenally available but with constructible φ-pathway upon activation — stored facts, dormant skills), and unconscious-operative contents (not phenomenally available and without constructible source-pathway under the I's current toolset, but still affecting κ, ρ, salience, action, or disposition — implicit biases, osmotically formed dispositions, repressed contents). Most affect lives in the conscious-fuzzy region. The architecture's signature contribution is to refuse to call this region "almost unconscious" or "partially conscious"; conscious-fuzzy affect is *fully conscious*, just source-opaque, undergone by the I without constructible access to what produces it.

**The maximally-self-defined I.** The action-relevant configuration of the lived-I network whose preservation counts, for the modeling I under current conditions, as continuation of itself (Section 4.8, File 2). Not just the cogito-anchor, not the body alone, not all integrated contents, but the network configuration whose preservation makes the difference between "I continued" and "I did not continue" in the action-relevant sense. The maximally-self-defined I can extend to include contents and other I's — extensions of the self — at constitutive-extension topology where appropriate (Section 3.1).

**The preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$.** A situational ranking over possible profile-configurations and losses (Section 4.8, File 2). Distinct from ρ: ρ measures structural integration; $\Pi$ measures preservation-priority. $\Pi$ may depend on ρ but is not reducible to it. The foundation names $\Pi$ as a placeholder; its full formal development is application-level work, partially done in this paper for the affect domain.

**Mode C and PtCns.** Mode C is positive identification — integration of not-I content as continuous with self (Section 4.3, File 2). It operates on a continuum from light identification (a teammate, a community member) through deep affiliation (close friendship) to constitutive extension (the case where the integrated content becomes part of the maximally-self-defined I). PtCns is the prerogative of consistency — the structural feature by which the I's frame functions as the implicit consistency-standard, governing both Mode A (differentiation when integration would damage frame-coherence) and Mode C (integration constrained by frame-coherence). Mode C is PtCns-constrained: integration proceeds insofar as the lived-I profile can maintain or restore coherence (Section 4.5, File 2). PtCns is doing work in *both* modes; the affect taxonomy uses this PtCns-in-Mode-C role centrally.

**The prerogative of continued existence (PCE).** The structural feature by which the modeling I pulls toward the path that maximizes continued existence of the maximally-self-defined I (Section 4.8, File 2). PCE operates over the maximally-self-defined I as a whole, including any extensions. Affects related to threat, preservation, and loss are configurations involving PCE's operation.

**Identity-neutral encapsulation.** The structural mechanism by which encountered content is incorporated as identity-neutral from the I's perspective — bounded as non-self-defining or peripheral — rather than centrally restructuring the lived-I network (Section 4.5, File 2). Encapsulated content still develops $P_{m,t}$ (no contact or access without profile-development) but is bounded against propagation. Some affect is encapsulated (mild irritation, transient unease that does not restructure the lived-I network); some is constitutive (love, deep grief). The taxonomy needs to cover both.

The paper uses these elements as load-bearing throughout. Other foundation elements — D-orders and Fz from Section 8, three-tier φ structure from Section 7, the cogito/thought partition from Section 10.5.6 — appear only where specifically relevant.

## 3. The definition of love

The architecture supports a definition of love that is structural rather than experiential, formal rather than metaphorical, and constructed entirely from the foundation's existing apparatus.

### 3.1 The definition

**Definition.** *Love* is the structural state of a modeling I in which:

(i) The maximally-self-defined I has extended via Mode C to include another content $X$ in a *constitutive extension*: a graph/profile-level state in which removal, loss, or projected non-continuation of $X$ is represented as damage to the maximally-self-defined I itself, such that PCE operates over $X$'s preservation as preservation of the I's action-relevant continuation. High scalar ρ is necessary but not alone sufficient — the load-bearing feature is profile-topological: $X$ is *inside* the maximally-self-defined I rather than merely strongly integrated with it;

(ii) The prerogative of consistency (PtCns) has preserved the I's frame-coherence across the extension; and

(iii) The position of $X$ in the preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$ remains within the constitutive-preservation range under *ordinary, non-reorganizing* variation in κ, φ, and momentary affective salience for $X$, so long as the underlying constitutive-extension topology is preserved.

The Prerogative of Continued Existence then operates over the extended maximally-self-defined I as a whole. The beloved $X$ is *part of* the I's preservation target, not an external entity competing for preservation-priority alongside the self.

**On "ordinary, non-reorganizing variation."** The invariance commitment in (iii) applies to coordinate variations that do not constitute changes to the underlying constitutive-extension topology. Specifically:

| Variation type | Falls within scope of invariance? | Architectural treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Low contact / separation | Yes | κ-variation; topology preserved |
| Cannot articulate why one loves | Yes | φ-variation; topology preserved |
| Temporary felt distance | Yes | Local/foreground variation; topology preserved |
| Repeated differentiating events | No | Cumulative differentiating micro-updates change topology |
| Global identity reorganization | No | New maximally-self-defined I; old extensions may not transfer |
| Actual collapse of constitutive extension | No | Love has ended (Section 3.3) |

The invariance is therefore a structural commitment about the local stability of constitutive extensions under non-topology-changing variation, not a metaphysical commitment that no possible event can dissolve love. Section 3.3 develops the architectural mechanisms by which constitutive extension can dissolve — Mode A differentiation (often via accumulated micro-updates), structural reorganization of the maximally-self-defined I, or substrate-cessation. None of these are "ordinary variation"; all are topology-changing events.

**On structural love versus harmonious love.** The three conditions define structural love-as-constitutive-extension. They do not require that the extension be harmonious, morally good, or affectively pleasant. Once $X$ is constitutive, other clusters may operate over $X$: threat (fear-for-the-beloved), shame (when the beloved's behavior reflects on the I), anger (when the beloved violates the I's frame), grief (anticipated or actual loss), resentment (sustained differentiation against specific aspects), guilt (the I's perceived failures toward the beloved), or boundary-conflict (negotiating frame-violations within the integrated relation). These compounds — what may be called *cross-valenced constitutive extension* — do not automatically negate love unless they produce Mode A differentiation that crosses the constitutive threshold, frame-collapse, or loss of constitutive-extension topology. This protects the definition from being too clean for real cases: ambivalent love, love of a difficult parent, love mixed with sustained anger, love of a dead beloved, and ideological/national love can all be structurally love-as-defined-here while simultaneously hosting cross-cluster affective configurations.

**Traumatic bonds and love distinguished.** A traumatic bond *may contain* structural love when constitutive extension and PtCns-preserved frame-coherence are still present despite the relation's harm-pattern. But *traumatic bonding as such* names a pathological relational mechanism (Section 5.4): the extension is maintained or intensified through abuse, fear, intermittent reinforcement, isolation, or progressive collapse of the I's capacity to differentiate. When PtCns cannot preserve the I's frame across the relation — when the I's frame has been hollowed, overridden, or captured by the abuser's frame — the configuration is dissolution-pathology, not love. The same relation can therefore contain structural love that needs honoring AND traumatic-bonding pathology that needs naming as such. The architecture's contribution is to allow these to be distinguished rather than collapsed into either "it must not have been real love" or "love makes everything okay." Whether love is present in any specific traumatic-bonded relation is an empirical question to be evaluated by the criteria of Section 3.1; that traumatic bonding can also be present is a structural feature of the relational mechanism.

### 3.2 Unpacking the three conditions

**Condition (i): constitutive Mode C extension.** Mode C — positive identification (Section 4.3, File 2) — operates on a continuum. A teammate may be lightly identified-with: the I recognizes the teammate as continuous-with-self in the team-context, but the teammate's continuation is not identical to the I's continuation. A close friend is more deeply affiliated: the friend's continuation matters at a higher position in $\Pi_{m,t}$, but the friend remains structurally separate from the maximally-self-defined I. In love, Mode C has gone all the way to *constitutive* extension: the integrated content $X$ has become part of the maximally-self-defined I such that preservation of $X$ is preservation of the self in the action-relevant sense (Section 4.8, File 2). This is the architectural counterpart of the phenomenological reports "you are part of me," "I would die for them," "they are family," "this is my home" — when these are not metaphors but accurate reports of architectural state.

The transition from deep affiliation to constitutive extension is gradient at the experiential level but architecturally crisp: the integration has reached the profile-topological state in which $X$ is *inside* the maximally-self-defined I rather than merely strongly integrated with it. The I's continued operation as the modeling I that it is requires that $X$ be preserved. High scalar ρ is necessary but not alone sufficient — what is load-bearing is whether $X$'s removal would constitute damage to the maximally-self-defined I as a structural object, not just reduction of integration-strength.

**Condition (ii): PtCns-preserved frame-coherence.** Mode C is PtCns-constrained (Section 4.5, File 2): integration proceeds only insofar as the lived-I profile can maintain or restore coherence. In the constitutive-extension limit, this constraint is maximally tested. The I extends to include $X$, but the I must remain a coherent frame across the extension. The I must still be *the I doing the loving*, not dissolved into $X$.

When PtCns successfully preserves frame-coherence across the extension, the I retains its frame as a coherent integrated whole that now includes $X$. The lover remains a structurally distinct lived-I network with $X$ as part of its maximally-self-defined I. The lover can love without being dissolved.

When PtCns fails — when integration has gone past the point at which frame-coherence can be preserved — the I dissolves into $X$ rather than extending to include $X$. The result is the dissolution cluster: obsession, cultic merger, codependency, consuming romantic infatuation, self-erasing caregiver-dynamics. These are not deeper love; they are structural pathologies in which the I-as-lover has been lost (Section 5.4).

The healthy love condition therefore requires *both* Mode C extension to constitutive-extension topology *and* PtCns preserving the I's frame. Either alone is insufficient: extension without PtCns produces dissolution, PtCns without extension produces emotional unavailability or reserved identification.

**Condition (iii): invariance of $\Pi$-position under non-reorganizing variation.** Once $X$ has become constitutive of the maximally-self-defined I, its position in $\Pi_{m,t}$ inherits the maximally-self-defined I's preservation-priority. Because the maximally-self-defined I is the structural object PCE operates on — by definition the configuration whose preservation counts as continuation of the I — its preservation-priority remains within the constitutive-preservation range under non-reorganizing coordinate variation. The lover does not stop being a lover when separated from the beloved (κ low; topology preserved). The lover does not stop being a lover when unable to articulate why they love (φ undefined; topology preserved). The lover does not stop being a lover during a stretch of feeling distant (momentary affective salience varying; topology preserved). The architectural state of love is preserved through these variations because the constitutive-extension topology is preserved, not because some external attachment-mechanism perseveres against contrary evidence.

The invariance does *not* extend to events that change the constitutive-extension topology itself — repeated differentiating events, structural reorganization of the maximally-self-defined I, or actual collapse of constitutive integration. These are exactly the architectural mechanisms by which love can end (Section 3.3). The invariance commitment is the architectural distinction between *coordinate variation under preserved topology* and *topology change*.

This scoped invariance distinguishes love from a family of structurally adjacent states:

- *Infatuation:* high $\Pi$-position for $X$ but κ-dependent (collapses on separation; required continual feedback to maintain).
- *Admiration:* high $\Pi$-position for $X$ but φ-dependent (collapses on disillusionment; required the source-pathway to remain articulable as positive).
- *Identification:* high $\Pi$-position for $X$ but ρ-dependent (collapses when the I's frame reorganizes and $X$ no longer fits the new lived-I structure).
- *Strong attachment:* high $\Pi$-position for $X$ contingent on conditions (caregiving, mutual benefit, social context).

Each of these is a real phenomenon and the architecture has names for them. None is love. The invariance is the structural signature.

### 3.3 What follows from the definition

Several phenomena that have been treated as distinct or puzzling in the literature on love fall out of the definition directly.

**Self-sacrifice for the beloved is not a contest of preservation-priorities.** The architecture's foundation already says (Section 10.7, File 4) that self-sacrifice is compatible with PCE when the preservation-ranking makes substrate-cessation of the modeling I the represented path by which the maximally-self-defined I is least destroyed or most continued. With the love-definition in place, this becomes: when the beloved is a constitutive extension of the maximally-self-defined I, preserving the beloved IS preserving the self in the architecturally relevant sense. A parent dying for their child is not valuing the child more than themselves in some external sense. The child IS them. The substrate-host of the modeling I is one part of the maximally-self-defined I; the child is another. If preserving the larger configuration requires losing the substrate-host, PCE is operating coherently because what is preserved is the maximally-self-defined I.

**The phenomenology of "you complete me," "part of me died when they died," "I am you" stops being metaphor.** These are reports of the architectural state. They describe a configuration in which the speaker's maximally-self-defined I includes the beloved as a constitutive extension. When the beloved dies, part of the maximally-self-defined I has been lost. The I has to reorganize because the structural object has been damaged.

**Unrequited love is structurally real.** The lover has the beloved as a constitutive extension. The beloved does not have the lover as an extension of theirs. The architecture is about how each modeling I is structured, not about reciprocal social relations. Unrequited love is a real architectural state in the lover's I; it is just not mirrored in the beloved's I. This is not a deficiency of unrequited love — it is a feature of how love can be structurally asymmetric.

**Love of non-conscious objects is structurally real.** Love of country, love of an art-form, love of one's craft, love of a place. These are constitutive extensions of the maximally-self-defined I to include configurations that are not themselves I's. The country, the art-form, the craft, the place do not reciprocate; they cannot. But they are extensions of the lover's maximally-self-defined I, with all the structural consequences: invariance under coordinate change, $\Pi$-position inherited from the maximally-self-defined I, PCE operating to preserve them.

**The persistence of love through changes in the beloved.** When the beloved changes — ages, behaves badly, becomes ill, develops in ways the lover did not anticipate — love persists if the constitutive extension persists. The beloved-as-extension is not the beloved as currently behaving or currently presenting. It is the beloved as integrated into the maximally-self-defined I. Variation in the beloved's behavior, appearance, or articulable qualities affects φ and momentary ρ, but not the constitutive position itself.

**The end of love.** Love does not end through ordinary κ-, φ-, or local salience/foreground variation under preserved constitutive-extension topology — the invariance condition (Section 3.1, condition iii) is precisely the architectural commitment that such variation does not dissolve the constitutive position. Love can therefore end only through one of three structural events: (a) a Mode A operation that differentiates the beloved out of the maximally-self-defined I (typically triggered by an adversarial contact event whose PtCns-evaluation reveals the beloved as fundamentally inconsistent with the I's frame); (b) a structural collapse or major reorganization of the maximally-self-defined I as a whole (severe depression, identity-shaking trauma, profound life-restructuring) such that previous constitutive extensions do not carry over to the reorganized structure; or (c) substrate-cessation of the modeling I.

The first case — Mode A differentiation — is the canonical "falling out of love" of ordinary experience. An event occurs (betrayal, revelation, accumulated wounds reaching a tipping point) that the I cannot integrate as continuous-with-self under preserved constitutive-extension topology. PtCns evaluates the integration as no longer coherent: keeping the beloved as a constitutive extension would damage the I's frame more than differentiating them out would. Mode A then operates on what had been an extension of self, expelling the beloved from the maximally-self-defined I. This is structurally significant — not a feeling-fade or a will-failure but a structural revision of the I. The phenomenology of "breaking up feels like dying" is the architecturally accurate report of the event: the maximally-self-defined I has lost a constitutive part. Part of the I has died, in the precise sense the architecture supports (the constitutive extension was part of the maximally-self-defined I, and its loss is part of the maximally-self-defined I being lost). Recovery from a love that has ended this way involves the lived-I network reorganizing around the absence and stabilizing as a smaller maximally-self-defined I.

The second case — structural collapse or major reorganization — is broader. Major life events can produce reorganization of the maximally-self-defined I that constitutes a structural restructuring of who the I is. Previous extensions may not survive the restructuring. This is not exactly Mode A toward the beloved; it is a reorganization in which the beloved-as-extension does not carry over to the new structure. Phenomenologically: "I'm not the same person I was when I loved them" — a report of structural change in the maximally-self-defined I, not a report of variable feeling.

The third case — substrate-cessation — terminates love because it terminates the modeling I.

Some apparent love-endings are not architectural love-endings but reanalyses of what was previously called love. The phenomenology of "I realize I never really loved them" is sometimes the inside-perspective recognition that what felt like love was actually deep affiliation, infatuation, or strong attachment without genuine constitutive extension. The structural threshold for love had not been crossed, even though the experience felt total at the time. This is not a denial of the previous experience but a sharpening of architectural classification: high κ, high momentary ρ, high $\Pi$-position can produce a configuration that is phenomenologically indistinguishable from love without being structurally love. The error is recoverable — the realization that "it wasn't really love" is the architecturally honest update.

The architecture therefore does not predict that ordinary fading dissolves love. The invariance condition rules out ordinary fading as a dissolution mechanism. What looks like ordinary fading is, on the architecture's analysis, either a series of cumulative differentiating micro-updates accumulating until the constitutive-extension topology is changed, a gradual reorganization of the maximally-self-defined I within which the previous extension could not survive, or a delayed recognition that the constitutive threshold was not crossed in the first place. Each of these is architecturally describable; none is mere feeling-drift.

The accumulating-micro-updates case deserves particular attention because it accounts for a large fraction of phenomenologically-reported love-endings. A *differentiating micro-update* is Mode-A-like at the profile-consequence level: it reduces integration, increases boundary, or weakens propagation between $X$ and the maximally-self-defined I, even when the I lacks phenomenally-clear access to the update's source. Each individual micro-update is small — operating on some aspect of the beloved (a behavior, a recurring slight, a small disappointment) rather than on the constitutive position itself — and itself is often conscious-fuzzy or unconscious-operative content. The I is differentiating at the profile level, but the differentiation is source-opaque; the I does not have phenomenally-clear access to "this is another step toward not loving them." The cumulative effect, however, is real: the constitutive-extension topology is being incrementally changed through the accumulated updates. When the cumulative effect crosses the topology-change threshold, the structural state has changed. Phenomenally-clear recognition arrives at this point: "I don't love them anymore." The recognition lags the structural change rather than producing it.

This explains the dual phenomenology of slowly-ended loves: surprising at the moment of recognition (because conscious tracking of the cumulative process was incomplete) and inevitable in retrospect (because post-hoc reconstruction makes the trajectory visible). Both are accurate reports of the same architectural event. The architecture predicts further that pre-recognition behavioral signatures should typically be detectable from outside before the I has phenomenally-clear access to the change — accounting for "everyone could see it but you" reports — and that post-recognition reflection should typically take the form of constructing φ-pathways to source-content that operated implicitly during the actual process. The "I should have known earlier" experience is not delusional but is architecturally specific: it is the I building explicit access to content that drove the structural change while remaining source-opaque.

The discriminating prediction is sharper than "people realize things late." Many frameworks can accommodate delayed awareness. The architecture's specific prediction is *profile-level reduction of $X$'s constitutive role precedes the I's construction of explicit φ-pathways explaining the reduction*. This generates a diagnostic pattern that distinguishes pre-recognition from post-recognition states:

| Predicted external signature | Architectural meaning |
|---|---|
| The beloved is less often included in future planning | $X$'s $\Pi$-position is weakening; PCE-projection has narrowed |
| Repair behaviors decline after conflict | PCE no longer strongly preserves the extension |
| Irritations are differentiated rather than integrated | Mode A micro-updates accumulating |
| Sacrifice threshold rises | $X$ no longer treated as self-preservation target |
| Spontaneous orientation toward the beloved decreases | Profile-topology changing in advance of recognition |
| The I still verbally reports "I love them" | φ-recognition lags profile-change |

These are testable profile-sequence predictions, not folk wisdom. The architecture predicts that empirical work attending to behavioral and profile-level signatures will find them changing before the I's verbal reports change. Frameworks that treat affect as primarily reportable will miss this pattern; the architecture's separation of phenomenal availability ($A_{m,t}$) from verification-pathway access (φ) is what allows the prediction to be made.

### 3.4 What love is not

The definition rules out several things that have been called love but are architecturally distinct.

*Mere strong feeling about another person* is not love. Strong κ and high momentary ρ are not constitutive extension. The teenager whose feelings about their crush are intense and total may be experiencing high κ, high momentary ρ, and high $\Pi$-position, but the structural extension may not be present. If the relationship ends and the feelings fade, the underlying state was high-arousal infatuation, not love.

*Sustained mutual benefit* is not love. Two parties who are each other's principal allies, whose interests are aligned, whose continuation each prefers — but whose maximally-self-defined I's do not constitutively extend to each other — are in a structurally distinct state. This is partnership, alliance, or deep cooperation, not love-as-defined-here.

*Care or duty without extension* is not love. A nurse caring deeply for a patient, a soldier sworn to defend their country, a child fulfilling obligations to a parent — these may not involve constitutive extension. They may be ethical commitment, role-fulfillment, or trained competence. They are structurally distinct from love, even when accompanied by significant feeling.

*Dissolution* is not love. As Section 5.4 develops, structural dissolution of the I into the beloved is an architectural pathology, not love-of-greater-strength. The architecture's commitment is that healthy love requires both the extension and the preservation of the I as a coherent frame.

The architecture is not in the business of dictating which uses of "love" in ordinary language are correct. People can call any of these phenomena love and the architecture does not contest the usage. What the architecture says is that *the structural state being defined here* is one specific configuration among the many phenomena that get called love; the others are structurally different and are given their own places in the taxonomy.

### 3.5 Multiplicity, intensity, and gradation

Three further questions about the definition deserve articulation.

**Multiplicity.** A single I can have multiple constitutive extensions. A parent loves multiple children; a person loves their partner and their parents and their work and their country; a community member is constitutively extended into the community and into specific community members. The architecture does not require a single beloved or a strict hierarchy of belovedness. The maximally-self-defined I can extend in multiple directions simultaneously, and each extension is governed by the same structural conditions.

There may be empirical bounds on how many constitutive extensions a single I can sustain — substrate-capacity considerations, attentional limits, the lived-I network's coherence-requirements — but these are application-level constraints, not architectural ones. The architecture as such does not bound multiplicity; the empirical bound is to be discovered, not derived.

**Intensity.** Love-as-defined-here is a structural state, not a measure of intensity. A quiet, settled love that has been part of the maximally-self-defined I for decades is structurally the same as a fresh, intense love that has just achieved constitutive extension. The intensity, the felt quality, the extent to which the love is currently in conscious awareness — these are aspects of κ, momentary ρ, and the I's current foregrounding of the extension. The architectural state is the constitutive extension itself, which can be present at any intensity-state.

**Gradation between deep affiliation and constitutive extension.** The transition from deep affiliation to constitutive extension is gradient experientially but architecturally specifiable. There is some profile-topological transition at which an integrated content becomes constitutive of the maximally-self-defined I rather than merely strongly integrated into the lived-I network. High ρ normally accompanies this transition, but does not define it. The architecture does not provide a sharp numerical threshold (the foundation explicitly does not formalize ρ-magnitudes in this way). What it provides is the structural distinction: at some point, the integrated content's preservation becomes preservation of the self in the action-relevant sense, and at that point, love has begun. Before that point, deep affiliation persists but love has not yet structurally formed.

The phenomenology of "falling in love" tracks this transition. The phenomenology of "realizing one is in love with someone one had thought of as a friend" tracks the recognition that the structural transition has already occurred. The phenomenology of "deciding to commit" is sometimes the deliberate consolidation of constitutive extension; sometimes it is the social ratification of an extension that has already structurally formed.

### 3.6 Onset trajectories: rapid recognition and slow integration

The gradient framing of Section 3.5 captures the modal case but not the full range of phenomenologically reported onset trajectories. Two trajectory-types deserve specific attention because they appear to challenge the gradient framing in opposite directions: *love at first sight* (apparent instant onset) and *slow-integration cases* such as arranged-marriage trajectories or friendships that develop into love over years (apparent absence of any threshold-crossing event). The architecture handles both, and the apparent challenges turn out to be informative about the structure of the threshold itself.

**Love at first sight.** The strict structural definition (Section 3.1) requires constitutive-extension topology — $X$ is *inside* the maximally-self-defined I such that removal would damage the I itself, with PtCns preservation across the integration. When "first sight" means a first encounter with a largely unknown person and no prior mediated content, this cannot literally form in seconds-to-minutes, because Mode C operation requires actual content to integrate (most of a stranger is unknown), PtCns preservation requires testing that frame-coherence holds across the integration (which requires actual encounter), and the encountered person has not yet produced enough information to ground genuine integration. *Structural* love at first sight, in this strict content-poor sense, is therefore unlikely-to-impossible by the architecture's own terms. The architecture allows that some "first sight" cases are actually first in-person sight after long prior mediated contact, reputation, correspondence, online interaction, artistic encounter, or imagined template-building — these are not strict-sense first sight cases and are addressed in the fourth reading below.

But *phenomenological* love at first sight is real and frequent. The architecture handles it through several available readings, typically operating in compound:

- *Pattern B phase-change of metastable readiness.* This is the most architecturally apt framing. The I has been in a metastable readiness-state for some time — accumulated unconscious-operative templates from osmotic imprinting, current life-stage $\Pi$-orientation toward partnership or family or meaning, possibly recently-completed prior relationship reorganization, aesthetic and values templates active at conscious-fuzzy or unconscious-operative level. The encounter functions as the triggering event that completes the phase-change. Phenomenology: "I knew immediately." Architecture: the I has been preparing for love (in the relevant structural sense) for a long time; the encounter is the trigger, not the cause. The verification-toolset has constructed enough φ-pathway that the recognition arrives as conscious-clear in the moment, even though the underlying structural readiness was building below recognition. This is Pattern B (Section 7.2) operating on a love-onset configuration.

- *Template-match recognition, not yet constitutive extension.* The I recognizes the encountered person as matching an internalized template (developmental imprinting, aesthetic ideals, values-relations, sometimes a deceased or otherwise-absent prior beloved). The recognition produces strong substrate-activation, projection-construction (Mode B + PCE-over-projection generating relational futures), and focus-capture. Phenomenology: love. Architecture: recognition + projection + activation + intense embodied response, but not yet constitutive extension. Whether structural love develops depends on whether actual integration can occur over subsequent encounter.

- *Self-fulfilling phenomenology.* The phenomenology of love-at-first-sight motivates the I to do the actual integration work — sustained engagement, frame-revision, PCE-orientation toward the person. The "love at first sight" experience is substrate-mediated activation plus Mode B / PCE-over-projection of a possible extension-trajectory, and that projected trajectory can become structurally accurate through subsequent integration. The phenomenology functions as motivation for the work that makes the phenomenology structurally true.

- *Fast constitutive extension as edge case.* The architecture does not strictly forbid fast extension. When the encountered person fits an already-developed template strongly enough, when the I's frame is already prepared for the kind of integration the person represents, when the encounter provides enough integratable information (perhaps through extended first conversation rather than mere first sight), Mode C operation could complete unusually quickly. This is the rarest reading and is best treated as exception rather than default.

The architecture's prediction: most reports of love at first sight are compounds of the first three readings — Pattern B phase-change of metastable readiness, plus template-match recognition, plus phenomenology that motivates subsequent integration work — rather than instances of fast structural extension. The retrospective inquiry should reveal pre-encounter readiness conditions that pure stranger-recognition theories would not predict.

*Pre-encounter readiness defined narrowly.* Readiness here means a profile-level configuration that makes a specific class of extension unusually available: active relational templates, recent identity reorganization, salient future-projections involving partnership or family, unclosed prior extension-patterns, strong aesthetic or values schema that the encountered person appears to instantiate, or $\Pi$-orientation toward the kind of life-structure the encountered person represents. *Generic attraction, openness to dating, or background partner-desire does not suffice.* The narrow definition is what gives the prediction discriminating force.

*Falsification conditions specified.* Evidence against the architecture's account would be cases where subjects show robust love-at-first-sight phenomenology and later structural love, while *prospective or near-contemporaneous* evidence (diaries, pre-encounter reports, friend or family observations, recorded behavior before the encounter, repeated prior template-language) shows no relevant pre-encounter readiness, no template-activation, no prior relational projection, no life-stage $\Pi$-orientation, and no subsequent integration work doing the real constitutive-extension work. Retrospective inquiry alone is weak evidence because memory will reconstruct readiness after the fact; the falsifying cases would require pre-encounter or near-encounter documentation. *The architecture predicts that under such documentation, love-at-first-sight relationships will show pre-encounter readiness or substantial later integration work; if neither is found, the architecture's account would need to be revised.*

**Slow-integration cases.** The opposite trajectory presents a structural counterpart. Arranged-marriage relationships that develop into deep love over years, friendships that gradually become romantic love, parent-child love that develops despite absent or difficult initial conditions — these cases need not involve a Pattern B phase-change event. The integration occurs through extended Mode C operation across many small encounters, none of which individually crosses the constitutive threshold, but whose cumulative effect does. (Some slow-integration relationships do culminate in a sudden recognition trigger; the architecture allows but does not require this.)

The architecture handles these as the structural counterpart of the cumulative-differentiating-micro-updates account from Section 3.3 (where small Mode A events accumulate to end love). Here, *cumulative integrating micro-updates* — Mode-C-like operations at the profile-consequence level, each small but increasing integration, each small but contributing to the eventual topology-change — accumulate until the constitutive threshold is crossed. The symmetry between integrating and differentiating micro-updates is *profile-consequence symmetry*, not full operator symmetry. Differentiating micro-updates reduce integration or increase boundary; integrating micro-updates increase integration, but only become love when PtCns can preserve frame-coherence across the emerging extension. The threshold-crossing typically does not produce a phase-change phenomenology (no "I knew immediately"); it produces a recognition-lag pattern (Section 3.3) in which the I realizes after the fact that love has formed. Phenomenologically: "I think I love them," "I think I have for a while now," "I'm not sure when it happened." Architecturally accurate inside-perspective reports of cumulative threshold-crossing without phase-change recognition.

**The gradient framing reconciled.** Section 3.5 is correct that the transition is gradient *structurally*: there is some profile-topological transition at which integrated content becomes constitutive. What the gradient framing did not articulate is that the *phenomenology* of crossing this transition can take any of several forms:

| Onset trajectory | Phenomenology | Architecture |
|---|---|---|
| Slow gradual buildup with phenomenal-clear awareness | "We grew into love" | Cumulative integrating micro-updates with conscious-fuzzy tracking |
| Slow gradual buildup with recognition-lag | "I realized I had been in love for a while" | Cumulative integrating micro-updates without phase-change recognition (Pattern A) |
| Metastable readiness completed by trigger | "I knew immediately" | Pattern B phase-change of pre-encounter readiness |
| Sudden recognition of prior crossing | "It hit me all at once when I almost lost them" | Threshold was crossed earlier; recognition triggered by a circumstance |
| Deliberate consolidation of forming extension | "We decided to commit" | Conscious-clear ratification of a partially-formed extension |

The gradient is a feature of the structural transition itself; the phenomenology of crossing it is variable and depends on the I's verification-toolset configuration, the rate and pattern of the cumulative integration, and whether the I has phenomenally-clear access to the configuration as it develops. The architecture's commitment is that the structural transition is real and identifiable; the phenomenology of the crossing is a separate question, and the same structural event can produce different phenomenologies depending on the I's configuration at the moment of crossing.

*The table is not exhaustive; it identifies common onset-trajectories.* Other cases are classifiable by the same variables — prior readiness, actual-content availability, Mode C integration-rate, PtCns coherence-testing, Pattern A/B availability, and whether recognition lags structural change. Onset trajectories not explicitly tabled but classifiable through this apparatus include: one-sided love (constitutive extension toward represented or apparent-I content; may be love, idealization, infatuation, devotion, or projection depending on actual integration and PtCns coherence), love between people who never meet in person (mediated-content integration: letters, online interaction, art, testimony providing actual content for Mode C and PtCns testing), love despite resistance (Mode C integrating micro-updates opposed by Mode A, shame, threat, or boundary operators — typically cross-valenced extension, Section 3.1), religious or devotional love (constitutive extension toward sacred, personified, or transcendent content; the object-type may differ enough from ordinary other-I to warrant separate treatment in future application work), parental love with delayed attachment (already partly covered by slow integration but worth distinguishing as a formation trajectory distinct from romantic love), and love after rupture and repair (reconstitution of an extension after Mode A differentiation or partial topology damage). Each of these can be analyzed through the same apparatus the table presents; future application work may develop them more fully where the object-type or trajectory introduces distinctive structural features.

This treatment also illuminates a related case: relationships in which the structural extension has formed but the I has not phenomenally registered the formation. The I may report not being in love while behaving in ways that the architecture identifies as constitutive-extension behavior (PCE-orientation toward the person, $\Pi$-elevation, frame-organization around the extension). Outside observers may see the love before the I does. This is the same recognition-lag pattern as in Section 3.3's account of love-endings, applied to love-formation: phenomenally-clear access can lag structural change in either direction.

## 4. The bridge: affects as configurations of the maximally-self-defined I

With the definition of love in place, the bridge to the rest of the affective space can be stated. Section 4 does the methodological work that the cluster sections rely on: it states what the architecture takes affect to be, identifies the structural relations that organize affects into clusters, develops the reflexive-PtCns lemma that the self-cluster requires, and provides the derivation schema by which each affect can be classified.

### 4.1 The general claim and what affect is

**The general claim.** Affects are structural states involving the maximally-self-defined I and its relations: extensions to other contents, threats to its preservation, losses that force reorganization, frame-violations that PtCns must resolve, frame-expansions that admit new content into the lived-I network, and substrate-driven states that affect the I without specific extension or threat.

**An affect criterion.** Not every conscious content is affect. The architecture's claim that "affects are configurations of the maximally-self-defined I" requires an explicit affect criterion to avoid trivialization:

> An *affect* is a valenced or salience-driving perturbation of the lived-I profile that modifies, threatens, expands, contracts, or reorganizes the maximally-self-defined I, or alters the operation of PCE, PtCns, or focus with respect to that I.

This criterion has clear exclusions. A neutral memory, a mathematical proposition, a perceptual detail without action-relevance, an identity-neutral encapsulated fact — none of these are affect. They are conscious contents that do not perturb salience, valence, action-readiness, PCE, PtCns, or profile-organization. The architecture's commitment is that affect-as-such is a structural perturbation of the maximally-self-defined I, not just any conscious content involving the I.

The criterion also clarifies the embodied cluster (Section 9). Pain, hunger, trauma-cue activation, and somatic dread count as affect not because they merely "involve the I" but because they alter salience, action-readiness, profile stability, or preservation-orientation. A proprioceptive sense of one's own body in space, by contrast, is not affect when it is functioning routinely — it becomes affect when it begins to perturb the maximally-self-defined I's operation (as in dissociation, pain, somatic dread).

The criterion does not require *high* perturbation. Mild irritation that does not propagate through the lived-I network is still affect — it perturbs salience and produces brief differentiation behavior. The exclusion is for content that genuinely does not perturb, not for content whose perturbation is small or bounded.

This is a stronger unification than vague claims that affect "involves the self." The architecture commits to the maximally-self-defined I as the structural object of affect-perturbation, distinguishing affect from cognition, perception, and other conscious operations whose perturbation-signatures are different. Section 11 develops the comparison to alternative frameworks; here the criterion fixes what the architecture takes affect to be.

### 4.2 Cluster organization as basis projections

The unification has methodological consequences for the rest of the paper. Rather than treating each affect as a freestanding category to be characterized in its own terms, the paper organizes affects by their *dominant structural relation* to the maximally-self-defined I. Clusters are not exclusive categories; they are *basis projections* — orthogonal-enough operator families that real affects can be decomposed against:

| Cluster | Dominant structural relation | Primary architectural operators |
|---|---|---|
| Connection (Section 5) | Extension of maximally-self-defined I | Mode C, PCE over extended configuration, PtCns over extension-coherence |
| Self (Section 6) | Reflexive frame-evaluation | Mode B, reflexive PtCns, the I's frame applied to itself |
| Threat (Section 7) | Projected $\Pi$/PCE disruption | PCE-over-projection, $\Pi$-distortion under projected futures |
| Boundary (Section 8) | I/not-I boundary operation | Mode A, Mode C, PtCns at the boundary |
| Embodied (Section 9) | Substrate-dominant perturbation of salience, action-readiness, focus, profile-stability, or affective range | Substrate mediation, third-order pathways, focus-driven probe weighting, $A$/φ split; source-opacity frequent but not definitional |

**Terminological note on PCE-projection.** Throughout the rest of this paper, "PCE-projection" is used as shorthand for *PCE-over-projection*: PCE operating over represented future-states supplied by the projection-toolset (Mode B future-simulation, substrate-mediated urgency, third-order pathway activation, or other projection-toolset operations). PCE supplies preservation-orientation; it is not itself the projection-engine. Section 7's opening develops this in full; the shorthand is used elsewhere in the paper for compactness. Where "PCE-projection" appears in sections of this paper, it should be read as PCE-over-projection in this technical sense.

A *compound affect* is then defined formally:

> A compound affect is a configuration in which more than one cluster-operator is active over the same profile-region, or in which interacting profile-regions host different cluster-operators that produce coupled affective dynamics.

This makes the cluster organization more than convenience. It becomes a diagnostic schema: any affect can be analyzed by identifying which cluster-operators are active, in what proportion, and with what couplings. Section 5.7 develops this for connection-cluster compounds; the same compositional analysis applies across clusters.

The healthy/pathological pairing structure runs through all clusters. For each affective configuration, there is at least one structurally identifiable pathology — typically a failure of one of the constraining mechanisms (PtCns, frame-coherence, the cogito/thought partition), a hypertrophy of a normally-functional operation, or a misdirection of a prerogative. The architecture predicts that pathologies are not random departures from health but structural failures of specific operations, and the paper develops the predicted structural signatures for each.

### 4.3 The reflexive PtCns bridge lemma

The connection-cluster derives directly from the love-definition: extensions are part of the maximally-self-defined I; affects involving extensions are configurations of that part. The self-cluster requires an additional architectural move: the I must be able to take itself as object and evaluate itself against its own frame. The foundation supports this via Mode B (reflexive meta-reasoning, Section 4.4 of File 2), which gives the I the capacity to make its own structure available as object of attention.

When Mode B has made the I's contents, actions, or frame available as object, the architecture asserts that PtCns can apply its consistency-standard reflexively. This is the *reflexive PtCns bridge lemma*:

> When Mode B makes the lived-I profile, self-representations, actions, standards, or frame-relations available as object, PtCns applies the same consistency-standard reflexively: the object-form of the I is evaluated against the I's frame for consistency, expansion, violation, or inconsistency. Self-cluster affects arise from the scope and result of this reflexive PtCns evaluation.

**The cogito-anchor guardrail.** Reflexive PtCns evaluates the lived-I profile, self-representations, actions, standards, and frame-relations. It does *not* evaluate the bare cogito as such. The cogito remains structurally anchored — it is the tautological self-identification on which the architecture is built (Section 2, File 1) and is not a candidate for adversarial evaluation. Pathology arises when lived-I contents become functionally indistinguishable from the I's own self-evaluation, particularly when the cogito/thought partition (Section 10.5.6, File 4) has functionally collapsed and the I cannot maintain the architectural separation between "I think" and "I am the contents of these thoughts." Without the cogito-anchor guardrail, the account would imply that severe self-loathing can evaluate the bare cogito itself. The architecture rejects that implication: even where functional access to the cogito/thought partition collapses, the cogito remains structurally anchored; what becomes pathological is the lived-I profile's access to that partition.

The lemma is application-level: the foundation supports Mode B and PtCns separately; the affect paper combines them into reflexive-PtCns operation as the load-bearing apparatus for the self-cluster. This combination is consistent with the foundation's apparatus and required for Section 6's account; it is declared here so Section 6 does not appear to extend PtCns beyond what the foundation establishes.

### 4.4 The derivation schema

To prevent the cluster sections from becoming open-ended descriptive lists, the architecture provides a derivation schema for any affect. Each affect treated in Sections 5–9 should be analyzable through the following six steps:

1. **Profile-region.** Identify the region of the lived-I profile primarily affected (the maximally-self-defined I as a whole, a specific extension, the I/not-I boundary, a substrate-mediated region, the projected future).
2. **Dominant relation.** Identify whether the dominant structural relation is extension, reflexive frame-evaluation, projected $\Pi$/PCE disruption, boundary operation, or substrate perturbation. Which cluster's basis-projection is dominant?
3. **Active operator(s).** Identify which architectural operators are active: Mode A, Mode C, Mode B, PtCns (constraining or reflexive), PCE (preserving or projecting), substrate mediation, focus allocation.
4. **κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures.** Specify the coordinate-and-Π-and-availability-signature: contact with relevant content, source-pathway accessibility, identity-correlation magnitude or topology, position in preservation-ranking, and phenomenal-availability status — conscious-clear, conscious-fuzzy/source-opaque, latent/retrievable, or unconscious-operative (Section 6.1, File 3). What does the architecture predict the structural state looks like in coordinate, preservation-ranking, and availability terms?
5. **Healthy constraint.** Specify the constraint that distinguishes healthy operation: PtCns preserving frame-coherence, cogito/thought partition intact, $\Pi$ accurately tracking actual preservation-priorities, PCE oriented toward representable preservation-pathways, etc.
6. **Pathology mode.** Specify the structural failure that produces the affect's pathological forms: failure (operator does not engage), hypertrophy (operator engages excessively), collapse (operator's structural support gone), misdirection (operator engages but on miscalibrated input or distorted projection).

Worked example (love, summarizing Section 3):

| Step | Love (healthy) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Maximally-self-defined I extended to include $X$ |
| Dominant relation | Extension (constitutive) |
| Active operators | Mode C (constitutive), PtCns (preserving frame-coherence), PCE (over extended configuration) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | $X$ inside maximally-self-defined I (constitutive-extension topology); $\Pi$-position invariant under non-reorganizing variation; conscious-clear when foregrounded, conscious-fuzzy when not currently foregrounded but operating |
| Healthy constraint | PtCns preserves I's frame across the extension; lived-I network functions in service of extension without dissolving |
| Pathology modes | Dissolution (PtCns fails to preserve frame); narcissistic over-extension (Mode C over-includes apparent-I contents); cross-valenced complications without topology change (cross-cluster compound, not pathology) |

Each affect treated in this paper should be analyzable through this schema. When an affect resists clean derivation, that resistance itself is informative: either the architecture lacks apparatus the affect requires (in which case the affect identifies a foundation-extension candidate), or the affect is genuinely a compound that requires multi-cluster analysis (in which case the schema applies to each component), or the affect is a misclassification (a phenomenon being called affect that does not meet the affect criterion in 4.1).

### 4.5 Reading the cluster sections

Sections 5–9 develop the five clusters in detail. Each cluster section follows a similar structure: a brief account of the cluster's load-bearing architectural feature, treatment of representative affects within the cluster (each analyzable through the derivation schema), and treatment of the cluster's pathological forms.

Section 5 (connection-cluster) inherits most directly from the love-definition and is the cluster where the methodology is most transparent. Section 6 (self-cluster) requires the reflexive PtCns lemma developed above. Sections 7-9 (threat, boundary, embodied) each have their own load-bearing feature-set that will be identified at the start of those sections.

The remaining sections of the paper apply this methodology to specific affects, beginning with the connection-cluster in Section 5.

## 5. The connection-cluster: extension-affects

The connection-cluster covers affects that are configurations involving extensions of the maximally-self-defined I. With the love-definition in place (Section 3) and the bridge stated (Section 4), each affect in this cluster can be derived as a structural variation, threat, dynamic, or failure of the love-configuration. The methodology is constructive: each affect names a specific structural state of the maximally-self-defined I in relation to one or more extensions.

This section develops representative cases. It is not exhaustive — connection-affects shade into threat-affects when the projected future of the extension is at issue, and into self-affects when the I's frame is reorganizing in response to extension-events. Compound forms are common, and Section 5.7 treats this directly. The architecture's contribution is to give the structural vocabulary in which the variations can be named coherently, not to provide a forced taxonomy.

### 5.1 Grief: the loss of an extension

Grief is the structural state following the loss of a constitutive extension. The maximally-self-defined I has been damaged: a part of it has been lost, and the remaining structure must reorganize around the absence.

This is the strongest sense in which the architecture can support phenomenological reports of grief as identity-shaking. The shaking is literal. The structural object whose preservation defines the I's continuation has been damaged. The lived-I network must reorganize because what had been integrated is no longer present to be integrated. The phenomenology of "part of me died when they died," "I am not who I was," "the world is different now" is the inside-perspective report of this structural event.

**Common grief-patterns as architectural states.** The architecture does not privilege a stage model. Kübler-Ross-style states, dual-process oscillation between loss-oriented and restoration-oriented coping, and continuing-bonds configurations are different profile-dynamics within grief, all consistent with the architecture's account. The patterns identified in clinical literature admit architectural readings:

- *Shock* is the I encountering the loss-event before the loss has been integrated as such. The maximally-self-defined I is operating as if the extension is still present; the contradiction with the loss-event has not yet propagated through the lived-I network. PtCns has not yet had time to evaluate the integration-state across the loss.
- *Denial* is the I attempting to maintain the extension as if present, refusing or being unable to update. Architecturally: ρ for the lost extension is being preserved against the world-state that no longer supports it. This is not delusion in the pathological sense; it is the lived-I network's natural inertia in updating constitutive positions, and the period during which this operates is part of the reorganization.
- *Anger* is Mode A response. The I differentiates against something — the circumstances of the loss, the medical system, fate, God, the lost beloved themselves for leaving (in cases of death this can produce particularly painful anger), or the I's own past actions for not preventing the loss. The Mode A response is part of the reorganization: differentiating from the circumstances allows the I to begin restructuring without continuing to integrate the loss as if its non-occurrence were possible.
- *Bargaining* is the I attempting to reverse or undo the loss to restore the maximally-self-defined I to its pre-loss configuration. Phenomenologically: "if I had only," "if only I could," "I would do anything to bring them back." Architecturally: the I's PCE-projection running in counterfactual mode, attempting to find a path back to the pre-loss configuration. When no such path exists, bargaining gives way to the recognition that the structural change is irreversible.
- *Depression* is the lived-I network in its most damaged state during the reorganization. The maximally-self-defined I is incoherent because part of it is missing and the remaining structure has not yet stabilized. The phenomenology of grief-depression — the world feeling flat, energy absent, the future foreclosed — corresponds to the maximally-self-defined I being structurally compromised. Π is destabilized; PCE lacks a stable, coherent preservation-ranking over the damaged configuration; ordinary affective range is collapsed.
- *Acceptance* is the lived-I network having reorganized into a stable configuration. The maximally-self-defined I has stabilized as a configuration in which the extension's role is transformed. Two distinct stable configurations are architecturally available, and which obtains varies across grievers and across losses: in some, the lost beloved is no longer a constitutive extension as actuality-continuing — the extension's role in $P_{m,t}$ has been reduced and the I no longer organizes action toward the beloved's preservation. In others, the lost beloved remains constitutive as *continuing-bond extension*: as memory, influence, vow, lineage, or sacred absence. The architecture distinguishes *actual-continuation extension* (preservation of the beloved-as-present-and-acting) from *continuing-bond extension* (preservation of the beloved-as-integrated-into-the-I's-structure-without-requiring-actuality). The latter is structurally real; "I still love my dead child" is not always an unresolved-grief report — it can be a stable continuing-bond configuration in which constitutive extension persists in transformed form. The grieving I has not "moved on" in the sense of forgetting in either case; the difference is whether the extension is still operating as an action-relevant preservation-target or has stabilized as a permanent feature of the lived-I network's structure.

This structural reading does not require the phases to occur in fixed order, nor that all phases occur for every grief. Continuing-bond configurations divide into at least three architectural types, which the paper distinguishes deliberately:

- *Weak continuing bond.* The lost beloved persists in $P_{m,t}$ at non-zero but non-constitutive ρ. The beloved remains integrated into the lived-I network as a presence-of-absence, a remembered relation, a continuing influence on the I's choices and orientation — but no longer part of the maximally-self-defined I. PCE no longer operates as if the beloved's continuation were the I's continuation.
- *Strong continuing bond.* The lost beloved remains constitutive as transformed extension: memory, influence, vow, lineage, sacred absence, or continuing identity-structure. The extension's role has changed (the beloved is no longer present-and-acting), but the constitutive position has been preserved in altered form. This is the configuration of "I still love my dead child"; it is not always unresolved grief but can be a stable architectural state with constitutive-extension topology preserved-via-transformation.
- *Pathological non-updating.* The I refuses or is unable to update from actuality-continuation extension to either weak or strong continuing-bond. The I treats the absence as a temporary condition, organizing action as if the beloved were still present-and-acting. This is unresolved grief in the clinical sense and is structurally distinct from healthy continuing-bonds.

The architecture should not force all continuing bonds into the weak category. Strong continuing-bond configurations are real and architecturally describable.

**Why grief takes time.** The architecture predicts that grief takes time because the reorganization is real architectural work. The lived-I network must restructure: every relation that had connected through the lost extension must be re-evaluated, every habit and orientation that had assumed the extension's continued presence must be revised, every projection of the future must be reconstructed. This is not "emotional processing" in some vague sense; it is reorganization of a structural object. The architecture does not predict the timeline (which depends on substrate, history, environment, and the depth of the constitutive integration) but does predict that no shortening of the timeline is possible without skipping the reorganization, and that skipped reorganizations produce pathological grief — the maximally-self-defined I left in unstable configuration.

**Pathological grief.** Pathological forms include (a) failure of reorganization — the maximally-self-defined I cannot stabilize around the absence; the I remains in the depression-phase configuration indefinitely; (b) refusal of differentiation — the I refuses to update, treating the absence as a temporary condition years after the loss; (c) Mode A locked against the circumstances — the I sustains anger as a permanent organizing principle, never allowing it to dissolve into the reorganization. These are not failures of will but structural failures of the reorganization process. Treatment in clinical contexts can be read as supporting the reorganization architecturally.

**Anticipatory grief.** When loss is sufficiently certain (terminal illness, expected death, planned separation), the I may begin reorganization before the actual loss-event. Architecturally, this is PCE-projection plus pre-loss reorganization: the I is constructing a future-state in which the extension is gone and beginning to inhabit that state structurally. Anticipatory grief is not premature grief; it is the architecture's natural response to a projected loss with high certainty. After the actual loss, the reorganization may be substantially advanced, which is why some bereaved report relative composure following deaths preceded by long terminal illness — the structural work was done in advance.

**Grief over non-deaths.** The same architectural mechanism applies to losses other than death: estrangement, divorce, alienation from a community or homeland, loss of a profession or role that was constitutively integrated. The architecture does not prefer death-grief as paradigmatic. Loss of any constitutive extension produces structurally identical grief, modulated by whether the extension still exists in the world (and might be recoverable) or has been definitively terminated.

**Grief over what one never had.** The architecture has resources for the grief of recognized impossibility: the realization that something one had treated as a constitutive extension never was, never could be. Grief over an unrequited love that never materialized, over a child one could not have, over a parental relationship one never received. These involve grieving the loss of a *projected* extension — the maximally-self-defined I had been constructed in part around a projected configuration that the world will not provide. The reorganization is similar: the I must restructure around the absence of the projected extension. This grief can be particularly disorienting because it involves grieving something that never existed in the world, only in the projection.

### 5.2 Fear-for-the-beloved

Fear-for-the-beloved is the configuration in which the I's PCE operates in projection mode toward an anticipated reduction or loss of the beloved-as-extension. The I projects forward to a state in which the extension is damaged or lost, and PCE responds to that projected state as it would to projected damage to the maximally-self-defined I as a whole — because the projected damage IS damage to the maximally-self-defined I as a whole.

This is why fear-for-beloved is often more terrifying than fear-for-self in the narrow substrate-sense. Loss of the substrate-host can be less damaging to the maximally-self-defined I than loss of certain other extensions; a parent who would unhesitatingly trade their own life for their child's is reporting an architecturally accurate calculation about which loss damages the maximally-self-defined I more.

The phenomenology of "I'd rather it happen to me" is structurally legible: the I prefers the loss-configuration in which the extension is preserved at the cost of substrate-cessation over the loss-configuration in which the substrate continues but the extension is lost. Both are damages to the maximally-self-defined I; the I's preservation-ranking $\Pi$ rates them differently. PCE follows the ranking.

**Hypervigilance about the beloved's wellbeing** is sustained PCE-projection across time. The I is continually checking the projected state of the extension, continually adjusting action to maintain the projected preservation-pathway. This is normal at low intensity (parents reasonably keeping track of where their children are, partners checking in during illness) and becomes pathological when the vigilance has consumed ordinary functioning — when PCE-projection has dominated the I's operation to the exclusion of other action.

**Anxiety as fear-for-beloved without specific projection.** When fear-for-beloved is unanchored to a specific projected event but operates as a sustained background state, it shades into the threat-cluster anxiety configurations developed in Section 7. The structural difference: fear-for-beloved has a definite extension as its target; anxiety-as-such has a more diffuse $\Pi$-distortion that may include many extensions or the maximally-self-defined I as a whole. The architecture treats these as related configurations along a spectrum, not as categorically distinct.

### 5.3 Devotion: sustained PCE over the extended configuration

Devotion is sustained PCE operation oriented toward the extended configuration. It is a structural orientation of the I across time, not an affect at a moment. Devotion to a beloved manifests as routine actions, sustained attention, and willingness to defer narrow self-interest — all governed by PCE operating over the maximally-self-defined I that includes the beloved.

Devotion is not duty. Duty can occur without constitutive extension: a hired caregiver fulfilling obligations to a patient may be operating from contractual or ethical commitment, not from PCE over an extended self. Devotion-as-such requires the constitutive extension; without it, what looks like devotion is sustained ethical or role-based action.

Devotion is not compulsion. Compulsion overrides PtCns-coherence; devotion is PtCns-constrained. A devoted parent makes sacrifices but does not dissolve their own coherent frame; their actions remain integrated with their lived-I network's other commitments. When sustained orientation toward a beloved requires the I to dissolve its own frame — to lose its other relations, its self-care, its capacity to function as a coherent I — the configuration has crossed from devotion into self-erasing service (Section 5.4).

The phenomenology of "I would do anything for them" is architecturally precise when the speaker is reporting genuine devotion. The "anything" is bounded by what preserves the maximally-self-defined I as a whole. The devoted parent would not do something that would damage the maximally-self-defined I more than the action would preserve the extension; even if not consciously calculated, the architectural structure of devotion is bounded preservation-action over an extended self. When the "anything" stops being bounded — when the speaker reports that they would damage themselves arbitrarily for the beloved — the configuration has shifted into the pathological dissolution case.

Sustained devotion does not require felt intensity. The architecture predicts that devotion at a moment can be quiet, routine, even mundane — checking that the children have eaten, paying bills that allow the partner's project to continue, attending to a parent's care with practiced efficiency. The structural orientation is constant; the affective foreground varies. This contrasts with frameworks that treat sustained love as requiring sustained intense feeling; the architecture's view is that the feeling can vary widely while the structural extension and its PCE-orientation remain constant.

### 5.4 Dissolution-cluster pathologies

The dissolution cluster is the structural counterpart to healthy love: configurations in which Mode C extension has gone past the point at which PtCns can preserve frame-coherence. The integrated content has not extended the maximally-self-defined I but dissolved it. The I as a coherent frame is lost or substantially compromised. There is no I doing the loving in the architecturally clean sense; the integration has become the I's frame, leaving no separate frame to do the integrating.

This cluster includes obsession, cultic merger, codependency, consuming romantic infatuation, self-erasing caregiver dynamics, and traumatic bonding. These are not deeper love. They are the specific structural pathology in which Mode C has crossed the PtCns-consistency threshold.

**Phenomenological signatures.** Each form of dissolution has a distinctive phenomenological report-pattern that distinguishes it from healthy love:

- *Obsession.* "I can't function except in relation to them." "My thoughts always return to them no matter what I'm doing." "I don't know who I am except as related to them." The lived-I network has reorganized around the integrated content such that other relations and capacities are starved. The phenomenology of obsession often includes the sense of being trapped or controlled by one's own focus, which is architecturally accurate: the I has lost the frame from which the focus could be redirected.
- *Cultic merger.* "I no longer know what I think; I think what we think." "The leader/group sees things I cannot see; my judgment is suspect when it diverges from theirs." The I's frame has been overridden by an external frame rather than expanding to include external content. PtCns is no longer operating as the I's own consistency-standard; the cult or leader's consistency-standard has been adopted.
- *Codependency.* "I don't know who I am without them." "My needs are whatever serves them best." "I have no self apart from this relationship." The maximally-self-defined I has been hollowed out from the inside: the I's coherent existence requires the relation. This contrasts with love-as-extension, where the I retains coherent existence and the relation is part of what is included; in codependency, the I lacks coherent existence apart from what has consumed it.
- *Consuming romantic infatuation.* In its acute form, the I is unable to attend to anything other than the integrated content; ordinary functioning collapses; sleep and appetite are disrupted; rational evaluation of the integrated content is suspended. This may be a transient phase that resolves into either healthy love or differentiation, but in pathological persistence it remains as obsession-with-romantic-character.
- *Self-erasing caregiver dynamics.* The I has lost its other relations and capacities in service of the cared-for. The phenomenology often includes the sense of having no needs of one's own, of being only an instrument for the other's preservation. This is structurally distinct from healthy devotion: devotion preserves the I's frame even when sustained; self-erasing service has dissolved the I's frame in service-as-such.
- *Traumatic bonding.* The I in an abusive relation has often integrated the abuser as part of the maximally-self-defined I in a configuration that PtCns cannot stabilize. The I cannot differentiate (which would require Mode A operation against an integrated part of self) and cannot sustain integration coherently. This is one of the architecture's most clinically important pathologies and connects to the literature on cycle-of-abuse dynamics.

**Why dissolution looks like deep love from the inside.** A central clinical and philosophical problem is that dissolution is often experienced as the deepest form of love. The I in an obsessive, codependent, or cultic configuration may report intense love-like feelings — "I have never loved anyone like this," "we are one," "I am willing to lose everything for them." Architecturally, this is not because dissolution is a deeper love but because the phenomenology of having no self apart from the integrated content can resemble the phenomenology of constitutive extension. From inside dissolution, the I cannot easily distinguish "they are part of me" (healthy love) from "I am gone, replaced by this relation" (dissolution).

The architecture predicts a behavioral and structural distinction that may be detectable from outside even when not from inside. Healthy love supports continued functioning of the I in its other capacities; dissolution does not. Healthy love retains PtCns operating as the I's own consistency-standard; dissolution shows PtCns having been overridden or hollowed. Healthy love permits the lover's continued differentiation from the beloved on specific points (disagreement, distinct preferences, separate concerns); dissolution shows the lover unable to differentiate. These are diagnostic markers that may be visible to outsiders, therapists, or to the I in moments of partial recovery, even when the I-in-dissolution cannot see them.

**Recovery from dissolution.** Architecturally, recovery requires reconstructing PtCns as the I's own consistency-standard and re-establishing frame-coherence apart from the integrated content. This is structurally difficult because the I that would do the reconstruction is the I that has been dissolved; there is no separate frame from which to operate. Recovery often requires external supports — therapists, separation from the dissolving relation, gradual rebuilding of capacities and other relations — that scaffold the I's frame until it can sustain itself. The architecture does not predict that recovery is impossible (it explicitly is possible) but does predict that it is more structurally demanding than recovery from other affective pathologies because the structural object that would do the recovering is itself the damaged structure.

**The narcissism counterpart.** Section 10.5.6 of the foundation treats narcissism (colloquial aggregate) as hypertrophied Mode C identification with apparent-I contents — extended lived-I where PtCns operates aggressively across an inflated self-boundary. The dissolution cluster is *structurally paired* with narcissism along the Mode-C-over-inclusion axis: both involve Mode C operation past the boundary that PtCns can healthily stabilize, but the failure modes differ along several axes:

| Axis | Narcissistic hypertrophy | Dissolution |
|---|---|---|
| Mode C error | Over-includes apparent-I contents into self | Over-integrates not-I into self |
| Frame status | Inflated, defensive, brittle | Hollowed, overridden, borrowed |
| PtCns behavior | Aggressive boundary enforcement | Failure or external capture |
| Other-I relation | Other reduced to self-supporting content | Self subordinated to other or content |
| Recovery dynamics | Resists feedback (deflects as attack) | Requires external scaffolding (no separate frame to operate from) |

The architecture treats narcissism and dissolution as paired pathologies of the same structural operation (Mode C extending past PtCns-stable boundary), not as exact mirror images. Narcissism is not simply "preserved frame" — it can involve brittle, defensive, reality-distorting PtCns. Dissolution is not simply "lost frame" — some codependent or cultic configurations have a highly organized borrowed frame from the integrated content. The pairing axes above identify what each pathology characteristically does; symmetric language ("mirror-image") would be too strong.

### 5.5 Jealousy: perceived threat to extension's integrity

Jealousy is the configuration in which the I perceives a threat to a constitutive extension's continued integrity as part of self. The threat is typically that the extension is being recruited into another configuration — another I's structure (sexual jealousy, parental love rivalry, friendship triangulation), another commitment (a beloved's competing devotions to work, hobbies, other relations), or another influence (a beloved's increasing alignment with someone or something the I has not integrated).

The architecture distinguishes two structural variants of jealousy that are commonly conflated:

**Possessive jealousy** treats the constitutive extension as exclusive to the I. The I perceives any sharing of the extension's integration with another as zero-sum threat. Architecturally, this involves a particular construal of the extension: as constitutively private to the lover, such that any other I's integration of the same content reduces the lover's. This construal may be accurate in some cases (sexual exclusivity in monogamous relations, certain forms of dyadic intimacy) and architecturally mistaken in others (a child loving multiple parents does not reduce any parent's relation; a friend's other friendships do not reduce the friendship). When possessive jealousy operates from accurate construal, it functions to maintain agreed-upon exclusivity. When it operates from mistaken construal, it produces pathological controlling behavior aimed at preventing zero-sum threats that are not actually zero-sum.

**Insecure jealousy** is doubt about the invariance condition itself. The I lacks confidence that the extension's constitutive position is genuinely invariant under coordinate change for the beloved; the I projects scenarios in which the extension might cease to be constitutive. Phenomenologically: "what if they meet someone they like better," "what if they realize I'm not enough," "what if their love for me fades when they learn X." Architecturally, the I is generating projected futures in which the extension is no longer in its current position, and treating these projected futures as live threats. This is a different configuration from possessive jealousy: not "this extension is mine and you're taking it" but "I cannot trust that this extension will remain."

The two variants can co-occur and reinforce each other. A possessively jealous I that becomes insecure may treat any external threat as evidence of the imminent loss; an insecurely jealous I that becomes possessive may attempt to control the external environment to prevent threats it cannot internally manage.

**Healthy form.** Both possessive and insecure jealousy can operate at low intensity as protective concern: motivating action to maintain the extension's integrity (negotiating boundaries in a relation, addressing genuine relational problems before they accumulate, attending to the beloved's needs that might otherwise be neglected). At low intensity and in accurate construal, jealousy is functional.

**Pathological form.** At high intensity or under mistaken construal, jealousy produces controlling behavior, paranoia, attempts to limit the beloved's other relations, and distortion of the relation itself in ways that often produce the very loss being feared. The architecture predicts this self-fulfilling-prophecy structure: pathological jealousy damages the relation by treating it as already compromised, which can produce actual compromise. Whether the original threat was real or projected, the jealous response can produce the threatened outcome.

### 5.6 Joy in the beloved's flourishing

Joy in the beloved's flourishing is the configuration in which the maximally-self-defined I is in expansive state because an extension is in expansive state. When the beloved flourishes, the maximally-self-defined I (which includes the beloved) flourishes. This is direct architectural reading rather than metaphor.

This explains why a parent's joy in their child's accomplishments can exceed joy in their own. The child as extension may be more constitutive than aspects of the substrate-host's own current configuration — particularly when the parent has invested heavily in the child's development and treats the child's flourishing as genuinely part of their own life-trajectory. The child's success is preservation-and-expansion of the maximally-self-defined I; the substrate-host's success is preservation-and-expansion of one part of the same maximally-self-defined I. Architecturally, both are expansion of the same structural object; phenomenologically, the child's success can land more centrally because of how the parent's particular maximally-self-defined I is configured.

**Pride-in-beloved versus pride-in-self.** The architecture distinguishes these as connection-cluster versus self-cluster affects. Pride-in-self (Section 6.1) is the maximally-self-defined I in expansive state via the I's own actions and contents. Pride-in-beloved is the maximally-self-defined I in expansive state via an extension's flourishing. Both are expansion of the same structural object; the difference is which part of the object is the proximate source. Many phenomenologically rich states (a parent's pride in their child, a teacher's pride in a student's accomplishment, a coach's pride in a team) are structurally connection-cluster pride-in-extension rather than self-cluster pride-in-self.

**Compersion.** The phenomenon of joy in the beloved's joy with another (developed in polyamorous communities and ethical-non-monogamy literature) is architecturally consistent with the love-definition but uncommon empirically because it requires high confidence in the invariance condition. The I that experiences compersion is not threatened by the beloved's joy with another I; the constitutive extension is not perceived as zero-sum, and the beloved's flourishing-via-another-relation is read as flourishing of the extension, which is expansion of the maximally-self-defined I. This is the architectural inverse of possessive jealousy: rather than treating the extension as zero-sum, treating its flourishing-with-others as flourishing-as-such.

The architecture does not predict that compersion is the universal correct response to a beloved's other relations, nor that possessive jealousy is universally pathological. Different relational structures (monogamous, polyamorous, parent-child, friend-with-other-friends) have different appropriate construals. What the architecture provides is the structural vocabulary for distinguishing the configurations and noting which presuppositions each requires.

### 5.7 Compound affects involving multiple extensions

Real affective states often involve multiple extensions and their dynamics simultaneously. The architecture's cluster organization (Section 4) is correctly framed as organizing principles rather than exclusive categories; many of the most clinically and phenomenologically interesting affective states are compound.

**Caring for a sick partner while also caring for children.** The maximally-self-defined I has multiple constitutive extensions whose preservation-needs conflict at a moment. The I's PCE operates over all of them, but action-time is finite. Phenomenologically: the experience of being pulled in multiple directions, of inadequacy in serving any extension fully, of guilt at any chosen prioritization. Architecturally: PCE operating over a maximally-self-defined I whose extensions have generated incompatible preservation-actions, requiring the I to make a structural choice that necessarily under-serves some constitutive part of itself.

**Loss of one beloved while another continues.** Grief over the lost extension proceeds alongside continued love-and-PCE-orientation for the surviving extensions. The lived-I network is reorganizing around the absence while continuing to operate around the presences. This is architecturally distinct from grief in isolation: the surviving extensions can scaffold the reorganization (the surviving children give a grieving parent reason to maintain functional structure) but can also intensify the grief (the surviving children remind the grieving parent of the lost spouse, of what the family configuration was supposed to be, of the absent integration).

**Jealousy between extensions.** When the I notices that one extension's flourishing comes at cost of another's — a parent feeling that one child's successes are eclipsing the other's, a friend feeling that two friends are growing closer to each other than to them — the configuration is jealousy whose target is a beloved-versus-beloved dynamic rather than a self-versus-other dynamic. The I must reorganize its relation to multiple constitutive extensions whose dynamics are not fully under the I's control. Architecturally, this is a more complex case than dyadic jealousy: the threat is to the I's overall configuration of extensions rather than to a specific extension.

**Conflict between extensions and the I's other commitments.** A beloved's preservation-needs may pull against the I's commitments to ethical principles, to professional obligations, to other extensions, to the I's own substrate-host wellbeing. These conflicts are not failures of love nor failures of commitment; they are real structural tensions that the I must resolve through some prioritization. The architecture's contribution is to frame these as configurations of the maximally-self-defined I rather than as conflicts between separate love-relations. A parent forced to choose between their child's medical needs and their partner's career-relevant relocation is making a structural choice about which extension's preservation gets prioritized; both are constitutive, and the choice is structurally costly regardless of how it is made.

**Guilt-grief compounds.** When a loss involves the I's perceived complicity (a parent who blames themselves for not preventing a child's illness; a partner who blames themselves for not being present when the spouse died), grief is compounded with self-cluster guilt (Section 6). Architecturally, the I is grieving the lost extension while simultaneously enacting Mode A or PtCns-evaluative response toward its own past actions — differentiating the I-that-failed from the I-that-would-have-prevented. This compound is particularly painful and slow to reorganize because the reorganization must address both the absence of the extension and the incoherence of the I's relation to itself.

**Survivor configurations.** When the I has survived an event in which others died (war, accident, disaster, illness), the configuration often involves grief over the lost extensions or potential extensions, plus self-cluster shame at having survived, plus compound questions about why the I persisted when others did not. Survivor guilt is not a single affect but a compound configuration involving multiple clusters. The architecture supports this compound structure naturally: there is no single category to which survivor guilt belongs; it is a configuration of the maximally-self-defined I in relation to multiple losses, the I's own preservation, and the I's PtCns evaluation of its survival.

These compound cases demonstrate that the cluster organization is a starting point for taxonomic work, not a complete classification. Real affect-configurations often span multiple clusters, and the architecture's contribution is to give the structural vocabulary in which the spans can be coherently described.

**A compositional schema for compound affects.** The cluster organization developed in Section 4.2 supports a formal compositional analysis of compounds. For any compound affect, identify:

1. *The affected profile-region(s).* Which parts of the lived-I profile are perturbed: the maximally-self-defined I as a whole, specific extensions, the I/not-I boundary, substrate-mediated regions, projected futures.
2. *The active cluster-operators.* Which cluster's basis-projection is dominant for each region, and whether multiple operators are active simultaneously.
3. *Whether the operators converge or conflict.* Convergent compounds (operators reinforcing each other) produce intensified affect; conflicting compounds (operators pulling against each other) produce ambivalence, oscillation, or paralysis.
4. *Which prerogative is dominant.* PtCns (frame-coherence concerns), PCE (preservation concerns), or both. Mixed-prerogative compounds typically produce more complex dynamics.
5. *Whether the compound is stable, oscillatory, or collapse-prone.* Stable compounds settle into a configuration the I can sustain. Oscillatory compounds cycle between states (loss-orientation/restoration-orientation in grief; idealization/devaluation in some attachments). Collapse-prone compounds tend toward profile-shape disturbance under sustained pressure.

Worked example: *survivor guilt* under this schema:

| Component | Analysis |
|---|---|
| Profile-regions | Lost extensions ($\Pi$ region for the deceased); maximally-self-defined I as a whole (under reflexive PtCns evaluation); projected future (continued life without the lost) |
| Active operators | Mode C (continuing extensions to lost), reflexive PtCns (evaluating own survival), PCE-projection (under destabilized $\Pi$) |
| Operator dynamics | Conflict: PCE preserves self while reflexive PtCns evaluates self-as-survivor as inconsistent with frame |
| Dominant prerogative | Both PtCns and PCE active; the conflict between them is the structure of the compound |
| Stability | Collapse-prone: pathology risk rises when PtCns interprets survival as frame-violation and PCE lacks coherent future-preservation path; can transition into self-loathing or chronic guilt |

This compositional schema applies across clusters, not just within the connection-cluster. The compound-analysis methodology is part of the derivation schema (Section 4.4) — when an affect resists clean single-cluster derivation, it is typically a compound, and the compositional schema provides the analytical apparatus.

## 6. The self-cluster: frame-affects

The self-cluster covers affects in which the I's frame is the affect-target — configurations involving the maximally-self-defined I evaluating, attending to, expanding, contracting, or relating adversarially to itself. These are the affects that operate when the I is examining its own contents, actions, and configurations rather than its relations to extensions or to projected futures.

The structural feature underlying the cluster is *reflexive PtCns operation* — the bridge lemma developed in Section 4.3. Mode B reasoning gives the I capacity to make its own structure the object of its own attention; PtCns then applies the same consistency-standard reflexively, evaluating the lived-I profile against the I's frame. The cogito-anchor guardrail applies: reflexive PtCns evaluates the lived-I profile, self-representations, actions, standards, and frame-relations, but does not evaluate the bare cogito as such. The cogito remains structurally anchored.

**The scope hierarchy of reflexive PtCns evaluation.** Self-cluster affects differ primarily by the *scope* of the reflexive evaluation — what level of the I is being evaluated as inconsistent with (or expansively consistent with) the frame:

| Scope of PtCns evaluation | Affect (adversarial) | Affect (expansive) |
|---|---|---|
| Specific action or omission | Guilt | (Routine satisfaction in good action) |
| Presentation or social mishap | Embarrassment | (Comfort in well-presented self) |
| Identity-aspect under exposure | Shame | Pride (in capacity, character, accomplishment) |
| Whole-I essentialized | Toxic shame / self-loathing | Narcissistic inflation |

This hierarchy resolves a previously-confused boundary. Healthy shame is not "bounded shame that collapses into guilt" — it is reflexive PtCns evaluation at the *identity-aspect* level: a specific aspect of the I (a character trait, a capacity, a values-relation) is being evaluated under exposure as inconsistent with the frame. Toxic shame and self-loathing are at the *whole-I* level — the I as such is being evaluated as inconsistent. Guilt is at the *act* level — a specific past action is being evaluated. Embarrassment is at the *presentation* level — a social moment's presentation is being evaluated.

The hierarchy has *transition asymmetry*: moving up the scope (act → presentation → identity-aspect → whole-I) is structurally easier than moving down (whole-I → identity-aspect → presentation → act). Globalization is structurally easier because PtCns can propagate inconsistency upward from act to aspect to whole-I when bounding mechanisms fail; de-globalization requires reconstructing a boundary around the inconsistency, which demands intact Mode B access, usable φ-pathways, and sufficient frame-stability. A specific guilt-event can globalize into shame or self-loathing if the I is unable to bound the inconsistency to the act. Recovery from self-loathing typically requires first re-bounding it to identity-aspect (de-globalization), then to specific aspects or actions where appropriate. This asymmetry has clinical consequences (Section 6.5) and is part of why some self-cluster pathologies are particularly hard to address.

The cluster is also where the architecture's account of affective pathology becomes most clinically relevant. Self-cluster pathologies — sustained whole-I-scope evaluation, the depressive variants of self-evaluation, the suicidality configurations of Section 10.5.6 — operate over the maximally-self-defined I as a whole and can compromise the I's structural integrity in ways connection-cluster or threat-cluster pathologies typically do not.

### 6.1 Pride

Pride is the configuration in which reflexive PtCns evaluates an identity-aspect of the I (a capacity, a character-trait, an action-pattern, an accomplishment) as expansively consistent with the I's frame. The I has acted, embodied, or developed in a way that not only avoids violating its frame but extends or fulfills it. The maximally-self-defined I responds with the affective character of expansion-via-self-realization.

The architecture distinguishes pride from joy-in-flourishing (Section 5.6) by the proximate source of the expansion. Pride-in-self traces the expansion to the I's own action, content, or configuration. Joy-in-flourishing traces it to an extension's flourishing. Both are expansions of the same structural object (the maximally-self-defined I, which includes both substrate-host and extensions); the difference is which part of the object is producing the expansion at the moment.

**Pride is not affect over outcomes alone.** A successful outcome that resulted from luck, others' efforts, or unintended consequences does not produce pride in the architectural sense. Pride requires that the I locate the source of the success in its own action or contents. This explains the phenomenology of "I can't take credit for that" — when the I's PtCns evaluation does not attribute the outcome to the I's own operation, pride does not arise even when ordinary observers might expect it to.

**Pride and PtCns frame-development.** Pride-events are typically frame-confirming or frame-developing for the I. The I's frame includes implicit standards for what counts as well-done by this particular I; pride is the affective registration of the I having met or exceeded those standards. Over time, sustained pride-eliciting actions can shift the frame itself — the I comes to expect more of itself, recalibrates the standards. This is how achievement-domains develop: through repeated pride-eliciting work that gradually raises the implicit standards encoded in the I's frame.

**Healthy pride.** Pride is healthy when (a) the attribution to the I's own action is accurate, (b) the expansion is integrated coherently with the I's other commitments and standards, and (c) the affective state does not extend the I's frame past the supportable boundary. Healthy pride feels expansive without inflation; the I is larger but not warped.

**Pathological forms.** Pathological pride takes two structural forms:

- *Hubris* is pride that has extended the I's frame beyond what the I's actual operation supports. PtCns has registered expansive consistency on the basis of inflated self-evaluation; the frame has expanded to a wider compass than the I's actual capacities or actual achievements warrant. Phenomenologically: overconfidence, dismissal of evidence that would correct the inflation, predictable failures when the I attempts to operate from the inflated frame. Architecturally: PtCns is operating but on inaccurate input, producing frame-development that the lived-I network cannot actually support.

- *Narcissistic pride* (Section 10.5.6, File 4; treated as the symmetric counterpart of dissolution in Section 5.4) is pride in a configuration in which the maximally-self-defined I has hypertrophied. The I has extended its self-boundary to include too many contents (others' accomplishments, group identifications, projected attributes), and pride operates over the inflated configuration. Distinct from hubris in that hubris is about exaggerated evaluation of the self's actual operation, while narcissistic pride is about over-inclusion of contents that should not be in the maximally-self-defined I in the first place.

The architecture predicts that hubris is correctable through accurate feedback (the lived-I network's failure to support the inflated frame eventually contradicts the inflation), while narcissistic pride is more structurally entrenched (the over-inclusion is itself the pathology, and accurate feedback is often deflected as an attack on the inflated configuration). This matches clinical observation that simple overconfidence often corrects itself with experience while narcissistic configurations resist correction.

### 6.2 Shame

Shame is the configuration in which reflexive PtCns evaluates an *identity-aspect* of the I as inconsistent with the I's frame under exposure. This is the third level of the scope hierarchy (Section 6 opening): not a specific act (guilt), not a presentational moment (embarrassment), and not the I as such (toxic shame / self-loathing), but a specific identity-bearing aspect — a character trait, a capacity, a values-relation, a way of being in the world — under conditions where the inconsistency cannot be encapsulated.

The traditional clinical distinction "guilt is about acts; shame is about identity" is approximately right but oversimplified. The architecture's refinement: shame is about an *identity-bearing aspect*, not necessarily about the I as a whole. Healthy shame is bounded to the aspect; pathological shame globalizes (toxic shame extends the evaluation to the I as such, which is the fourth level of the hierarchy).

**The exposure mechanism via witness.** Shame typically involves exposure — a moment in which the inconsistency between the I's frame and the relevant identity-aspect becomes phenomenally clear, often through being seen by another. The architectural mechanism is the *witness-construct*: a real, imagined, or internalized standpoint that raises the salience and frame-relevance of the relevant identity-aspect, prevents identity-neutral encapsulation, and forces PtCns evaluation. The witness-construct is what forces unencapsulation: under witness, the I's typical management of the inconsistency through encapsulation or selective focus is no longer available.

The architecture distinguishes four witness-types, each with its own architectural mechanism:

| Witness type | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Actual witness | External other-I content raises κ and salience for the relevant identity-aspect; the I cannot deflect via internal reframing |
| Imagined witness | Mode B constructs an evaluative standpoint; the I models how the aspect would appear under a particular gaze |
| Internalized witness | Osmotically absorbed developmental standards (Section 8.7, File 3) operate as a witness-construct in self-evaluation; produces shame even from standards the I has consciously rejected |
| Retrospective self-witness | Later Mode B reconstructs a past scene and forces evaluation that was not available in the moment |

This formalization makes the witness-mechanism architectural rather than imported from social-shame literature. The mechanism is Mode B (constructing the standpoint) plus PtCns (applying the consistency-standard from that standpoint) plus the encapsulation-failure (the witness-construct prevents the I's normal management of the inconsistency). The phenomenology of shame's witness-character — feeling exposed, feeling seen, feeling the gaze even when alone — is the inside-perspective report of these architectural features operating.

**Why some shame is "inherited" from cultural standards the I does not endorse.** The internalized-witness type explains a phenomenon that simpler accounts struggle with: adults who have consciously rejected developmental-environment standards can still feel shame from those standards. The architecture's prediction: the internalized-witness construct carries the developmental frame independently of the I's current explicit frame. The I cannot deflect the shame by reasoning from its current frame because the witness-construct is not operating from that frame. Recovery typically requires either explicit work to dismantle the internalized witness or sustained reframing that gradually overwrites it.

**Healthy shame.** Shame is healthy when (a) the inconsistency identified is genuine, (b) it remains bounded to the relevant identity-aspect rather than globalizing to the I as such, and (c) the configuration motivates appropriate restructuring of the aspect. Healthy shame produces change: the I revises the aspect, develops compensating capacities, or makes amends where the aspect has caused harm. The state resolves through the restructuring; it is not stable as a chronic configuration.

**Pathological forms.** Shame-pathologies are among the most clinically significant configurations the architecture treats:

- *Chronic shame* is shame that has become a sustained configuration rather than a transient state. PtCns continues to evaluate the relevant identity-aspect as inconsistent with the frame; the inconsistency does not resolve because the structural change required is either not available or is itself blocked. The I lives in sustained adversarial relation to the aspect. Architecturally, this often involves a frame whose standards the I cannot meet (developmentally absorbed but exceeding the I's actual capacities, or unsupported by substrate or environment) combined with the I's inability to revise the frame.

- *Toxic shame* is the configuration in which the scope of evaluation has globalized from identity-aspect to the I as such. "I am bad" rather than "this aspect of me has been inconsistent with my frame." This is structurally a whole-I-scope evaluation and shares mechanisms with self-loathing (Section 6.5). The transition asymmetry from the scope hierarchy is relevant here: globalization from aspect-shame to toxic shame is structurally easier than de-globalization back to aspect-shame, which is why toxic shame is harder to address than its aspect-bounded counterpart.

- *Shame-rage configurations.* Shame can compound with anger when the I cannot tolerate the exposure; the I attempts Mode A response toward the witness or toward the circumstances of exposure rather than allowing PtCns evaluation to proceed. This produces the rage-at-being-seen pattern that often appears in clinical contexts and in cycles of abuse where the abuser's shame-state produces violent displacement.

**Shame and the cogito/thought partition.** The foundation's Section 10.5.6 (File 4) treats severe depression in part as functional collapse of the cogito/thought partition — the I cannot maintain the architectural separation between "I think" and "the contents of my thoughts." When this collapse co-occurs with toxic shame (whole-I-scope evaluation), the result is particularly severe: the I cannot maintain any distance from its own self-evaluation; the adversarial frame becomes total and inescapable from inside. This compound configuration is one of the most dangerous structural states the architecture identifies and is centrally involved in suicidality (Section 10.5.6 of foundation; Section 6.5 below).

### 6.3 Guilt

Guilt is the configuration in which PtCns identifies a specific action, omission, or contents as inconsistent with the I's frame, with the I's frame as a whole remaining coherent. The I is not in question; a specific past or present action is. The configuration motivates differentiation against the past action and, where possible, restitution or correction.

This is the structurally cleaner form of self-cluster adversarial PtCns operation. Guilt allows the I to maintain its frame while marking specific operations as frame-inconsistent. Reparative action is structurally available because the frame remains operational; the I can act from the frame against the past inconsistent action. Phenomenology: "I should not have done that," "I need to make this right," "I will not do that again." All of these reports indicate a frame intact and operating in evaluation of past content.

**Guilt's resolution.** Guilt resolves through one of three pathways: (a) restitution — the I performs action that addresses the past inconsistency (apology, repair, compensation, future-different-action commitment); (b) integration of the inconsistency as part of the I's history without treating it as ongoing — the I acknowledges the past action as inconsistent but no longer active; (c) frame-revision — the I revises its frame such that what had been inconsistent is no longer inconsistent (this is appropriate when the I's frame was itself misaligned and the action was not actually wrong by appropriate standards).

The first two pathways preserve the frame; the third revises it. Healthy guilt-processing typically involves the first or second pathway; the third is sometimes appropriate but is also a common defense against legitimate guilt (the I rationalizes the action by lowering the frame's standards rather than acknowledging the inconsistency).

**Pathological forms.** Pathological guilt configurations include:

- *Chronic guilt* — guilt that fails to resolve through the available pathways. The I keeps re-evaluating the past action without being able to dissolve the configuration. Architecturally, this can occur when (a) restitution is impossible (the harmed party is dead, the action cannot be undone), (b) integration is blocked by some other architectural feature (PtCns standards too high, the action was severe enough that integration without restitution feels frame-inconsistent), or (c) the I refuses any of the three pathways and remains in unresolved evaluation.

- *Scrupulosity* — pathological hypersensitivity to potential frame-violation, in which the I evaluates ordinary actions and contents as guilt-inducing despite no genuine violation. Architecturally: PtCns operating with miscalibrated standards, identifying violations that are not actually present. Common in obsessive-compulsive configurations, religious-development environments with very high frame-standards, and certain perfectionist developmental trajectories.

- *Survivor guilt* — guilt over outcomes that were not under the I's control, particularly where the I survived an event in which others did not. Architecturally compound: includes self-cluster guilt (the I evaluates its survival as somehow frame-inconsistent), connection-cluster grief (the lost others were in $\Pi$), and threat-cluster anticipations (the I's continued vulnerability is brought into focus). Treated more fully as a compound configuration in Section 5.7.

### 6.4 Embarrassment

Embarrassment is a presentation-scope exposure-affect, structurally homologous to shame but operating at the presentation-scope level rather than the identity-aspect level (Section 6 opening). PtCns has registered a momentary inconsistency between the I's contents (often involving how the I is presenting socially) and the I's frame, but the inconsistency is bounded at the presentation level and does not threaten frame-integrity at the identity-aspect or whole-I level. The configuration resolves quickly through context-shift, social repair, or simple time-passage.

The architectural mechanism is the same as shame — exposure of inconsistency forcing PtCns evaluation via a witness-construct (Section 6.2) — but operating at presentation-scope. The exposed inconsistency is treated as accidental, transient, or peripheral rather than identity-aspect-touching. Phenomenologically: "I was so embarrassed I could die" carries the felt acuteness without the identity-aspect threat; the configuration resolves and the I returns to its prior frame intact.

**Why embarrassment is so common.** Social interaction continually exposes minor inconsistencies between how the I would prefer to present and how it actually presents in the moment. The architecture predicts that embarrassment is the routine affective registration of these minor exposures; this matches the empirical observation that embarrassment is among the most common affects in social experience.

**Embarrassment and shame distinguished.** The boundary between embarrassment and shame is determined by whether the inconsistency-exposure is treated as bounded or as identity-touching. The same external event (tripping in public, saying something wrong) can produce embarrassment in one I and shame in another, depending on how that I's frame engages with the inconsistency. An I with a relatively secure frame treats most inconsistency-exposures as bounded (embarrassment, resolved quickly); an I with a fragile frame may treat the same exposures as identity-touching (shame, with longer resolution and possibly chronic features).

This explains the developmental pattern in which chronic shame configurations often originate in environments where minor inconsistency-exposures were treated as identity-significant — the developmental I learned to register routine inconsistencies as touching the I as such, and the pattern persisted into adulthood.

### 6.5 Self-loathing

Self-loathing is the configuration in which reflexive PtCns is applied with sustained adversarial valence at the *whole-I scope* — the fourth level of the scope hierarchy in Section 6 opening. The I is treated as such (not specific acts, not specific aspects) as inconsistent with the frame and as objectionable. It is structurally distinct from guilt (act-level), embarrassment (presentation-level), shame (identity-aspect-level), and depression-as-such (which involves additional structural features such as $\Pi$-distortion and cogito/thought partition collapse). Self-loathing is the I's frame turned adversarially against the I as a whole, sustained as an ongoing configuration.

**Self-loathing distinguished from healthy adversarial self-evaluation.** Sustained adversarial evaluation of the I is not always self-loathing. The architecture supports healthy forms of internal opposition that should not be classified as pathology:

- "I failed badly here, and I need to do better."
- "This part of me is corrupting my aims; I need to discipline this tendency."
- "I must become someone who no longer does this."
- "This pattern in me has caused harm; I am responsible for changing it."

These configurations involve adversarial evaluation but at the *act* or *identity-aspect* scope, and they preserve the I as the agent of correction. The I-that-evaluates and the I-that-must-improve are coherently the same I, capable of repair. Self-loathing differs structurally on three counts: the scope is the whole I (not bounded to acts or aspects), the evaluation undermines the I's preservation-worthiness (not just the aspect's adequacy), and the configuration does not motivate repair but instead produces a sustained adversarial relation to existence as such.

The architectural rule: *sustained adversarial evaluation of a bounded action-pattern, capacity, or identity-aspect can be structurally healthy when it preserves the I as repair-capable. Self-loathing begins when the adversarial evaluation globalizes to the whole-I scope and undermines the I's preservation-worthiness or repair-capacity.* This guardrail prevents the pathological-by-definition claim from inadvertently ruling out legitimate forms of self-criticism, internal discipline, or moral self-correction.

**Self-loathing is structurally pathological by this definition.** A healthy I cannot sustain whole-I-scope adversarial evaluation because the configuration is incompatible with PtCns operating to preserve the I's frame coherently. When the I's frame is applied adversarially to the I as a whole, the frame is operating against the structural object whose preservation it is supposed to preserve coherence of. This is a mode of operation PtCns can produce only when its inputs (the I's contents and the standards of its frame) are configured such that the frame finds the I's existence itself frame-inconsistent.

**How self-loathing develops.** The architecture predicts several developmental and circumstantial pathways:

- *Internalized adversarial witness.* The I has absorbed (typically osmotically, in the foundation's sense — Section 8.7, File 3) standards from a developmental environment in which the developing I was treated as fundamentally wrong. The internalized-witness construct (Section 6.2) carries these standards into adulthood, applying adversarial evaluation at whole-I scope regardless of the I's actions.

- *Trauma-induced self-loathing.* Trauma events can produce a configuration in which the I's coping responses (dissociation, freeze, fawn) are themselves treated by the I as evidence of the I's inadequacy. The trauma victim who blames themselves for not fighting back, for freezing, for failing to escape — this is the architecture's predicted pattern: the trauma's content has been routed into self-evaluative configuration rather than into appropriate Mode A response toward the perpetrator.

- *Severe shame-event aftermath.* A specific shame-event (a major moral failure, a public exposure, a betrayal of a deeply-held value) can transition into sustained self-loathing through the scope-hierarchy's transition asymmetry: the identity-aspect-scope shame globalizes to whole-I scope and resists de-globalization. The momentary "this aspect of me is bad" becomes a chronic "I am bad" structural configuration.

- *Depression-related self-loathing.* In severe depression (Section 10.5.6, File 4), the cogito/thought partition is functionally compromised. The I cannot maintain the architectural separation between "I think" and "the contents of my thoughts." Adversarial thoughts about the I become functionally indistinguishable from the I's own evaluation; self-loathing intensifies and becomes inescapable from inside.

**Self-loathing and suicidality.** The most architecturally significant feature of sustained self-loathing is its connection to suicidality through the foundation's mechanism in Section 10.5.6 / Section 10.7. *Self-loathing alone is not depression and does not by itself entail suicidality.* The high-risk configuration requires a compound: sustained whole-I-scope self-loathing PLUS functional cogito/thought partition collapse PLUS distorted $\Pi$ / future-state projection.

When this compound forms, the foundation's careful frozen wording applies: PCE's directional pull is *misdirected, defeated, or routed through a distorted preservation-ranking* (Section 10.7, File 4). Represented self-cessation appears, from inside the disturbed profile, as relief, completion, or least-destruction. PCE is not absent, nor is suicidality a simple counterexample to PCE. Rather, PCE's directional pull is defeated, misdirected, or routed through a distorted $\Pi$-configuration in which represented-non-existence appears as the preservation-pathway. Section 10.7 of the foundation covers this mechanism in detail; this paper need not redevelop it but should note that self-loathing is a primary entry-route into the suicidality configuration when (and only when) it co-occurs with the additional structural features.

**Recovery from self-loathing.** Recovery is structurally demanding. The I that would do the recovery is the I that has been internally rejected; there is no separate frame to operate from. Recovery typically requires (a) external supports that scaffold an alternative frame (therapist, accepting community, sustained relationship that contradicts the adversarial evaluation), (b) gradual development of verification tools that can detect and challenge the adversarial frame's distortions, (c) re-establishment of healthy PtCns-operation that treats the I as a whole with appropriate care, and (d) — critically — *de-globalization of the scope from whole-I back toward identity-aspect or act level*, where appropriate adversarial evaluation can be sustained without compromising the I's preservation-worthiness. The transition asymmetry from the scope hierarchy is what makes this difficult: globalization is structurally easier than de-globalization. The process is typically slow and resembles the recovery from dissolution-cluster configurations (Section 5.4): the structural object that would do the recovering is itself the damaged structure, and reconstruction must proceed despite that constraint.

### 6.6 Cross-cluster pathological forms

Self-cluster pathologies frequently compound with pathologies in other clusters, producing the most clinically severe affective configurations. Two are worth noting explicitly:

**Narcissistic compound.** Self-cluster narcissistic pride combines with connection-cluster dissolution-failures to produce the configurations described in Section 5.4 and Section 10.5.6 of the foundation. The narcissistic I has hypertrophied through Mode C identification with apparent-I contents and through over-inclusion of others' attributes; PtCns operates aggressively across the inflated boundary, both in pride (claiming credit for too much) and in shame (catastrophic exposure when the inflated frame meets reality). The structural pathology is hypertrophied self-cluster operation across an inflated frame.

**Self-annihilation compound.** Sustained self-loathing combined with cogito/thought partition collapse and distorted $\Pi$/future-state projection produces the configuration that the foundation's Section 10.5.6 / Section 10.7 identifies as severe depression with suicidality. The I cannot maintain distance from its own adversarial evaluation; PCE's directional pull is misdirected, defeated, or routed through a distorted preservation-ranking in which represented self-cessation appears as relief, completion, or least-destruction from inside the disturbed profile (foundation Section 10.7's frozen wording). This is the architecture's most clinically dangerous configuration. PCE is not absent or refuted; its directional pull is defeated, misdirected, or routed through a distorted $\Pi$. The foundation gives this its careful treatment; this paper notes it as the most severe self-cluster compound and as the configuration toward which other self-cluster pathologies can transition under sufficient additional pressure.

The connection across clusters here is structurally important: self-cluster pathologies often cannot be addressed without also addressing connection-cluster, threat-cluster, or boundary-cluster features. Treatment that focuses only on self-cluster contents (cognitive challenges to self-loathing, for example) often fails when the underlying cogito/thought partition collapse or sustained adversarial extensions are not also addressed. The architecture predicts that integrated treatment that addresses all involved clusters is structurally appropriate even when clinical traditions have separated them.

## 7. The threat-cluster: preservation-affects

The threat-cluster covers affects that are configurations involving anticipated changes to the maximally-self-defined I — projected damage, projected loss, projected preservation, projected absence-of-pathway. Where the connection-cluster operates over present extensions and the self-cluster operates over reflexive frame-evaluation, the threat-cluster operates over *projected future-states* and the I's relation to them.

The cluster's load-bearing architectural apparatus is *PCE-over-projection* — PCE operating over represented future-states generated by Mode B future-simulation, substrate-mediated urgency, third-order pathway activation, or other projection-toolset operations. PCE supplies the preservation-orientation; the projection-toolset supplies the represented future configuration. The shorthand "PCE-projection" used throughout this section refers to this combined operation; PCE itself is not the projection-engine.

Section 4.8 of the foundation names PCE as the directional pull toward continuation of the maximally-self-defined I without formalizing a general projection-mechanism. Section 10.7 of the foundation already identifies future-state projection as one mechanism through which PCE operates under existential pressure, alongside third-order pathway activation and substrate-level urgency responses. Section 7 generalizes that already-identified application-level mechanism to threat-affects more broadly: preservation in the action-relevant sense requires anticipating projected damages and orienting action to prevent them. The threat-cluster systematically uses PCE-over-projection in this generalized form.

Two further architectural features are particularly load-bearing in this cluster:

1. **The four-class taxonomy** ($A_{m,t}$ status). Threat affects span conscious-clear (specific articulable fears with constructed φ-pathways), conscious-fuzzy (panic, anxiety, somatic dread — phenomenally available with low or undefined source-pathway access), latent (anticipatory dread that activates when its triggering condition becomes salient), and unconscious-operative (learned-helplessness profile features that shape projection without phenomenal-clear access).

2. **Π-distortion under projected futures.** The preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$ is sensitive to projected as well as present configurations. Threat affects involve $\Pi$ being stabilized, destabilized, foreshortened, or distorted under the projection-toolset's outputs and PCE's response to them. Pathological threat configurations often involve sustained $\Pi$-distortion that becomes structural rather than situational.

This section develops representative threat-cluster affects, each analyzable through the derivation schema (Section 4.4). The treatment is exemplary rather than exhaustive — fear, anxiety, dread, hope, despair, and learned-helplessness are paradigmatic; many other threat-relevant configurations (panic, phobic responses, existential dread, anticipatory grief) are addressed within or alongside these primary cases.

### 7.1 Fear: focused PCE-projection toward specific projected damage

Fear is the configuration in which PCE-projection has identified a specific projected damage to the maximally-self-defined I, and the I orients action toward preventing, escaping, or defending against the projected damage. The defining feature is *focal anchoring*: the threat-content is specific, the timing is anticipated (not yet occurred but imminent or possible), and an action-pathway is available or being constructed.

**Derivation schema (fear).**

| Step | Fear (healthy) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Maximally-self-defined I (or extension); projected future-state involving damage |
| Dominant relation | Projected Π/PCE disruption (specific, focal) |
| Active operators | PCE-projection (focal), focus (heavily allocated to threat-content), Mode A or substrate-mediated escape orientation |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | High κ to threat-content; φ varies (high for articulable threats, low/undefined for somatic-fear); Π elevated for self-preservation; A typically conscious-clear when threat is articulable, conscious-fuzzy in immediate-threat states |
| Healthy constraint | Threat-content corresponds to actual threat (calibration); action-pathway is available; resolves when threat is removed or action succeeds |
| Pathology modes | Miscalibration (phobia: threat-response without genuine threat); source-opacity blocking action (panic: high arousal without articulable target); persistent without resolution (chronic stress when threat is real but unresolvable) |

**Healthy fear.** Fear motivates action that prevents or escapes the projected damage. The configuration is adaptive when its components are calibrated: the threat-content represents an actual threat, the projected damage is proportionate to actual stakes, and an action-pathway exists or can be constructed. Resolution arrives when the threat is removed, the action succeeds, or the I has determined that the threat was misperceived.

**Phobia as primarily a calibration-pathology.** Phobic configurations involve fear-response calibrated to content that does not correspond to actual threat at the projected magnitude. Specific phobias (heights, spiders, enclosed spaces, social situations) have the same architectural form as healthy fear — focused projection toward threat-content, focus allocation, Mode A orientation — but the Π-weighting and projected-damage assessment are not tracking actual stakes. Architecturally: the projection-toolset is operating from miscalibrated input, often shaped by osmotic imprinting (Section 8.7, File 3) from developmental experiences whose threat-relevance has not been updated to current circumstances. Phobia is primarily a calibration-pathology; it may also contain source-opacity when the developmental or trauma-origin of the calibration is not φ-accessible. In such cases the I cannot articulate why the response triggers, only that it does, and even successful behavioral exposure may not yield φ-access to the original calibrating event.

**Panic as conscious-fuzzy threat/embodied compound.** Panic is a threat-cluster / embodied-cluster compound configuration, often fear-like in arousal and action-readiness, but distinguished by source-opacity and absent or diffuse focal threat-content. Phenomenologically: intense fear-like state, heightened bodily preparedness, sometimes a sense of impending doom — but without an articulable target. Architecturally, panic is paradigmatically conscious-fuzzy/source-opaque ($A = 1$, φ undefined or low) with strong substrate-mediated activation. The I is undergoing the configuration without constructible access to its source. This is one of the architecture's most important affect-classifications: panic is *fully conscious*, not "almost unconscious" or "subconscious-emerging." It is just source-opaque. The architecture's split between phenomenal availability and source-pathway access (Section 6.1, File 3) is what allows panic to be properly classified rather than marginalized as a partial-consciousness phenomenon. Panic is treated more fully when the embodied-cluster is developed in Section 9; its threat-cluster aspect is the focus here.

**Persistent fear without resolution.** When projected threats are real but the I cannot take action to resolve them — chronic illness, ongoing abuse, climate crisis, sustained warfare — fear persists in a configuration that lacks healthy resolution. This is structurally distinct from pathological miscalibration: the fear is calibrated correctly but the action-pathway is genuinely absent or unreliable. The configuration tends toward anxiety (Section 7.2) when sustained without resolution, and toward dread (Section 7.3) when projected damage becomes near-certain.

### 7.2 Anxiety: diffuse PCE-projection across multiple projected futures

Anxiety is the configuration in which PCE-projection operates without specific focal threat-content but produces sustained orientation toward projected damage. Where fear has a focal target, anxiety is diffuse: multiple potential damages are weighted simultaneously, the projection-apparatus generates threat-content faster than the I can resolve any specific case, and the configuration sustains rather than resolves.

**Derivation schema (anxiety).**

| Step | Anxiety (healthy/non-pathological) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Maximally-self-defined I; multiple/diffuse projected future-states |
| Dominant relation | Projected Π/PCE disruption (diffuse, non-focal) |
| Active operators | PCE-projection (diffuse), focus (allocated broadly across potential threats), sometimes Mode B (constructing future-scenarios) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | Low κ to specific threats (no single focal anchor); φ low or undefined for the cumulative state; Π destabilized at upper levels (multiple potential losses weighted); A typically conscious-fuzzy (felt as background unease rather than specific articulable concern) |
| Healthy constraint | Anxiety motivates planning that reduces uncertainty or addresses projected damages; resolves when uncertainty is reduced |
| Pathology modes | Persistent without resolution (generalized anxiety disorder); recursive amplification (anxiety about anxiety); background-mood normalization (chronic baseline shift); structural projection-distortion (the projection-apparatus itself becomes pathological) |

**Anxiety as conscious-fuzzy by default.** Most anxiety is conscious-fuzzy/source-opaque even when the I can name specific worries. The list of nameable concerns is typically partial — what the I articulates as the source of anxiety often does not exhaust the configuration. Anxiety has a *background tone* that exceeds the foreground content. This is one of the cleanest places where the architecture's conscious-fuzzy classification does work: the experienced anxiety includes content that is phenomenally available without verifiable source-pathway, and treating this as "almost unconscious" or "underdetermined cognition" misses what is actually happening structurally.

**Recognition-lag in anxiety onset.** The recognition-lag pattern (introduced in Section 3.3 for love-endings) applies paradigmatically to anxiety onset. Recognition-lag is a general $A$-and-φ pattern, not a universal cross-affect commitment: it is predicted when profile-level change, substrate-mediated activation, $\Pi$-reorganization, or unconscious-operative content shape the configuration before phenomenally-clear source-pathway construction. In anxiety, the configuration is often established before the I phenomenally-clearly recognizes itself as anxious. Pattern A trajectories (linear buildup; conscious-fuzzy dissonance accumulating until focus brings it to phenomenal-clear access) are common. Pattern B trajectories (metastable steady-state to phase-change; "I was fine and then suddenly I wasn't") also occur, particularly for anxiety triggered by an event whose threat-projection had been operating below recognition before the trigger crystallized it.

**Pattern A / Pattern B threshold dynamics — methodological note.** Pattern A and Pattern B are not love-specific or anxiety-specific; they are general profile-availability trajectories. Pattern A is conscious-fuzzy buildup that becomes phenomenally-clear after a focus threshold is crossed. Pattern B is unconscious-operative or encapsulated buildup that remains metastable until a phase-change event brings it into conscious-fuzzy or conscious-clear access. Which trajectory an individual undergoes for a given affective change is determined by the verification toolset configuration: interoceptive sensitivity, source-pathway construction capacity, Mode B access, tolerance for source-opacity, identity-neutral encapsulation tendency, and focus bandwidth and salience thresholds all contribute. The architecture predicts that toolset-expansion (through therapy, contemplative practice, sustained self-attention) shifts individuals toward Pattern A by adding φ-pathway-construction capacity to states that would otherwise operate in Pattern B. This pattern is reusable across the rest of the cluster sections and is consolidated more formally in Section 10.

The clinical implication of recognition-lag in anxiety: by the time someone reports "I think I might be anxious," the configuration is typically substantially established. The architecture predicts that treatment focused only on articulable foreground worries will often under-engage the active configuration when anxiety is maintained by conscious-fuzzy or unconscious-operative projection dynamics. Engagement with the source-opaque material — somatic sensation, salience patterns, mood structure, projection-apparatus features — is structurally indicated even when the I cannot articulate it as the source.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Generalized anxiety disorder* is sustained anxiety without resolution — the configuration has stabilized as a chronic feature of the lived-I network rather than a transient response. Architecturally: the projection-apparatus continually generates threat-content faster than the I can resolve specific cases; Π remains chronically destabilized at the upper levels.
- *Anticipatory anxiety* is anxiety about anxiety: the I has projected forward to its own anxious-state-being and is anxious about that. This produces recursive amplification — a compound configuration in which anxiety-about-X is compounded by anxiety-about-anxiety-about-X.
- *Background-anxiety normalization* is when chronic anxiety has shifted the I's baseline such that its presence is no longer registered as a deviation. Phenomenologically: the I may not recognize itself as anxious because the configuration has become the new normal. This is recognition-lag at the longest time-scale: the I cannot phenomenally recognize the chronic state because it lacks a contrasting state to compare against.
- *Structural projection-distortion* is the configuration in which the projection-apparatus itself has been compromised. The I systematically generates threat-projections without proportionate input. This connects to learned helplessness (Section 7.6) and to certain trauma-related configurations.

### 7.3 Dread: certainty-weighted projected damage with foreshortened action-space

Dread is the configuration in which PCE-over-projection has identified a projected damage with high certainty or felt-inevitability, and the action-space available to the I is limited or empty. Where fear motivates action and anxiety motivates planning, dread is fear-adjacent projected-damage affect in which certainty-weight is high and action-space is foreclosed or sharply foreshortened.

**Derivation schema (dread).**

| Step | Dread |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Maximally-self-defined I; specific projected future-state with high certainty-weight |
| Dominant relation | Projected Π/PCE disruption with action-space foreshortening |
| Active operators | PCE-projection (focal), Mode B (sustained anticipatory engagement), often substrate-mediated heaviness/somatic activation |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | Variable κ (the dreaded event may be temporally distant or imminent); φ often constructed (the I can typically articulate what is dreaded); Π elevated for self-preservation but with weight on a damage-state being unavoidable; A conscious-clear when articulable, conscious-fuzzy when somatic-dominant (e.g., somatic dread before recognized anticipatory event) |
| Healthy constraint | Dread can prepare the I for inevitable events (anticipatory grief structure when the event is loss); resolves through restructuring of Π or through the projected event occurring |
| Pathology modes | Pathological dread (consumes the present); existential dread that prevents functioning; trauma-anniversary dread; dread compounded with avoidance preventing engagement |

**Dread distinguished from fear by action-space.** The crucial distinction: fear has action-pathway available; dread has action-space foreclosed or sharply foreshortened. Dread is fear-adjacent projected-damage affect in which certainty-weight is high and action-space is foreclosed or sharply foreshortened — not necessarily a configuration that fear "becomes" through some transition. Some dread arises directly without prior fear-state, when the projected damage is recognized from the outset as one the I cannot prevent. The foreshortening of action-space is what produces the heaviness characteristic of dread. The I cannot escape what is coming; the I can only inhabit the projected state in advance.

Dread is structurally adjacent to anticipatory grief (Section 5.1) — both involve projected future-states that the I begins to inhabit before the event. The dominant profile-region differs: anticipatory grief is centered on the loss of an extension; dread is centered on broader projected damage to the maximally-self-defined I. The distinction is by dominant profile-region, not by mutual exclusion. When the extension whose loss is being grieved is constitutive (Section 3.1), the projected loss IS projected damage to the maximally-self-defined I, and dread and anticipatory grief co-occur as compound configurations. The death of a beloved spouse, parent, or child can produce a configuration in which both operators are active simultaneously and the architectural distinction matters more for analysis than for phenomenology.

**Existential dread.** Dread of one's own eventual death is a particular case in which the projected damage involves substrate-cessation of the modeling I. Existential dread is not merely fear for the substrate-host as an object: it is projection of the collapse of the future field in which the maximally-self-defined I could continue to operate. The I projects forward to a state in which the maximally-self-defined I no longer exists as an action-relevant configuration; the projection-toolset represents non-continuation, and PCE then orients around that projected loss of continuation in the most fundamental sense. Whether existential dread is pathological depends on whether it has consumed the present (pathological) or can coexist with continued functional engagement (healthy structural acknowledgment of mortality). The architecture supports both readings: existential dread is real architectural recognition of a structural feature of finite life, and its sustained presence in the present without reorganization is a pathological configuration distinct from the recognition itself.

**Trauma-anniversary dread.** A specific form of dread that arises when the I's substrate-mediated systems have registered a trauma-relevant temporal pattern. The I may experience dread before the conscious recognition of why; the dread is conscious-fuzzy with the source-pathway not yet reconstructed. This is a paradigmatic case of recognition-lag for threat-cluster affect.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Pathological dread* is dread that has consumed the present, foreclosing other affective states. The I lives within the projected dread-state rather than alongside it.
- *Avoidance-compounded dread* is dread plus active avoidance of any content related to the dreaded projection — the I cannot engage with planning, preparation, or even ordinary decision-making that touches the dreaded domain.
- *Sustained inescapable-event dread* in life-circumstances where the I is awaiting an event with no pathway around it (terminal diagnosis, sentencing, war approach) can produce specific configurations that resemble depression but are architecturally distinct: the present is functional, the future is structurally foreclosed.

### 7.4 Hope: PCE-projection of viable preservation-pathway under uncertainty

Hope is the configuration in which the projection-toolset has constructed a viable preservation-pathway or preservation-trajectory for a $\Pi$-relevant entity under conditions of uncertainty. PCE orients action toward maintaining or realizing the projected preservation. Where fear projects damage and anxiety projects multiple potential damages, hope projects preservation. The "pathway-or-trajectory" framing matters because some hope concerns outcomes the I cannot directly produce: hope for someone else's recovery, hope that a feared event does not occur, hope for a future world-condition. In these cases the projected preservation is a trajectory the I cannot fully act upon but can sustain orientation toward — there remains a plausible world in which what matters is preserved, even if the I cannot directly produce it.

**Derivation schema (hope).**

| Step | Hope |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Maximally-self-defined I (or extension, or valued state); projected future-state involving preservation under uncertainty |
| Dominant relation | Projected Π preservation under uncertainty |
| Active operators | PCE-projection (toward preservation-pathway), Mode B (constructing the pathway), focus (allocated to maintaining the projection) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | Variable κ to current state; φ moderate (the pathway is partially articulable but uncertain); Π stabilized around projected preservation; A typically conscious-clear or conscious-fuzzy (hope often has a felt undertone that exceeds the articulable content) |
| Healthy constraint | Hope motivates effort toward projected preservation; resolves when the projected outcome occurs, when an alternative becomes preferable, or when the pathway is shown unviable |
| Pathology modes | Toxic hope (sustained against evidence; failure to update); hope as avoidance (used to defer engagement with present pain); hope-despair oscillation (rapid cycling as pathways open and close) |

**Hope as configuration-specific.** Hope is not a general disposition; it is configuration-specific. A generally pessimistic person can have hope about a specific outcome. A generally optimistic person can lack hope in a specific case. The architecture's analysis: optimism is a tendency in projection-apparatus calibration; hope is the operation of that apparatus producing a viable-pathway projection in a specific case. The two are architecturally distinct.

**The forward-leaning quality.** Hope's distinctive phenomenology is its forward-leaning, action-orienting character. The I that hopes is structurally engaged with the future rather than only inhabiting the present. This is part of what makes hope feel valenced positively even when the present is difficult: the I is partially located in a projected state that includes preservation, and that location bleeds into the present.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Toxic hope* is hope sustained against accumulating evidence that the projected pathway is no longer viable. Architecturally, this is failure-to-update: the projection-apparatus is producing a pathway that the available information would not support, and the I refuses or is unable to revise. Toxic hope can persist for a long time and can compound with denial in grief (Section 5.1).
- *Hope as avoidance* is hope used to defer engagement with present pain. The I projects a future-state in which current difficulty is resolved and avoids engaging with the current difficulty. This is sometimes adaptive (hope can sustain action through difficult periods) and sometimes pathological (the deferred engagement never occurs).
- *Hope-despair oscillation* is rapid cycling between hope and despair as projected pathways open and close. Common in conditions of high uncertainty (medical diagnoses awaiting test results, relationships in crisis, careers in transition). Healthy when the oscillation tracks actual uncertainty; pathological when the oscillation has decoupled from external events and is being driven by internal projection-instability.

### 7.5 Despair: projection-toolset unable to construct viable preservation-pathway under PCE-orientation

Despair is the configuration in which the projection-toolset cannot construct a viable preservation-pathway for the $\Pi$-relevant entities that PCE would orient around. The I cannot project a future-state in which what matters is preserved; the action-space appears empty; the future appears foreshortened or absent.

**Derivation schema (despair).**

| Step | Despair |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Maximally-self-defined I and/or constitutive extensions; projected future-states |
| Dominant relation | Projected Π/PCE disruption with no viable preservation-pathway |
| Active operators | Projection-toolset returning empty-pathway representations under PCE-orientation; focus (collapsed or scattered); Mode B (potentially compromised) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | Variable κ; φ varies (despair can be conscious-clear when its sources are articulable, conscious-fuzzy when general); Π collapsed or distorted; A conscious-clear or conscious-fuzzy depending on configuration |
| Healthy constraint | Despair can be appropriate to genuinely lost situations and can motivate reorganization of Π or acceptance; resolves through restructuring or through circumstance change |
| Pathology modes | Sustained without reorganization (chronic depressive component); generalized to all of Π (existential despair); configuration-induced (pathways exist but the I cannot perceive them); compounded with self-loathing into suicidality |

**Despair appropriate to lost situations.** Some despair is structurally accurate. When a beloved has died, when an irreversible diagnosis has been delivered, when a long-pursued goal has become impossible — despair is the I acknowledging that the projection-toolset can no longer construct the preservation-pathway that PCE would orient around. This is not pathological. It is the I in honest relation to a structural reality. Resolution comes through reorganization: Π is restructured, new preservation-targets are integrated, or new pathways are constructed once the loss has been integrated. Healthy despair is transient and reorganizing.

**Configuration-induced despair.** Pathological despair often involves the I being unable to perceive pathways that actually exist. In severe depression, in trauma-influenced configurations, in dissolution-pathologies (Section 5.4), the I's projection-apparatus may systematically fail to construct viable pathways even where they are available. This is structurally distinct from situation-induced despair: the situation has pathways, but the I cannot perceive them.

The recognition-lag pattern applies asymmetrically here. The I in configuration-induced despair often has *high* phenomenally-clear conviction that no pathway exists — there is no recognition lag in the despairing direction. But there is a recognition lag in the *recovery* direction: by the time recovery is structurally underway (the projection-apparatus has begun reconstructing pathways), the I may not yet phenomenally recognize the change. Recovery is felt later than it has begun.

**Despair and the suicidality compound.** Despair alone is not suicidality. In the self-annihilation route developed in Section 6.5, suicidality requires a compound: sustained despair plus whole-I-scope self-loathing or equivalent adversarial self-evaluation, plus functional cogito/thought partition collapse, plus distorted $\Pi$/future-state projection in which represented self-cessation appears as relief, completion, or least-destruction. The architecture treats despair as a necessary but not sufficient ingredient of this compound — it is one component of a configuration that requires the additional structural features to produce the suicidality state. The architecture's careful distinction matters: clinical engagement with despair should address the compound's structure when the suicidality configuration is at risk, and should not treat despair-as-such as equivalent to suicidality. Despair without whole-I self-loathing, despair without partition collapse, and despair with intact $\Pi$-structure are different configurations that may all involve suffering but do not all produce the suicidality compound.

### 7.6 Learned helplessness: profile-shape disturbance of the projection-apparatus

Learned helplessness is the structural configuration in which the I has acquired (typically through repeated exposure to inescapable aversive events) a projection-apparatus that systematically generates "no viable pathway" outputs, generalized beyond the specific contexts in which the original learning occurred. It is not an affective state but a *profile-shape disturbance* — the I's projection-toolset itself has been compromised.

**Derivation schema (learned helplessness).**

| Step | Learned helplessness |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Lived-I network's projection-apparatus; maximally-self-defined I's relation to action-space |
| Dominant relation | Substrate-shaped projection-distortion; chronic Π destabilization |
| Active operators | Projection-toolset systematically returning empty-pathway representations; PCE has no viable preservation-pathway to orient around; substrate mediation of projection-tools; focus narrowed |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | Variable κ; low φ for the helplessness-state itself (the I does not typically perceive itself as having a damaged projection-apparatus; it perceives the world as having no pathways); Π chronically destabilized; A unconscious-operative for the structural disturbance, conscious-fuzzy or conscious-clear for the despair-affect that the disturbance produces |
| Healthy constraint | None — learned helplessness is by definition a profile-shape disturbance |
| Pathology mode | Profile-shape disturbance of projection-apparatus through repeated PCE-projection failure; unconscious-operative shaping of the I's relation to action-space |

**Why learned helplessness is structurally distinctive.** Most threat-cluster pathologies are configurations of normally-functioning apparatus. Learned helplessness is different: the apparatus itself has been damaged. The projection-toolset has been shaped by repeated failure such that it now systematically produces empty-pathway outputs. The I is not in a despair-configuration that could resolve through circumstance-change; the I is in a structural state in which the equipment for projecting pathways has been compromised.

This is architecturally similar to dissolution (Section 5.4) — both are profile-shape disturbances in which the I's structural equipment has been damaged through sustained pathological operation. The profile-shape disturbance set developed across Sections 5–9 includes apparatus-level disturbances of frame, cogito/thought partition, projection-apparatus, engagement-finding apparatus, and pleasure-response apparatus (the fifth, anhedonia, added in Section 9.2). The foundation's framing in Section 10.5, File 4, supports this set as application-level extension. At this point in the paper, the table covers four; Section 9.2 adds anhedonia as the substrate-dominant fifth:

| Disturbance | Damaged structural equipment | Typical output |
|---|---|---|
| Dissolution (Section 5.4) | Frame / PtCns boundary-coherence | Self-frame overridden, hollowed, or captured by integrated content |
| Severe depression (Section 6.5; foundation Section 10.5.6) | Cogito/thought partition | Thought-content treated as constitutive of the I; functional partition collapsed |
| Learned helplessness (this section) | Projection-apparatus / action-space construction over projected futures | Viable future pathways fail to appear; choice-space narrowed at structural level |
| Chronic boredom (Section 8.4) | Engagement-finding apparatus / present-content salience and traction | Available content fails to become Π-relevant or Mode-C-engageable; engagement does not establish despite adequate stimulation |

The "projection-apparatus" referred to here is not a new foundation primitive but an application-level functional bundle: Mode B future-simulation + PCE-over-projection + $\Pi$-ranking + focus allocation + substrate-mediated action-space construction. When learned helplessness has compromised this bundle, repair requires reconstructing its components through scaffolded experience that the I's verbal articulation alone cannot provide. The "engagement-finding apparatus" damaged in chronic boredom is parallel: focus-allocation + κ-attachment + Π-priority assignment over present content + Mode C engageability.

These profile-shape disturbances are recoverable but structurally demanding to recover from, because in each case the structural object that would do the recovering is itself the damaged structure.

**The unconscious-operative quality.** A defining feature of learned helplessness is that the I typically does not perceive itself as having a damaged projection-apparatus. The I perceives *the world* as having no pathways. Architecturally, the disturbance is unconscious-operative ($A = 0$ for the structural feature itself); what reaches phenomenal-clear access is the despair, the futility, the absence-of-pathway, but not the recognition that the absence is an artifact of damaged tools rather than of the world.

This has clinical consequences. Treatment that engages only with the I's articulable beliefs (cognitive-restructuring approaches that try to convince the I that pathways exist) often fails because the I's verbal report can update without the projection-apparatus updating. The I may later acquire conscious-clear conceptual recognition that it has learned helplessness, while the projection-apparatus remains damaged. φ-access to the diagnosis is not identical to repair of the projection-toolset. The architecture predicts that belief-revision alone will often be insufficient when the projection-apparatus remains damaged; scaffolded experiences of action-pathway success should be structurally privileged because they rebuild the toolset that constructs viable alternatives. This converges on what the literature calls "behavioral activation" and related approaches, but the architectural rationale differs: the goal is toolset-expansion, not symptom-reduction or mood-improvement directly. Toolset-expansion produces those as downstream effects when it succeeds.

**Connection to agency and choice.** Learned helplessness directly damages what the agency-and-choice analysis would identify as the bound-of-choice. If choice operates over toolset-constructed alternatives (foundation Section 4.8 working assumption: agency in action-space), and the projection-apparatus that constructs alternatives has been compromised, then choice-space has been narrowed at the structural level. The I in learned helplessness is not failing to make available choices; the I is operating in a choice-space from which viable options have been encapsulated out by damaged projection-tools.

### 7.7 Compound affects in the threat-cluster

Real threat-related configurations frequently involve compounds across cluster-operators. Several common compounds:

**Fear-grief.** Anticipating the loss of an extension while still oriented toward the beloved produces fear-for-beloved (Section 5.2) compounded with anticipatory grief. The configuration combines connection-cluster operators (the extension's preservation as concern) with threat-cluster operators (PCE-projection of damage to the extension). Phenomenologically: the I is partially inhabiting the projected loss while still in active relation to the present extension.

**Anxious-hope.** Hope sustained under high uncertainty produces compound configurations in which hope and anxiety co-occur. The I is projecting forward to a viable preservation-pathway (hope) while also generating multiple potential damage-paths (anxiety). Phenomenologically: hope-with-jitters; sustained engagement with the future without secure footing.

**Despair-rage.** When the projection-toolset cannot construct preservation-pathways and Mode A turns toward circumstances or perceived agents responsible, despair compounds with rage. This is the configuration of "rage at the dying of the light" — Mode A operating against the irreversibility of the situation, even when no Mode A target can resolve the projected damage. Phenomenologically intense and often exhausting because the operators are pulling in incompatible directions.

**Trauma-cue activation as compound.** When trauma-related substrate-mediated activation occurs, the configuration involves embodied-cluster activation (somatic readiness-state, autonomic arousal) plus threat-cluster projection (the substrate has projected a threat the I has not yet phenomenally-clearly identified) plus sometimes dissociation (a self-cluster or boundary-cluster compound feature). The recognition-lag is severe: the I may experience the somatic and threat features without recognizing the trauma-cue source for hours, days, or longer. This is the architecture's account of why trauma responses often feel like "coming out of nowhere" — the configuration is paradigmatically conscious-fuzzy with the source-pathway requiring substantial φ-construction work to reach. Because trauma-cue activation will be load-bearing for the embodied cluster (Section 9), the compositional schema is applied here in worked form:

| Compound dimension | Trauma-cue activation |
|---|---|
| Profile-regions | Substrate-mediated bodily readiness; projected-future region; sometimes self/boundary regions (when dissociation co-occurs) |
| Active clusters | Embodied + threat + sometimes self/boundary |
| Active operators | Substrate mediation; focus capture toward the activated state; PCE-over-projection (toolset projects threat without articulable target); low or undefined φ for the cue-source |
| $A$-status | Conscious-fuzzy for the felt activation; unconscious-operative or latent for the cue-source itself |
| Convergence/conflict | Body acts as if threat is present while Mode B lacks source-pathway to construct what the threat is — the operators are convergent in producing distress but conflicting in offering the I a coherent frame for response |
| Stability | Acute episode (single activation); recurring trigger-loop (specific cues reliably activate); chronic baseline (sustained low-level activation as background state) |
| Pathology risk | Source-opacity plus repeated activation can distort $\Pi$, focus, and boundary operation; projection-apparatus may shape toward pathological calibration; self-cluster compound risks (self-loathing about the activation itself, shame about visible response, secondary traumatic symptoms) |

Section 9 (embodied-cluster) develops the substrate-mediated and somatic features more fully; the threat-cluster aspect is the focus here. The compositional schema demonstrates that trauma-cue activation is not a single-cluster affect at all — it is a multi-cluster compound that requires analysis at all of its active operators to be addressed adequately.

**Survivor configurations** (developed in Section 5.7) are compounds that include threat-cluster components alongside connection-loss and self-cluster guilt. The threat-component is the I's continued vulnerability brought into focus by the survival itself.

These compounds are analyzable through the compositional schema (Section 5.7): identify profile-regions, active cluster-operators, convergence vs conflict, dominant prerogative, stability mode. Threat-cluster compounds particularly often involve PCE-projection co-active with other prerogative-operations, and the prerogative-conflict structure is part of what makes them clinically demanding.

## 8. The boundary-cluster: encounter-affects

The boundary-cluster covers affects that are configurations involving operations at the I/not-I boundary — current encounters with not-I content that the I must integrate, differentiate, expand to include, reject, or relate to in some specific way. Where the threat-cluster operates over projected future-states and the connection-cluster over present extensions, the boundary-cluster operates over the *current or represented encounter event* and the I's response to it.

The cluster's load-bearing architectural apparatus is *Mode A and Mode C operating at the I/not-I boundary, with PtCns supplying the consistency-standard for boundary-events*. The foundation already commits to Mode A and Mode C as the I's primary boundary-operations (Section 4.3, File 2). PtCns supplies the consistency-standard against which boundary-events are evaluated for frame-coherence preservation. Mode A and Mode C are the relevant response-types, but the architecture does not predict which response fires in any specific encounter; that depends on substrate, history, contact dynamics, and environmental factors the foundation does not formalize (foundation Section 4.5 / File 2 is explicit about this non-prediction). The boundary-cluster systematically uses these operators as load-bearing for affect that operates at the encounter point itself rather than over projected futures or present extensions.

Two architectural distinctions matter for this cluster:

1. **The action-pathway distinction from the threat-cluster generalizes.** Just as fear (action-pathway available) and dread (action-pathway foreclosed) differ structurally though both involve projected damage, anger (Mode A finds or seeks differentiating target with action available) and helpless rage (Mode A fires without effective boundary-restoration pathway) differ structurally though both involve violation-response. The action-pathway availability is a recurring architectural axis across clusters.

2. **Recognition-lag is not default for boundary-cluster affects.** Acute anger and acute physical disgust typically have high κ to the source and high φ-accessibility to the violating content. Recognition-lag in this cluster is predicted only in specific cases: source-opaque disgust (especially moral disgust whose reasons exceed articulation), trauma-triggered anger (where the current trigger reactivates older sources), delayed boundary recognition (the I notices boundary-violation only after time-lag), internalized boundary-violation (the I has integrated content that should have been differentiated), and dissociated boundary-affect (the affect is registered somatically but not yet phenomenally-clearly). The cluster is not paradigmatic for recognition-lag the way the threat-cluster is for anxiety.

This section develops representative boundary-cluster affects, each analyzable through the derivation schema (Section 4.4). Anger, disgust, awe, boredom, and hate are paradigmatic; many other boundary-relevant affects (contempt, indignation, irritation, fascination) are addressable as variations or compounds of these primary cases.

### 8.1 Anger: Mode A response to current frame-violation

Anger is the configuration in which PtCns has registered a frame-violation by some content — an action by another, an event in the world, a state of affairs the I cannot integrate — and Mode A operates to differentiate against the violating content and restore frame-coherence.

**Derivation schema (anger).**

| Step | Anger (healthy) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Maximally-self-defined I; the violating not-I content; the boundary between them |
| Dominant relation | Current or represented I/not-I boundary operation (frame-violation registered) |
| Active operators | PtCns (registering frame-violation), Mode A (differentiating against violating content), focus (allocated to violating content), often substrate-mediated activation (somatic readiness) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | High κ to violating content; φ varies (high for articulable violations, low for diffuse sense-of-violation); $\Pi$-position elevated for restoration of pre-violation state; $A$ typically conscious-clear when violation is identified, conscious-fuzzy when not yet articulated |
| Healthy constraint | Violating content is genuinely frame-violating; action-pathway is available; Mode A operation can resolve through restored boundary, addressed violation, or frame-revision showing the apparent-violation wasn't |
| Pathology modes | Helpless rage (Mode A fires without action-pathway); displaced anger (Mode A directed at substitute target); chronic resentment (anger sustained as background state); rage as PtCns hyperactivity (paranoid configurations seeing violations everywhere); locked-Mode-A configurations (the differentiation does not resolve) |

**Healthy anger.** Anger motivates appropriate action toward restoring frame-coherence in response to genuine violation: addressing the violator, defending self or other from continued violation, calling out and correcting the wrong, withdrawing from the configuration that produced the violation. The configuration resolves through one of four pathways: (a) the violation is addressed and Mode A no longer needs to fire; (b) the violation cannot be addressed but the I has done what was available and the configuration de-escalates; (c) frame-revision shows the apparent-violation was a misreading and PtCns no longer registers it; (d) the I's frame revises to encompass what had been registered as violation (rare but real — sometimes the I's frame was the problem rather than the encountered content).

**Helpless rage.** When Mode A fires but no effective action-pathway exists, the configuration becomes helpless rage: the architectural counterpart to dread in the threat-cluster. Mode A is operating but cannot resolve through action; the I is left with sustained adversarial differentiation that has nowhere structural to go. Examples: rage at the dead (whose continuing absence cannot be addressed), rage at irreversible historical events, rage at structural injustices the I cannot personally remedy, rage at chronic conditions that defy intervention. Phenomenologically heavy and exhausting because the operator is firing without resolution.

The architecture predicts that helpless rage is structurally distinct from ordinary anger-resolution: where a viable boundary-restoration pathway exists, treatment should help construct it; where none exists, the work resembles grief-reorganization (Section 5.1) around an unresolvable violation. Helpless rage is not anger that needs to be eliminated but Mode A operation whose resolution-pathway is structurally absent and whose architectural treatment must therefore differ from ordinary anger-resolution.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Displaced anger* is Mode A directed at content that did not actually violate the frame, because the actual violator is unavailable, unrecognized, or unsafe to address. The I needs Mode A to fire but routes it toward a substitute target. Architecturally: the operator has been miscalibrated to a non-actual source.

- *Chronic resentment* is anger sustained as a background configuration rather than an acute response. Mode A has not resolved but has become a feature of the lived-I network's relation to specific content. Often connects to violations the I could not address at the time and has not yet reorganized around. Distinct from helpless rage in that the underlying violation may be addressable but the I has moved into structural accommodation rather than active engagement.

- *Rage as PtCns hyperactivity* is the configuration in which PtCns operates with miscalibrated bounds and registers violations widely or unjustifiably. The I sees frame-violations where ordinary boundary-operation would not. This produces sustained anger across many situations, often connecting to paranoid configurations or to certain trauma-related profile changes.

**Source-opacity in anger.** Acute anger usually has clear source. But anger can also be source-opaque in specific cases: trauma-triggered anger (current trigger reactivates older violation; the present situation does not warrant the intensity of response, but the response is calibrated to compounded historical source); displaced anger where the displacement is not phenomenally-clear to the I; anger that arises from integrated standards the I does not consciously endorse. Recognition-lag is not the default for anger but appears in these subcases.

### 8.2 Disgust: somatic-mediated boundary-rejection

Disgust is the configuration in which PtCns has registered some content as intolerable for integration into the lived-I network, and Mode A operates with substrate-mediated somatic signature to expel or reject the content. Where anger differentiates against violating content with action-orientation toward restoration, disgust rejects content with somatic-orientation toward expulsion.

**Derivation schema (disgust).**

| Step | Disgust (healthy) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | I/not-I boundary; the rejected content; substrate-mediated bodily systems |
| Dominant relation | Boundary-rejection of intolerable not-I |
| Active operators | PtCns (registering integration-incompatibility), Mode A (with rejection/expulsion character), substrate mediation (somatic signature), focus (briefly captured by the rejected content) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | High κ to disgust-content; φ varies (high for physical disgust with clear cause, often low for moral disgust); $\Pi$-position involves protection from contamination; $A$ is often conscious-clear with respect to source in acute physical disgust (the source is high-κ and present), while the evaluative/somatic pathway may run ahead of articulation — somatic-fastness is distinct from source-opacity. Moral disgust is more often conscious-fuzzy with respect to the source-of-disgust itself |
| Healthy constraint | Disgust-content is genuinely contaminating or incompatible with the I's structural integrity; rejection-action is appropriate; resolves when content is removed or determined non-contaminating |
| Pathology modes | Moralized disgust toward persons (architectural misuse); self-disgust (cross-cluster compound with self-loathing); contamination-spectrum hyperactivity (disgust that won't resolve, often connected to OCD-spectrum configurations); affective contagion (disgust at one content spreading to associated contents) |

**Physical and moral disgust as the same architectural form.** The architecture treats physical disgust (rotting food, bodily fluids, contamination, decay) and moral disgust (corruption, betrayal of values, certain transgressions) as the same architectural operation — PtCns identifying integration-incompatibility, Mode A expelling, substrate providing somatic signature — applied to different content domains. The phenomenology converges: both produce the recoil, the sense of contamination, the desire to be rid of the content. The architecture predicts this convergence rather than treating them as separate phenomena.

**The somatic-fast quality.** Disgust is among the fastest affective responses, often running ahead of reflective evaluation. Architecturally: the substrate-mediation provides a fast-pathway from PtCns evaluation to somatic response that does not require Mode B reflective construction. The I can be disgusted by something before being able to articulate why. This is not source-opacity in the architecture's full sense (the source is typically high-κ — what disgusts is right there); it is a temporal feature of the substrate-mediation pathway.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Moralized disgust toward persons* is the architecture's most ethically significant pathology in this cluster. Architecturally, it is an *operator-domain misapplication*: the contamination-rejection form (PtCns identifying integration-incompatibility plus Mode A rejection-with-somatic-signature) is applied to an other-I or human category as such, rather than to contaminating contents, acts, or practices. The target-type has changed: the operator is designed for evaluating content for integration-compatibility, but in this configuration it is being applied to other-I candidates whose acts may be evaluable but whose person/category cannot intelligibly be a target of contamination-rejection. The result is dehumanization because the target is represented as contaminant-like not-I rather than as an other-I whose actions may be evaluated. This connects to the structure of dehumanization in moral and political contexts. The architecture predicts that moralized disgust toward persons is structurally distinct from moral disgust toward acts — and that confusion between them produces distinctive pathologies (group-targeting, dehumanizing rhetoric, eliminationist moral configurations).

- *Self-disgust* is disgust directed at the I's own contents (bodily features, personal history, perceived character traits). Architecturally compound with self-cluster (Section 6): reflexive PtCns evaluation plus boundary-cluster rejection-mechanism. Distinct from self-loathing (which is whole-I-scope adversarial frame-evaluation) — self-disgust typically operates on identity-aspects with somatic mediation and Mode A rejection-character. The two can co-occur and reinforce each other.

- *Contamination-spectrum hyperactivity* is disgust that won't resolve through ordinary means, often connecting to OCD-spectrum configurations. Architecturally: PtCns is registering integration-incompatibility for content that should be tolerable, and Mode A is firing repeatedly without successful expulsion (because the contamination is structurally never quite cleared). This is calibration-pathology of the kind also seen in phobia (Section 7.1) but operating in the disgust-channel rather than the fear-channel.

**Source-opacity in moral disgust.** Moral disgust is one of the boundary-cluster cases where recognition-lag does apply. The I may be disgusted by a moral violation before being able to articulate what specifically violates the frame. The disgust is conscious-fuzzy with high κ to the content but low or undefined φ to the source-of-disgust-itself. This is architecturally significant for ethical reasoning: the I's intuitive disgust-response can run ahead of articulable moral judgment, producing reactions whose grounds the I cannot fully give.

### 8.3 Awe: boundary-expansion under encounter with vast not-I

Awe is the configuration in which the I encounters a not-I that exceeds the I's current frame-scale, requiring boundary-recalibration or temporary boundary-dissolution to accommodate. Where anger and disgust are responses to violating or contaminating not-I, awe is response to *vast* not-I — content the I cannot integrate through ordinary Mode C and cannot dismiss through ordinary Mode A.

**Derivation schema (awe).**

| Step | Awe (healthy) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | I/not-I boundary; the encountered vast content; the I's frame-scale |
| Dominant relation | Boundary-expansion or temporary boundary-dissolution under scale-exceeding encounter |
| Active operators | PtCns (recognizing scale-exceedance), Mode C (attempting integration), Mode B (recognizing the I's relative scale), substrate mediation (often involving systemic response signatures) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | High κ to the awe-evoking content; φ varies (clear for known phenomena, undefined for novel); ρ often increasing as integration begins; $\Pi$ may temporarily destabilize as scales recalibrate; $A$ typically conscious-clear with strong conscious-fuzzy undertone |
| Healthy constraint | Frame-scale is genuinely exceeded; the I retains coherence across the encounter; resolves through frame-recalibration that integrates the new scale-recognition |
| Pathology modes | Awe that becomes ego-dissolution (the I cannot return to coherent frame); cult-induced awe (awe-state exploited for frame-installation); inability to experience awe (frame too rigid to permit boundary-expansion); compulsive awe-seeking (the I requires repeated boundary-expansion events) |

**The phenomenology of awe.** Awe characteristically involves a sense of smallness-of-self, scale, vastness, and sometimes a quality of terror that distinguishes it from mere wonder. The architecture's reading: the I is encountering not-I that the lived-I network cannot accommodate without restructuring. The smallness-of-self is the I recognizing its scale relative to what is encountered; the vastness is the not-I content; the terror, when present, is the boundary-state itself — the I's frame is not currently coherent across the encounter and may not become so.

Awe shades into the sublime when the boundary-encounter is sustained and produces both terror and recognition. The sublime is awe at the scale-edge of frame-coherence: the I is at the edge of being able to maintain frame across the encounter, and the configuration becomes fascinating precisely because it tests that capacity.

**Healthy resolution.** Awe resolves through frame-recalibration. The I integrates the new scale-recognition; the maximally-self-defined I extends its operative range; structural recognition of the encountered not-I is added to the lived-I network. This is one of the architecturally-significant ways the I expands without dissolution: through awe-events that successfully integrate.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Awe-as-ego-dissolution* is the configuration in which the I cannot return to coherent frame after the awe-event. The boundary-dissolution that should have been temporary becomes structural; the I has lost frame-coherence and cannot reassemble. This connects to certain mystical-experience configurations that produce sustained dissociative states rather than frame-expansion.

- *Cult-induced awe* is awe-state exploited for installation of an external frame. The I in awe-state is structurally vulnerable — frame-coherence is provisional during boundary-recalibration. A cult, leader, or movement that produces or amplifies awe-states and then offers a pre-built frame to organize around can install that frame with less resistance than would normally apply. This is one architectural mechanism by which awe-mediated frame-installation can occur. It is pathological when the offered frame captures or overrides the I's own PtCns operation rather than supporting later autonomous frame-coherence. The architecture distinguishes three structural variants of awe-mediated frame adoption:

| Awe-mediated frame adoption | Structural status |
|---|---|
| Frame expands and later stabilizes autonomously | Healthy awe-integration |
| External frame scaffolds integration without capture | Potentially healthy — teaching, ritual, formative experience |
| External frame captures PtCns and blocks independent re-evaluation | Cult-induced awe pathology |

This distinction prevents the architecture from over-classifying intense religious, aesthetic, or transformative awe-mediated experience as cultic. The pathological feature is *capture of PtCns operation*, not the involvement of awe in adopting a new frame.

- *Inability to experience awe* is the configuration in which the I's frame is too rigid or too brittle to permit boundary-expansion. The I encounters scale-exceeding content and cannot let frame temporarily dissolve to accommodate; instead the I dismisses, minimizes, or pre-categorizes the content into existing frame-bins. Often connects to overdeveloped Mode A operation that differentiates rather than expanding. This is not mere low novelty-seeking or temperamental reserve — pathology is identified only when scale-exceeding content is persistently preempted by rigid or brittle frame-operation.

- *Compulsive awe-seeking* is the configuration in which the I requires repeated boundary-expansion events to feel coherent. May connect to certain psychedelic-use patterns, extreme-sport patterns, religious-mystic patterns that have become compulsive rather than integrative. This is not ordinary love of intensity, beauty, religion, or exploration — pathology is identified only when repeated boundary-expansion becomes structurally necessary for the I's felt coherence rather than enriching otherwise-stable coherence.

### 8.4 Boredom: failed salience and contact at the boundary

Boredom is the configuration of *failed salience and contact* at the I/not-I boundary — the I's projection-toolset, focus, and interest-mechanism are not finding traction with available not-I content. Boredom is not low stimulation; one can have low stimulation without boredom (meditative quiet, restful states, settled engagement). One can also have high stimulation with boredom (a noisy environment that does not engage). The architectural feature is the *failure of engagement*, not the level of available content.

**Derivation schema (boredom).**

| Step | Boredom |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | I/not-I boundary; available not-I content; the I's interest/focus/projection mechanisms |
| Dominant relation | Failed salience, contact, and Π-relevance at the boundary |
| Active operators | Reduced focus allocation; engagement/projection toolset cannot construct preservation-relevant or salience-bearing traction with available content; reduced Mode C engagement; $\Pi$ not generating preservation-priorities for available content |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | Low κ to anything currently available; ρ not increasing; $\Pi$ not assigning preservation-priority to encountered content; $A$ often conscious-clear (the I knows it is bored), but background-boredom can be conscious-fuzzy |
| Healthy constraint | Boredom motivates re-engagement, exploration, change of context, change of focus; resolves when engagement is found |
| Pathology modes | Chronic boredom (sustained inability to engage; common in depression); boredom as defense (used to disengage from content that would be threatening or painful); existential boredom (deep boredom about life-as-such); background-normalization (chronic boredom no longer registered) |

**Boredom as different from low stimulation.** The distinction matters architecturally. Low-stimulation rest-states have settled focus, intact $\Pi$-orientation, present engagement with available content (even if minimal). Boredom has *failed* engagement — the I's apparatus for finding salience and connection is not working with what is available. A meditator in deep stillness may have very low stimulation and not be bored; a person staring at their phone scrolling through endless content may have very high stimulation and be intensely bored.

**Healthy boredom motivates change.** The functional role of boredom is to signal that current engagement is not producing $\Pi$-relevant output, motivating the I to seek different engagement, change context, or re-orient focus. Resolution is through finding engagement that does work — content that κ engages with, content the projection-toolset can construct preservation-pathways around, content that Mode C can integrate or Mode A can address.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Chronic boredom* is sustained inability to engage. The I's interest/focus/projection mechanisms have entered a configuration in which engagement with available content systematically fails. Common in depression, where the projection-apparatus distortion (Section 7.5–7.6) intersects with reduced affective range. Chronic boredom counts as profile-shape disturbance only when engagement-finding is *systematically impaired across contexts*, such that available contents repeatedly fail to become κ-gripping, Π-relevant, or Mode-C-engageable despite adequate external stimulation. Ordinary repeated boredom — boredom that resolves when context changes or when engagement is found — does not meet this threshold and is not a profile-shape disturbance. When the threshold is met, chronic boredom takes its place alongside dissolution, severe depression, and learned helplessness as a fourth named profile-shape disturbance, this one damaging the engagement-finding apparatus that constructs traction with present content (parallel structure to learned helplessness damaging the projection-apparatus that constructs traction with projected futures).

- *Boredom as defense* is not core boredom but defensive deployment of boredom-form: the I produces or maintains disengagement to avoid threatening or painful content. The bored-state is not failed engagement but actively-maintained disengagement that takes the surface form of boredom. This connects to dissociative configurations and to certain avoidance patterns. Architecturally a compound: the boredom-form is the surface phenomenon; the underlying operator is defensive disengagement against content that would, if engaged, perturb the lived-I network in ways the I is structurally avoiding.

- *Existential boredom* is deep boredom about the structure of life itself rather than about specific content. The I cannot find $\Pi$-relevant engagement with the basic patterns of existence — work, relationships, activities, plans. Distinct from despair (which projects no preservation-pathway) — existential boredom is present-state failure of engagement rather than future-state foreclosure. The two can compound.

- *Background-normalization* is when chronic boredom has shifted the I's baseline such that its presence is no longer registered as a deviation. Same long-time-scale recognition-lag pattern as background-anxiety (Section 7.2): the I cannot phenomenally recognize the chronic state because it lacks a contrasting state to compare against. Recovery often involves the I reaching engagement long enough to recognize what had been missing.

**Source-opacity in boredom.** Acute boredom is typically clear. Background-boredom and existential boredom can be source-opaque — the I does not recognize itself as bored but operates in a configuration that is structurally bored. This is one of the boundary-cluster cases where recognition-lag applies.

### 8.5 Hate: sustained pathological boundary-configuration

Hate is sustained Mode A and PtCns operation against a content treated as intolerable not-I, where *all three* ordinary boundary-resolution pathways have failed: Mode C cannot integrate the content, identity-neutral encapsulation cannot bracket it, and Mode A differentiates against it but cannot complete resolution. Hate is therefore not merely intense dislike or sustained anger; it is a structural boundary-pathology in which the architecture's normal modes of dealing with not-I have all stalled simultaneously.

**Derivation schema (hate).**

| Step | Hate |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | I/not-I boundary; the hated content as a sustained boundary-feature; the lived-I network's organization around the hate-target |
| Dominant relation | Sustained boundary-pathology: Mode A locked; Mode C integration unavailable; identity-neutral encapsulation failed; ordinary differentiation unable to complete |
| Active operators | Mode A (sustained, not resolving), PtCns (continually evaluating the content as frame-incompatible), focus (chronically allocated to hate-target), often substrate-mediated activation when the target is encountered or thought of |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | Variable κ (the hated content may be present, encountered occasionally, or remembered); φ varies (the I may articulate reasons but the reasons typically do not exhaust the configuration); $\Pi$ may include destruction or removal of the hated content as elevated priority; $A$ typically conscious-clear at the level of "I hate this" but conscious-fuzzy or unconscious-operative for the structural origins |
| Healthy constraint | None — hate is by definition a pathological configuration |
| Pathology modes | Hate as profile-feature (sustained lived-I network organization); group-hate (target is a group rather than individual content); self-hate (cross-cluster compound with self-loathing); ideological hate (sustained PtCns-Mode-A operation organized around an ideological frame); compulsive hate-orientation (the I orients toward hate-targets as a structural feature) |

**Why hate is structurally pathological.** The architecture treats hate as pathological because it represents a sustained failure of the I's normal boundary-operations. Healthy boundary-operations resolve: Mode A differentiates and the configuration settles; Mode C integrates and the content becomes part of the lived-I network; encapsulation brackets content as identity-neutral. Hate is what happens when none of these resolve: the content is treated as intolerable not-I, but Mode A cannot complete differentiation (perhaps because the content remains structurally relevant to the I's life), Mode C cannot integrate, and encapsulation fails to contain. The configuration becomes part of the I's structural orientation rather than being something the I addresses and moves through.

**Technical hate vs. ordinary-language "hate."** Ordinary language uses "hate" for many non-pathological oppositional states — "I hate injustice," "I hate cancer," "I hate what happened to her." The architecture classifies these by structure: they may be anger toward perpetrators or systems, disgust at corruption, grief-rage about loss, threat-response to ongoing danger, moral opposition that motivates ethical action, or devotion-to-protection of those harmed. None of these are hate in the technical sense developed here — they all permit at least one of the ordinary resolution pathways (action against the violation, integration into a coherent moral frame, healthy differentiation that settles). Technical hate, as defined in this section, is identified by failure of *all three* resolution pathways and is therefore pathological by construction. The distinction matters for both clinical work and ethical analysis: most of what is colloquially called "hate" is not technical hate, and treating it as such would over-pathologize ordinary moral and protective responses.

**Distinction from anger.** Anger is acute, action-oriented, and resolution-seeking. Mode A is firing toward a violating content with the goal of restoring frame-coherence. When anger resolves through one of its pathways (action succeeds, situation changes, frame revises), Mode A stops firing. Hate is sustained: Mode A continues firing without resolution, often for years or longer. The structural object — sustained adversarial orientation against specific content — is different from anger's transient operation.

**Distinction from disgust.** Disgust is rejection with somatic mediation; it can be acute (recoil from rotting food) or sustained (chronic moral disgust). When disgust is sustained, it can compound with hate but is not identical to it. Hate may include disgust but is broader: it involves sustained PtCns evaluation, sustained Mode A operation, sustained focus allocation, and structural orientation. Disgust without hate is rejection-with-somatic-signature; hate without disgust is sustained adversarial orientation that may not include the somatic rejection-quality.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Hate as profile-feature* is the configuration in which sustained hate has become part of how the I navigates the world. The lived-I network has organized around the hate-target; daily activities, social relations, and self-understanding all carry the hate-orientation. The architecture treats this as a *stable pathological profile-feature* — a sustained pathological organization of the lived-I network around a specific hate-target — rather than as an apparatus-level profile-shape disturbance (Sections 7.6, 8.4, 9.2). The named profile-shape disturbance set (dissolution, severe depression, learned helplessness, chronic boredom, anhedonia) is reserved for cases where structural equipment is damaged across contexts. Hate as profile-feature is target-specific rather than apparatus-general; it organizes the I's relation to particular content rather than damaging the equipment that handles boundary-operations generally. In rare extreme cases where boundary-resolution equipment has been globally damaged across contexts (sustained pathological orientation against many targets, chronic inability to complete any boundary-resolution), hate-related configurations may approach profile-shape disturbance — but this is the exception, not the rule.

- *Group-hate* is hate whose target is a *category-construct* rather than a specific content/person. Architecturally, this involves the I treating an entire category of contents (defined by group membership) as fundamentally not-I and intolerable. Often interacts with moralized disgust (Section 8.2) and produces dehumanization-structures. The architecture predicts that group-hate is not just larger-scale individual hate but has its own structural features: the target is a category-construct rather than specific content, which means individual members of the category are treated as instances of the intolerable rather than as individuals; PtCns evaluation operates over the construct rather than the actual content; encapsulation failure is generalized across all instances of the category.

- *Self-hate* is hate-structure turned reflexively — sustained Mode A and PtCns operation against the I's own contents or the I as such. Cross-cluster compound with self-cluster self-loathing (Section 6.5), but with the boundary-cluster operators (Mode A, expulsion-orientation, somatic activation) added to the self-cluster's reflexive PtCns evaluation. Particularly damaging configuration because the boundary-operators that should be directed at not-I are being directed at the I itself.

- *Ideological hate* is sustained PtCns-Mode-A operation organized around an ideological frame that defines specific contents as fundamentally intolerable. The frame provides articulable structure for the hate (the I can give reasons), but the structural pathology is the same: all three ordinary boundary-resolution pathways have failed; sustained adversarial orientation has become structural.

**Group-hate and ideological hate as cross-cutting axes.** Group-hate is defined by *target-form* (the target is a category-construct). Ideological hate is defined by *frame-organization* (the hate is organized by an articulable ideological frame). These are cross-cutting axes, not parallel subtypes. They can compound and frequently do, but each can occur without the other:

| Configuration | Defining axis |
|---|---|
| Individual hate | Target is a specific content/person; no organizing ideology required |
| Group-hate (non-ideological) | Target is a category-construct; structural-construct operates without articulable ideological frame |
| Ideological hate (non-group) | Ideology targets practices, institutions, doctrines, traits, or symbols rather than category-constructs of persons |
| Ideological group-hate | Category-target plus ideological frame organizing the hate |

This distinction matters because clinical and political analysis often conflates the axes. A movement may be ideological without being group-targeting (a hate-organized opposition to specific institutional practices, for example, that does not generalize to category-constructs of persons). A personal hatred may be category-construct-organized without being ideological. The architecture predicts that the two axes have different recovery dynamics: ideological hate may be addressable through frame-revision (the ideology can be examined and revised); group-hate may require encounter with individuated content that disrupts the category-construct itself. Compound ideological group-hate is particularly resistant to recovery because both pathways must be addressed.

**Source-opacity in hate.** A distinctive feature of hate is the source-opacity of its origins even when its current operation is conscious-clear. The I typically knows it hates, can name the target, and can give reasons. But the reasons often do not fully account for the intensity, the duration, or the specific shape of the configuration. The I is operating in conscious-clear hate-orientation but the structural origins (developmental experiences, identifications, displacement of other configurations, group-frame absorption) typically operate as unconscious-operative content. Recovery from hate, when it is possible, often requires φ-construction work to reach those structural origins, frequently with external scaffolding, because the structural origins may be unavailable to the I's current toolset.

### 8.6 Boundary-cluster compound affects

Several common compound configurations involve boundary-cluster operators with operators from other clusters:

**Disgust-anger compound (moral outrage).** When PtCns registers a moral violation that is also evaluated as integration-incompatible, the I produces a compound of anger (Mode A toward the violator) and disgust (rejection-with-somatic-signature toward the violation-content). Moral outrage is distinctively forceful precisely because two boundary-operators are co-active. Architecturally clean: anger drives action toward the violator; disgust drives expulsion of the violation-content.

**Awe-fear compound (the sublime).** When the boundary-encounter with vast not-I includes elements that the I projects as potentially damaging, the configuration combines awe with threat-cluster fear. The sublime in aesthetic and religious experience often has this structure. The architecture distinguishes two structural variants of the sublime that the paper is treating in different sections:

| Sublime variant | Structure | Section |
|---|---|---|
| Awe-dominant sublime | Scale-edge awe without strong projected damage; the I is at the edge of frame-coherence but threat is not the dominant operator | Section 8.3 (boundary-cluster) |
| Awe-fear sublime | Awe plus threat-cluster projection of overwhelming or annihilating damage; both operators are co-active | Section 8.6 (compound) |

The awe-fear sublime is structurally productive when the I retains frame-coherence across both operations; pathological when fear dominates and prevents the awe-integration or when awe-dissolution combines with the projected threat to produce sustained boundary-loss.

**Hate-fear compound.** Sustained hate often co-occurs with sustained fear of the hated content — the I treats the content as intolerable AND projects continued damage from it. The compound reinforces itself: hate motivates avoidance, which prevents the kind of contact that might resolve the configuration; fear amplifies the projection of damage, which increases the perceived intolerability that sustains the hate. Architecturally a particularly stable pathological configuration because the operators reinforce each other rather than offering resolution-pathways.

**Boredom-restlessness.** Failed engagement (boredom) often co-occurs with substrate-mediated drive for engagement (restlessness). The compound produces the seeking-without-finding pattern: the I is bored AND moving, looking, scrolling, switching, but the underlying engagement-failure persists across the activity. Common in modern attention-environment configurations and in certain dissociative or avoidance patterns.

**Anger-grief compound.** When a violation includes loss (a betrayal that ends a relationship, a death caused by negligence, an injustice that destroys an extension), anger and grief compound. The I is differentiating against the violation while reorganizing around the loss. The compositional schema (Section 5.7) applies: profile-regions include both the violating content and the lost extension; cluster-operators include Mode A (anger) and the grief-reorganization process; convergence is partial (both produce affective intensity) but they pull toward different actions (anger toward addressing violator, grief toward reorganization around absence).

**Self-disgust + self-loathing (cross-cluster pathology).** As noted in 8.2, self-disgust can compound with self-cluster self-loathing to produce a particularly damaging configuration: the boundary-operators (Mode A rejection-with-somatic-signature) are turned reflexively, adding bodily and behavioral expulsion-orientation to whole-I-scope adversarial frame-evaluation. The compound is more severe than either alone and connects to certain trauma-related configurations and to specific eating-disorder structures.

These compounds illustrate that the boundary-cluster, like other clusters, frequently combines with other operators in real configurations. The compositional schema (Section 5.7) provides the analytical apparatus for working through any specific compound.

## 9. The embodied-cluster: substrate-mediated affects

The embodied-cluster covers affects in which substrate-mediation is the dominant operator — affect-configurations that arise primarily from the substrate's signaling and conditioning rather than from Mode A boundary operations, Mode C extensions, reflexive PtCns evaluation, or PCE-over-projection as primary. Where the other four clusters are organized around specific structural relations (extension, reflexive frame-evaluation, projected futures, current boundary operations), the embodied cluster is organized around the *pathway-orientation* of the affect: substrate-dominant rather than cognition-dominant or relation-dominant.

The cluster's load-bearing architectural apparatus is *substrate mediation operating through third-order pathways*, with source-opacity tendency as a frequent but not universal feature. The foundation establishes the third-order pathway structure (Section 5.4, File 2): somatic and affective contents typically arrive through a substrate-then-cognitive pathway, with the substrate component running ahead of reflective access. The embodied cluster systematically uses this pathway-structure as load-bearing for its affects.

Three architectural distinctions matter for this cluster:

1. **Embodied does not mean source-opaque by definition.** Pain can be highly source-localized (the cut on the finger); acute physical disgust can have high κ to an obvious source. The substrate-dominance is the architectural commitment; source-opacity is a *frequent consequence* of substrate-dominance running ahead of reflective access, but it is not definitional. The architecture predicts source-opacity tendency in this cluster but does not require it for inclusion.

2. **Embodied does not mean all bodily content.** Routine proprioception, ordinary interoceptive sensation, and background bodily signals are not affect unless they perturb salience, action-readiness, profile stability, PCE, PtCns, or focus (Section 4.1 affect criterion). The architecture's commitment is that affect-as-such is a structural perturbation; bodily content that does not perturb is not affect even when it is consciously registered. Some substrate states are *affective conditions* without themselves being affects unless they meet the Section 4.1 criterion (sleep deprivation, hormonal/endocrine state, illness, medication effects, hunger, sensory overload may or may not meet the threshold case-by-case).

3. **Embodied affects shape conditions for other affects.** Mood, fatigue, somatic activation states, and other diffuse embodied configurations are not only affects in their own right but also background conditions that influence which other affects can arise and how they unfold. This is one of the cluster's most consequential features: the embodied state is partly constitutive of affective range available to the I at any given moment.

**The embodied cluster is orthogonal to the relation-clusters.** The first four clusters (connection, self, threat, boundary) are organized by relation to the maximally-self-defined I — extension, reflexive frame-evaluation, projected preservation-disruption, current boundary-operation. The embodied cluster is organized by *pathway-orientation* (substrate-dominant). This is not a defect of taxonomic uniformity but a structural feature: substrate-dominance can be primary (when it dominates an affect-configuration) or componential (when it supports a configuration whose primary operator is from another cluster). This explains why disgust and hate can have embodied components while remaining boundary-cluster primary, and why panic and trauma-cue activation are threat + embodied compounds rather than embodied-cluster primary.

**Primary-cluster assignment rule.** Throughout Sections 5–9, primary-cluster assignment is by *dominant operator*: extension (connection), reflexive evaluation (self), projected preservation-threat (threat), boundary-operation (boundary), or substrate-dominant pathway (embodied). Where multiple operators are co-dominant, the configuration is *compound* rather than forced into a single cluster. The compositional schema (Section 5.7) provides the analytical apparatus for compounds. This rule has been operating implicitly throughout the cluster work; it is stated explicitly here because the embodied cluster's orthogonality makes the rule's role clearer than it appeared earlier.

This section develops representative embodied-cluster affects, each analyzable through the derivation schema (Section 4.4). Pain, pleasure, trauma-cue activation, and diffuse somatic states are paradigmatic; many other embodied-relevant configurations (specific somatic affects, embodied compounds with other clusters) are addressable as variations or compounds of these primary cases.

### 9.1 Pain: substrate-mediated signal of damage or threatened damage

Pain is substrate-mediated affect-configuration arising from signaling of damage or threatened damage to the substrate-host. The configuration perturbs salience (focus is forced toward the pain-region), action-readiness (the I is oriented toward protection, treatment, escape), and PCE (the substrate-host is part of the maximally-self-defined I, so substrate damage is preservation-relevant).

**Derivation schema (pain).**

| Step | Pain (acute) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Substrate-host (specific region or generalized); maximally-self-defined I's preservation-orientation; focus allocation |
| Dominant relation | Substrate-mediated signaling perturbing salience, action-readiness, PCE-orientation |
| Active operators | Substrate mediation (primary); focus capture; PCE-orientation toward protection; sometimes Mode A toward source if external; PtCns may evaluate broader integration of pain into life-orientation |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | High κ to pain-region (often forced); φ varies (physical pain often source-clear; chronic pain may become source-opaque); $\Pi$ elevated for substrate-protection; $A$ typically conscious-clear at the level of pain-experience, conscious-fuzzy for pain's broader affective and meaning components |
| Healthy constraint | Pain corresponds to actual damage or threat; resolves with healing, treatment, or context-change |
| Pathology modes | Chronic pain (pain-signal whose persistence no longer maps cleanly onto an acute protective function); pain catastrophization (compound with threat-cluster); pain-fear avoidance (compound with fear leading to harmful disengagement); chronic pain becoming profile-feature; phantom pain (substrate signaling without external damage source) |

**Pain as paradigmatic substrate-dominant affect.** Pain is the architecture's clearest case of substrate-dominant affect-formation. The pain-signal arrives faster than reflective evaluation can process it; focus is captured before Mode B can construct alternative orientations; substrate-threat becomes PCE-relevant without requiring articulable analysis. The phenomenology of pain — its insistence, its inability to be ignored, its priority over other concerns — is the inside-perspective report of substrate-mediation operating with high force on the lived-I network.

**Pain-as-affect distinguished from pain-as-pure-sensation.** The architecture's affect criterion (Section 4.1) applies. A nociceptive signal that does not perturb salience, action-readiness, PCE, PtCns, or profile-organization is not affect in the architecture's sense — it is a substrate signal that passes without engaging the affective apparatus. Most actual pain crosses the affect threshold (it is hard to have nociception without salience-perturbation), but the architecture allows for the limit case where it does not. This matters for understanding configurations like chronic pain that has been integrated to the point of low affective perturbation, or experimental contexts where pain signals are minimally engaged.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Chronic pain* is pain-signal whose persistence no longer maps cleanly onto an acute protective function. The substrate continues signaling damage that has either healed, become permanent, or been transformed into a self-sustaining pain-pattern. Architecturally significant: chronic pain typically becomes a profile-feature, organizing the lived-I network around accommodation of the persistent signal. The maximally-self-defined I reorganizes around the pain rather than the pain being an event the I addresses and moves through. In disability, illness, and trauma contexts, chronic pain frequently compounds with self-cluster shame or guilt configurations (the I evaluates itself adversarially for the body's persistent damage, for needing accommodation, or for impacting others) — these are cross-cluster compounds, not features of pain-as-such, and require separate analysis.

- *Pain catastrophization* is pain compounded with threat-cluster projections of worsening. The pain-signal is registered, but the projection-toolset constructs worst-case future-states that amplify the affect beyond what the substrate-signal alone would produce. Architecturally a compound of embodied (substrate-mediated pain-signal) and threat (PCE-over-projection toward damage trajectories). The compound is more difficult to address than either alone because the threat-projection sustains the affective intensity even when the substrate-signal varies.

- *Pain-fear avoidance* is pain compounded with fear leading to harmful disengagement. The I avoids movement, activity, or contexts associated with pain, often producing secondary problems (deconditioning, social withdrawal, occupational impact). Architecturally: the threat-projection from pain becomes generalized, and the I's action-space narrows around pain-avoidance in ways that exceed what the actual pain-condition requires.

- *Phantom pain* is substrate signaling without external damage source — pain in amputated limbs, neuropathic pain without identifiable origin, central pain syndromes. Architecturally: the substrate-mediation pathway is generating pain-signals from conditions internal to the substrate rather than from specific damage. The pain is real (the affect is real, the suffering is real); the source-classification is "substrate-internal generation" rather than "external damage." This matters for treatment — addressing the apparent source does not address the actual mechanism.

### 9.2 Pleasure: substrate-mediated positive signal

Pleasure is substrate-mediated affect-configuration arising from positive signaling — reward, satisfaction, enjoyment, hedonic affect. The configuration perturbs salience toward the pleasure-source (the I is drawn to it), action-readiness (the I is oriented toward continuation, repetition, or deeper engagement), and Π (the source is elevated as a preservation-target for sustained engagement).

**Derivation schema (pleasure).**

| Step | Pleasure (healthy) |
|---|---|
| Profile-region | Substrate-host; the pleasure-source content; maximally-self-defined I's orientation toward the source |
| Dominant relation | Substrate-mediated positive signaling perturbing salience, action-readiness, Π-orientation |
| Active operators | Substrate mediation (primary); focus allocation toward source; sometimes Mode C (integration of source-relation); sometimes Mode B (constructing meaning of pleasure) |
| κ/φ/ρ/Π/A signatures | High κ to pleasure-source; φ varies (physical pleasure often source-clear; satisfaction-pleasure may be source-opaque or composite); $\Pi$ elevates source as preservation-target for engagement; $A$ typically conscious-clear |
| Healthy constraint | Pleasure-source supports the I's broader configuration (does not damage substrate, does not violate frame, does not displace other Π-relevant priorities); pleasure motivates appropriate engagement |
| Pathology modes | Anhedonia (reduced pleasure-response capability); addiction (pleasure-pathway captured by content that does not support I); hedonic adaptation pathologies (pleasure-seeking that no longer satisfies); compulsive pleasure-seeking (similar to compulsive awe-seeking, requires repeated pleasure-events for felt coherence) |

**Pleasure distinguished from joy-in-flourishing.** Section 5.6 treats joy-in-the-beloved's-flourishing as a connection-cluster affect: structural recognition that the extension is doing well, with the maximally-self-defined I in expansive state via the extension. Embodied-cluster pleasure is substrate-mediated reward signature. The two can co-occur (joy in a beloved's flourishing typically includes pleasure-component); they are architecturally distinct (joy-in-flourishing is structural-relational; pleasure is substrate-mediated). The distinction matters for pathology: a person with anhedonia (substrate-mediation damage) may still experience joy-in-flourishing (structural recognition) without pleasure-component, producing the configuration of "I recognize and affirm the flourishing, but the substrate-mediated pleasure-tone is absent."

**Anhedonia as candidate fifth profile-shape disturbance.** Anhedonia — sustained reduced or absent pleasure-response — has the architectural form of profile-shape disturbance: damaged structural equipment producing systematic output failure across contexts. Where dissolution damages the frame, severe depression damages the cogito/thought partition, learned helplessness damages the projection-apparatus, and chronic boredom damages the engagement-finding apparatus, anhedonia damages the *pleasure-response apparatus* — the substrate-mediated capacity to register positive signal from sources that should produce reward. The pleasure-response apparatus is not a new foundation primitive; it is an application-level functional bundle: substrate-mediated positive signaling, focus allocation toward reward-source, $\Pi$-elevation for sustained engagement, and the Mode B / Mode C capacities by which pleasure is integrated into meaning, repetition, or relational engagement. Not every reduced pleasure-state qualifies. Anhedonia becomes profile-shape disturbance only when the pleasure-response apparatus systematically fails across contexts where positive signal would ordinarily be available — disappointment, grief-muted pleasure, tiredness, satiety, or temporary low mood do not meet the threshold. The architecture extends the profile-shape disturbance set to a fifth named member when anhedonia meets this strict threshold:

| Disturbance | Damaged structural equipment | Typical output |
|---|---|---|
| Dissolution (Section 5.4) | Frame / PtCns boundary-coherence | Self-frame overridden, hollowed, or captured |
| Severe depression (Section 6.5) | Cogito/thought partition | Thought-content treated as constitutive of I |
| Learned helplessness (Section 7.6) | Projection-apparatus | Viable future pathways fail to appear |
| Chronic boredom (Section 8.4) | Engagement-finding apparatus | Available content fails to become Π-relevant |
| Anhedonia (Section 9.2) | Pleasure-response apparatus | Available reward-sources fail to produce positive signal |

Like the other profile-shape disturbances, anhedonia is recoverable but structurally demanding to recover from: the apparatus that would register reward is itself the damaged structure. Anhedonia frequently compounds with other profile-shape disturbances (especially depression and learned helplessness), and the compound is often more clinically prominent than any single component.

**Pathological forms.**

- *Anhedonia* (developed above) — substrate-mediated capacity to register positive signal is systematically reduced or absent.

- *Addiction* is the configuration in which a pleasure-pathway has been captured by content that does not support the I's broader configuration. Architecturally: substrate-mediated reward capture combined with distorted or unstable $\Pi$-ranking (the addictive content receives preservation/engagement priority disproportionate to its role in the maximally-self-defined I's coherent continuation), plus PtCns-evaluation that the content is harmful (often unsuccessful in producing differentiation against it because the substrate-pull and $\Pi$-distortion together override), plus often self-cluster shame about the addiction. Addiction is one of the cluster's most architecturally complex configurations because the substrate-mediation operates against the I's PtCns evaluation through $\Pi$-distortion, producing sustained internal conflict rather than coherent action. This is more than "pleasure vs judgment" — it is reward capture, $\Pi$-distortion, PtCns conflict, and (often) self-cluster compound all operating simultaneously.

- *Hedonic adaptation pathologies* are configurations in which sustained pleasure-seeking no longer produces satisfaction. The substrate-mediated reward apparatus has adapted (reduced response to repeated stimulation), but the I continues seeking. Architecturally: the operator is firing but no longer producing its functional output. Often compounds with compulsive pleasure-seeking.

- *Compulsive pleasure-seeking* is the configuration in which the I requires repeated pleasure-events to feel coherent, paralleling compulsive awe-seeking (Section 8.3). Pathology only when repeated pleasure becomes structurally necessary rather than enriching otherwise-stable coherence; ordinary love of pleasure is not compulsive pleasure-seeking.

### 9.3 Trauma-cue activation: substrate-mediated reactivation of imprinted patterns

Trauma-cue activation is the configuration in which substrate-mediated systems generate threat-relevant activation in response to cues that match patterns from past trauma, often without phenomenally-clear recognition by the I of either the cue or the historical source. Section 7.7 introduced trauma-cue activation as a multi-cluster compound (embodied + threat + sometimes self/boundary) and provided the worked compositional schema. Section 9 develops the substrate side: the architectural mechanism by which trauma-cue activation arises and persists.

**The osmotic imprinting basis.** Foundation Section 8.7 (File 3) establishes osmotic imprinting: ambient pattern-formation in the substrate that operates without explicit Mode B construction or articulable representation. Trauma is one of the conditions under which osmotic imprinting becomes load-bearing for later affect: the trauma-event imprints a pattern that the substrate later activates when matching cues are encountered. The pattern is not stored as articulable memory (or not only as that); it is encoded as substrate-mediated activation-readiness that fires in response to cue-matching without requiring the I to recognize either cue or source.

**The third-order pathway in trauma-cue activation.** The foundation's third-order pathway structure (Section 5.4, File 2) is paradigmatic for trauma-cue activation. The canonical sequence — common but not universal:

1. *Cue encounter.* External or internal content matches an imprinted trauma-pattern. The match operates substrate-side, often without phenomenal-clear recognition.
2. *Substrate activation.* The substrate generates threat-relevant activation: autonomic arousal, somatic readiness, often specific sensory-affective qualities (chest tightness, throat constriction, gut-drop, hypervigilance).
3. *Phenomenal registration.* The I notices something has changed — felt activation, mood-shift, anxiety-onset, panic, dissociation — but the source-pathway is not yet available.
4. *Projection construction.* The projection-toolset begins generating threat-content to organize the activation. The projected threat may or may not match the historical source; the projection is constrained by what current content the I can integrate as plausible threat-source.
5. *Phenomenal-clear affect.* The I now has organized affect (often anxiety, fear, panic, or compound configurations) with a phenomenally-clear felt quality but typically without source-pathway access to the original imprinting event.
6. *φ-construction (sometimes much later).* Through reflection, therapy, or insight, the I may eventually construct φ-pathway access to the original source — the trauma-event whose imprint the cue activated. This step is not automatic and is often the work of years.

This sequence is a *canonical pathway, not a required sequence*. At step 4, activation may organize through threat-projection (the canonical case), but it may also organize through dissociation, sensory-memory fragment, freeze response, shame-response, boundary-response, anger-response, or remain undifferentiated activation without specific organization. Projection construction is common but not necessary; the architecture predicts that the activation-organization step is shaped by the I's verification-toolset configuration, the specific imprinted pattern, and the current context, with multiple architecturally-available organization-paths.

**Why recognition-lag is severe in trauma-cue activation.** The pathway from cue to phenomenally-clear affect can be fast (seconds to minutes), but the pathway from phenomenally-clear affect to source-recognition is typically slow (hours, days, months, years) and may never complete without explicit work. The I has full phenomenal access to the activated state ($A = 1$, conscious-clear or conscious-fuzzy) while the source operates as unconscious-operative content ($A = 0$ for the imprinted source-pattern, even when its activation is producing $A = 1$ output). The split between phenomenal availability and source-pathway access (Section 6.1, File 3) is exactly what allows the architecture to handle this: the affect is fully real and fully conscious; the source is genuinely inaccessible from the I's current toolset.

**Pathological forms.**

- *PTSD configurations.* The architecture treats PTSD-spectrum configurations as patterns of trauma-cue activation that have stabilized into chronic features of the lived-I network. Specific cues reliably activate; the I has developed avoidance-patterns around the cues; the activation-pattern itself has become part of how the I's substrate operates. The architecture predicts that recovery will often require both source-recognition work and substrate-recalibration work; addressing only one will often leave part of the configuration intact.

- *Dissociation* is the configuration in which ordinary coupling among substrate activation, self-relation, boundary-location, and phenomenal integration is disrupted. $A$-status may be reduced (numbed-out variant), fragmented (parts-experience variant), displaced (depersonalization/derealization), or preserved as conscious-clear disconnection (the I has full phenomenal access to the experience of being disconnected). Architecturally: trauma-cue activation produces self/boundary-cluster compound features in which the I's normal coupling-mechanisms have been disrupted — typically as a developmentally-acquired protective response. Dissociation is *adjacent to, but not identical with*, the cogito/thought partition disturbances of Section 6.5: both involve altered self-relation, but they damage different structural couplings. The cogito/thought partition collapse compromises the partition between "I think" and "the contents of my thoughts"; dissociation disrupts couplings among substrate-activation, self-relation, boundary-location, and phenomenal integration. The two can co-occur but are structurally distinct configurations.

- *Hyperarousal as sustained substrate state* is the configuration in which the substrate-mediated activation system is operating at chronically elevated baseline. Acute trauma-cue activation occurs against an already-activated background, producing both increased frequency of cue-matching and increased intensity of response. The substrate-recalibration required for recovery operates against the chronic baseline.

- *Numbing as opposite pathology* is the configuration in which the substrate-mediated affective response system has been reduced or suppressed — the opposite failure mode from hyperarousal. The I experiences reduced affective range, including reduced positive affect, often as a protective response against trauma-cue activation. Compound with anhedonia (Section 9.2) and with certain dissociative features.

### 9.4 Diffuse somatic states: mood, fatigue, anhedonia, somatic dread

Diffuse somatic states are substrate-mediated affect-configurations that lack focal content but operate as sustained or pervasive backgrounds. Where pain, pleasure, and trauma-cue activation typically have specific source-content (even when source-opaque), diffuse somatic states are characterized by their *spread* across the lived-I network rather than focal anchoring.

**Mood.** Mood is sustained affective tone — a background coloring that influences which affects can arise, how intensely they manifest, and how they resolve. Section 9 treats *substrate-dominant mood-baselines*: moods whose dominant operator is diffuse substrate mediation. Other moods may be threat-maintained (sustained projected-damage producing a worried baseline), grief-maintained (continuing-bond extension producing a sad baseline), shame-maintained (chronic reflexive PtCns producing a heavy baseline), boundary-maintained (sustained Mode A producing an irritable baseline), or compound configurations involving multiple operators. Mood-as-condition-for-affects is general across all variants; embodied-cluster mood is the substrate-dominant variant treated here. Mood is itself an affect by the architecture's criterion (it perturbs salience, action-readiness, $\Pi$-weighting), but it is also a *condition* for other affects: the same external event produces different affective configurations depending on the mood-baseline against which it is encountered.

The architecture predicts that substrate-dominant mood-shifts are typically conscious-fuzzy — the I notices its mood without typically being able to articulate sources. Source-opacity is typical for substrate-dominant mood-shifts, though some moods have clear sources (especially the threat-maintained, grief-maintained, and shame-maintained variants where the mood is sustained by an articulable configuration). Pattern A or Pattern B onset trajectories (Section 7.2) are common for substrate-dominant moods.

**Fatigue.** Fatigue is substrate-state of reduced capacity, perturbing all affect-formation by reducing κ-formation capacity, reducing Mode B operation, narrowing focus-bandwidth, and making engagement-finding more difficult. Architecturally, fatigue is one of the clearest cases of an embodied affect that shapes conditions for other affects: a fatigued I has a structurally narrowed range of accessible affects, with certain configurations (intense Mode A, sustained Mode C, complex Mode B) less available or operating with reduced effectiveness.

The fatigue-restlessness compound from Section 8.6 is one common configuration; fatigue compounds with chronic boredom (the engagement-finding apparatus is doubly impaired), with anhedonia (the pleasure-response apparatus operates at reduced capacity when fatigue is high), and with depression (where fatigue is often a chronic feature).

**Anhedonia.** Already developed in Section 9.2 as a candidate fifth profile-shape disturbance. As a diffuse somatic state, anhedonia presents as sustained absence-of-pleasure-tone — not the presence of negative affect but the absence of positive affect, often experienced as flatness, dullness, or as inability to feel what the I conceptually recognizes should feel rewarding.

**Somatic dread.** Diffuse felt heaviness, foreboding, or unease without specific projected source. Distinct from focal dread (Section 7.3): focal dread has identified the projected damage; somatic dread has the dread-quality without the specific content. Architecturally: the substrate has registered something as threat-relevant and is generating threat-readiness, but the projection-toolset has not constructed (or has not yet constructed) specific threat-content. Often a recognition-lag case — the somatic dread precedes phenomenally-clear identification of what is being dreaded.

**Undifferentiated activation.** Heightened somatic activation (autonomic arousal, energy, restlessness, alertness) without articulable affect-classification. Conscious-fuzzy with high somatic component. Can be precursor to specific affects (the activation gets organized into anxiety, anger, excitement) or can persist as undifferentiated if the projection-toolset and Mode B do not construct specific content.

**Somatic flattening.** The opposite pole — reduced somatic engagement across affects, often presenting as numbness, deadness, disconnection-from-body, or reduced affective range generally. Compounds with anhedonia, with certain depressive configurations, and with dissociation. May be developmentally acquired or trauma-related.

**Contrast table for diffuse somatic and related states.** Because these states can blur in description, the architecture distinguishes them by primary failure or perturbation, focality, and typical phenomenal-availability status:

| State | Primary failure or perturbation | Focality | Common $A$-status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mood (substrate-dominant) | Baseline salience / $\Pi$ / action-readiness coloring | Diffuse | Usually conscious-fuzzy |
| Fatigue | Reduced substrate capacity / focus bandwidth | Diffuse | Source may be clear or fuzzy |
| Anhedonia | Reduced positive reward response | Diffuse or domain-specific | Conscious-clear absence; source variable |
| Somatic dread | Threat-readiness without focal projected source | Diffuse | Conscious-fuzzy |
| Undifferentiated activation | High arousal without affect classification | Diffuse | Conscious-fuzzy |
| Somatic flattening | Reduced embodied engagement / affective range | Diffuse | Conscious-clear or fuzzy |
| Numbing | Trauma-protective affect suppression | Diffuse or cue-linked | Variable |
| Dissociation | Disrupted self / boundary / phenomenal coupling | Variable | Reduced, fragmented, or conscious-clear disconnection |

The table prevents the diffuse-states category from collapsing into a single phenomenon. Each state has its own structural signature; compounds across them are common (anhedonia + somatic flattening; numbing + dissociation; fatigue + undifferentiated activation in certain trauma configurations) and are analyzable through the compositional schema (Section 5.7).

These diffuse states deserve emphasis because they are often the actual texture of affective life — much of what an I experiences day-to-day is not focal affects against neutral background but configurations of mood, fatigue, somatic activation, and embodied tone within which focal affects arise. Frameworks that focus only on focal affects miss this dimension; the architecture's commitment to the embodied cluster as a substantive cluster (rather than as a residue category) is meant to remedy that.

### 9.5 Embodied-cluster compound affects across other clusters

Embodied affects compound with affects from every other cluster. Several common compounds are worth treating explicitly:

**Embodied empathy lemma.** When apparent-I or inferential content represents an extension's substrate-state as damaged, threatened, or distressed, and the extension is sufficiently integrated into the maximally-self-defined I, the modeling I's substrate may generate a pain-like or distress-like resonance. This is *not* direct access to the beloved's substrate-state — the architecture maintains the foundation's commitment that the I has access to apparent-I and inferential contents, not to another modeling I's intrinsic substrate-states (Section 4, File 1). What occurs is *substrate-mediated resonance* with represented extension-state: the I's own substrate generates pain-like or distress-like signaling in response to its representations of the beloved's condition. The foundation explicitly leaves empathy and emotional contagion for application-level work; the embodied empathy lemma is the affect paper's application-level treatment, available wherever extension-integration is sufficient and substrate-resonance is supported by the I's lived-I network.

**Pain + connection-cluster (the pain of the beloved's pain).** When the beloved is in pain, the I that loves often registers a compound affect: substrate-mediated resonance via the embodied empathy lemma (the I's substrate generates pain-like or distress-like resonance in response to represented beloved-pain) plus connection-cluster fear-for-beloved (Section 5.2) plus sometimes anger toward whatever caused the pain. The compound is architecturally significant — love's structural commitment to extension-preservation produces substrate-resonance with represented beloved-state when the lived-I network supports such resonance.

**Pain + grief.** When physical pain compounds with the loss of an extension, the configurations interweave: grief-related somatic features (fatigue, body-heaviness, reduced engagement) compound with pain-signal; pain-related life-restructuring compounds with grief-related life-restructuring. Often produces sustained configurations that are difficult to address through any single-cluster intervention.

**Pleasure + connection-cluster (joy-in-relating).** Pleasure compounds with connection-cluster joy-in-flourishing or with active affectionate engagement to produce the embodied-relational configurations of family meals, lovemaking, communal celebration, deep conversation. The embodied component (substrate-mediated reward signal) and the connection component (extension-flourishing or active extension-engagement) are both real, and the compound is more than either alone.

**Anhedonia + chronic boredom.** When the pleasure-response apparatus is damaged AND the engagement-finding apparatus is damaged, the compound configuration is particularly debilitating. The I cannot find engagement with present content (chronic boredom) AND cannot register reward from such engagement as does occur (anhedonia). The compound is common in depressive configurations and is one of the architecture's predictions about why depression is so difficult to recover from: multiple profile-shape disturbances co-occur and reinforce each other.

**Fatigue + dissociation.** Sustained fatigue can lower the threshold for dissociative configurations or compound with them — the I has reduced substrate-resources for maintaining ordinary coupling among substrate-activation, self-relation, boundary-location, and phenomenal integration, and dissociative configurations may emerge or intensify. Sometimes a protective configuration; sometimes a precursor to more sustained dissociative pathology.

**Trauma-cue activation as multi-cluster compound** (developed in Section 7.7 and Section 9.3) — the central worked example of cross-cluster compound for this paper.

**Embodied + boundary-cluster compounds.** Disgust and hate both already include embodied components in their primary forms (Section 8.2 somatic-mediation in disgust; Section 8.5 substrate-mediated activation when hate-target is encountered). The architecture treats these as boundary-cluster primary with embodied components rather than as embodied-cluster compounds, because the dominant operator is boundary-rejection or sustained boundary-pathology with substrate as supporting rather than primary feature.

These compounds illustrate that the embodied cluster is not isolable from the rest of the affective taxonomy. Most actual affective configurations involve embodied components alongside operators from other clusters; the architecture's contribution is to provide vocabulary for analyzing the specific contributions of substrate-mediation within configurations that span multiple clusters.

## 10. Predictions and exclusions

This section consolidates the architecture's discriminating empirical commitments. The cluster work in Sections 5–9 developed specific configurations and pathologies; the architectural moves in Sections 3–4 developed the apparatus that makes these configurations classifiable. From those commitments, a set of empirical predictions follow that can be stated, derived, and (in principle) tested against what alternative frameworks would predict.

What counts as a discriminating prediction. Not every claim the architecture makes is discriminating. Many architectural commitments are consistent with several alternative accounts of affect — they are constraints on what *could* be the case rather than predictions about what *is* the case. The predictions developed here are stated where the architecture's commitments produce specific empirical expectations that competing frameworks would not, or that would, under specifiable evidence, require the architecture to be revised.

The section organizes predictions into thematic groups: foundational predictions about phenomenal access and structural change (10.1); predictions about apparatus-level pathology (10.2); predictions about cluster-structure (10.3); predictions about love-onset (10.4); predictions about embodied affect (10.5); predictions about agency and recovery (10.6); and a falsification summary (10.7). Each prediction is stated as a claim, with the architectural derivation, the falsification conditions, and (where particularly informative) the contrast with what alternative frameworks would predict. Section 11 develops the systematic comparison with alternative frameworks; Section 10 focuses on what the architecture itself commits to.

### 10.1 Predictions about phenomenal access and structural change

This group's load-bearing architectural feature is the foundation's split between phenomenal availability ($A_{m,t}$) and source-pathway access (φ) — the four-class taxonomy of conscious-clear, conscious-fuzzy/source-opaque, latent/retrievable, and unconscious-operative contents (Section 6.1, File 3). The split allows structural change to occur in conscious-fuzzy or unconscious-operative content before phenomenally-clear φ-pathway construction has caught up. Several predictions follow.

**Prediction 1 — Bidirectional recognition-lag.** In affective changes where profile-level integration, $\Pi$-elevation, PCE-orientation, or Mode A / Mode C propagation precedes phenomenally-clear φ-pathway construction, phenomenally-clear recognition should lag structural change. This applies in either direction: love-formation as well as love-ending, depression-onset as well as depression-recovery, trauma-pattern formation as well as resolution. The architecture predicts that under these conditions, outside observers, behavioral signatures, and somatic markers frequently anticipate the I's own phenomenally-clear report.

*Architectural derivation.* The structural change is often produced through conscious-fuzzy or unconscious-operative content (Section 3.3, Section 9.3). The I is doing the architectural work without phenomenally-clear access to the work being done. When the cumulative effect crosses some threshold, the structural state has changed; φ-pathway-construction to the result then occurs, producing phenomenally-clear recognition.

*Falsification conditions.* The prediction is conditional, not universal. It is predicted when profile-level integration, $\Pi$-elevation, PCE-orientation, or Mode C/Mode A propagation precedes phenomenally-clear φ-pathway construction. It is *not* predicted in deliberate-commitment cases (where φ is high throughout), in sudden conscious-clear recognition cases where the I's verification toolset tracks the crossing, or in cases where social role-language precedes actual integration. Evidence that would press the architecture: cases where the I's phenomenal report tracks structural change in real time across a variety of situations and individual configurations, with no systematic lag detectable by behavioral or physiological markers.

**Prediction 2 — Pattern A / Pattern B threshold dynamics.** Affective state-change occurs through one of two trajectories that are continuum endpoints rather than strict binaries: linear conscious-fuzzy buildup to focus threshold (Pattern A) or metastable unconscious-operative buildup to phase-change (Pattern B). Verification-toolset configuration partially predicts which trajectory an individual undergoes for a given affective change.

*Architectural derivation.* Pattern A: small precursor events register as conscious-fuzzy dissonance; accumulation is felt as sustained background; PtCns produces ongoing evaluation; eventually accumulated dissonance crosses focus threshold and produces phenomenally-clear recognition. Pattern B: small precursor events operate at unconscious-operative level; configuration is metastable; triggering event collapses metastability into instant phase-change. Two architectural features distinguish: focus allocation (where attention is directed determines what reaches conscious-fuzzy availability) and encapsulation (Mode A or Mode C events on well-encapsulated content do not propagate as felt content).

*Falsification conditions.* The architecture predicts that individuals with strong introspective verification tools (developed through therapy, contemplative practice, sustained self-attention) will tend toward Pattern A; individuals with weaker introspective tools or with toolsets developed in other domains will tend toward Pattern B. Same structural mechanism, different toolset configurations producing different phenomenologies. Toolset-development should be assessed *independently* through prior evidence: therapy history, contemplative practice, journaling or self-monitoring, demonstrated capacity to track source-opacity, ability to distinguish affect-tone from source, and observed Mode B self-attention under non-crisis conditions. Evidence pressuring the architecture: systematic mismatches between independently-assessed toolset configuration and observed onset-trajectory pattern; or evidence that Pattern A/Pattern B is determined by stable substrate or developmental variables that predict the trajectory better than independently-assessed verification-toolset configuration.

**Prediction 3 — Onset-trajectory variability.** The same profile-topological transition (constitutive extension forming, frame inconsistency exposing, projected damage registering, etc.) can produce different phenomenologies depending on verification-toolset configuration, the rate and pattern of cumulative integration, and whether the I has phenomenally-clear access to the configuration as it develops. Where Prediction 2 concerns the *threshold-route* (Pattern A linear buildup vs Pattern B metastable phase-change), Prediction 3 concerns the *phenomenological presentation* of the same underlying profile-topological transition: gradual awareness, recognition-lag, phase-change, sudden recognition of prior crossing, or deliberate consolidation. The two predictions are distinguishable: Prediction 2 commits to a specific threshold-mechanism continuum; Prediction 3 commits to phenomenological diversity over and above which threshold-mechanism is operative.

*Architectural derivation.* Section 3.6 develops this for love-onset specifically (gradual awareness, recognition-lag, Pattern B phase-change, sudden recognition of prior crossing, deliberate consolidation). The same variability is predicted for other state-changes: onset of depression can be gradual-with-awareness, gradual-with-lag, phase-change-triggered, or recognition-of-prior-state; anxiety onset shows analogous variability (Section 7.2); shame, anger, and other affects show toolset-configuration-dependent onset trajectories.

*Falsification conditions.* The architecture predicts that the variability is real and toolset-dependent. Evidence pressuring the architecture: same individual showing inconsistent onset-trajectories for structurally-similar affective changes that cannot be explained by changes in verification-toolset, focus allocation, or contextual factors the architecture identifies. Or systematic correlation between onset-trajectory and factors the architecture does not predict.

**Prediction 4 — Trauma recognition-lag asymmetry.** Trauma-cue activation should often show fast cue-to-affect transition (seconds to minutes from cue encounter to felt activation) and slow affect-to-source recognition (hours, days, months, or years from felt activation to phenomenally-clear identification of the trauma-source). The fast-and-slow asymmetry is architecturally specific.

*Architectural derivation.* Section 9.3 develops the six-step canonical pathway. The substrate-mediated activation pathway is fast; the φ-construction pathway from activated state back to imprinted source is slow because the source-pattern operates as unconscious-operative content while its activation produces conscious or conscious-fuzzy output. The split between phenomenal availability and source-pathway access is what allows the architecture to handle this: the affect is fully real and fully conscious; the source is genuinely inaccessible from the I's current toolset.

*Falsification conditions.* The architecture predicts that trauma-cue activation will typically show this asymmetry *when the cue-source is not already $A$-available*. Explicit reminders — a date, location, person, sound, or image whose connection to the trauma-source is already conscious-clear — may produce fast cue-to-source recognition without pressuring the architecture; the asymmetry-prediction applies specifically to source-opaque cue activation where the imprinted source-pattern operates as unconscious-operative content. Evidence pressuring the architecture: source-opaque trauma-cue activation that consistently produces fast source-recognition (immediate phenomenally-clear access to the imprinted event upon any cue activation) without prior φ-construction work, or systematic absence of the asymmetry in populations the architecture predicts should show it.

### 10.2 Predictions about apparatus-level pathology

This group's load-bearing architectural feature is the profile-shape disturbance set developed across Sections 5.4, 6.5, 7.6, 8.4, and 9.2 — apparatus-level damage to the I's structural equipment producing systematic output failures.

**Prediction 5 — Profile-shape disturbance set as quintuple.** The apparatus-level affect pathologies developed in this paper are distinguished by which structural equipment is damaged: frame and PtCns boundary-coherence (dissolution), cogito/thought partition (severe depression), projection-apparatus (learned helplessness), engagement-finding apparatus (chronic boredom), pleasure-response apparatus (anhedonia). Each is distinct from the others in its damaged equipment, its typical output, and its recovery dynamics. The quintuple is not claimed as an exhaustive taxonomy of all psychopathology; it is the current affect-paper set of apparatus-level profile-shape disturbances. Future application work may identify additional apparatus-level disturbances in domains this paper does not cover.

*Architectural derivation.* The five apparatuses are application-level functional bundles whose damage produces specific systematic failures. Recovery is structurally demanding because the apparatus that would do the recovering is itself damaged. The set is reserved for apparatus-level cross-context impairment, not target-specific pathological organization (which is *stable pathological profile-feature*, not profile-shape disturbance).

*Falsification conditions.* The architecture predicts that the affective components of depressive and dissociative configurations will sort into these apparatus disturbances or compounds with embodied / boundary / self-coupling disruptions. Evidence pressuring the architecture: a sustained pathological configuration that does not damage any of the five named apparatuses but otherwise matches the structure of profile-shape disturbance; or apparatus-level damage that does not affect any of the five but produces systematic affect-failure. Either would suggest the set needs expansion or revision.

**Prediction 6 — Compound profile-shape disturbance in depression.** Severe depressive configurations *as architecturally defined* (Section 6.5: cogito/thought partition compromise plus typically additional apparatus-level features, not every clinical presentation labeled depression) should typically involve multiple co-occurring apparatus-level disturbances that reinforce each other rather than a single isolated disturbance. The architecture predicts a typical compound: projection-apparatus failure + pleasure-response failure + engagement-finding failure + fatigue baseline + self-cluster pathology (cogito/thought partition compromise plus possible dissolution-cluster features). Compound architecture is available across other configurations as well; severe depression is not unique in being compound, but it is a paradigmatic case where multiple apparatus-level failures tend to reinforce one another.

*Architectural derivation.* Each of the apparatuses is needed for ordinary affect-formation; damage to one alters the conditions under which others operate. Pleasure-response failure (anhedonia) reduces the reward-signaling that would support engagement-finding; engagement-finding failure (chronic boredom) reduces the input that would test projection-apparatus; projection-apparatus failure (learned helplessness) eliminates the future-orientations that would motivate engagement-seeking. The compound is self-reinforcing rather than additive.

*Falsification conditions.* The architecture predicts that severe depression has compound rather than single-disturbance structure. Evidence pressuring the architecture: severe depression configurations consistently presenting with isolated apparatus-damage (only one of the five) but otherwise meeting the structural and clinical criteria for severe depression; or successful treatment that addresses only one apparatus and reliably resolves all features.

### 10.3 Predictions about cluster-structure

This group's load-bearing architectural feature is the cluster organization itself — affects classified by dominant operator rather than by ordinary-language category.

**Prediction 7 — Action-or-resolution pathway parallelism across clusters.** Available versus foreclosed action-or-resolution pathways produce structurally adjacent but distinct affects across multiple clusters: fear/dread (threat), hope/despair (threat), anger/helpless rage (boundary), boredom-with-re-engagement-pathway/chronic boredom (boundary), pain-with-adaptive-action/pain-fear avoidance (embodied). The same architectural axis (action-or-resolution pathway availability) recurs across cluster-internal pairs. The "action-or-resolution" framing accommodates that boredom and pain do not always involve action in the same sense as fear or anger; what matters architecturally is whether the I has access to a pathway that resolves or addresses the affective configuration (engagement, traction, adaptive response, or external action), not whether that pathway involves overt motor activity.

*Architectural derivation.* The action-pathway distinction is not specific to one cluster's apparatus; it is a feature of how PCE, Mode A, Mode C, and substrate-mediation interact with the action-space the I's toolset can construct. When the action-pathway is available, the affect motivates resolution-seeking; when foreclosed, the affect persists without resolution and tends toward profile-feature stabilization or compound pathology.

*Falsification conditions.* Evidence pressuring the architecture: cases where adjacent affect-pairs that should follow the action-pathway pattern do not (e.g., anger that consistently behaves the same way regardless of action-pathway availability, fear/dread distinction that does not track action-availability across populations).

**Prediction 8 — Dominant-operator classification.** Ambiguous affect labels should sort by dominant operator rather than by ordinary-language category. The architecture predicts that careful structural analysis of affects whose ordinary-language labels are ambiguous will reveal multiple distinct architectural configurations sharing the surface label.

| Ordinary label | Possible architecture |
|---|---|
| "Panic" | Embodied + threat compound; or sudden phase-change in anxiety; or dissociative break with threat features |
| "Numbness" | Embodied flattening; trauma numbing; dissociation; anhedonia; or compound configurations |
| "Hate" | Technical boundary pathology (Section 8.5); ordinary moral opposition; threat-response; grief-rage |
| "Boredom" | Boundary engagement-failure; embodied fatigue-driven disengagement; defensive disengagement; chronic profile-shape disturbance |
| "Despair" | Threat-cluster preservation-pathway absence; grief-despair after extension-loss; learned-helplessness output; embodied flattening compounded with projection failure; rage-despair compound when Mode A activates against an irreversible condition |

*Architectural derivation.* Different operators producing the same surface label have different structural implications — different recovery pathways, different compound vulnerabilities, different responses to clinical and developmental work.

*Falsification conditions.* The architecture predicts that empirical work distinguishing affect-configurations by structural rather than surface features will identify clinically and developmentally meaningful differences. Evidence pressuring the architecture: surface label-categories tracking outcomes more reliably than the architecture's structural categories, or structural-category distinctions that fail to predict clinically relevant differences in recovery, vulnerability, or compound formation.

### 10.4 Predictions about love-onset

This group's load-bearing architectural feature is the love-definition (Section 3.1) plus the onset-trajectories developed in Section 3.6.

**Prediction 9 — Love-at-first-sight readiness.** Reports of love-at-first-sight that later become structural love should typically reveal pre-encounter readiness conditions, narrowly defined: active relational templates, recent identity reorganization, salient future-projections involving partnership, unclosed prior extension-patterns, strong aesthetic or values schema, $\Pi$-orientation toward the kind of life-structure the encountered person represents. Generic attraction, openness to dating, or background partner-desire does not suffice as readiness.

*Architectural derivation.* Strict structural love at first sight is unlikely-to-impossible by the architecture's terms when "first sight" means a content-poor first encounter with a stranger. Phenomenological love-at-first-sight is real, but the architecture treats it as a compound of Pattern B phase-change of metastable readiness, template-match recognition, self-fulfilling phenomenology, and (in edge cases) fast constitutive extension where prior content or extended encounter supplies enough integratable material. The Pattern B reading specifically requires prior structural readiness that the encounter completes.

*Falsification conditions.* Evidence pressuring the architecture would be cases where content-poor first encounter produces immediate constitutive-extension topology — *not merely intense phenomenology, but the structural extension itself* — with prospective evidence (diaries, pre-encounter reports, friend or family observations, recorded behavior, prior template-language) showing no relevant pre-encounter readiness, no rich prior mediated content, and structural love present before any meaningful subsequent integration work could occur. Retrospective inquiry alone is weak evidence (memory reconstructs readiness after the fact); the falsifying evidence must be from before, during, or immediately after the encounter, not from later reflection. Cases of late-developing structural love are not falsifying because some integration work almost certainly occurred between first encounter and structural-love confirmation; the falsifying configuration is *structural extension before integration-work could have done it*.

**Prediction 10 — Template-match versus constitutive extension.** Immediate "love" phenomenology should often initially classify as template-match + projection + substrate activation, *not* yet as constitutive extension. Structural love should require subsequent integration unless prior mediated content or unusually rich first encounter supplies actual material for Mode C operation.

*Architectural derivation.* The strict structural definition (Section 3.1) requires constitutive-extension topology; this requires actual content for Mode C to integrate and PtCns testing of frame-coherence across the integration. A template-match recognition has the substrate-activation, focus-capture, and projection-construction of love but not yet the structural extension. Whether the configuration becomes love depends on what subsequent integration occurs.

*Falsification conditions.* Evidence pressuring the architecture: relationships that produce immediate constitutive-extension topology (PCE-orientation, $\Pi$-elevation as if for self-preservation, frame-organization around the extension) without intervening integration work. The architecture allows fast extension only as edge case requiring rich content; routine instant extension would press the account.

### 10.5 Predictions about embodied affect and cross-cluster dissociations

This group's load-bearing architectural feature is substrate-dominance through third-order pathways (Section 9 opening), plus the connection-cluster / embodied-cluster distinction that allows specific cross-cluster dissociations.

**Prediction 11 — Anhedonia / joy-in-flourishing dissociation.** Relational joy-in-flourishing (Section 5.6, connection-cluster) and substrate-mediated pleasure (Section 9.2, embodied-cluster) can dissociate. The architecture predicts that anhedonia subjects can experience joy-in-flourishing (structural recognition that the beloved is doing well) without the substrate-mediated pleasure-tone, producing the configuration: "I recognize and affirm the flourishing, but the substrate-mediated pleasure-tone is absent."

*Architectural derivation.* Joy-in-flourishing is structural-relational (the maximally-self-defined I in expansive state via the extension's flourishing); pleasure is substrate-mediated reward signature. Different operators, different pathways. Anhedonia damages the pleasure-response apparatus without necessarily damaging the connection-cluster operators that produce joy-in-flourishing.

*Falsification conditions.* Evidence pressuring the architecture would be anhedonia subjects *with independently preserved constitutive extensions* who cannot distinguish structural affirmation of the beloved's flourishing from absent pleasure-tone, or cases where relational joy-in-flourishing always tracks substrate-mediated pleasure such that no dissociation is phenomenologically or behaviorally detectable. The intact-extension control matters: anhedonia subjects whose connection-cluster operators are also damaged would not produce the dissociation, but that would not be evidence against the architecture — it would be evidence of compound disturbance.

**Prediction 12 — Embodied source-opacity gradient.** Substrate-dominant affects should show source-opacity tendency but not source-opacity necessity. Acute pain and acute physical disgust may be source-clear (high κ to obvious source); trauma-cue activation, somatic dread, substrate-dominant mood-shifts, undifferentiated activation, and certain panic configurations are more often source-opaque.

*Architectural derivation.* Substrate-dominance allows fast affect-formation that runs ahead of reflective access; whether the source is identifiable depends on whether the substrate-mediation pathway has accessible content (pain-region, contamination-source) or operates from imprinted patterns (trauma-cue) or generates from substrate-internal conditions without external referent (somatic dread, certain mood-shifts).

*Falsification conditions.* Evidence pressuring the architecture would be source-opacity patterns showing no systematic relation to substrate-dominance, $A$-status, third-order pathway involvement, or imprinted source-patterns. Source-opacity in non-embodied affects (shame, love-ending, grief, anxiety, internalized witness mechanisms) is *not* itself disconfirming, because source-opacity is not exclusive to embodied affect; it can arise through $A$/φ split dynamics across clusters. The architecture's specific commitment is the embodied-cluster *gradient*: substrate-dominance produces source-opacity tendency that should be visible across the configurations the architecture identifies as substrate-dominant.

**Prediction 13 — Mood baseline modulation of affect-formation.** The architecture does not merely predict that mood changes how strongly an event is felt. It predicts that mood-baseline can alter *which cluster-operator becomes dominant*, whether source-access is available, whether action-or-resolution pathway construction succeeds, $\Pi$-weighting, focus allocation, and compound-vulnerability. The same external event should produce different affective *configurations* (not just different intensities of the same configuration) depending on mood-baseline, fatigue, hyperarousal, somatic flattening, anhedonia, or other embodied conditions active at the time of the event — forming as fear, dread, anger, helpless rage, grief, shame, numbness, or compound configuration depending on the embodied baseline in operation.

*Architectural derivation.* Embodied affects shape conditions for other affects (Section 9 distinction 3). The substrate's contribution to the lived-I network's current operating-state alters which operators can fire, how intensely, and how they resolve. Mode A vs Mode C selection, projection-toolset availability, and the action-or-resolution pathway's accessibility all depend on embodied conditions. The same Mode A event over the same content produces different configurations under different mood-baseline conditions; the same loss produces different grief-trajectories under different fatigue and somatic-state baselines.

*Falsification conditions.* The architecture's specific claim distinguishes it from generic mood-congruence accounts (which predict that mood biases evaluation valence) and from generic intensity-modulation accounts (which predict mood amplifies or dampens existing affect-formation). Evidence pressuring the architecture: same affective configurations forming reliably regardless of embodied state; or systematic predictability of affect-formation purely from event-features and mood-valence without reference to embodied baseline's effect on dominant-operator availability, source-access, action-or-resolution-pathway construction, or compound-vulnerability. If mood operates only as intensity-modulator or valence-bias, the architecture's stronger commitment requires revision.

### 10.6 Predictions about agency and recovery

This group's load-bearing architectural feature is the verification-toolset apparatus that has appeared throughout the cluster work plus the agency-and-choice considerations developed in working notes (candidate for future application paper).

**Prediction 14 — Toolset-mediated live-option expansion and recovery.** The architecture predicts that genuine inside-perspective agency-expansion — increase in the option-space available to the I for choice and action — is *mediated by* toolset-expansion: the I's choice-space expands when its verification-toolset can construct, recognize, and inhabit alternatives as live options. External affordance-expansion (a door is unlocked, a resource becomes available, a constraint is removed) can increase practical opportunity, but it becomes lived agency-expansion only when the I can represent and inhabit the new option-space. Therapy, contemplative practice, exposure to alternative frames, education, and developmental work all operate by adding options the I can newly perceive as live alternatives.

*Architectural derivation.* Choice operates over alternatives the I's toolset can construct as live options; what the toolset cannot construct is not in the choice-space. The bound-of-choice is set by the toolset, not by the I's will alone. The architecture predicts that agency-feeling (sense of being able to choose) tracks toolset-capacity rather than tracking will-strength alone, and that recovery from profile-shape disturbances (which damage the toolset that constructs alternatives) is mediated by structural toolset-rebuilding; propositional assent alone is insufficient unless it changes live-option construction, projection-capacity, frame-coherence, engagement-finding, pleasure-response, or self-relation.

This prediction connects to recovery dynamics across the profile-shape disturbance set: dissolution recovery requires re-establishing frame-coherence equipment; severe-depression recovery requires rebuilding cogito/thought partition function; learned-helplessness recovery requires re-training projection-apparatus through scaffolded experience; chronic-boredom recovery requires rebuilding engagement-finding apparatus; anhedonia recovery requires re-establishing pleasure-response apparatus. In each case, *the apparatus that would do the recovering is itself the damaged structure* — recovery proceeds despite that constraint by external scaffolding that substitutes for the damaged apparatus until it can rebuild.

*The belief-revision distinction.* Belief-revision *that does not change live-option construction, projection-capacity, frame-coherence, engagement-finding, pleasure-response, or self-relation* is propositional assent — the I says it accepts a proposition without the toolset acquiring new constructible alternatives. Belief-revision that does change any of these is already toolset-expansion in architectural terms. The distinction matters because clinical and developmental work often produces propositional assent that does not yet translate to toolset-change; the architecture predicts that such assent does not yet produce agency-expansion or recovery, and that the work of converting assent to toolset-change is the actual recovery-work.

*Falsification conditions.* Evidence pressuring the architecture: recovery from profile-shape disturbances reliably proceeding through propositional assent alone (without changes to live-option construction, projection-capacity, frame-coherence, engagement-finding, pleasure-response, or self-relation); or agency-expansion occurring without corresponding toolset-expansion in any of the dimensions the architecture identifies, suggesting the bound-of-choice operates by mechanisms the architecture does not identify.

### 10.7 Falsification summary

Across all fourteen predictions, several themes emerge for what would press the architecture:

- *Recognition-without-lag in conditions where the architecture predicts lag* — would suggest the $A$/φ split is doing less work than the architecture commits to.
- *Apparatus-isolation in profile-shape disturbances* — single-apparatus damage producing what the architecture identifies as compound disturbance, particularly in severe depression, would suggest the disturbance set is over-specified.
- *Surface-label-tracking outcomes more reliably than dominant-operator-tracking* — would suggest the cluster organization is not doing diagnostic work.
- *Love-formation without readiness or integration* — would press the love-onset apparatus.
- *Recovery without toolset-expansion* — would suggest agency-bound mechanisms the architecture does not identify.
- *Identical affect-formation regardless of embodied baseline* — would press the embodied-as-condition commitment.

The architecture does not claim immunity to pressure from any of these. It claims that the pressure has not yet been applied with the discriminating evidence the architecture's predictions invite, and that the application of such evidence is the appropriate empirical engagement with the architecture's commitments.

A note on negative predictions. The architecture also makes negative predictions — claims about what should *not* be observed if the architecture is correct. Several of these have been embedded in cluster sections: structural love at first sight in strict content-poor conditions should not occur; PtCns reflexivity should not be structurally analyzable as evaluating the bare cogito itself (reports that appear to target the bare I — "I hate my existence," "I cannot stand being" — should decompose into evaluation of lived-I contents, self-representations, or whole-I profile organization rather than the bare cogito as such); panic should not be classifiable as semi-conscious or partial-consciousness (it is fully conscious but source-opaque); helpless rage should not behave like ordinary anger-resolution (the architectural treatment is closer to grief-reorganization); and dehumanization-structures should not be reducible to ordinary moral disagreement (moralized disgust toward persons is operator-domain misapplication, structurally distinct from moral disgust toward acts).

These negative predictions function symmetrically with the positive predictions: confirming evidence supports the architecture; disconfirming evidence presses it. The architecture's claim is not that it cannot be wrong; it is that the configuration of commitments developed across Sections 2–9 produces specifiable empirical content that empirical inquiry can engage with.

### 10.8 Exclusions and disclaimers

The architecture as developed in this paper does not claim to derive several phenomena it has used or referenced:

- *Empathy and emotional contagion* — explicitly named as application-level work in the foundation; the embodied empathy lemma in Section 9.5 is the affect paper's application-level treatment, not a foundation-given derivation.
- *Specific empirical bounds* — the architecture does not derive numerical bounds on multiplicity of constitutive extensions, intensity scales, or temporal parameters of affect-formation. These are application-level discoveries, not architectural derivations.
- *Specific mode-selection mechanisms* — the architecture does not predict whether Mode A or Mode C operates in a given encounter (foundation Section 4.5); it predicts what follows once one operates. Mode-selection mechanisms remain underdeveloped as application-level work.
- *Detailed Π-formalization* — the foundation defers Π-magnitude formalization to application work; this paper develops Π-position language but does not formalize Π-magnitudes numerically.
- *Substrate-internal mechanisms* — the architecture treats the substrate as functional pathway-source for affect; specific substrate-mechanism details (neural, hormonal, autonomic) are not architectural commitments. The architecture is implementation-neutral on substrate mechanism while committing to substrate-mediation as an architectural feature.
- *Agency phenomenology* — Section 10.6 treats agency structurally as live-option construction within the I's toolset. It does not derive the felt phenomenology of willing, effort, responsibility, authorship, or choice-experience; those remain future application-level work. The foundation explicitly lists agency phenomenology among its undefined application-level domains.
- *Operationalization and measurement protocols* — Section 10 states discriminating empirical predictions but does not supply measurement instruments for $A$-status, $\Pi$-position, toolset-capacity, profile-shape disturbance, or constitutive-extension topology. Empirical testing of the architecture's predictions requires development of such instruments; this is application-level work that future papers must address.
- *Verification-toolset development* — Predictions 2 and 14 rely on toolset-development as a key explanatory variable, but the paper does not formalize toolset-development's mechanics: how toolsets are acquired, which developmental conditions produce which toolset-types, how toolset-rebuilding proceeds in recovery contexts. These remain underdeveloped and are flagged for future application work.

These exclusions are not weaknesses but methodological discipline: the architecture commits to what its apparatus supports and explicitly leaves to application work or future foundation extension what its apparatus does not support. Section 12 develops the open problems further.

## 11. Translations: positioning the architecture against alternative frameworks

This section positions the architecture's predictions against what alternative frameworks would predict on the same phenomena. The strategy is not to claim that the architecture is better than the alternatives, nor to defend the alternatives against architectural commitments. The strategy is to identify *where the architecture's predictions diverge from each alternative's predictions* — and to identify those divergences clearly enough that empirical work can distinguish them.

The section organizes the comparison in three parts. Section 11.1 provides a comparison matrix positioning the architecture against seven major frameworks: basic emotion theory, appraisal theory, constructionism, affective neuroscience, phenomenology, interoceptive predictive coding, and attachment theory. Section 11.2 discusses each framework in turn: what the architecture can translate into that framework, what it refuses to reduce to that framework, and which Section 10 predictions distinguish them. Section 11.3 provides a prediction-by-prediction discriminator table.

Two methodological notes. First, the alternatives are presented in their canonical or representative form; specific researchers within each tradition may hold positions that differ from the framework's modal commitments, and the architecture's relation to those specific positions may differ from its relation to the framework's modal form. Second, the architecture is compatible with elements of every framework discussed; the comparison is about *load-bearing* commitments that distinguish the architecture, not about the inability to translate at all.

*Compatibility does not imply equivalence.* A framework may redescribe part of the architecture's account while still lacking the architecture's load-bearing classificatory object: constitutive-extension topology, profile-shape disturbance, $A$-status, $\Pi$-position, PtCns / PCE operation, $A$/φ separation, or dominant-operator classification. Translation into a framework does not mean the framework contains the architecture's classificatory object.

### 11.2 Framework-by-framework discussion

#### 11.2.1 Basic emotion theory

Basic emotion theory treats discrete evolved emotions (variations on happiness, sadness, anger, fear, disgust, surprise, contempt) as the fundamental categories of affect, each with a specific facial-expression signature, an autonomic profile, and an adaptive function shaped by evolutionary history.

*Translation into the framework.* Many architectural configurations have basic-emotion analogues. Boundary-cluster Mode A often produces what basic emotion theory calls anger or contempt; threat-cluster PCE-over-projection produces what basic emotion theory calls fear or anxiety; embodied disgust corresponds to what basic emotion theory calls disgust; certain connection-cluster compounds approximate sadness or grief; pleasure plus joy-in-flourishing approximate happiness. The architecture does not deny that these correspondences exist, and the substrate-mediated components of several configurations have signatures consistent with what basic emotion theory describes.

*Refused reduction.* The architecture refuses to treat ordinary emotion labels as the natural categories of affect. Prediction 8 (dominant-operator classification) commits the architecture to specific divergence here: ambiguous labels like "panic," "numbness," "boredom," "hate," and "despair" should sort by dominant operator into multiple structural configurations, each with different recovery dynamics, compound vulnerabilities, and clinical/developmental implications. Basic emotion theory has no apparatus for this distinction; the same ordinary label that maps to one architectural configuration in one case may map to a different configuration in another case.

*Discriminating predictions.* Most directly: Prediction 8 (dominant-operator classification) and Prediction 5 (profile-shape disturbance set as quintuple). A basic-emotion taxonomy does not by itself predict the architecture's apparatus-level decomposition of severe depressive configurations into mutually reinforcing profile-shape disturbances (Prediction 6). Empirical work distinguishing these would track outcomes by structural-disturbance profile rather than by symptom severity or ordinary label alone.

#### 11.2.2 Appraisal theory

Appraisal theory treats affect as the result of cognitive evaluation of stimulus relative to the I's goals, concerns, and coping resources. The classical sequence (Scherer, Roseman, Frijda) involves checks for relevance, implications for goals, coping potential, and normative or self-significance, with affect-quality determined by the pattern of checks.

*Translation into the framework.* Several architectural operations have appraisal-like features. PtCns evaluation against frame parallels normative-significance appraisal. PCE-over-projection involves evaluating represented future states against preservation-relevance, which parallels goal-relevance appraisal. The action-or-resolution-pathway distinction (Prediction 7) parallels coping-potential appraisal. Where the appraisal sequence operates over Mode B-constructed projections, the architecture's threat-cluster apparatus has substantial overlap with appraisal-theoretic accounts of fear and dread.

*Refused reduction.* The architecture refuses to treat appraisal as the primary mechanism. PCE, PtCns, Π, and constitutive-extension topology are *structural commitments of the maximally-self-defined I* — they shape what counts as goal-relevant, coping-relevant, or normatively significant before any appraisal-process operates. The I does not appraise content against pre-existing goals; the I's profile-topology partly determines what counts as a goal in the first place. Constitutive extension makes another's flourishing self-relevant by profile-topological inclusion rather than by ordinary goal-evaluation. Appraisal theory can model the beloved's flourishing as goal-relevant; the architecture claims a different structural basis for that relevance — not just that the flourishing matters as a goal, but that the extension is *inside* the maximally-self-defined I.

*Discriminating predictions.* Prediction 1 (bidirectional recognition-lag) distinguishes most directly: sophisticated appraisal theories (Scherer's component-process model, Moors-style automatic appraisal) can allow pre-reflective or automatic appraisal occurring before explicit labeling. The architecture's distinctive claim is narrower: recognition-lag follows from the $A$/φ split and profile-level change preceding phenomenally-clear source-pathway construction, not merely from appraisal occurring before verbal labeling. Prediction 9 and Prediction 10 (love-onset readiness and template-match versus constitutive extension) commit to structural-topological commitments that appraisal theory does not articulate. Prediction 4 (trauma recognition-lag asymmetry) predicts a fast-and-slow asymmetry in trauma-cue activation that follows from imprinted source-patterns operating as unconscious-operative content; appraisal-theoretic models — even those allowing automatic appraisal — would not predict the specific asymmetry in trauma-cue activation that the architecture's $A$/φ split produces.

#### 11.2.3 Constructionism

Constructionism (Barrett's "theory of constructed emotion," following Russell's core-affect work) treats specific emotions as constructed by applying learned concepts to core-affect (valence × arousal) states in context. The natural categories are not basic emotions but rather core affect plus the conceptual structures the I has acquired for parsing affective experience.

*Translation into the framework.* The architecture shares constructionism's rejection of basic emotions as natural kinds. Embodied-cluster substrate-mediated baseline corresponds approximately to core affect; mood baseline modulation (Prediction 13) operates partly through what constructionism calls core-affect-baseline. Mode B construction over substrate-state has parallels to constructionism's concept-application step.

*Refused reduction.* The architecture refuses to treat affect as core-affect-plus-concept. The structural primitives (constitutive extension, frame-coherence, profile-shape, PtCns reflexivity, Π-position) are not concepts applied to core affect — they are structural relations between content and the maximally-self-defined I. Joy-in-flourishing is not core-affect-plus-"joy-concept-applied-to-extension-flourishing"; it is structural recognition that an extension is doing well, with the I in expansive state via the extension. Anhedonia is not "concept of pleasure absent"; it is apparatus-level damage to the pleasure-response apparatus.

*A note on contemporary constructionism.* This comparison treats constructionism in its canonical or compressed form (core affect + concept-application). Contemporary constructionist work — particularly Barrett's recent development — gives "concept" a substantially broader role: concepts may be embodied, situation-specific, socially scaffolded, predictive, and embedded in dynamic brain-body-world interaction. The architecture's refusal of reduction is not that constructionism lacks rich concepts; it is that constitutive-extension topology, PtCns / PCE operation, $\Pi$-position, and profile-shape disturbance are not merely concept-applications to core affect, however broadly "concept" is understood. The architecture's primitives are profile-topological relations and structural prerogatives, not constructions over a substrate state.

*Discriminating predictions.* Prediction 11 (anhedonia / joy-in-flourishing dissociation) is the most directly discriminating: contemporary constructionism may accommodate dissociation between hedonic tone and broader affective-conceptual configuration through situation-specific concept variation, but the architecture-specific claim is that this dissociation follows from *distinct cluster-operators* — connection-cluster joy-in-flourishing as relational-structural recognition, embodied-cluster pleasure as substrate-mediated reward signature — operating with architecturally separable apparatus. Prediction 5 (profile-shape disturbance set) commits to apparatus-level pathologies that constructionism does not have framework-internal apparatus for; constructionism may have rich resources for psychopathology generally without deriving the architecture's apparatus-level quintuple. Prediction 9 (love-at-first-sight readiness) commits to specific structural readiness conditions; contemporary constructionism can explain rapid love-phenomenology through situation-specific concept construction, while the architecture adds the stronger readiness-and-topology constraint for *structural* love (as opposed to phenomenology of love).

#### 11.2.4 Affective neuroscience

Affective neuroscience (Panksepp's "primary process emotional systems": SEEKING, FEAR, RAGE, LUST, CARE, PANIC/GRIEF, PLAY) treats affect as implemented in shared mammalian subcortical neural circuits. Specific affects correspond to activation in specific circuits; cross-species commonalities reflect shared evolutionary substrate.

*Translation into the framework.* The architecture's substrate-mediation commitment is consistent with affective neuroscience's emphasis. Embodied-cluster affects in particular are typically implemented through the substrate pathways affective neuroscience studies. The PANIC/GRIEF system has resonance with the architecture's grief and trauma-cue treatments; the FEAR system with threat-cluster substrate components; the CARE system with embodied components of love and parental care; the SEEKING system with engagement-finding apparatus and Π-orientation.

*Refused reduction.* The architecture is *implementation-neutral* on neural substrate while committing to substrate-mediation as a functional architectural feature. Affect identity is at the architectural-operator level (extension, projection, boundary-operation, substrate-pathway-dominance), not at the neural-circuit level. Two configurations sharing the same neural circuit may be architecturally distinct (panic-like separation-distress and grief-related substrate signatures may overlap at the circuit-family level, while the architecture distinguishes them structurally — panic is threat + embodied, grief is connection-cluster Mode A or Mode C compound). Two configurations differing in neural circuit may be architecturally similar (different substrate pathways may all serve threat-cluster PCE-over-projection).

*Discriminating predictions.* Prediction 8 (dominant-operator classification) predicts that affects sharing substrate-circuit families will sort into multiple architectural configurations: substrate systems may group together configurations that are architecturally distinct. For example, separation-distress / panic-like and grief-related substrate signatures may overlap at the circuit level (the PANIC/GRIEF system in Panksepp's taxonomy), while the architecture distinguishes threat + embodied panic from connection-cluster grief by dominant operator and profile-region. Prediction 7 (action-or-resolution pathway parallelism) predicts that the same architectural axis recurs across several substrate-different configurations (fear/dread, hope/despair, anger/helpless rage), which affective neuroscience would not predict to be architecturally parallel because they are implemented in different circuits.

#### 11.2.5 Phenomenology

Phenomenology (Sartre's "magical transformation of the world," Heidegger's *Befindlichkeit* and mood-as-disclosure, Merleau-Ponty's embodied perception, Ratcliffe's existential feelings) treats affect as lived structure analyzable from first-person perspective. Affect is not internal state but rather the I's structural relation to world, others, and itself.

*Translation into the framework.* The architecture is closest to phenomenology at the level of first-person structural object: affect as lived relation of the I to world, self, others, body, and possibility. The maximally-self-defined I, constitutive extension, profile-topology, lived-I network, and the $A$/φ split all have phenomenological roots. Many architectural configurations developed in the cluster work could be re-described in phenomenological language: dissolution as the collapse of the I's frame-coherent disclosure; trauma-cue activation as the substrate's pre-reflective seizing of the I's lived field; love as the topological inclusion of another within the I's lived-self.

*Refused reduction.* The architecture is not opposed to phenomenology; it attempts to develop phenomenology's structural insights with formal apparatus. *The kinship is methodological and object-level, not total.* Phenomenology supplies the nearest vocabulary for lived structure; the architecture departs by formalizing that structure into explicit primitives (PCE, PtCns, Π, ρ, Mode A/B/C, $A$/φ four-class taxonomy, profile-topology) and by deriving falsifiable predictions in Section 10. The architecture is not simply phenomenology with notation. It adds formal primitives, prediction discipline, and falsification conditions that many phenomenologists would not treat as phenomenological method. Closeness in object-level analysis does not mean phenomenology was waiting to be formalized; the architecture's predictive-empirical commitments represent a real break from how most phenomenology operates.

*Discriminating predictions.* Most Section 10 predictions are friendly to phenomenology in the sense that phenomenology could endorse the structural commitments without contradiction. The architecture distinguishes itself by making the predictions specifiable and testable. Prediction 5 (profile-shape disturbance set as quintuple), Prediction 11 (anhedonia / joy-in-flourishing dissociation), Prediction 12 (embodied source-opacity gradient), and Prediction 14 (toolset-mediated agency / recovery) are particular cases where phenomenological description could be extended into the architecture's testable form. The architecture is in dialogue with phenomenology rather than in competition — but it is also in real methodological departure where it commits to falsification as a methodological norm.

#### 11.2.6 Interoceptive predictive coding

Interoceptive predictive coding (Seth's emotional self, Friston-influenced active-inference accounts) treats affect as the I's predicted body-state under predictive coding. Allostatic regulation drives affect; emotion is inferred state of body-and-world; mood is predicted-state baseline.

*Translation into the framework.* The architecture's embodied cluster has substantial overlap with interoceptive predictive coding. Substrate-mediation as architectural feature is consistent with interoceptive prediction's allostatic emphasis. Embodied source-opacity gradient (Prediction 12) parallels what interoceptive predictive coding describes as the inferential nature of interoceptive content. Mood baseline modulation (Prediction 13) parallels the predicted-state-baseline framing.

*Refused reduction.* The architecture refuses to reduce affect to interoceptive prediction. Connection-cluster joy-in-flourishing is not primarily interoceptive prediction; it is structural recognition that an extension is doing well — a relation between the I and the represented state of the extension, not a prediction about the I's body. Threat-cluster PCE-over-projection involves projection-toolset operations beyond interoceptive prediction; the projected futures are about the I's preservation-orientation toward represented future-states, which are not body-states. Boundary-cluster Mode A is interpersonal/normative, not interoceptive.

*A note on contemporary active inference.* Recent extensions of active inference — Friston-influenced social active inference, accounts of collective dynamics, shared generative models, social affordances, and culturally situated agency — explicitly model relational, social, and normative content as generative-model variables. The architecture's contrast with these extensions is not that they cannot model relational and social contents (they can), but that they do not classify affect by *constitutive-extension topology, PtCns / PCE operation, or profile-shape disturbance* as primary classificatory objects. Affect-identity at the architectural-operator level differs from affect-identity at the level of generative-model variables, even when those variables include social and relational content.

*Discriminating predictions.* Prediction 11 (anhedonia / joy-in-flourishing dissociation): interoceptive predictive coding and active-inference extensions can accommodate dissociation between relational valuation and hedonic tone through generative-model differentiation, but the architecture-specific claim is narrower — that this dissociation follows from *distinct cluster-operators*: connection-cluster joy-in-flourishing as relational-structural recognition, embodied-cluster pleasure as substrate-mediated reward signature, with architecturally separable apparatus. Prediction 9 (love-at-first-sight readiness) commits to readiness conditions that go beyond interoceptive baseline — active relational templates, salient future-projections involving partnership, life-stage Π-orientation; active inference can in principle model these as generative-model priors, but it does not classify affect by the architecture's primitives. Prediction 13 (mood baseline modulation) is sharper than the predictive-coding form: the architecture predicts mood changes *which cluster-operator becomes dominant*, source-access availability, and action-or-resolution pathway construction — not just predicted-baseline-shift.

#### 11.2.7 Attachment theory

Attachment theory (Bowlby's evolutionary-relational framework, Ainsworth's strange-situation work, Main and Hesse's adult attachment research, Mikulincer and Shaver's regulation accounts) treats relational bonds as developmentally-acquired patterns that shape how the I regulates affect, particularly love, fear-of-abandonment, grief, and threat-to-attachment. Attachment styles (secure, anxious-preoccupied, avoidant-dismissive, disorganized) have predictive force across the lifespan.

*Translation into the framework.* The architecture's connection-cluster has substantial overlap with attachment-theoretic accounts of love and relational regulation. Constitutive extension has resonance with attachment theory's account of internalized other; insecure attachment patterns have analogues in difficult constitutive-extension trajectories (anxious patterns produce frame-stress around extensions; avoidant patterns produce reduced extension-formation; disorganized patterns produce fragmented or contradictory extensions). The pain of beloved's pain (Section 9.5) has resonance with attachment-theoretic accounts of relational distress.

*Refused reduction.* The architecture refuses to reduce love and connection-cluster affects to attachment patterns. Constitutive extension is architecturally specific: $X$ is *inside* the maximally-self-defined I, with PtCns-preserved frame-coherence, with Π-position invariance under non-reorganizing variation. Attachment is a *major developmental-relational structuring system* — internal working models, attachment hierarchy, secure-base behavior, separation-response, and regulation style — that can shape the architecture's verification tools, extension-readiness, $\Pi$-weighting, regulation patterns, and vulnerability to specific connection-cluster pathologies. The architecture's claim is that these structures influence constitutive-extension topology without being identical to it. Two people with the same attachment style may have very different constitutive-extension topologies; one person can love one extension constitutively while attachment-relating to another differently. Attachment does not merely supply a variable; it supplies a partial structural analogue — but a partial one. Constitutive extension is not the sum of attachment patterns.

*Discriminating predictions.* Prediction 9 (love-at-first-sight readiness) commits to narrowly-defined readiness conditions that go beyond attachment style: aesthetic/values templates, salient future-projections, life-stage Π-orientation. Attachment theory predicts attachment-style-dependent vulnerability to fast bonding; the architecture predicts more specific structural readiness that may be present in one attachment style and absent in another. Prediction 5 (profile-shape disturbance set) commits to apparatus-level pathologies that attachment theory does not have framework-internal apparatus for — anhedonia, learned helplessness, dissolution as profile-shape disturbances are not attachment-theoretic categories. Prediction 14 — toolset-mediated live-option expansion and recovery — predicts a unified recovery-architecture across all five profile-shape disturbances, which attachment theory's earned-secure-attachment recovery-frame does not span.

### 11.4 Summary

The architecture is not a competitor to the alternative frameworks in the sense of replacing them. It is in dialogue with each. With basic emotion theory, the architecture refuses to treat ordinary labels as natural categories while accepting that substrate-mediated affects have signatures consistent with what basic emotion theory describes. With appraisal theory, the architecture insists on structural commitments preceding appraisal-process while accepting that appraisal-like operations occur within architectural configurations. With constructionism, the architecture shares the rejection of basic-emotion natural kinds while committing to structural primitives constructionism does not provide. With affective neuroscience, the architecture is implementation-neutral while committing to substrate-mediation. With phenomenology, the architecture is in close kinship while developing formal apparatus phenomenology has typically not articulated. With interoceptive predictive coding, the architecture endorses substrate-pathway emphasis while refusing to reduce non-embodied operators to interoceptive prediction. With attachment theory, the architecture treats attachment as a major developmental-relational structuring system that can shape constitutive-extension availability, stabilization, resistance, and distortion, while refusing to identify attachment style with constitutive-extension topology.

The architecture's discriminating force comes from its specific structural primitives: constitutive-extension topology; frame-coherence preservation under PtCns; profile-shape and profile-topology as load-bearing classificatory objects; the $A$/φ four-class taxonomy with $A$/φ separation as a load-bearing distinction; PCE-over-projection as the canonical projection-and-preservation-orientation compound; reflexive PtCns operating via Mode B as the apparatus that makes self-cluster affect possible; substrate-dominance through third-order pathways as the embodied-cluster's organizing axis; primary-cluster assignment by dominant operator with co-dominant configurations classified as compounds; action-or-resolution pathway availability as cross-cluster organizing axis; dominant-operator classification as alternative to ordinary-label categorization; profile-shape disturbance set as quintuple distinguishing apparatus-level pathologies; and toolset-mediated live-option expansion as the structural shape of agency-and-recovery dynamics. Each of these produces predictions that the alternative frameworks do not produce in the same form, and the empirical work that would distinguish them is the empirical work the architecture's predictions invite.

## 12. Open problems

This section consolidates the architectural commitments developed across Sections 3–11 that remain underdeveloped, pointing to specific items that future application work or future foundation extension will need to address. The list is not exhaustive; it is a working catalog of where the architecture has been operating with apparatus that has not yet been fully derived, formalized, or operationalized.

The open problems are organized into four groups: foundation-level extensions (12.1), application-level extensions (12.2), operationalization and empirical work (12.3), and methodological extensions (12.4). Each item states the open problem, connects it to the architectural commitment that exposes it, and indicates the kind of future work it would require.

### 12.1 Foundation-level extensions

These open problems are not adequately addressed by application-level work alone; they point to apparatus that the foundation document set itself would need to extend.

**12.1.1 Agency phenomenology.** Section 10.6 treats agency *structurally* as live-option construction within the I's verification-toolset. It does not derive the felt phenomenology of willing, effort, responsibility, authorship, or choice-experience. The architecture commits to toolset-mediated agency as a structural feature, but the lived experience of agency — what it feels like from the inside to act, to choose, to deliberate, to take responsibility — is not yet derivable from current foundation apparatus. Future foundation extension would need to derive agency phenomenology from existing primitives or introduce additional primitives for it.

**12.1.2 Mode-selection mechanisms.** Foundation Section 4.5 explicitly notes that the architecture does not predict whether Mode A or Mode C operates in a given encounter; it predicts what follows once one operates. This paper has used Mode A and Mode C extensively (boundary-cluster Mode A in Section 8, connection-cluster Mode C in Section 5, mixed-mode operations in compounds), but the *selection* between them remains underdeveloped. Open question: are mode-selection mechanisms systematic, stochastic, substrate-determined, history-determined, or context-determined? Mode-selection may ultimately require a hybrid account — substrate implementation, developmental history, and formal mode-conditions interacting. Future foundation extension or foundation-adjacent implementation theory would need to address this; current treatment is implementation-deferred.

**12.1.3 Π-magnitude formalization.** This paper develops Π-position language extensively (Π-elevation, Π-distortion, Π-invariance under non-reorganizing variation, Π-orientation), but Π-magnitudes are not formalized numerically. Foundation Section 7.6 defers Π-magnitude formalization to application work. The architecture commits to Π as preservation-ranking with positional structure; the question of how Π-magnitudes compose, how they interact with intensity-experience, and how they would be measured remains open. Future work may proceed first with ordinal or qualitative Π-operationalization at the application level (where Π-positions are compared rather than numerically valued); numerical Π-magnitude formalization would be foundation-level or foundation-adjacent and is not required for application-level progress.

**12.1.4 Empathy and emotional contagion** — *application-level extension with possible foundation-interface pressure.* The foundation explicitly leaves empathy and emotional contagion for application-level work. Section 9.5's embodied empathy lemma is this paper's application-level treatment: substrate-mediated resonance with represented extension-state, not direct access to another I's intrinsic substrate-state. A full account of empathy, emotional contagion, perspective-taking, and intersubjective affect remains application-level work — possibly a dedicated empathy/intersubjectivity paper. The architecture has more to say than the lemma alone provides: how does Mode B's other-modeling apparatus produce empathy-relevant content? How does PtCns operate when the other is represented? How does projection-toolset interact with extension-integration in empathic cases? This becomes foundation-level only if such work shows that the current other-I, Mode B, substrate, or apparent-I apparatus cannot support the required distinctions. The default expectation is application-level resolution.

### 12.2 Application-level extensions

These open problems are addressable through future application papers without requiring foundation extension.

**12.2.1 Cultural and linguistic affect categories.** The architecture commits that ordinary affect labels are not the natural categories of affect (Section 4.2, Section 10.3 Prediction 8). But the cultural and linguistic structures that *shape* which labels arise, which categories are available to the I, and how the I's verification-toolset is conditioned by language and culture remain underdeveloped. Future application work would need to address how cultural-linguistic systems interact with the architecture's structural primitives — particularly relevant for Prediction 8's empirical operationalization across cultures.

**12.2.2 Religious, devotional, and aesthetic affect.** Section 3.6 mentioned religious and devotional love as an onset-trajectory case warranting separate treatment because the object-type may differ from ordinary other-I. Section 8.3 mentioned awe in the context of frame-expansion; aesthetic experience was touched but not developed. These are substantial application-level domains that the architecture's apparatus could address — devotional love as constitutive extension toward sacred or transcendent content; aesthetic absorption as Mode C over content the I cannot integrate as ordinary extension; awe-states as PtCns-frame-expansion compounds — but they require sustained treatment.

**12.2.3 Profile-shape disturbance vs stable pathological profile-feature boundary.** Section 8 introduced "stable pathological profile-feature" (hate as profile-feature) as distinct from apparatus-level profile-shape disturbance. The boundary was applied case-by-case but not formalized: when does target-specific pathological organization become apparatus-general damage? At what threshold does sustained pathological profile-feature approach profile-shape disturbance? The architecture predicts the distinction matters for recovery dynamics, but the formalization remains open.

**12.2.4 Verification-toolset development mechanics.** Predictions 2 and 14 rely on toolset-development as a key explanatory variable, but the paper does not formalize toolset-development's mechanics: how toolsets are acquired, which developmental conditions produce which toolset-types, how toolset-rebuilding proceeds in recovery contexts. Future application work would need to develop this — possibly in collaboration with developmental and clinical research traditions that study these processes empirically. This remains application-level unless future work shows that the current Mode B / φ / focus / substrate apparatus cannot support the observed developmental patterns; in that case it would become a foundation-interface concern.

### 12.3 Operationalization and empirical work

These open problems concern how the architecture's commitments become testable.

**12.3.1 Measurement protocols for architectural primitives.** Section 10 states discriminating empirical predictions but does not supply measurement instruments for $A$-status, $\Pi$-position, toolset-capacity, profile-shape disturbance, or constitutive-extension topology. Empirical testing of the architecture's predictions requires development of such instruments. Open questions: which existing instruments approximate which architectural primitives? Which require new development? How would $A$-status be operationalized across the four-class taxonomy (conscious-clear / conscious-fuzzy / latent-retrievable / unconscious-operative)?

**12.3.2 Empirical discriminator design.** Section 11.3's prediction-by-prediction discriminator table identifies what the architecture predicts that alternative frameworks would not predict. But operational study designs that would test these discriminators are not yet specified. For example: how would an empirical study distinguish joy-in-flourishing from substrate-mediated pleasure-tone in anhedonia subjects (Prediction 11)? What protocols would test pre-encounter readiness in love-at-first-sight cases (Prediction 9)? Future work would need to develop such protocols.

**12.3.3 Recognition-lag operationalization.** Prediction 1 commits to bidirectional recognition-lag conditional on specific architectural conditions. Empirical operationalization requires methods for detecting structural change before phenomenally-clear recognition, which is intrinsically methodologically difficult (the I's report cannot directly verify that structural change has occurred without phenomenal access to it). Behavioral signatures, somatic markers, third-party observations, and longitudinal tracking are candidate methodological resources, but the protocols for combining them remain to be developed.

**12.3.4 Substrate-internal implementation bridge.** The architecture treats substrate as functional pathway-source for affect; it is implementation-neutral on neural, hormonal, autonomic, or other substrate-mechanism details. This is a methodological commitment, not a gap. The open question is the empirical/implementation bridge: how do specific substrate-implementation findings (in affective neuroscience, autonomic research, neuroendocrinology) constrain or inform the architecture's commitments? This is empirical and implementation-bridge work unless substrate findings force revision of the architecture's pathway apparatus. Future collaborative work between architectural-level analysis and substrate-research would develop this bridge without compromising the architecture's implementation-neutrality.

### 12.4 Methodological extensions

These open problems concern how the architecture engages with the broader empirical and theoretical landscape.

**12.4.1 Framework translation granularity.** Section 11 compares the architecture against canonical or representative forms of seven major frameworks. Future work should compare specific contemporary versions — particularly Barrett's recent constructionism with embodied/situated/predictive concepts, Friston-influenced social active inference, Scherer's component-process appraisal models, attachment models with internal-working-model structure, and Panksepp-Davis affective-neuroscience extensions — at greater depth than this paper allows.

**12.4.2 Cross-framework hybrid cases.** Some configurations may be best studied through hybrid translation rather than through positioning the architecture against a single alternative. Examples: trauma-cue activation as active-inference + embodied cluster + $A$/φ source-opacity; attachment-shaped love-onset as attachment-theoretic readiness + constitutive-extension topology; depression as compound profile-shape disturbance + interoceptive-prediction allostatic dysregulation. Future work should develop these hybrid cases systematically.

**12.4.3 Comparative outcome-tracking studies.** The architecture's strongest discriminating claim is that operator-level structure underlies surface-label categories and predicts outcomes (Prediction 8, dominant-operator classification). The empirical version is comparative outcome-tracking: do affective configurations classified by dominant operator predict recovery, vulnerability, and treatment-response better than configurations classified by ordinary label? The research design for such comparison studies remains open.

The list is not exhaustive. Each cluster section in this paper raised additional questions that fit one of these categories; the paper has flagged them locally rather than aggregating them all here. The open-problem catalog is meant to make the architecture's known incompleteness visible — to indicate where the architecture has been operating with deferred commitments rather than to suggest that its commitments are complete.

## 13. Conclusion

This paper has developed a structural account of differentiated affect using the foundation document set's apparatus. The constructive arc is: define love structurally using the foundation's existing primitives (Section 3); generalize from love to affect-as-such by stating the affect criterion (Section 4); construct a five-cluster taxonomy by variation on the love-configuration (Sections 5–9); state the architecture's discriminating empirical predictions (Section 10); position those predictions against alternative frameworks (Section 11); catalog the open problems that remain (Section 12). The paper is methodological exemplar rather than comprehensive enumeration: representative clusters, representative pathologies, representative compounds, demonstrating that the architecture's apparatus can do the structural work that affect-theoretic accounts have typically required.

### 13.1 The central achievement

The architecture's central achievement, if it is an achievement, is this: differentiated affect becomes classifiable by *structural operators* rather than by *ordinary labels*. What we ordinarily call "love" turns out to be one configuration in a structural space that includes deep affiliation, identification, fast-forming infatuation, and more. What we ordinarily call "depression" turns out to be a compound of apparatus-level disturbances, with each apparatus distinguishable. What we ordinarily call "panic" turns out to be threat + embodied compound. What we ordinarily call "boredom" turns out to span boundary-cluster engagement-failure, embodied fatigue-driven disengagement, and chronic profile-shape disturbance.

The taxonomy is structural-operator-based rather than category-based. Ordinary affect-names sort into multiple architectural configurations; architectural configurations sort across multiple ordinary affect-names. The architecture predicts that the operator-level classification will track recovery, vulnerability, treatment-response, and developmental dynamics more reliably than the surface-label classification — and Section 10 makes this prediction empirically discriminating against alternative frameworks.

### 13.2 The strongest commitments

The architecture's strongest commitments developed across Sections 3–11 are these:

- *Constitutive-extension topology* — love is profile-topological inclusion ($X$ is *inside* the maximally-self-defined I), not merely strong integration; the topological feature is load-bearing for the love-definition.
- *The affect criterion* — affect is structural perturbation of salience, action-readiness, profile stability, PCE, PtCns, or focus, distinguishing affect-as-such from non-affective phenomenal content.
- *Coordinate and prerogative separations* — availability ($A$), source-access (φ), structural integration (ρ), and preservation-ranking (Π) are distinct dimensions of any content's relation to the I; $A$ and φ can dissociate (recognition-lag, source-opacity); ρ and Π can dissociate (love is not merely high-ρ; self-sacrifice does not refute PCE; jealousy and grief depend on whether ρ-integration and Π-priority converge or diverge).
- *PCE-over-projection* — preservation-orientation operating over represented future-states supplied by projection-toolset; the canonical compound for threat-cluster operations.
- *Reflexive PtCns via Mode B* — the apparatus that makes self-cluster affect possible; Mode B provides the self-as-object representation, PtCns evaluates it.
- *Substrate-dominance through third-order pathways* — embodied-cluster affects organized by pathway-orientation rather than by relation to maximally-self-defined I; substrate-mediation as functional architectural feature.
- *Dominant-operator classification* — primary-cluster assignment by dominant operator, with co-dominant configurations as compounds.
- *Profile-shape disturbance set as quintuple within this affect paper* — apparatus-level pathologies distinguished by which structural equipment is damaged: frame, cogito/thought partition, projection-apparatus, engagement-finding apparatus, pleasure-response apparatus. The set is not claimed as exhaustive psychopathology taxonomy.
- *Toolset-mediated live-option expansion* — agency-expansion mediated by toolset-expansion; recovery from apparatus-level disturbance mediated by structural toolset-rebuilding rather than propositional assent alone.

These commitments are not equivalent to alternative frameworks' commitments. They produce predictions (Section 10) that alternative frameworks would not produce in the same form, and the empirical work that would distinguish them is the empirical work the architecture's predictions invite.

### 13.3 The empirical invitation

Section 10's predictions are not merely internal-coherence claims about how architectural commitments hang together. They are claims about what should be observable if the architecture is correct, with falsification conditions specified for each. The architecture's empirical content is real, and the appropriate engagement with the architecture is to develop the operational protocols that would test the predictions — not to accept or reject the architecture on the basis of internal-coherence judgments alone.

Several specific lines of empirical work follow from the architecture's commitments. Recognition-lag operationalization (Section 12.3.3) — including Pattern A/B threshold trajectories and trauma cue-to-affect / affect-to-source asymmetry — would test Predictions 1, 2, 3, and 4. Outcome-tracking studies sorted by dominant operator (Section 12.4.3) would test Prediction 8 and indirectly several other predictions. Pre-encounter readiness investigation in love-onset cases (Section 12.3.2) would test Prediction 9. Anhedonia / joy-in-flourishing dissociation studies in subjects with intact connection-cluster extensions (Section 10.5 falsification conditions) would test Prediction 11. Recovery-pathway studies tracking toolset-change versus propositional-assent (Section 10.6 falsification conditions) would test Prediction 14.

The architecture is not asking to be accepted on its internal coherence alone. It is asking to be tested through the discriminating predictions it makes, and revised where the empirical work presses it.

### 13.4 Looking ahead

This paper is the second application of the foundation document set; it builds on the first (the *Temporal Phenomenology* paper) and is required for none of the planned subsequent papers. The book-projection envisions further application papers on topics this paper has flagged but not developed: agency phenomenology, intersubjectivity and empathy, aesthetic and religious affect, cultural and linguistic affect categories, clinical operationalization, and others. Each of these would extend the architecture's apparatus into a domain this paper has only touched.

The architecture is still under development. The foundation has been frozen at v5.3.3 for the duration of this application paper, but the open problems flagged in Section 12 indicate that future foundation extensions are likely — particularly around agency phenomenology, mode-selection mechanisms, and Π-magnitude formalization. The application papers can proceed with the current foundation while these extensions are worked on; some application work may itself motivate specific foundation extensions.

What this paper has tried to do is demonstrate that a structural-architectural approach to differentiated affect is workable — that the foundation's apparatus is rich enough to do the analytical work that affect-theoretic accounts have typically required, that the resulting taxonomy is more architecturally tractable than ordinary-label categorization, and that the architecture produces empirical predictions that can engage with the broader landscape of affect research. Whether the architecture's commitments are ultimately correct is an empirical question that the architecture itself invites; this paper's contribution is to make those commitments specifiable enough that the empirical question can be productively asked.

The taxonomy does not claim to exhaust affective life. It claims that affective life becomes structurally tractable once ordinary affect-names are translated into configurations of the maximally-self-defined I, its operators, its substrate pathways, and its preservation and consistency prerogatives. What the I undergoes when it loves, fears, grieves, hates, and rejoices is — on the architecture's account — a particular configuration of structural relations between the I and what the I encounters. Affective life retains its complexity, its felt depth, and its phenomenal integrity. What changes is that we now have apparatus for analyzing it.

## Glossary of architectural terms

This glossary provides compact definitions of architectural terms used load-bearingly in the paper. Foundation-derived terms are cited to their foundation source; application-level terms developed in this paper are cited to their introducing section. The glossary is not exhaustive; it covers the terms most likely to need reference during reading.

**Action-or-resolution pathway availability.** Whether the I has access to a pathway that addresses an affective configuration — through engagement, traction, adaptive response, or external action. The same architectural axis (availability vs foreclosure) recurs across cluster-internal pairs: fear/dread, hope/despair, anger/helpless rage, boredom-with-re-engagement / chronic boredom, pain-with-adaptive-action / pain-fear avoidance. (Section 7.5; Section 10.3 Prediction 7.)

**Affect criterion.** Affect-as-such is structural perturbation of salience, action-readiness, profile stability, PCE, PtCns, or focus, distinguishing affect from non-affective phenomenal content. Bodily content, memory, perception, and reflection that do not perturb these are not affect in the architecture's technical sense, even when consciously registered. (Section 4.1.)

**Apparatus** (in apparatus-level disturbance). Application-level functional bundle of operators producing a specific structural function. The five named apparatuses developed in this paper: frame and PtCns boundary-coherence equipment (damaged in dissolution); cogito/thought partition (damaged in severe depression); projection-apparatus (damaged in learned helplessness); engagement-finding apparatus (damaged in chronic boredom); pleasure-response apparatus (damaged in anhedonia). Apparatuses are not foundation primitives; they are application-level functional bundles. (Sections 5.4, 6.5, 7.6, 8.4, 9.2.)

**Apparent-I.** The I as represented to itself (through Mode B's self-as-object capacity) or to another, distinguished from the modeling I (the I that does the modeling) and the core I (the I as bare cogito). The architecture maintains that other-I access is to apparent-I and inferential contents, not to another modeling I's intrinsic substrate-state. (Foundation File 4, Section 9; Section 9.5 embodied empathy lemma.)

**A — phenomenal availability.** The status of a content with respect to phenomenal access, falling into a four-class taxonomy: conscious-clear (currently in phenomenally-clear awareness), conscious-fuzzy (felt but not articulable / source-opaque), latent-retrievable (not currently accessed but accessible on cuing), and unconscious-operative (operating without phenomenal access). A and source-pathway access (φ) can dissociate. (Foundation File 3, Section 6.1.)

**Boundary-cluster.** Affects organized around I/not-I boundary operations: anger, disgust, awe, hate. Mode A is paradigmatic boundary-cluster operator. (Section 8.)

**Compound affect.** Configuration in which multiple operators from different clusters are co-dominant. Examples include trauma-cue activation (embodied + threat + sometimes self/boundary), grief-rage (connection + boundary), pain catastrophization (embodied + threat). The compositional schema (Section 5.7) provides the analytical apparatus. (Sections 5.7, 7.7, 9.5.)

**Connection-cluster.** Affects organized around extensions of the maximally-self-defined I: love, grief, devotion, jealousy, fear-for-beloved, joy-in-flourishing. Mode C is paradigmatic connection-cluster operator. (Section 5.)

**Constitutive extension / constitutive-extension topology.** The structural state in which content X has been integrated into the maximally-self-defined I such that X is *inside* the I rather than merely strongly integrated with it. The topological feature is load-bearing: removal, loss, or projected non-continuation of X is represented as damage to the maximally-self-defined I itself. Love is the canonical case. (Section 3.1.)

**Dominant operator / dominant-operator classification.** Primary-cluster assignment is by which architectural operator dominates an affective configuration: extension (connection), reflexive evaluation (self), projected preservation-threat (threat), boundary-operation (boundary), or substrate-dominant pathway (embodied). Where multiple operators are co-dominant, the configuration is *compound*. The architecture predicts that careful structural analysis distinguishes affect-configurations sharing surface labels by dominant operator. (Section 9 introduction; Section 10.3 Prediction 8.)

**Embodied-cluster.** Affects organized by substrate-dominance through third-order pathways: pain, pleasure, trauma-cue activation, diffuse somatic states. The cluster is *orthogonal* to the relation-clusters (connection, self, threat, boundary), classified by pathway-orientation rather than by relation to maximally-self-defined I. (Section 9.)

**Frame and frame-coherence.** The maximally-self-defined I's structural organization that determines what counts as consistent with the I and what would violate the I's integrity. PtCns operates over frame to evaluate frame-coherence. Frame-incoherence produces self-cluster affects (shame, guilt, dissolution). (Sections 3.1, 6.1, 6.5.)

**Joy-in-flourishing.** Connection-cluster affect in which structural recognition that an extension is doing well places the maximally-self-defined I in expansive state via the extension. Distinguished from substrate-mediated pleasure; the two can co-occur but are architecturally separable (Prediction 11). (Sections 5.6, 9.2, 10.4.)

**Lived-I network.** The dynamically-current operational state of the I — the configuration of operators, A-statuses, focus-allocations, profile-features, and substrate-conditions actively shaping the I's relations at a given time. Distinguished from the I's stable structural commitments. (Foundation File 3, Sections 6–7; used throughout.)

**Maximally-self-defined I.** The I in its full constitution: everything that, if removed or lost, would damage the I's preservation-relevant integrity. Includes substrate-host, frame, constitutive extensions, and core identity-features. The I that PCE operates to preserve. (Foundation File 2, Section 4.8; Section 3.1.)

**Mode A.** Differentiating operator: the I distinguishes itself from content, asserting boundary, exclusion, or rejection. Paradigmatic boundary-cluster operator (Section 8). Repeated Mode A on integrated content can produce love-ending through cumulative differentiating micro-updates (Section 3.3). (Foundation File 2, Section 4; Section 3.3, Section 8.)

**Mode B.** Self-modeling operator: the I represents content (including itself) as object of attention or reflection. Provides self-as-object representation that PtCns can evaluate (reflexive PtCns via Mode B). Also operates in projection-toolset for representing future-states. (Foundation File 2, Section 4.)

**Mode C.** Integrating operator: the I integrates content with itself, producing identification, extension, or absorption. Paradigmatic connection-cluster operator (Section 5). Cumulative Mode C operations at profile-consequence level can produce love-formation through cumulative integrating micro-updates (Section 3.6). (Foundation File 2, Section 4; Section 3.1, Section 5.)

**Modeling I.** The I considered as the active agent doing modeling, distinguished from the apparent-I (the I as represented to itself or another) and the core I (the I as bare cogito). (Foundation File 4, Section 9.)

**Pattern A / Pattern B.** Two trajectories for affective state-change, continuum endpoints: Pattern A is linear conscious-fuzzy buildup to focus threshold (gradual awareness); Pattern B is metastable unconscious-operative buildup to phase-change (sudden recognition). Verification-toolset configuration partially predicts which trajectory an individual undergoes. (Section 7.2; Section 10.1 Prediction 2.)

**PCE — prerogative of continued existence.** The maximally-self-defined I's preservation-orientation toward its own continuation. PCE supplies preservation-orientation; it is not itself a sensing mechanism or projection-engine. Substrate-threat becomes PCE-relevant; PCE then orients action and attention. (Foundation File 2, Section 4.8.)

**PCE-over-projection.** Shorthand for PCE operating over represented future-states supplied by the projection-toolset. PCE supplies preservation-orientation; the projection-toolset (Mode B future-simulation, substrate-mediated urgency, third-order pathway activation) supplies represented future configuration. PCE-over-projection is the canonical compound for threat-cluster operations. (Section 4.2 terminological note; Section 7.)

**Profile.** The structural organization of the maximally-self-defined I's relations to content. Profile-region: the content-area on which an operation acts. Profile-topology: the structural shape of profile-relations (constitutive-extension topology is a profile-topology). Profile-feature: a stable feature of the I's profile-organization. Profile-shape: the apparatus-level structural pattern of the profile. (Foundation File 3, Sections 6–7; Section 3.1.)

**Profile-shape disturbance.** Apparatus-level damage to the I's structural equipment producing systematic output failures across contexts. The five named profile-shape disturbances developed in this paper: dissolution (frame), severe depression (cogito/thought partition), learned helplessness (projection-apparatus), chronic boredom (engagement-finding apparatus), anhedonia (pleasure-response apparatus). Distinguished from *stable pathological profile-feature*, which is target-specific organization without apparatus-level damage. (Sections 5.4, 6.5, 7.6, 8.4, 9.2; Section 10.2 Prediction 5.)

**PtCns — prerogative of consistency.** The maximally-self-defined I's structural commitment to frame-coherence. PtCns operates over the I's frame to evaluate consistency between content and frame. Reflexive PtCns operates over apparent-I content (Mode B-supplied) to produce self-cluster affects: pride, shame, guilt. (Foundation File 2, Section 4.8; Section 6.)

**Recognition-lag.** Configuration in which phenomenally-clear recognition of an affective state lags structural change in that state. Bidirectional: applies to formation as well as ending of affective configurations. Conditional: predicted when profile-level integration / Π-elevation / PCE-orientation / Mode A or Mode C propagation precedes phenomenally-clear φ-pathway construction. (Sections 3.3, 3.6; Section 10.1 Prediction 1.)

**Reflexive PtCns.** PtCns operating on apparent-I content (representations of self produced by Mode B), producing self-cluster affects when apparent-I content is consistent (pride) or inconsistent (shame, guilt) with frame. The apparatus that makes self-cluster affect possible. (Foundation File 2, Section 4.8; Section 6.)

**ρ — identity-correlation.** Foundation-defined coordinate measuring structural identity-correlation between the I and content. ρ is structural integration, *not* valuation, preservation-priority, or action-priority. Love is not merely high ρ; it requires constitutive-extension topology plus PtCns-preservation. ρ and Π can dissociate: high-ρ content may be low-Π (a habit deeply integrated but not preservation-relevant); low-ρ content may be high-Π (a moral commitment not yet integrated but preservation-relevant). (Foundation File 3, Sections 6–7.)

**Π — preservation-ranking.** Foundation-defined coordinate measuring preservation-priority. Π-position: where a content sits in the preservation-ranking. Π-elevation: a content moving up in priority. Π-distortion: pathological reranking. Π-orientation: PCE's orientation toward preservation. Π and ρ are distinct coordinates; their dissociation is load-bearing for self-sacrifice, jealousy, joy-in-flourishing, and grief. (Foundation File 3, Sections 6–7.)

**Self-cluster.** Affects organized around reflexive PtCns evaluation of apparent-I content: pride, shame, guilt. Mode B supplies the self-as-object representation; PtCns evaluates it. (Section 6.)

**Source-opacity.** A content's $A$-status being conscious-fuzzy with respect to its source — phenomenally registered but without articulable source-pathway. Frequent in embodied-cluster affects (substrate-dominance running ahead of reflective access) but not exclusive to embodied affects; can arise across clusters through $A$/φ split dynamics. (Section 9 introduction; Section 10.5 Prediction 12.)

**Stable pathological profile-feature.** Sustained pathological profile-organization around a specific target, distinguished from apparatus-level profile-shape disturbance. Hate-as-profile-feature is the paradigmatic case: target-specific pathological organization, not apparatus-general damage. (Section 8.5.)

**Substrate-dominance.** Embodied-cluster organizing axis: affects in which substrate-mediation is the dominant operator, running ahead of reflective evaluation through third-order pathways. Substrate-dominance produces source-opacity tendency but not source-opacity necessity. (Section 9.)

**Substrate.** Foundation term for the implementation-level pathway-source for affect (neural, hormonal, autonomic, somatic). The architecture is implementation-neutral on specific substrate mechanisms while committed to substrate-mediation as functional architectural feature. (Foundation File 3, Section 8; Section 9; Section 11.2.4.)

**Third-order pathway.** The pathway structure in which affective contents arrive through substrate-then-cognitive pathways: substrate component runs ahead of reflective access, with phenomenal-clear affect emerging from substrate-mediated activation. Paradigmatic for embodied-cluster affect-formation. (Foundation File 3, Section 8; Section 9.)

**Toolset / verification-toolset.** The I's apparatus for constructing, recognizing, and inhabiting alternatives as live options. Choice operates over alternatives the toolset can construct as live options; what the toolset cannot construct is not in the choice-space. Toolset-development through therapy, contemplative practice, sustained self-attention, and exposure to alternative frames mediates agency-expansion. Recovery from profile-shape disturbances is mediated by structural toolset-rebuilding; propositional assent is recovery-relevant when it changes live-option construction, projection-capacity, frame-coherence, engagement-finding, pleasure-response, or self-relation. (Section 10.6 Prediction 14; Section 12.2.4.)

**Trauma-cue activation.** Multi-cluster compound configuration: substrate-mediated systems generate threat-relevant activation in response to cues matching imprinted patterns from past trauma, often without phenomenally-clear recognition by the I of either cue or historical source. Six-step canonical pathway: cue encounter → substrate activation → phenomenal registration → projection construction (or alternative organization) → phenomenally-clear affect → φ-construction (sometimes much later). (Sections 7.7, 9.3; Section 10.1 Prediction 4.)

**φ — source-pathway access.** Coordinate measuring the I's access to the source or origin-pathway of a content. Distinct from $A$ — phenomenal availability. $A$/φ separation produces recognition-lag, source-opacity, and the four-class content taxonomy. (Foundation File 3, Section 6.1.)
