## 9. Other I's and Perspectival Realism

### 9.1 The three-level distinction

**Level 1: Actual other I.** $o \in \mathsf{O}_m \subseteq \mathsf{N}_m$. If $o$ is genuinely cogniting, it has its own core I in its own instantiation. From $m$'s perspective, $\hat\iota_o$ is not directly given.

**Level 2: Content-about-other-I.** $h^m_t(S) \in \mathsf{A}^m_t$, an inferential content. Has its own coordinates.

**Level 3: Apparent-I-as-presented.** $a \in \mathsf{AppI}^m_t \subseteq \mathsf{A}^m_t$ — body-contents, behavior-contents, communication-contents.

The three levels are formally distinct.

### 9.2 Direct-givenness asymmetry

Other I's are "potentially less real" only in the epistemic-perspectival sense $\operatorname{Direct}_m(o) < \operatorname{Direct}_m(\hat\iota_m)$. This is not an ontological ranking.

### 9.3 Conditional form-invariance

If other cogniting beings exist, their model instances satisfy the same form. But the modeling I cannot verify from inside that any other instance exists.

### 9.4 Perspectival realism, not solipsism

The architecture permits $\mathsf{O}_m \neq \emptyset$ and permits every $o \in \mathsf{O}_m$ to be a genuine cogniting being.

### 9.5 The prerogative of consistency and inter-I encounter

The prerogative of consistency is the structural feature by which the I's frame functions as the implicit consistency-standard against which encounters with not-I are evaluated. It was introduced in Section 4.2 (File 2) as the frame-privileging component of Mode A. It is fundamental to lived consciousness: without PtCns, encounters with not-I would not register against any standard; Mode A and Mode C would lack the criterion that distinguishes consistency-damaging from consistency-extending not-I; the lived I would not differentiate from or integrate with not-I in any structured way; the I/not-I boundary would not form. The architecture's commitment that the lived I emerges through differentiation from not-I that would damage consistency and integration of not-I that extends consistency (Section 4.2, Section 4.5) is also a commitment that the I's frame is the implicit consistency-standard for both responses — PtCns is built into how Modes A and C operate at all.

PtCns is therefore not specific to encounters with other I's. It operates whenever the I encounters anything against its frame: an unexpected object behavior, a contradicting fact, a violated expectation, a misbehaving system, a wrong answer, a felt sense that something is "off," or conversely something that registers as a consistent extension of the I's existing patterns. In each case, the I's frame is what serves as the consistency-standard; the I's frame is treated, structurally and implicitly, as the standard against which integratability is evaluated. This is not deliberation — the I does not pause to consider "perhaps my frame should change"; the registration of (in)consistency is itself the privileging of the I's frame as consistency-anchor.

What is special about inter-I encounter is that *two* PtCns structures operate simultaneously, each from its own perspective. Two cogniting beings encountering each other are simultaneously treating each other as not-I from their respective frames; each evaluates the other's frame against its own consistency-standard; each treats its own frame, implicitly and structurally, as the consistency-anchor. This is the structural form of intersubjective asymmetry: not that one I is actually correct and the other wrong, but that each I's structural prerogative-of-consistency operates from its own perspective, with no view-from-nowhere available to either.

The structural disturbances of Section 10.5.1 (hypertrophied or atrophied PtCns) and the colloquial-aggregate narcissism pattern of Section 10.5.6 are therefore disturbances of the general phenomenon, not just the inter-I case. Hypertrophied PtCns operates in all encounters — the I treats any inconsistency with its frame as the not-I's defect, refusing accommodation that would require frame-revision — though its inter-I manifestation (treating other I's frames as illegitimate) is often the most visible and socially consequential.

## 10. Derived Concepts and Boundary Phenomena

### 10.1 The lived I

For each $m, t, k$:

$$\mathsf{L}^{m,k}_t := \{x \in V^m_t : \exists \text{ directed positive-weight path from } \hat\iota_m \text{ to } x \text{ in } G^{m,k}_t\}.$$

The lived I is a historically accumulated network rooted at $\hat\iota_m$.

### 10.2 Shells as ρ-contours

Shells are contour bands of identity-correlation, useful for visualization but not fundamental. They are coarse-grained projections of the identity-correlation profile $P_{m,t}$ (Section 7.6, File 3) onto threshold bands.

### 10.3 Partial-I and strict not-I

$$\operatorname{PartialI}_{m,t}(x) \iff 0 < \rho_{m,t}(x) < 1.$$

$$\operatorname{StrictNotI}_{m,t}(x) \iff \rho_{m,t}(x) = 0.$$

### 10.4 The four kinds of imprinting

- **Content imprinting** ($\rho_{m,t_2}(x) > \rho_{m,t_1}(x)$ — increased identity-correlation through Mode A → Mode C transition or direct Mode C operation). Content imprinting operates across all three pathway-orders (Section 8.5, File 3), with characteristic signatures depending on whether the imprinting concerns first-order (salience-captured), second-order (cognitive), or third-order (somatic/affective) content.
- **Contact imprinting** (κ-changes through development of contact-relationships)
- **Verification-tool imprinting** (Tools-changes through encounter with intrinsic structure)
- **Mode-development imprinting** (developmental actualization of Modes A, B, C)

All four are governed by the focus-and-contact dynamics of Section 8.

*Osmotic imprinting (Section 8.7, File 3) is transverse to these four kinds, not a fifth parallel kind.* Osmotic imprinting is a substrate-level mechanism — pattern formation through repeated co-occurrence in ambient experience, always operating, requiring neither focus nor verification — that runs underneath and alongside the four kinds above. Each kind can have osmotic and non-osmotic variants: content can integrate into the lived-I network osmotically (through repeated exposure) or non-osmotically (through deliberate Mode C identification); contact relationships can develop osmotically before explicit recognition; verification tools can ground osmotically in substrate proto-patterns before explicit articulation; mode-development can be partly osmotically shaped through the substrate-level "sufficiently distanced" thresholds osmotic imprinting establishes.

### 10.5 Boundary shifts as profile-shape disturbances

The architecture's mechanisms can develop in ways that produce characteristic profile-shape disturbances. Some of these are sometimes classified as mental illness. **The architecture's commitment about responsibility is graduated rather than absolute.**

The architecture commits to the existence of a developmental window — ill-defined in its precise boundaries but real in its existence — during which conscious direction is insufficient to overcome substrate and environmental pressure. Within this window, patterns are not the I's chosen errors. They emerge from substrate features the I did not choose, environmental conditions the I did not control, and contact-driven dynamics that operated before the I had any intentional capacity to direct them. The window's boundary is gradual: substrate maturation is continuous, Mode B onset varies across cogniting beings, and self-directed focus capacity grows incrementally rather than stepwise. Many mental-illness conditions originate inside this window, particularly those with childhood or adolescent onset, those with strong familial/genetic substrate components, and those rooted in trauma or environmental stress during developmental periods.

The "developmental window" is not only chronological. The architecture's commitment is that the I is not responsible for patterns formed under conditions where conscious direction is insufficient to overcome substrate response. Two broad classes of such conditions exist:

*Developmental conditions.* Early life, before mature self-direction is available. Mode B is not yet operating or only weakly operating; substrate is highly malleable; the I has no accumulated structure capable of intentional direction. Patterns formed in this period — including childhood-onset PTSD, attachment disturbances, developmental trauma — are paradigmatically inside the window.

*Situational-overwhelm conditions.* Moments of substrate response so intense that the I's normal integration capacity is inadequate, regardless of chronological age. Trauma is the canonical case: extreme third-order pathway activation combined with salience-gating dominance produces imprinting under conditions where the I's mature self-direction is overwhelmed by the substrate response. Adult-onset PTSD from combat, assault, accident, or sudden loss originates in functionally analogous conditions to developmental-window pattern-formation, even though the chronology differs.

Both classes share the architecturally-relevant feature: conscious direction was insufficient to overcome substrate and environmental pressure at the moment of pattern-formation. The substrate response was determinative; the I's intentional capacity, whether undeveloped or overwhelmed, was not in a position to direct alternative pattern-formation. Patterns originating from either class are not the I's chosen errors.

PTSD specifically illustrates several architectural features simultaneously. The traumatic imprinting under situational overwhelm produces a profile-shape disturbance with characteristic structure: high local ρ for trauma-related content (the trauma is deeply self-relevant), defective persistence relation $\sim^m_H$ (intrusive re-experiencing as the past content presents with present-time κ), recalibrated salience-gating in $\mathsf{S}_m^{\text{salience}}$ (hyperarousal to trauma-related stimuli), unstable φ for trauma content (verification pathways fail to integrate the trauma into normal cognitive structure), and characteristic partitioning between ρ-integration and φ-access (dissociative phenomena). The architecture's commitment that substrate constraints limit modification capacity throughout life applies forcefully here: the substrate-level changes from trauma (recalibrated salience-gating, persistent third-order dysregulation, defective persistence-relation for traumatic content) are not within the I's choice. Effective treatment must engage substrate-level processes; pure cognitive engagement alone is typically insufficient because the disturbance is not primarily at the cognitive level.

Outside the window, in mature operation, the I has accumulated structure and self-directed focus capacity available. Patterns developing under these conditions may have contributions from the I's own choices, alongside continuing substrate and environmental factors. These contributions can be accidental (the I does not recognize what its choices are doing to its own profile structure), misintentional (the I makes choices it later regrets that contribute to harmful pattern-formation), or in some cases more deliberate. The substrate constraints limiting modification persist; the I cannot choose to have a different bandwidth, a different mediation profile, or fundamentally different substrate properties. But within the space of choices the I can make, some pattern-contributions are real.

The architecture's commitment is therefore that a substantial portion of mental-illness phenomena originates inside the developmental window or has strong substrate components, while the share attributable to mature self-direction varies by condition, individual, and circumstance. The architecture does not assert universal innocence regarding mental-illness patterns; it asserts that the developmental window protects against responsibility for patterns rooted there, that substrate constraints limit modification capacity throughout life, and that the share of mature self-direction in any specific case is an empirical question the architecture does not pre-judge.

Mental illness conditions, on this account, are not reducible to ρ-magnitude problems (e.g., "depression is low ρ"). They are profile-shape disturbances: characteristic patterns in how the I's field is structured. The same overall ρ statistics could correspond to very different structural conditions; what matters diagnostically is the *shape* of the profile, including where ρ is high and low, how integration is distributed across modes K, what regions of the profile correspond to what kinds of contents, and how the profile coordinates with κ and φ across its support.

*Extended lived-I is baseline-normal, not itself a disturbance.* The architecture must distinguish *extended lived-I* — high-ρ integration extending broadly into relational others, family, community, environment, ancestral lineage, or sacred objects — from *profile-shape disturbance*. Extended lived-I is baseline-normal across many cultural and spiritual traditions; profiles in which the I's lived structure extends extensively into relational others, communal continuity, or land are not themselves disturbances. Western-individualist framings sometimes pathologize such extension as enmeshment, codependency, or merger; the architecture rejects this default reading. Extension itself is structurally neutral. Pathology arises when extension combines compositionally with other shape-features — atrophied PtCns producing self-erasure within the extended lived-I, hypertrophied prerogative producing imposition on others within the extension, failure of integration producing instability across the extended structure. The narcissism entry below (10.5.6) is correctly compositional in this way: extended-self combined with hypertrophied PtCns. The same compositional logic applies to all extension-related profile features. The architecture's diagnostic vocabulary is about *structure*, not about how far the lived-I extends.

*Osmotic-imprinting components in profile-shape disturbances.* Many of the disturbances catalogued below have substantial osmotic-imprinting components (Section 8.7, File 3): trauma-cue dynamics in PTSD operate through osmotically-imprinted somatic-context associations; attachment-related disturbances reflect osmotically-imprinted relational patterns from early development; implicit-attitude dispositions that contribute to several profile shapes are osmotically formed; depressive cognition's substrate features include osmotically-imprinted self-referential associations. Disturbances are rarely purely conscious-level pathologies — substrate-level osmotic patterns are typically involved, often providing the persistence and treatment-resistance that distinguish profile-shape disturbances from transient distress. This is consistent with the architecture's general observation that substrate constraints limit modification capacity throughout life: osmotically-imprinted substrate patterns are not directly within the I's choice and require substrate-level engagement (rather than purely cognitive engagement) for substantive revision.

#### 10.5.1 Disturbances of Mode A (boundary regulation)

- *Insufficiently strong adversarial differentiation*: weak I/not-I boundary; identity is fluid; difficulty distinguishing self from intimate others.
- *Excessively strong adversarial differentiation*: rigid boundaries; nearly all not-I registered as inconsistent and rejected.
- *Hypertrophied PtCns*: other I's frames treated as illegitimate sources of inconsistency that must be eliminated rather than accommodated.
- *Atrophied PtCns*: the I's frame not registered as the implicit consistency-standard; the I treats its own contents as more inconsistent than not-I, deferring to other frames as authoritative.

#### 10.5.2 Disturbances of Mode B (meta-reasoning capacity)

- *Atrophied Mode B*: difficulty with reflexive thought; concrete thinking; lack of insight.
- *Hypertrophied Mode B*: obsessive self-monitoring; dissociative observation of self-from-outside.

#### 10.5.3 Disturbances of Mode C (positive identification)

- *Atrophied Mode C*: failure to positively identify; difficulty integrating experiences as part of self.
- *Hypertrophied Mode C*: excessive identification; merger states; loss-of-self in relationships.

#### 10.5.4 Boundary-shifts in φ-applicability

- *Insight*, *repression*, *skill automatization*, *memory consolidation*, *dissociation*, *verification-tool loss*.

#### 10.5.5 Boundary-shifts from the not-I side

- *Intrusive thoughts*, *compulsions*, *hallucinations and delusions*.

#### 10.5.6 Conditions traditionally classified as mental illness as profile-shape disturbances

The architecture frames these as conjunctions of profile-shape features rather than as discrete categories or as scalar ρ-deficits:

- *Depression*: at the basic level, collapsed profile support — previously high-ρ contents lose integration; the lived-I network narrows. Often involves disturbed Mode C (atrophied positive identification limiting profile breadth) and disturbed PtCns (the I's frame deprivileged within its own profile, with depressive content treated as more "consistent with what the I really is" than the rest of the lived-I network). The signature is structural narrowing, not low-ρ-everywhere. Affects the third-order region of the profile (anhedonia, somatic flattening) most directly, with second-order region affected as cognitive ruminations narrow.

  Severe depression involves a further, deeper structural disturbance: *functional collapse of the cogito/thought partition*. The architecture normally maintains a partition between the cogito-I ($\hat\iota_m$, the indexed self-identity at structural $\rho = 1$) and the contents of thought (which sit in the asymptotic field with their own ρ values determined by integration into the lived-I network). The normal form of an I-thought relation is "the I thinks $X$" — where $X$ is a content the I attends to, with $X$ having a structural position distinct from $\hat\iota_m$. In severe depression, the I's *functional access* to this partition collapses for specific contents. Depressive thoughts — "I am worthless," "I should not exist," "things will never get better," "I am beyond help" — acquire ρ-values approaching 1 within the lived-I network and get treated phenomenologically as constitutive of who-the-I-is rather than as contents the I has. Structurally, $\hat\iota_m$ remains at its tautological self-identity throughout — the cogito cannot fail because any move that would deny it presupposes the I performing the denial (Section 2.4, File 1). Functionally, the I cannot access the distinction between $\hat\iota_m$ and the very-high-ρ depressive content from inside its current configuration. The architectural partition is preserved; the I's access to it has failed.

  The collapse spreads through the depressive constellation. Despair, worthlessness, hopelessness, anhedonia stop being felt as states the I has and come to be felt as the I itself. *Depression becomes proto-self in the functional sense.* The phenomenology often described as "I am not myself, I am the depression" or "the depression is who I am right now" has architectural cash-out: very-high-ρ depressive content is functionally indistinguishable from cogito from inside the I's current configuration, even though the structural cogito-anchoring is preserved throughout.

  With depression as functional proto-self, the lived-I network reorganizes around the depressive structure. What was integrated into a normally-functioning I now integrates into the depression-as-self. *Represented death-content attains high ρ within the depressive proto-self.* Represented death — death-as-imagined, death-as-completion, death-as-relief — becomes integrated into the depression-as-self as the natural completion of who-the-I-is. (Actual substrate-cessation is not a content in the lived-I graph; only its representation can have ρ.) The depressive proto-self's structural arc points toward its own termination; represented death-content is ρ-integrated as the endpoint that completes the proto-self rather than as the not-I state that negates it.

  The Prerogative of Continued Existence (Section 4.8, File 2) then operates over this disturbed profile. Within the disturbed profile, the represented path of least profile-destruction may become distorted such that self-cessation is represented as completion, relief, or the only coherent continuation of the depressive proto-self. This does not mean that actual substrate-cessation is continued existence — the modeling I's substrate-cessation ends its operation entirely. It means that PCE may be misdirected, defeated, or routed through a disturbed preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$ (Section 4.8, File 2) and inaccurate future-state projection. The disturbance is in the profile, the preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$, and the projected future-states. Under these conditions, PCE's directional pull may be misdirected, defeated, or routed through distorted representations rather than successfully preserving the modeling I's continued operation.

  This is the architecture's structural account of why severe depression specifically is associated with suicidality in a way that distinguishes it from sadness, grief, or even significant ρ-displacement without functional partition collapse. Depressive suicide, on this account, is not the I overriding its preservation prerogative but the preservation prerogative operating on a profile in which the I's functional access to the cogito/thought partition has collapsed and represented death-content has acquired high ρ as the represented natural completion of the depressive proto-self.

  The recovery direction is structurally specifiable: functional access to the partition reasserting. Depressive thoughts re-occupy positions in the asymptotic field rather than near-cogito ρ-positions; the depression-as-functional-proto-self dissolves; represented death-content loses high-ρ integration as the proto-self that pointed toward it dissolves. The lived I as it normally is can return. This is consistent with the phenomenology of depression recovery often described as "getting myself back" — the I that returns is the one whose functional access to the architectural partition has been restored. The cogito itself was always there structurally; what returns is access to it.

  A downstream implication: rational arguments against suicide often fail in severe depression not because the arguments are unsound but because they are addressed to the I as it normally is — to the cogito separated from depressive content. In severe depression that separation has collapsed functionally; the arguments do not reach the cogito because the I cannot currently access the cogito as separate from the depressive content holding near-cogito ρ. This is not failure of reasoning but failure of address: the addressee's normal architecture is structurally present but functionally inaccessible.
- *Anxiety*: hyperactive verification pathways for threat-relevant contents reshape the profile through focused weighting; threat-relevant items may have elevated $\rho$ alongside disrupted overall structure. Profile shows disproportionate density in threat-related regions with thinning elsewhere. Operates strongly in third-order region (somatic activation) with second-order region pulled into threat-relevant focus.
- *Psychosis*: profile partitions irregularly; some previously unconscious-operative or source-opaque contents become phenomenally available with apparent local verification pathways without integration into the rest of the network (delusional certainty as profile region with high local φ but disconnected from main lived-I structure); some verifiable contents lose access (loss of insight as profile region with disconnected ρ). The lived-I network develops disjoint regions visible in profile structure.
- *Dissociative phenomena*: profile partitions: some contents retain ρ-integration but lose φ-access; some retain φ-access but lose ρ-integration. The partition itself is the diagnostic feature, not any single ρ value. Often partitions across orders: third-order content may be severed from second-order processing (alexithymia); second-order content may be severed from first-order salience (intellectualization).
- *Obsessive-compulsive phenomena*: verification pathways for specific contents do not close; the profile shows local instability around specific contents whose ρ-status cannot resolve.
- *Cognitive impairment / dementia*: verification-tool loss progressively removes the I's capacity to maintain φ-pathways for whole categories of profile content. The profile loses φ-coordinated structure even where ρ is preserved.
- *Sociopathy/psychopathy* (colloquial aggregate): characteristic atrophy of Mode C identification with apparent-I contents; profile excludes other-I-suggestive contents from integration.
- *Narcissism* (colloquial aggregate): hypertrophied Mode C identification with apparent-I contents combined with under-recognition of other-I frames; profile shows extended support with PtCns operating across it, producing characteristic intolerance of perceived inconsistencies from inside the extended self.

In every case, the architecture's diagnostic value is in identifying the characteristic *structure* of the profile — what it includes, what it excludes, how it partitions, what coordinate signatures appear where — not in reducing pathology to a scalar measure.

### 10.6 Contact (κ) as independent coordinate

Contact remains independent of φ and ρ. The phantom limb case is the canonical instance of high integration without current contact.

### 10.7 Action-priority and the structural-integration distinction

ρ measures structural integration into the lived-I network, formally via max-flow rooted at the cogito-anchor. ρ does not measure preservation-priority, valuation, or action-priority. The anchoring $\rho_m(\hat\iota_m) = 1$ is mathematical normalization, not a claim that the cogito-I is what the modeling I most values, seeks to preserve, or prioritizes in action above all else (Section 2.9, File 1; Section 7.6, File 3).

The structural feature that does direct action under existential pressure is the *prerogative of continued existence* (PCE), introduced in Section 4.8 (File 2) as one of the two structural prerogatives of lived consciousness. PCE is the structural feature by which the modeling I pulls toward the path that maximizes continued existence of the *maximally-self-defined I*: the action-relevant configuration of the lived-I network whose preservation counts, for the modeling I under current conditions, as continuation of itself. This involves a situational preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$ over possible profile-configurations and losses. $\Pi_{m,t}$ may depend on ρ but is not reducible to ρ; ρ measures structural identity-correlation, not valuation, preservation-priority, or action-priority (Section 11.2 exclusion 19). PCE operates always as a structural feature; the conditions under which its directional pull can be defeated, misdirected, or routed through distorted projections are extreme and identifiable. Self-sacrifice is compatible with PCE when the preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$ makes substrate-cessation of the modeling I the represented path by which the maximally-self-defined I is least destroyed or most continued. This is a structural-coherence claim about represented future-states and preservation-ranking — not a claim that actual substrate-cessation is continued existence, an accuracy claim, a clinical claim, or a normative endorsement.

This distinction is necessary to account for self-sacrifice — cases where the substrate conditions for the modeling I's continued operation cease in connection with action that preserves a high-ρ non-core network member. A parent pushes a child out of the path of a car and is hit instead. A sibling shields another sibling from violence. A soldier throws themselves on a grenade to save comrades. In each case the surface reading "the cogito-I, sitting at $\rho = 1$, is what gets preserved first" fails to obtain. Self-sacrifice is therefore an architectural test case: does the architecture handle these cases without revision?

The architecture's response is the negative result — these cases do not refute the architecture — not a worked-out action theory. Self-sacrifice is consistent with the existing apparatus once structural integration and preservation-priority are kept distinct. How action-priority is structured in detail remains an open problem within scope (Section 13.3, File 5).

*Self-sacrifice is not a single phenomenon.* The architecture distinguishes at least three kinds, none of which the architecture privileges over the others:

- *Pre-reflective / substrate-routed sacrifice.* Third-order pathway activation (visceral protective response) drives the action before $\hat\iota_m$ has any chance to deliberate. The parent's body moves to push the child out of the way before cognitive comparison occurs. Section 8.5 (File 3) handles this as a third-order pathway operating with high salience-gating and minimal cognitive mediation. The action is not chosen in any deliberative sense; it is substrate-routed.
- *Reflective sacrifice.* Mode B operates on projected post-action profiles; the modeling I imagines the lived-I in each represented future-state and prefers the one in which a high-ρ network member's projected actuality-continuation obtains. Mode B's involvement makes the action deliberative rather than purely substrate-routed.
- *Mixed.* Substrate response and Mode B operate together. The visceral pull and the reflective endorsement coincide; the architecture does not pretend to resolve which contributes how much, and most actual sacrifice is mixed in this way.

In all three kinds, what is at stake is not the cogito-I's own status as $\rho = 1$ — that anchoring is structural-mathematical and not directly relevant to the action. What is at stake is the substrate conditions for the modeling I's continued operation: if those conditions cease, the modeling I no longer operates (from inside-perspective, no further field $\mathsf{A}^m_t$ obtains).

For *reflective* sacrifice specifically, the action-comparison can be reconstructed as a comparison between represented future-states from inside the modeling I's perspective:

- *Future A (represented):* substrate conditions for the modeling I's continued operation cease; the high-ρ network member's projected actuality-continuation obtains.
- *Future B (represented):* substrate conditions for the modeling I's continued operation persist; the high-ρ network member's projected actuality-continuation does not obtain.

In Future B, the lived-I network is represented as having a member-shaped hole; the modeling I projects experiencing the loss across subsequent time. In Future A, the modeling I projects no further experience, but the network member is represented as continuing in actuality. The asymmetry is that the modeling I's own non-continuation removes its experience either way — what differs between represented worlds is whether the network member's projected actuality-continuation obtains. Mode B operating on these projections can prefer Future A.

The modeling I does not have direct access to actual other-I continuation; the network member's actuality-continuation is *projected* or *represented* from inside the modeling I's perspective (cf. Section 9.1, on the three-level distinction between actual other I, content-about-other-I, and apparent-I-as-presented). The whole comparison is internal to the modeling I; what looks from an external description like "valuing the other's life over one's own" is, from inside the I, a comparison of represented future-states.

For *pre-reflective* sacrifice, no such Mode B comparison occurs. The third-order pathway fires; the action obtains; $\hat\iota_m$ may register what happened only after, or never (if substrate-continuation has already ceased). The architecture does not require pre-reflective sacrifice to be reducible to a comparison of represented futures; it accounts for it via third-order pathway dynamics under high salience-gating, without further structural commitments.

The architecture identifies — without claiming to formalize how they combine — three mechanisms by which PCE operates under existential pressure:

- Third-order pathway activation: PCE's directional pull operating through visceral protective response with minimal cognitive mediation.
- Future-state projection across the lived-I (Mode B operating on represented post-action profiles): PCE's directional pull operating through deliberative comparison of represented future-states.
- Substrate-level urgency responses (salience-gating recalibrated by threat-context): PCE's directional pull operating through substrate-level reorganization that drives action without cognitive mediation.

These are *identified mechanisms* of PCE operation, not components of a completed action-priority theory. The architecture does not specify how they combine when more than one operates simultaneously, nor how they trade off when their direction-of-pull diverges. How they interact to produce specific action choices is application-level work, possibly drawing on affective neuroscience, decision-theoretic frameworks, and the phenomenology of urgency. Section 13.3 (File 5) records this as an open problem within scope.

What the architecture *does* claim is the negative result: self-sacrifice does not refute the cogito-anchoring $\rho_m(\hat\iota_m) = 1$, does not require any non-core ρ to exceed 1, and does not require a hidden valuation coordinate beyond the existing apparatus. The PCE provides the structural directional drive that orients action under existential pressure, operating through the three mechanisms above. The architecture's primary descriptive object remains the identity-correlation profile $P_{m,t}$; PCE operates over this profile together with the preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$ rather than supplanting them. The dynamics of $\Pi_{m,t}$ — how it is established, updated, and brought to bear in action-events — are action-relevant and remain application-level work. Application work on pathological configurations — including a four-mechanism typology of completed suicide (underestimated risk; functional cogito/thought partition collapse with represented death-content attaining high ρ as in severe depression, Section 10.5.6; PtCns recruited into self-elimination loops; network-altruistic projection) — will engage with cases where PCE is misdirected, defeated, or routed through distorted preservation-ranking and inaccurate future-state projections.

### 11.2 What the architecture rules out

**1. Rich self from cogito alone.** The cogito licenses only $\hat\iota_m$.

**2. Verification capacity from cogito alone.** $\mathsf{Cogito}_m \not\Rightarrow \mathsf{Tools}^m_t$.

**3. Developmental actualization from cogito alone.** Modes A/B/C require encounter, accumulation, and time.

**4. Primitive identity-indexing.** No primitive `IndexedTo` relation.

**5. Body as core I.** Bodily identification is acquired.

**6. Asymptotic content with $\phi$ at intrinsic universality.** Only intrinsic items have intrinsic-universality status (Tier 1).

**7. Non-core content with $\rho = 1$.** Only the core has $\rho = 1$.

**8. Direct access to other I's as I.** $\operatorname{Direct}_m(o) = 0$ for $o \in \mathsf{O}_m$.

**9. Shared lived-I.** No global lived-I network.

**10. Direct observation of between-I substrate, toolset, or developmental-mode-history.**

**11. Cogito-chosen capacity, toolset, or mode-development.**

**12. Uniform φ across mathematical complexity.** Intrinsic items are not assigned φ values; the uniform-treatment claim was over-specification. Properly: intrinsic items are in Tier 1, outside the φ-domain.

**13. Conscious-only asymptotic field.** The asymptotic field contains both conscious and unconscious contents.

**14. Uniform verification capacity across I's.** Different I's have different toolsets.

**15. Intentional cultivation of one's own early development.**

**16. Normative correctness of any specific developmental trajectory.**

**17. Per-encounter prediction of mode-selection.** The architecture predicts what *if* a mode operates; it does not predict which mode fires for a given encounter. Mode-selection depends on factors the architecture does not formalize.

**18. Consciousness reducible to identity-correlation.** The architecture does not claim consciousness is identity-correlation, that ρ measures degree of consciousness, or that conscious entities can be ranked by overall ρ-strength. Identity-correlation is a perspectival relation within a conscious field; the field is presupposed. The architecture taxonomizes relations within consciousness, not consciousness itself.

**19. ρ measures preservation-priority, valuation, or action-priority.** ρ is a structural measure of integration into the lived-I network. Preservation-priority and action under existential pressure depend on factors beyond ρ-magnitude — third-order pathway dynamics, future-state projection, substrate urgency — that the architecture does not yet formalize. The cogito-anchoring $\rho_m(\hat\iota_m) = 1$ is mathematical normalization, not a claim that the cogito-I is most-valued or most-preservation-prioritized. High-ρ non-core network members can drive action in which the substrate conditions for the modeling I's continued operation cease; the self-sacrifice case (Section 10.7) is consistent with the architecture, not a counterexample to it.

**20. PCE is metaphysically inviolable.** The Prerogative of Continued Existence (Section 4.8, File 2; Section 10.7) operates always as a structural feature of lived consciousness, but is not metaphysically inviolable. The conditions under which its directional pull can be defeated, misdirected, or routed through distorted projections are extreme and identifiable. Completed suicide is not a simple counterexample to PCE: in severe profile-shape disturbance (e.g., severe depression with functional collapse of the cogito/thought partition; Section 10.5.6), PCE may be defeated, misdirected by inaccurate future-state projections, or routed through a distorted preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$ such that self-cessation is represented as the least-destructive or only coherent continuation, even though actual substrate-cessation ends the modeling I's operation. The architecture's commitment is structural primacy, not metaphysical inviolability; the foundation makes only a structural claim about possible prerogative defeat and distortion. PCE is fundamental to lived consciousness in the sense that the lived I cannot operate without it; it is not absolute in the sense of being undefeatable. The foundation does not offer a clinical risk model, treatment model, or normative endorsement of self-cessation; that work belongs to the planned PCE-and-action-priority application paper, which must engage existing suicidology (interpersonal theory, IMV model, etc.) rather than presenting the architecture's mechanism typology as freestanding.
