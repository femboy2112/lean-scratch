## Preface: Methodological Status

This document is a foundation in active development, not a finalized theory. Its commitments have been accumulated through iterative work grounded in direct phenomenological access by the author and refined under adversarial pressure. The architecture is presented in declarative voice for clarity, but the development history is not hidden: load-bearing structures have been added, modified, and tightened across multiple rounds. This is consistent with the architecture's own account of how cogniting beings acquire and refine verification capacity — through encounter, recognition, refinement, and continued attention. The architecture is one cogniting being's articulation of structures she has accessed through her own developmental verification, presented as a working foundation that other cogniting beings may engage with, refine, or stress-test through their own verification capacities.

The architecture is not claimed to be derived from first principles in some abstract way independent of the author's experience. It is claimed to be a structural articulation of what self-relational experience is like for a cogniting being who has reflected carefully on her own verification capacities and the in re structure they encounter. Whether the articulation generalizes to other cogniting beings is itself a structural prediction the architecture makes (form-invariance conditional on existence) but does not strictly prove from inside any single perspective.

Subsequent work addresses application, stress-testing, formalization extensions, and translation papers. File 6 in this set is the first such application — temporal phenomenology — demonstrating that the architecture's existing apparatus produces structural accounts of phenomena it was not built specifically to address.

## Short Abstract

This paper presents a formal architecture describing the structure of conscious experience, built from a single tautological postulate — that the I is itself — and from mathematical and logical truth treated as structurally pervasive in the system the I is embedded in. Three independent measures are placed on the contents of experience: how strongly something is currently in contact with the I, how indistinguishable from truth it appears under reasoning, and how integrated it is into the lived self. These measures are usually correlated but can dissociate; their independence explains phenomena that single-dimensional theories of self struggle with — vivid hallucinations, anesthetized limbs, depersonalization, phantom limbs, alien thoughts, and the boundary-disturbances characteristic of various forms of mental illness. The architecture distinguishes the intrinsic structure of mathematics, logic, and reason — which holds in re, woven into the structure of the system regardless of any I — from the I's developmental access to that structure, which is acquired through encounter with the system's pervasive instantiations of intrinsic forms. The lived I emerges through three distinct modes of developmental actualization: adversarial differentiation against sufficiently-distanced not-I, positive identification with what is encountered as continuous with self, and reflexive meta-reasoning by which the I takes its own structure as object of attention. The architecture contains both conscious and unconscious contents, treats consciousness as presupposed (the fact that there is an I with a field of contents) rather than as something it derives, and treats identity-correlation as a perspectival relation within consciousness rather than as consciousness itself. It is descriptive rather than prescriptive: structural patterns including many of those classified as mental illness emerge from substrate features, environmental conditions, and developmental dynamics that operated within a window where conscious direction is insufficient to overcome them; substrate constraints limit modification capacity throughout life, while the share of pattern-formation attributable to mature self-direction varies by condition and individual. The architecture is honest about its limits: source-attribution, the structure of the world independent of the I, and the actual existence of other minds are all outside what can be verified from within. It is translatable into prior theories of mind without depending on them. It is a foundation in development, not a complete theory.

## Long Abstract

This paper presents a two-layer architecture for the structure of conscious experience, built from two foundational commitments. The first is that mathematics, logic, and reason are intrinsic — universal-by-reason-alone, woven into the structure of the system regardless of whether any particular I is present to verify them. This is a naturalist-realist commitment: the system in which the I is embedded contains pervasive mathematical correlations, encounterable through any cogniting being's embedding in the system. The architecture does not require the stronger Platonist commitment to abstract objects existing in a separate realm; it requires only that the system contains structure cogniting beings can access. The second commitment is the tautological cogito: the I is taken as a postulate of self-identity ("I = I"), the indexed instance of the universal logical principle of reflexivity. Existence-as-I is performatively self-evident; the form of that existence is self-identity; reflexivity characterizes the form, not the source. The cogito does not derive existence from logic; it recognizes that the self-evident existence has the structure of indexed self-identity.

These commitments place the modeling I at φ = 1 - δ for an infinitesimal δ — the unique indexed perspectival position one structural step from intrinsic universality. The architecture distinguishes three tiers of φ-status: intrinsic items (Logic, Math, Reason — including all mathematical truths regardless of proof complexity) are outside the φ-domain entirely, with their own ontological status as in re; the cogito sits at φ = 1 - δ structurally-ontologically, the same value for any cogniting being; other asymptotic contents have I-relative epistemic-φ values determined by the I's verification toolset, with both n_{m,t}(x) and ε_{m,t}(x) toolset-dependent and decomposition-dependent.

The I's access to intrinsic structure is itself acquired developmentally. Mathematics, logic, and reason exist in re — instantiated in the world's structure regardless of whether any I cognizes them. The I, through interaction with this in re structure, acquires verification tools — beginning typically with object persistence and cause-and-effect, proceeding through modal, hypothetical, and inductive structures perceptible at longer temporal scales. Three kinds of in re encounter drive verification-tool imprinting: direct world-encounter, shadow encounter (abstract structures encountered through their consequences for the structure of reasoning, recognized via three operationally-specifiable forms), and self-encounter through meta-reasoning (with both pure and externally-mediated sub-types).

The lived I itself emerges through three modes of developmental actualization. Adversarial differentiation (Mode A): encounter with sufficiently-distanced not-I generates an "other" effect; the I's frame functions as the implicit standard against which discrepancy is registered (the prerogative of consistency, fundamental to lived consciousness). Positive identification (Mode C): encounter with not-I that registers as continuous with self produces integration as part of the (proto-)self. Reflexive meta-reasoning (Mode B): the I attends to its own structure, with both internally-triggered and externally-triggered sub-types; this is stochastic with environment-shaped odds, with structural-onset constraints (zero at developmental origin, non-zero in mature operation, variation across I's) but no architecture-derived specific distributional shape.

Modes are response-types to encounters, not predictive mechanisms. The architecture does not predict which mode fires for a given encounter; it predicts what structural consequences follow from each mode's operation. Mode-selection depends on substrate, history, environment, and stochastic elements that the architecture does not formalize. Two structural prerogatives operate alongside the modes as fundamental features of lived consciousness: the prerogative of consistency (the I's frame as implicit consistency-standard against which encounters with not-I are evaluated, providing the unifying standard for both Mode A as consistency-preserving differentiation and Mode C as consistency-extending integration) and the prerogative of continued existence (the structural pull toward the path that maximizes continued existence of the maximally-self-defined I). Both are presupposed by the architecture's account of how the lived I emerges and operates; neither is metaphysically inviolable.

Three independent coordinates are placed on the asymptotic field. Contact (κ) tracks current interaction with primal not-I. Identity-correlation (ρ) tracks integration into a directed historical network rooted at the I and is defined by max-flow over weighted graphs. Truth-indistinguishability (φ) tracks the I's reason-test profile under focus-driven probe weighting, applying only to contents for which a verification pathway is constructible from the I's current toolset. For contents lacking a constructible verification pathway, φ is undefined while ρ and κ remain well-defined; such contents may be conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque when phenomenally available, or unconscious-operative when not phenomenally available. The conscious/unconscious distinction depends on the joint configuration of phenomenal availability $A$ and φ-status (Section 6.1, File 3).

Consciousness is presupposed by the architecture as the fact that there is an I with a field of contents; it is not equated with identity-correlation. Identity-correlation is a perspectival relation between the modeling I and items in its field, expressed as the identity-correlation profile $P_{m,t}$ which is the architecture's primary structural object. Mental-illness conditions, when treated by the architecture, are profile-shape disturbances rather than ρ-magnitude problems.

Contents reach the modeling I through causal pathways of varying mediation length. The architecture identifies three orders of imprinting: first-order (minimal-mediation salience capture through substrate, with the world's impingement directly altering focus allocation), second-order (cognitive-substrate mediated, producing thoughts), and third-order (somatic-then-cognitive mediated, producing emotions and physical sensations). Each substrate-mediation stage involves lossy compression. Higher-order content tends to have lower explicit propositional information density from the I's inside perspective, explaining the phenomenology of "intense but fuzzy" affective experience.

The architecture is descriptive, not prescriptive. The architecture commits to a developmental window — ill-defined in its precise boundaries — during which conscious direction is insufficient to overcome substrate and environmental pressure. Patterns rooted in this window, including many of those classified as mental illness, are not the I's chosen errors; they emerge from substrate features the I did not choose, environmental conditions the I did not control, and contact-driven dynamics that operated before the I had any intentional capacity to direct them. Patterns developing in mature operation may have contributions from the I's choices alongside continuing substrate and environmental factors; the share attributable to mature self-direction varies by condition, individual, and circumstance. The architecture is single-perspective, perspectivally realist about other I's, form-invariant across modeling I's conditional on their existence, and translatable into self-model theory, predictive processing, phenomenology, no-self traditions, extended mind, and embodied cognition without depending on any of them.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The diagnostic problem

Classical theories of selfhood mix together three coordinates that are formally and phenomenologically distinct.

The first is *contact*: what currently interacts with the I — the world impinging on perception, the body in current sensation, the environment causally engaging the subject. The second is *truth-indistinguishability*: what holds up under reasoning, what coheres, what cannot be denied without contradiction. The third is *identity-correlation*: what is part of who one is, what would alter the I's lived structure if removed, what is integrated into the network of things treated as me or mine.

In ordinary cases these three correlate. The body is currently contacted, presents itself as undeniably real, and is part of the self. A distant rumor is weakly contacted, weakly truth-stable, and not part of the self.

The dissociations are diagnostic:

- A *vivid hallucination* has high truth-indistinguishability and low identity-correlation: it appears undeniably real but is not me.
- An *anesthetized limb* has low contact and low truth-indistinguishability in the moment, but its identity-correlation remains high: the limb is mine even when I cannot feel it.
- *Depersonalization* shows preserved truth-indistinguishability with reduced identity-correlation: contents present themselves as real but no longer as me.
- A *phantom limb* shows low contact, variable truth-indistinguishability, and persistent high identity-correlation: the limb is mine without being there.
- *Alien clarity* — found in the "thought insertion" of certain psychiatric phenomena, but also in milder cases of inspiration not felt as one's own — shows high truth-indistinguishability with low identity-correlation: the content is undeniable, yet not mine.

These cases are difficult for any framework that operates with a single dimension of self-relation.

### 1.2 The two-layer move

This paper develops an architecture in which contact, truth-indistinguishability, and identity-correlation are three independent coordinates on the contents of experience. None reduces to the others. They correlate dynamically but the correlations are contingent, not definitional.

The architecture distinguishes two layers:

- An **intrinsic layer** containing mathematical, logical, and rational truth, treated as universal-by-reason-alone. Mathematical truths hold whether or not anyone reasons about them — the way a river bringing one stone next to another simply makes two stones, without anyone needing to count. Mathematical structure is *in re*, woven into the system the I is embedded in, regardless of whether any I cognizes it.

- An **asymptotic layer** containing everything else: all contents in the I's field, conscious or unconscious, that do not hold with universal-by-reason-alone necessity.

The cogito sits at the unique boundary between these layers. It asserts the indexed self-identity of the I — "I am I" — as a postulate, an indexed instance of universal reflexivity. Universal reflexivity ("x = x") holds in the intrinsic layer; its instance for *this particular I* is one perspectival step removed from universality, infinitesimally closer to truth than anything else in the asymptotic field. This is the I's unique position: φ = 1 - δ.

### 1.3 Developmental access and developmental emergence

Two developmental dimensions structure the architecture.

First, the I's *access* to intrinsic structure is acquired. Mathematics, logic, and reason hold in re — instantiated in the system regardless of whether any I cognizes them. But the I's capacity to recognize and apply intrinsic structure is built through interaction with a system that itself instantiates intrinsic forms. Object persistence is the indexed-developmental form of universal reflexivity; cause-and-effect the indexed-developmental form of universal implication. These are imprinted early because they instantiate at short temporal scales. More abstract intrinsic structures are imprinted later as systems are watched evolve. The architecture predicts a developmental escalator: short-timescale tools first, enabling perception of longer-timescale patterns.

Second, the lived I itself emerges through developmental modes — there is no fully-formed lived I at the moment of cogito-anchoring. Three modes of emergence operate, often concurrently: adversarial differentiation against sufficiently-distanced not-I, positive identification with what registers as continuous with self, and reflexive meta-reasoning by which the I takes its own structure as object of attention. Different I's, with different substrates and different environments, develop along different trajectories through these modes. Verification capacity therefore varies between I's; the architecture predicts variation rather than presupposing uniformity.

### 1.4 What the architecture is and is not

The architecture is **a formal taxonomy of identity-correlation**, not a freestanding theory of consciousness. Consciousness is presupposed — the fact that there is an I with a field of contents — and not defined or derived. Identity-correlation is taxonomized as a perspectival relation within the conscious field. The architecture provides a structure that disciplines the distinctions among contact, truth-likeness, and identity-integration; that derives the lived self from imprinting dynamics on a primal substrate; and that handles dissociation and boundary-shift cases through coordinate independence.

The architecture is **single-perspective**. Each instantiation is anchored to one modeling I. The architecture is form-invariant across modeling I's: any cogniting being can construct it for itself. But the architecture does not posit a view-from-nowhere lived self.

The architecture is **perspectivally realist about other I's**. It does not deny that other cogniting beings exist; it acknowledges that, from any one I's perspective, other I's are in primal not-I and are accessible only through apparent-I contents and inferential contents. The asymmetry is epistemic, not metaphysical.

The architecture is **descriptive, not prescriptive.** It describes structural patterns and predicts what conditions produce them. It does not assert that any developmental outcome, any verification toolset, or any pattern of mode-operation is normatively correct or incorrect. The architecture commits to the existence of a developmental window — ill-defined in its precise boundaries but real in its existence — during which conscious direction is insufficient to overcome substrate and environmental pressure. Patterns rooted in this window, including many of those classified as mental illness, are not the I's chosen errors: they emerge from substrate features the I did not choose, environmental conditions the I did not control, and contact-driven dynamics that operated before the I had any intentional capacity to direct them. Substrate constraints limit how much adult self-direction can subsequently modify these patterns. Patterns developing in mature operation, when the I has accumulated structure and self-directed focus capacity, may have meaningful contributions from the I's own choices — accidentally (when the I does not recognize the consequences of its choices), misintentionally (when the I makes choices it later regrets), or in rare cases more deliberately. The architecture's commitment is that a substantial portion of mental-illness phenomena originates inside the developmental window or has strong substrate components, with the share attributable to mature self-direction varying by condition, individual, and circumstance.

The architecture is **structural-classificatory rather than predictive at the individual encounter level**. Its commitments are about (a) what coordinates are independent of which others, (b) what modes of developmental actualization exist and what their structural consequences are, (c) what kinds of in re encounter drive verification-tool imprinting, (d) what kinds of boundary disturbances are possible. It does not commit to predicting, for a given encounter at a given time for a given I, which mode will fire or what specific outcome will result. Mode-selection is shaped by substrate, history, and stochastic elements that the architecture does not formalize.

The architecture is **honest about its posits**. Beyond the intrinsic layer and the cogito, it explicitly declares: primal not-I (with content multiplicity), time, body as not-I, contact interface, persistence relation across time, integration modes, and substrate-determined focus capacity. Each is named, justified as minimal, and acknowledged as not derivable from the cogito alone.

## 2. Foundational Commitments

### 2.1 The intrinsic layer (naturalist-realist)

Let $\mathsf{Int}$ denote the **intrinsic domain**:

$$\mathsf{Int} = \{\mathsf{Logic},\; \mathsf{Math},\; \mathsf{Reason}\}.$$

These are taken as **dual presentations of one structure**: reason is truth manifested as inferential capacity; truth is reason manifested as content. This is a substantive rationalist commitment, but it is naturalist-realist rather than Platonist-realist: the architecture commits to the system the I is embedded in containing pervasive mathematical and logical structure, instantiated throughout, regardless of whether any cogniting being verifies it. This is consistent with what the empirical sciences find at every scale — mathematical correlations in physics, biology, chemistry, statistics. The architecture does not require the stronger commitment that mathematical objects exist in a separate Platonic realm; it requires only that the system has structure the I can encounter.

The river example is diagnostic. When a river flows and brings one stone next to another, two stones are now present. The arithmetic 1 + 1 = 2 is realized in the structure of the situation, without anyone needing to count. Mathematics holds *in re*, not merely as something cogniting beings construct. The cognized instance — an I picks up one stone, picks up another, and reasons that there are two — is a separate phenomenon: the access to mathematics by an I, which depends on the I existing.

Mathematics and logic are therefore intrinsic in two senses simultaneously. They hold ontologically — true regardless of any I, woven into the structure of the system. They are universally accessible — any cogniting being can in principle reach them by reason, given sufficient developmental access. The first claim is about the system; the second is about reason. Both hold.

### 2.2 The three-tier φ-status structure

The architecture distinguishes three tiers of relationship to intrinsic universality:

**Tier 1: Intrinsic items.** Logic, mathematics, reason — including Fermat's Last Theorem, ZFC theorems, every valid mathematical and logical truth regardless of proof complexity. These are in re, ontologically true, universal-by-reason-alone. They are *outside the φ-domain*. The architecture does not assign them a φ value; they have a different status as intrinsic. (Earlier formulations stated "φ = 1 for intrinsic items" — this was shorthand. Properly, intrinsic items are not on the φ-scale at all; they are the ontological reference against which the φ-scale measures distance.)

**Tier 2: The cogito.** Sits at φ = 1 - δ *structurally-ontologically*. The position is determined by the perspectival-step relationship between universal reflexivity (∀x: x = x) and its indexed instance for a particular I (Î = Î). This is one perspectival step, exactly, for any cogniting being. The position holds regardless of which I is doing the indexing — it is structural, not I-relative. δ measures the perspectival cost specifically: the move from universal-content to perspectival-content.

**Tier 3: Other asymptotic content.** All non-intrinsic, non-cogito content. These have I-relative epistemic-φ values determined by the I's verification toolset, the conditional decomposition the I's tools support, and the empirical robustness of contents within those decompositions. Both n_{m,t}(x) and ε_{m,t}(x) are I-relative; the values are decomposition-dependent and toolset-dependent.

This three-tier structure resolves an over-specification in earlier formulations. Mathematical truths do not all sit at "the same φ value"; they are not on the φ-scale at all. The cogito has a structurally-determined position; other contents have I-relative positions. The architecture's invariant claims are about the *form* of the structure, not specific values within Tier 3.

### 2.3 The asymptotic structure: the infinitesimal δ

Let $\mathbb{K}$ be an ordered field extending $\mathbb{R}$. Fix a positive infinitesimal $\delta$ such that $0 < \delta < r$ for every positive real $r > 0$. The hyperreal numbers are one such field; any equivalent construction will do.

The value $1 - \delta$ marks **maximal indexed proximity to intrinsic universality**. δ is the cost of *one perspectival step* — one move from universal-content to its indexed instance for a particular I. Crucially, δ tracks *perspectival* indexing specifically, not all particularization. Mathematical instantiation (F(17) from ∀x F(x)) is within universal reasoning — 17 has no perspective, so the move stays in Tier 1. The cogito's instantiation (Î = Î from ∀x: x = x) crosses into perspectival territory — Î has a perspective, which makes the move structurally distinct from mathematical particularization.

For Tier 3 contents with apparent verification pathway, the layered structure is:

$$\phi_{m,t}(x) = 1 - n_{m,t}(x)\,\delta - \epsilon_{m,t}(x),$$

where $n_{m,t}(x) \geq 1$ counts the indexed conditional depth from universal reasoning to the content *given the I's current verification toolset*, and $\epsilon_{m,t}(x) \in [0, 1 - n_{m,t}(x)\,\delta]$ measures empirical robustness uncertainty within that depth. Both are I-relative and decomposition-dependent. The architecture does not claim a canonical decomposition that would fix n(x) across I's; the decomposition is determined by the I's toolset and the I's chosen way of analyzing the content.

This is a real concession compared to earlier formulations: n(x) is not I-invariant. What is invariant is the *form* of the structure (any I has some φ-structure with n-like and ε-like components, layered around a cogito at structural 1 - δ), not specific values for specific contents.

### 2.4 The cogito as tautological postulate

The cogito is taken as a **postulate**, not a derivation. The classical Cartesian formulation — "I think, therefore I am" — does not establish the I; it presupposes it (the thought presupposes the thinker). The tautological reformulation does not derive existence from logic either. It recognizes that existence-as-an-I is performatively self-evident — any move that would deny it presupposes the I performing the denial — and that the form of this self-evident existence is self-identity.

The cogito asserts:

$$\hat\iota_m = \hat\iota_m.$$

The I is itself. Reflexivity is the universal logical structure that characterizes this form. The cogito does not use universal reflexivity to derive the I's existence; it uses universal reflexivity to articulate what form the I's already-self-evident existence takes. The I is performatively prior; the recognition is that this prior I has reflexive structure; reflexivity gives the form of the recognition, not its source.

For modeling I $m$, the cogito gives:

$$\mathsf{Cogito}_m \Rightarrow \hat\iota_m, \qquad \phi(\hat\iota_m) = 1 - \delta, \qquad \rho_m(\hat\iota_m) = 1.$$

The cogito licenses only the I's self-identity. It does not license any specific content of the I — no body, no thoughts, no memories, no perceptual contents. It also does not license any developed verification capacity or any actualized lived I: the cogito gives bare cognitive capacity and the formal anchor of self-identity, not the toolset, the lived structure, or the boundary that come with developmental actualization. File 2 develops how those acquire.

The architecture is silent on what the I metaphysically is — substance, process, pattern, or otherwise — and treats this as out of scope. The commitment is structural: there is something that the cogito's "I" picks out, and that something has the form of self-identity. What it is in deeper metaphysical terms is a different paper.

### 2.5 Primal not-I as posit

The cogito and the intrinsic layer together do not yield content multiplicity, time, embodiment, or interaction. These cannot be derived from "I am I" plus mathematics; they must be posited.

**Primal not-I** $\mathsf{N}_m$ is the field of what exists independent of the modeling I. It includes:

- **Content multiplicity:** $|\mathsf{N}_m| \geq 2$.
- **Time:** $T_m$ with linear order $\leq_m$ and a developmental origin $0_{T_m}$.
- **Body:** $\mathsf{B}_m \subseteq \mathsf{N}_m$, a non-empty subset.
- **Contact interface:** $\chi_m: T_m \times \mathsf{N}_m \to [0, 1]$.
- **Optional other I's:** $\mathsf{O}_m \subseteq \mathsf{N}_m$.

Each posit is acknowledged as not derivable from the cogito plus mathematics.

### 2.6 The asymptotic layer contains conscious and unconscious contents

The asymptotic layer contains everything in the modeling I's field that is not intrinsic structure — items that present to the I, items that affect the I without presenting, items in the I's history no longer in current awareness, items that influence the lived-I network without phenomenal access.

Phenomenal availability and φ-applicability are distinct architectural features. The asymptotic layer contains contents that are phenomenally available to the I, contents that are latent or retrievable without being presently noticed, and contents or dispositions that affect the I without phenomenal availability. φ measures the constructible access to a content's intrinsic source or verification pathway through the I's current toolset; phenomenal availability measures whether the content is present-to-the-I at all. Let $A_{m,t}(x) \in \{0, 1\}$ be the phenomenal-availability status predicate, with $A_{m,t}(x) = 1$ meaning content $x$ is currently present-to the modeling I.

Conscious contents are phenomenally available ($A = 1$). They may have defined φ — *conscious-clear*, as in explicit reasoning, source-traceable memory, worked arithmetic — or low/undefined source-level φ — *conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque*, as in panic, dread, somatic affect, trauma-cue activation, or novel-but-not-yet-articulated content. Conscious-fuzzy contents are *fully conscious* but source-opaque; they are not "almost unconscious."

Latent or retrievable contents are not phenomenally available at the current moment ($A = 0$), but a φ-pathway is constructible upon activation. Stored facts, dormant skills, and available concepts fall here. They are not unconscious in the strong architectural sense merely because they are not presently noticed.

Unconscious-operative contents are not phenomenally available *and* lack a constructible source/verification pathway under the I's current toolset ($A = 0$, φ undefined), while still affecting κ, ρ, salience, action, disposition, or later profile-development. Implicit biases, osmotically formed dispositions, repressed contents, and substrate-level patterns operating below phenomenal availability fall here.

The conscious/unconscious distinction is therefore a property of the *joint configuration* of phenomenal availability and verification-pathway access — a property of the I's relationship to contents, not of contents themselves. Section 6.1 (File 3) formalizes the four-class taxonomy and the phenomenally available subset $\mathsf{A}^{+,m}_t$. The "no apparent verification pathway" judgment is *the I's view*, not an outside fact; whether a content has actual verification structure invisible to the I is outside-perspective and unavailable to the I from inside. The architecture is honest about this limit.

This treatment has two consequences. First, the conscious/unconscious distinction is a property of the I's relationship to contents, not of contents themselves; contents can move between classes as $A$ and φ change with attention, toolset, and activation. Second, ρ and κ apply uniformly across the asymptotic field. A repressed memory may have high ρ without having any defined φ; a panic episode may be phenomenally available with extreme κ while φ remains undefined.

Osmotic imprinting (Section 8.7, File 3) is substrate-level pattern formation through repeated co-occurrence in ambient experience, operating continuously without requiring focus or verification-pathway construction. It is transverse to the four-class $A$/φ taxonomy: some osmotically formed patterns remain unconscious-operative, affecting κ, ρ, salience, action, or disposition without phenomenal availability; others become conscious-fuzzy / source-opaque when activated, as in "I don't know why I feel this way about X" — the activation is phenomenally available but its source-pathway remains opaque; still others may later become conscious-clear when a verification pathway is articulated. Osmotic imprinting can also populate the substrate with proto-patterns that later become accessible as explicit verification tools.

### 2.7 Perspectival anchoring

The architecture is constructed from the perspective of one modeling I. The modeling I is cogito-direct to itself; other I's, if they exist, are part of primal not-I from the modeling I's perspective. This is not solipsism. The shared object across modeling I's is not a lived self but the formal architecture itself, which belongs to the intrinsic layer.

### 2.9 Consciousness and identity-correlation

The architecture distinguishes consciousness from identity-correlation. These are not the same thing, and the architecture is not a theory of consciousness — it is a theory of identity-correlation as a structural relation within consciousness.

For purposes of this architecture, *consciousness* is presupposed as the fact that there is an I to whom phenomenal contents appear. The architecture does not define, reduce, or derive consciousness. What it takes for granted is that for any modeling I $m$, there is an asymptotic content domain $\mathsf{A}^m_t$ at each time and a phenomenally available subset $\mathsf{A}^{+,m}_t \subseteq \mathsf{A}^m_t$ that is present-to-the-I. The total asymptotic domain $\mathsf{A}^m_t$ also contains latent/retrievable and unconscious-operative contents that affect the I (κ, ρ, action, disposition) without currently appearing phenomenally; these influence the lived-I network without being undergone or noticeable.

*Identity-correlation* is a perspectival relation between the modeling I and items in its field. It is indexed to the I and to time:

$$\rho_{m,t}(x).$$

This expresses how identity-correlated item $x$ is with the modeling I $m$ at time $t$. The relation is perspectival — it is from I $m$'s frame, not absolute. The same item $x$ may have different ρ values for different I's.

The core I has maximal identity-correlation with itself by anchoring convention:

$$\rho_{m,t}(\hat\iota_m) = 1.$$

For all other contents:

$$0 \leq \rho_{m,t}(x) < 1.$$

The strict inequality holds because non-core integration capacity is finite, and $\rho = C/(1+C) < 1$ for all finite $C$ (see File 3, Section 7.2 for the formalization).

The architecture does **not** claim:

- consciousness equals identity-correlation
- ρ measures degree of consciousness
- conscious entities can be ranked by overall ρ-strength
- having a higher ρ-profile makes one more conscious

The architecture **does** claim:

- consciousness involves an I for whom identity-correlation can be measured across the I's field
- identity-correlation is one structural coordinate by which the contents of a conscious field are taxonomized
- the architecture's predictive and explanatory work is about the structure of self-relation within consciousness, not about consciousness itself

A parallel distinction holds within identity-correlation itself. $\rho_{m,t}(x)$ is a structural measure of integration into the directed lived-I network rooted at the cogito-anchor (formally, max-flow over weighted graphs — see Section 7.6, File 3). The anchoring $\rho_m(\hat\iota_m) = 1$ is a mathematical normalization: the network must have a root for max-flow to be defined, and the cogito-I is the natural root.

The architecture does **not** claim:

- ρ measures preservation-priority (what the I would sacrifice for what)
- ρ measures valuation (what the I treats as most valuable)
- ρ measures action-priority (what drives the I's choices under existential pressure)
- the cogito-I, by virtue of $\rho_m(\hat\iota_m) = 1$, is what the I would prioritize preserving over all else

The architecture **does** claim:

- ρ measures structural integration into the lived-I network
- the cogito-I's $\rho = 1$ is anchoring convention, not preservation-primacy
- high-ρ non-core network members can drive action that sacrifices the substrate conditions for the modeling I's continued operation; this is consistent with the architecture rather than a counterexample to it
- action under existential pressure depends on additional dynamics (third-order pathway response, future-state projection across the lived-I, substrate-level urgency) that the architecture does not yet formalize

The self-sacrifice case — a parent dying to save a child, a sibling for a sibling, a soldier for a comrade — is the canonical instance. It is treated as an architectural test case in Section 10.7, File 4. The architecture handles it without modification once structural integration and preservation-priority are kept distinct.

The taxonomy is therefore about *degrees of self-relation inside consciousness*, not about *degrees of consciousness*. A conscious I has, at any time t, a complete identity-correlation profile (formally introduced in File 3, Section 7.6). The profile, not single ρ values, is the architecture's primary structural object.

This distinction has consequences for several downstream commitments. Mental-illness conditions (File 4, Section 10.5) are profile-shape disturbances rather than ρ-magnitude problems. The exclusions (File 4, Section 11.2) explicitly rule out the consciousness-as-identity-correlation reading. The conclusion (File 5, Section 14) frames the thesis around profile-as-primary-object rather than around scalar ρ measures.
