## 12. Translations and Tensions

The architecture maps onto several prior frameworks. None is a dependence; each is a structural correspondence the architecture admits when interpreted in a particular way. This section also surfaces tensions where the architecture takes sides rather than translating neutrally.

**Convergences:**

- **Self-model theory (Metzinger / Friston / Seth).** The lived I is the content of the phenomenal self-model.
- **Psychological continuity (Parfit / Schechtman).** $\sim^m_H$ is the cross-temporal identity relation.
- **Phenomenology (Husserl / Zahavi / Merleau-Ponty).** φ ≈ evidential force; ρ ≈ ego-relevance; κ ≈ embodied contact.
- **Hegelian / Sartrean / Lacanian self-through-other.** Mode A's adversarial differentiation has structural cousins in master-slave dialectic, "the look," mirror stage, I-Thou.
- **No-self traditions (Buddhist analytical philosophy / Albahari).** ρ-structure is the constructed self.
- **Extended mind (Clark / Chalmers).** Contents whose source maps extend beyond the body can have non-trivial ρ.
- **Embodied cognition.** Substrate-bound focus capacity encodes embodiment as constitutive. The order-typology of Section 8.5 (File 3) makes the embodied-cognition mapping more specific: third-order imprinting is the architecture's structural account of body-mediated content, with the lossy-compression observation explaining why bodily content has the phenomenological signatures embodied-cognition theorists describe.
- **Naturalist epistemology of mathematics.** Verification tools acquired through encounter with a system that itself instantiates intrinsic structure.
- **Foundationalism in epistemology.** The cogito is the foundational indexed truth.
- **Modal logic and proof theory.** δ-depth corresponds to nested indexing operators.
- **Developmental psychology.** Temporal-scale escalator predicts typical developmental order; Mode B structural-onset constraints predict variation across cogniting beings without specifying distributional shape, with the empirical distribution itself an open question for application work.
- **Predictive processing (information-bottleneck variants).** The lossy-compression observation maps onto predictive-coding accounts that treat perception and cognition as compression hierarchies. The architecture is consistent with this picture but does not depend on it; the orders are characteristic patterns of substrate-mediation, formalizable in predictive-processing terms but also in other frameworks.
- **Classical conditioning and Pavlovian learning theory.** Osmotic imprinting (Section 8.7, File 3) is the structural form of associative learning at substrate level. Pavlovian conditioning is the canonical pure-substrate case; the architecture predicts the structural form of association formation without committing to specific learning-rate parameters.
- **Implicit attitudes and implicit cognition (IAT, dual-process theories).** The implicit/explicit cognition distinction has direct architectural cash-out: implicit cognition operates through osmotically-imprinted substrate patterns with undefined φ; explicit cognition operates through verification-pathway construction. The architecture predicts that implicit and explicit cognition can diverge (osmotically-imprinted patterns that conflict with explicit verification commitments) and that explicit anti-bias commitments do not directly modify substrate patterns — substrate-level engagement is required. This matches the empirical robustness of implicit attitudes despite explicit anti-bias commitments.
- **Attachment theory (Bowlby, Ainsworth, modern developments).** Attachment patterns are substantially osmotically imprinted in early development through repeated co-occurrence between caregiver-presence and somatic-affective states. The architecture's account of third-order pathway operation combined with osmotic imprinting matches attachment theory's core observations about pattern-formation and pattern-persistence.
- **Tacit knowledge and expertise (Polanyi; Dreyfus on skill acquisition).** Expertise's tacit dimension — knowing how without being able to articulate the rules — corresponds to osmotically-imprinted substrate patterns that ground skilled performance. The architecture's developmental arc from substrate-held proto-pattern to explicitly-deployable tool matches the phenomenology of skill acquisition through long exposure.
- **Phenomenology of mood and affect (Heidegger; Ratcliffe).** The fuzziness-as-low-source-resolution observation tracks phenomenological accounts of mood that distinguish intensity from articulability. The architecture is structural rather than phenomenological in method, but the structural prediction matches the phenomenological description.

**Tensions and incompatibilities:**

The architecture takes substantive positions that are incompatible with some prior frameworks:

- **Humean bundle theory.** The architecture posits a structurally-positioned cogito with $\rho = 1$ as a unique anchor. This is incompatible with bundle theories that deny any privileged self-pole. The architecture's commitment is rationalist on this point; it does not translate into Humean frameworks without losing the cogito's special status.
- **Strong eliminativism about selves.** The architecture treats identity-correlation as a real structural feature of conscious experience. Eliminativists who treat selfhood as folk-psychological residue cannot translate the architecture without giving up its core commitments.
- **Pure formalism / fictionalism about mathematics.** The architecture's naturalist-realist commitment to in re mathematical structure is incompatible with frameworks that treat mathematics as purely formal manipulation or convenient fiction. The architecture sides with realism about mathematical structure (in the naturalist, not Platonist, sense).
- **Strong predictive processing accounts of φ.** Under strong predictive-processing readings, all of φ would be predictive confidence. The architecture's three-tier φ-status structure (intrinsic items outside φ; cogito at structural 1 - δ; Tier 3 I-relative) is incompatible with reducing φ to predictive confidence alone.
- **Strong introspective rationalism.** Although meta-reasoning is one of the three encounter kinds, the architecture constrains meta-reasoning by the developmental escalator: it cannot bootstrap from nothing. Frameworks that treat introspection as a self-sufficient route to mathematical knowledge are incompatible with this constraint.
- **Reductive identifications of consciousness with identity-correlation.** Frameworks that equate selfhood-strength with consciousness-degree, or that read consciousness as something the architecture's ρ-coordinate measures, are incompatible with the consciousness-is-presupposed commitment in Section 2.9 (File 1).

The architecture's translatability is real but bounded. It maps onto multiple prior frameworks under specific interpretive readings but takes substantive positions that some frameworks reject. The convergences are not curated; they are genuine structural correspondences. The tensions are also genuine; they reflect real philosophical commitments the architecture makes.

## 13. Open Problems and Limitations

The architecture has explicit free functions and explicit declared posits. Several questions arise; this section distinguishes those that are *out of scope by architectural design*, those that are *working assumptions* the architecture rests on, and those that are *genuine open problems within the architecture's scope* — questions the architecture could in principle answer with further development but currently does not.

### 13.1 Out-of-scope by architectural design

These are not gaps the architecture is failing to fill; they are questions outside what a single-perspective architecture can address.

**The criterion for membership in $\mathsf{O}_m$.** What makes some $n \in \mathsf{N}_m$ an actual other I rather than merely an apparent-I content? This is an outside-perspective question. The architecture is single-perspective; from any one I's perspective, the actual existence of other cogniting beings is not verifiable from inside the I's own apparatus. The architecture *assumes* other I's exist in actuality (perspectival realism, Section 9.4, File 4) — it does not deny that other cogniting beings are real — but acknowledges that the modeling I cannot establish this from within its own perspective. No single-perspective architecture could; this is not a defect to be remedied but a structural feature of the perspectival framing the architecture commits to.

**Direction of time at the physics level.** Why thermodynamics has an arrow, why causation is asymmetric, why entropy increases. These are physics problems, not phenomenology problems; the architecture's treatment of temporal experience (File 6) addresses the felt asymmetry without claiming to derive temporal direction from phenomenology.

**The metaphysics of the I.** What the I substantively is — substance, process, pattern, computational fixed-point, illusion, or otherwise. The architecture commits only to the structural fact of indexed self-identity; what that index points to metaphysically is a different paper (Section 2.4, File 1).

**Physical implementation.** What physical structures realize the architecture's relations. The architecture is formal; whether brains, bodies, computers, or other physical systems implement substrate is an empirical question the architecture does not address.

### 13.2 Working assumptions

These are foundational positions the architecture takes. They are not derived within this paper; they are stated as working commitments. Future work might argue for them more rigorously or motivate alternative framings, but the current architecture rests on them as posits.

**The intrinsic layer.** Naturalist-realism about pervasive mathematical structure in the system the I is embedded in (Section 2.1, File 1). The architecture takes this as a foundational assumption consistent with how empirical sciences treat the universe and with major naturalist philosophical accounts. The river example and the appeal to pervasive mathematical correlations in the system are motivations for the assumption, not derivations of it. The architecture stands on this assumption as a working commitment; its dependence on the assumption is acknowledged rather than concealed.

**The cogito as postulate.** The tautological "I = I" is taken as a postulate, not derived (Section 2.4, File 1). The architecture argues that any move that would deny the I presupposes the I performing the denial, but treats this performative self-evidence as the basis for the postulate rather than as a derivation from more basic commitments.

**The primal not-I posits.** Content multiplicity, time, body, contact interface, optional other I's (Section 2.5, File 1). Each is acknowledged as not derivable from the cogito plus mathematics; each is named as a working posit.

**The profile-preservation principle.** The operating lived-I tends to preserve or restore coherent continuation of its identity-correlation profile under encounter and projected action, subject to disturbance, inaccurate inputs, and competing prerogative dynamics. This is a working assumption, not derivable from the bare cogito alone; it is the condition under which the lived-I remains the same lived-I through change rather than dissolving into arbitrary content-flow.

**Agency in action-space.** The lived I operates not merely as a field of contents but as an agent situated among possible actions and projected future states. This is a working assumption distinct from the cogito and primal not-I posits; the bare cogito gives only indexed self-identity, not action-orientation under projected futures. In the Lean v0.4 formalization, this is represented by a deterministic projection structure `ProjectMap α Act`, where `Act` is an arbitrary action carrier with a distinguished no-action element and a projection map from actions and current profiles to projected profiles. The old singleton-like action wrapper is only a degenerate default case.

**The structural prerogatives of lived consciousness.** The architecture identifies two structural prerogatives — the prerogative of consistency (PtCns; Section 4.2, generalized in 9.5, File 4) and the prerogative of continued existence (PCE; Section 4.8, File 2) — as fundamental features of lived consciousness. Both are treated as theorem-candidates relative to the operating lived-I apparatus together with the profile-preservation principle and the agency/action-space assumption above; neither is derivable from the bare cogito alone. PtCns is profile-preservation under encounter (providing the consistency-standard for Modes A and C). PCE is profile-preservation under future-directed action (providing the continuation-target under existential pressure). The verified foundation-default `PCE` is action-constant over arbitrary `Act`; action-sensitive continuation appraisal is represented by the application-ready `ProjectedPCE` layer. The full formal derivation of both prerogatives from the operating architecture together with the named assumptions is application-level work, planned for a future paper; at the foundation level the load-bearing assumptions are made explicit so the prerogatives are neither smuggled in as cogito-consequences nor presented as freestanding posits. Their deeper grounding (in evolved substrate properties, in deeper metaphysical features of cogniting beings, or otherwise) remains out of scope. Neither is metaphysically inviolable.

### 13.3 Genuine open problems within scope

These are questions the architecture could in principle address with further development but currently does not.

**The structure of integration modes K and the choice of weights $a_k$.** Underspecified; different choices yield different applied predictions.

**The choice of max-flow as the ρ-aggregation.** Defensible (matches phenomenology of fragility) but not strictly forced; alternatives are compatible with the foundation.

**The detailed mechanism of mode-selection.** What determines whether Mode A or Mode C fires for a given encounter? The architecture treats this as outside the foundation's predictive scope, governed by substrate/history/environment/stochastic elements; full formalization is application-level.

**The detailed cognitive mechanism of shadow-recognition.** The three forms (known pattern in unexpected location; composite of knowns; sub-decomposition) operationally specify the kinds of events. The substrate-level structural mechanism is partially specified by the osmotic-imprinting machinery (Section 8.7, File 3; Section 3.3, File 2): patterns recognized in shadow encounters are often osmotically held in substrate before explicit recognition, and shadow encounter is in part the I gaining explicit access to substrate-held patterns. What remains application-level is the cognitive-operations layer — attention dynamics, memory-retrieval mechanics, the specific substrate-to-explicit transition operations.

**Prerogative arbitration.** PtCns and PCE can conflict, especially in grief (a high-ρ network member's death produces tension between PtCns pushing the profile toward coherence with the death-event and PCE pushing to preserve the maximally-self-defined I that included the dead member at high ρ), self-sacrifice, trauma, depressive profile-shape disturbance, and cases where preserving local profile-coherence undermines projected continuation. The foundation distinguishes the prerogatives but does not provide a general arbitration rule for which prerogative dominates in a given action-event. PtCns can preserve local coherence while worsening global profile health; PCE can be misdirected by inaccurate future-state projections; PCE can be defeated by profile disturbance; PCE can be routed through a damaged preservation-ranking $\Pi_{m,t}$. Application work must treat prerogative dominance as substrate-, history-, and context-sensitive rather than assuming automatic PCE or PtCns priority.

**The relation between time and space.** The architecture is neutral.

**Pathological cases beyond those treated in Section 10.5.** Application-level work.

**Empirical anchoring.** Specific predictions require committing to specific instantiations of the free functions.

**The relative weighting of orders in mixed-pathway content.** Many actual contents involve all three pathways simultaneously: a felt sense (third-order) accompanied by an articulable thought about it (second-order) directing focus toward something in the environment (first-order). The architecture treats the orders as dominant-pathway descriptions, but the dynamics of mixed-pathway contents — how the three pathway-contributions combine, conflict, or reinforce — are not yet specified in detail.

**The compression characteristics of specific substrate components.** The general lossy-compression observation does not specify what features each substrate component preserves and which it loses. This is application-level work that connects the architecture to specific empirical findings about cognitive processing, somatic-cognitive integration, and the structure of subjective experience.

**Variation in compression characteristics across I's.** Different I's may have different compression profiles based on substrate variation, training (interoceptive, contemplative, expert), neurological condition, and so on. The architecture predicts variation but does not specify the dimensions of variation in detail. Trained inside-perspective access to third-order content (interoceptive expertise, somatic skill, contemplative practice) is empirically robust and the architecture should ultimately be able to characterize it structurally.

**Action-priority dynamics under existential pressure.** What determines the I's choices under existential pressure — self-sacrifice cases, urgent action under threat, irreversible choices? The architecture identifies three contributing factors (third-order pathway activation, future-state projection across the lived-I, substrate-level urgency responses; Section 10.7, File 4) but does not formalize how they combine to produce specific action choices. ρ measures structural integration, not preservation-priority; the dynamics of action-priority under pressure are application-level work, possibly drawing on affective neuroscience, decision-theoretic frameworks, and the phenomenology of urgency.

**The distributional shape of Mode B onset across cogniting beings.** The architecture commits to structural-onset constraints (zero at developmental origin, non-zero later, variation across I's) but does not currently derive a specific distributional shape. Bell-shaped distributions are intuitive but not forced; log-normal, gamma, Weibull, sigmoid-CDF, or mixture distributions could equally fit the structural constraints. The empirical distribution is application-level work engaging developmental psychology and individual-differences literature.

**Phenomena the architecture does not yet address.** The foundation explicitly does not derive: the felt continuous-stream character of experience; dreams and altered states (sleep, anesthesia, intoxication, meditation, psychedelics); narrative self distinct from lived-I network; differentiation among specific affective states (shame vs guilt vs embarrassment); empathy and emotional contagion; body schema and proprioceptive integration; agency phenomenology; aesthetic experience; religious and contemplative experience; the phenomenology of understanding. Application papers can extend the foundation to these phenomena; the foundation does not currently treat them.

## 14. Conclusion

The architecture is a formal taxonomy of identity-correlation, anchored to one modeling I, built from the tautological cogito and naturalist-realist commitment to in re mathematical structure plus a small set of declared posits in primal not-I. Its central claim is that contact, truth-indistinguishability, and identity-correlation are three independent coordinates on the contents of experience, with identity-correlation defined as max-flow integration over a directed weighted graph rooted at the cogito-given core. The architecture's primary structural object is the identity-correlation profile $P_{m,t}$, which encodes the I's complete relational structure to its field at any time.

The architecture's contribution is structural rather than metaphysical. It disciplines the distinctions among the things classical theories of selfhood mix together. It derives the lived self developmentally — through three modes of emergence (adversarial differentiation, positive identification, reflexive meta-reasoning) operating on substrate that determines the I's basic capacity. It handles dissociation cases, boundary-shifts, and the structural patterns sometimes classified as mental illness through coordinate independence and profile-shape disturbance dynamics. It distinguishes consciousness (presupposed) from identity-correlation (the perspectival relation taxonomized).

The architecture is a foundation in development. Its commitments are accumulated through iterative work grounded in direct phenomenological access by the author and refined under adversarial pressure. The iterative development is consistent with the architecture's own account of how cogniting beings acquire and refine verification capacity. The work is foundation-building, not finalization. File 6 begins the application work with temporal phenomenology; subsequent application papers will address narrative self, differentiated affect, dreams and altered states, intersubjectivity beyond the prerogative of consistency, agency phenomenology, aesthetic experience, and the formal derivation of the structural prerogatives from the operating lived-I architecture.

The architecture survives the most pointed critiques when its commitments are stated correctly and its scope is acknowledged honestly. Where critiques have surfaced real problems (over-specification of φ, under-specification of shadow recognition, conflation of in re with strong Platonism, lack of explicit non-prescriptive methodological framing, conflation of consciousness with identity-correlation, over-strong responsibility-disclaimer, treatment of meta-reasoning as purely introspective, treatment of the prerogative of consistency as inter-I-specific, freestanding distributional claims about Mode B onset), they have been addressed in this version. Where critiques have surfaced substantive philosophical commitments the architecture genuinely makes (rationalism about intrinsic structure, perspectival anchoring, structural rather than predictive scope), the architecture stands on those commitments rather than evading them.

**Final thesis sentence:**

> Conscious entities are organized around a core I. The core I has maximal identity-correlation with itself, while the contents, bodies, memories, roles, perceptions, thoughts, and other apparent beings in its field vary in their degree of identity-correlation to that I. Consciousness is therefore not identical to identity-correlation; identity-correlation is one structural coordinate by which the contents of a conscious field are taxonomized. The architecture is an intrinsic mathematical-logical form discoverable by any cogniting being through reason, with the system the I is embedded in containing pervasive mathematical correlations that the I encounters through its embedding. Each I builds its access to the intrinsic layer through encounter with the system's structure, and each I's lived structure emerges through three developmental modes — adversarial differentiation that establishes the boundary (with the prerogative of consistency as fundamental component), positive identification that populates the inside, and reflexive meta-reasoning that refines the whole through self-attention (with both pure and externally-mediated forms). The architecture's primary descriptive object is the identity-correlation profile $P_{m,t}$, anchored at the cogito-given core and extending outward through the lived-I network into the strict not-I limit. This profile, structured by the three independent coordinates κ, φ, ρ, shaped dynamically by substrate-bound focus, contact-effects, historical integration, the I's accumulating toolset, the developmental modes that produced the lived I in the first place, and the order-typology of imprinting pathways through which contents reach the I, is what the architecture taxonomizes. Consciousness is the field within which this taxonomy operates, presupposed rather than derived. The architecture is a foundation in development, accumulated through dialectical work consistent with its own account of how cogniting beings acquire and refine verification capacity, presented as a working basis for further application, stress-testing, and translation.
